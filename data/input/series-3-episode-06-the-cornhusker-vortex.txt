		<h2 class="title">Series 3 Episode 06 &#8211; The Cornhusker&nbsp;Vortex</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The stairwell. The guys are carrying kites.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Kites, ho!</p>
<p>Howard: Kites ho!</p>
<p>Raj: Kites ho!</p>
<p>All three: Kites, ho!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Excuse me. You&#8217;re misusing the word ho. It&#8217;s an interjection used to call attention to a destination, not an object, as in, uh, Land, ho! Or, uh, Westward, ho!</p>
<p>The three guys: Kites, ho!</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, guys. What you doing? Going out to discover electricity?</p>
<p>Leonard: If you&#8217;re referring to the work of Benjamin Franklin, he did not discover electricity, he merely used a kite to determine that lightning consists of electricity. He also invented the Franklin stove, bifocals and the flexible urinary catheter. Kites, ho.</p>
<p>Leonard: We&#8217;re heading out for some kite fighting.</p>
<p>Penny: Kite fighting?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, yeah. It&#8217;s an extremely competitive, cutthroat sport.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, actually, the risk of throat cutting is very low. On the other hand, severe string burn is a real and ever-Present danger.</p>
<p>Leonard: You want to come watch?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, gee, sounds amazing, but, um, I&#8217;ve got some friends coming over. Not a big thing, we&#8217;re just gonna watch the Nebraska game.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh. Football, sure.</p>
<p>Howard: Good guess.</p>
<p>Penny: I would&#8217;ve invited you, but I know you&#8217;re not a football fan.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, no, I&#8217;m not, so, great. You&#8217;ve got plans doing something you like, I&#8217;ve got plans doing something I like, so it&#8217;s good.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, maybe we&#8217;ll hang out later, you know, after everybody&#8217;s gone.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, great.</p>
<p>Penny: See ya.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, this sucks.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry, I got bored and drifted off. Where exactly in the interaction did the sucking lie?</p>
<p>Raj: Leonard just realized that Penny&#8217;s been hiding him from her friends because he&#8217;s a tiny, little man who flies kites.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, that certainly would suck.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence. </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>Scene: The park.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Wolowitz is trying to outflank us. Let out some string, add altitude and I&#8217;ll go under and cut his line.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why wouldn&#8217;t Penny want her friends to meet me?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Focus, Leonard, focus! The heat of battle is upon us, the dogs of war are unleashed.</p>
<p>Leonard: Maybe Koothrappali&#8217;s right, maybe I embarrass her.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You&#8217;re embarrassing me right now, a grown man worrying about such nonsense when in the middle of flying kites.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sorry.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Sorry won&#8217;t bring their kites down. Ow! String burn! String burn!</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, they think we&#8217;re flanking, they&#8217;re playing right into our hands. On the count of three, we execute the flying scissor. One, two&#8230;</p>
<p>Howard: Whoa! Did you see that?</p>
<p>Raj: See what?</p>
<p>Howard: That chick, she smiled at me.</p>
<p>Raj: No, she didn&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Howard: Yes, she did.</p>
<p>Raj: Come on, scissors, scissors!</p>
<p>Howard: Hold my line.</p>
<p>Raj: Wait, what are you doing? I can&#8217;t scissors by myself! Howard! Come back!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Victory!</p>
<p>Raj: Son of a bitch.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Leonard’s car.</em></p>
<p>Raj: You&#8217;re a sucky friend, you know that? A sucky, sucky friend.</p>
<p>Howard: What was I supposed to do? She gave me that come-hither look.</p>
<p>Raj: If she gave you any look at l. It was a you suck look.</p>
<p>Howard: I would&#8217;ve caught up to her if I hadn&#8217;t pulled a hammy.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, please, you weigh 80 pounds. You don&#8217;t have a hammy.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, Penny doesn&#8217;t want me around her friends, I embarrass her. What else could it be?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, her actions could be out of concern for your feelings. Perhaps she&#8217;s excluded you from these gatherings because she&#8217;s scouting for a new mate and doesn&#8217;t want to do it in front of you.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, how kind of her.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Agreed. Most primates don&#8217;t show that sort of discretion. A female bonobo will copulate with a new male in front of the old one without so much as a how do you do?</p>
<p>Raj: You always do this, you know? You ditch me for a woman you don&#8217;t have a shot with.</p>
<p>Howard: I totally had a shot.</p>
<p>Raj: With a woman you were chasing through a park? That&#8217;s not a shot, that&#8217;s a felony. What&#8217;s worse, it cost me my prized Patang fighting kite. Sheldon, I don&#8217;t suppose there&#8217;s any chance you could give me my kite back?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry, Raj, but the rules of aerial warfare dictate at the fallen kite go to the victor. And without rules, the competition has no meaning. And without meaning, the following would be an empty gesture. <em>(Sings) </em>I have your kite. I have your kite.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny’s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Hey.</p>
<p>Penny: Hi.</p>
<p>Leonard: How was your football party?</p>
<p>Penny: It was pretty good. We won.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, that&#8217;s excellent. It&#8217;s a weird figure of speech, isn&#8217;t it, we won when you weren&#8217;t</p>
<p>actually playing. When we watch Star Wars, we don&#8217;t say, we defeated the Empire.</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m glad to hear it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, hey, on a related subject, are you embarrassed to have me around your friends?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, my god, no. Why would you ask that?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, you know, I just noticed I haven’t really met any of them.</p>
<p>Penny: Sure you have.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, yeah, no, I met the huge ex-boyfriend and the smaller yet still larger than me ex-boyfriend. Were they here today?</p>
<p>Penny: Of course not.</p>
<p>Leonard: Of course not. Why would they be? Why would I ask? Why am I rambling? Why don&#8217;t you stop me?</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, look, if you want to meet my friends, that would be great. I just, you know, I didn&#8217;t want you to be bored.</p>
<p>Leonard: I wouldn&#8217;t be bored. Why would I be bored?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, &#8217;cause they&#8217;re not genius scientists.</p>
<p>Leonard: Penny, I like all sorts of people. In fact, some of my best friends aren&#8217;t geniuses.</p>
<p>Penny: Like who?</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, some of my Facebook friends aren&#8217;t geniuses. My point is, if we&#8217;re going to be a couple, I should be friends with your friends.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, great. Well, then why don&#8217;t you come over next Saturday and watch the game with us.</p>
<p>Leonard: Another football game?</p>
<p>Penny: They have them every week.</p>
<p>Leonard: Did not know that.</p>
<p>Penny: You wanted to meet my friends.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sure, sure, just I don&#8217;t know much about football.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, that&#8217;s okay, a lot of the guys&#8217; girlfriends don&#8217;t know football. They just kind of drink and talk in the kitchen.</p>
<p>Leonard: Great.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. Leonard and Raj are watching a football game on the television. </em></p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, a complete pass. First down, New England. I think I&#8217;m starting to get this.</p>
<p>Raj: Really? The only thing I&#8217;ve learned in the last two hours is that American men love drinking beer, pee too often and have trouble getting erections.</p>
<p>Leonard: Focus on the game, not the commercials, Raj.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m just saying, maybe if you people cut back on the beer, you could get out of the bathroom and satisfy your women without pharmaceutical help.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(arriving)</em>: Raj, what are you doing here? You were supposed to help me pimp out my Vespa.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m sorry, are you under the impression that we&#8217;re still friends?</p>
<p>Howard: Oh come on, you&#8217;re not still grinding on the kite thing, are you?</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s not just the kite thing. Every time we go some place, you think you can just dump me whenever someone prettier comes along, even though you don&#8217;t have a shot with them.</p>
<p>Howard: But I had a shot with that jogger.</p>
<p>Raj: Fine. Paint green flames on your little scooter with her. <em>(Leaves)</em></p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s not a little scooter. It&#8217;s the second biggest Vespa they make! Are you watching football?</p>
<p>Leonard: There&#8217;s no fooling you. Now, what is this sacks statistic they put up there?</p>
<p>Howard: All I know about Saks is, my mother shops there.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sacks, sacks&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s football nomenclature for when a quarterback is tackled behind the line of scrimmage.</p>
<p>Leonard: Huh&#8230; Scrimmage&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: The line of scrimmage is the imaginary transverse line separating the offence from the defence.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh.</p>
<p>Howard: Sheldon knows football?</p>
<p>Leonard: Apparently.</p>
<p>Howard: I mean, Quidditch, sure. But football?</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, how do you know this stuff?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I grew up in Texas. Football is ubiquitous in Texas. Pro football, college football, high school football, peewee football, in fact, every form of football except the original, European football, which most Texans believe to be a Commie plot.</p>
<p>Leonard: Unbelievable.</p>
<p>Sheldon: If you&#8217;re interested, I also know all about frying meat that isn&#8217;t chicken as if it were chicken.</p>
<p>Leonard: So you could teach me?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Football or chicken-fried meats?</p>
<p>Leonard: Football. I&#8217;m going to Penny&#8217;s on Saturday to watch a game with her friends and I don&#8217;t want to look like an idiot. I want to blend in.</p>
<p>Sheldon: If you want to blend in with Penny&#8217;s friends, I&#8217;d think looking like an idiot would be the perfect camouflage.</p>
<p>Leonard: Come on, Sheldon, please teach me about football. It&#8217;ll be fun.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s exactly what my father said. Come to the games, watch the games, week in and week out from the time I was five until I went off to college. Longest seven years of my life.</p>
<p>Leonard: Please, I&#8217;m asking you as a friend.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Are you making this a tier one friendship request?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Fine.</p>
<p>Leonard: I really appreciate this.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, yeah. All right, Poindexter, sit down, shut up and listen.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m sorry?</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s how my father always began our football conversations. And if you&#8217;d like, after the game, I’ll take you outside and teach you how to shoot close enough to a racoon that it craps itself.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The kitchen. Sheldon is making toast in a cylon toaster which burns an image of a cylon onto the toast. </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Leonard: When are you going to stop making Cylon toast?</p>
<p>Sheldon: When I have enough to destroy all the human toast on the battlestar known as Galactica. Is that what you&#8217;re wearing to watch football at Penny&#8217;s?</p>
<p>Leonard: What&#8217;s wrong with a football jersey?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Nothing. That, however, appears to be a football cocktail dress.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;s the smallest size they had, except the one for dogs. I can&#8217;t believe they had one for dogs.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, yes. Canine football fans are a common sight in Texas. Cats, however, refuse to wear sporting apparel. My sister found that out the hard way.</p>
<p>Leonard: Anyway, wish me luck.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, wait. Am I correct in assuming that your attempt to be accepted by Penny’s peers is based on your desire to ensure your continuing mating privileges with her?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, I wouldn&#8217;t put it exactly that way.</p>
<p>Sheldon: How would you put it?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, okay, like you said.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Huh. Seems like an awful lot of trouble to go through for intercourse. Don&#8217;t you have access to women who will do it for money? By the way, another accepted usage for the term ho.</p>
<p>Leonard: Good-Bye, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hold on I believe that social convention dictate you not arrive empty-Handed. Would you like to bring some Cylon toast?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, no, I&#8217;m trying to fit in, not get laughed at.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What&#8217;s funny about Cylon toast?</p>
<p><em>Scene: Raj’s apartment. Raj is watching a Bollywood movie. There is a knock on the door.</em></p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s open.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, pal.</p>
<p>Raj: What do you want?</p>
<p>Howard: I brought you a little gift. New kite.</p>
<p>Raj: The kite you made me lose was an authentic Patang, an Indian fighting kite that my brother sent to me from New Delhi. It took me a day to put together and two days to paint. This is Hello Kitty.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, but it comes with a little coin purse. Does a Patang?</p>
<p>Raj: Wow, you just don&#8217;t get it, do you? Buying me something pretty isn&#8217;t going to make our problem just go away.</p>
<p>Howard: Look, I admit I haven&#8217;t always been the best friend I could be.</p>
<p>Raj: You&#8217;ve been a sucky friend, a sucky, sucky friend.</p>
<p>Howard: Stipulated.</p>
<p>Raj: And you do it all the time. Last week in the mall at Radio Shack, we were looking for a phone with giant numbers for your mother, and I suddenly realize you&#8217;re not even there.</p>
<p>Howard: I know, I know.</p>
<p>Raj: And where were you?</p>
<p>Howard: Getting shot down by the girl at Hot Dog on a Stick. But in my defence, she was gorgeous, and working that squeezer to make the lemonade, going up and down and up and down. It was like a free pole dance right in the middle of the food court.</p>
<p>Raj: You&#8217;re impossible.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, at least I can talk to women without being drunk.</p>
<p>Raj: Excuse me, I have selective mutism, a recognized medical disorder. You&#8217;re just a douche.</p>
<p>Howard: No. You know what? Maybe that&#8217;s what this whole thing&#8217;s about. You&#8217;re not mad at me, you&#8217;re mad at yourself.</p>
<p>Raj: No, I&#8217;m mad at you. I hate myself, but I&#8217;m mad at you.</p>
<p>Howard: Fine. You&#8217;re mad at me. I get it. Now, how about we go spend the day together? Just the two of us. We&#8217;ll go anywhere you want.</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t know.</p>
<p>Howard: Come on. Let me take you someplace nice.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8230; I do enjoy the La Brea Tar Pits.</p>
<p>Howard: Really, now? With the traffic and the parking, it’s&#8230; okay, fine. The Tar Pits. Let&#8217;s go.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, why can&#8217;t I stay mad at you?</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny’s apartment. Everyone is watching football.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Go! Go! Go! Go-Go-Go-Go! Yes! Are you people watching this? Is this amazing or what?</p>
<p>Penny: Sweetie, that&#8217;s a highlight from the &#8217;98 championship game.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh. Did not know that.</p>
<p>Penny: How much beer have you had?</p>
<p>Leonard: None, why?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh. I was just kind of hoping you were drunk. Now we&#8217;re back live.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );

				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).load( function( e ) {
					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );
					$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loaded' );

						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
				setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 250 );
			}
