		<h2 class="title">Series 5 Episode 04 &#8211; The Wiggly Finger&nbsp;Catalyst</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: The entrance to the dungeon is a moss covered door. You manage to open it only to find yourself face-to-face with a hideous, foul-smelling, moss-covered ogre. What do you do?</p>
<p>Howard: I say, hey Ma, what&#8217;s for dinner?&#8221;</p>
<p>Sheldon: Seventeen. The ogre is amused by your joke and allows you to pass. By the by, I liked it, too.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, how go the wedding plans, Howard?</p>
<p>Howard: Great. We spent five hours last night at Macy&#8217;s registering for gifts. Looks like I&#8217;m finally going to have that darling little earthenware asparagus dish I&#8217;ve always wanted.</p>
<p>Leonard: See, this is the good thing about having a girlfriend 9,000 miles away. I can spend my nights doing whatever I want.</p>
<p>Howard: You mean like playing nerd games with us and then taking a suspiciously long shower?</p>
<p>Leonard: Maybe. We enter the dungeon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You see a dragon.</p>
<p>Howard: Really? So we&#8217;re playing Dungeons and Dragons, and we walk into a dungeon and see a dragon? Isn&#8217;t that a little on the nose?</p>
<p>Sheldon: When you play Chutes and Ladders, do you complain about all the chutes and all the ladders?</p>
<p>Leonard: Are you gonna eat that whole pie?</p>
<p>Raj: Maybe. Why not? Who do I have in my life to watch my figure for?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, God, did you watch Bridget Jones again?</p>
<p>Raj: No, it&#8217;s just that everybody&#8217;s got someone. Sheldon&#8217;s with Amy, Howard&#8217;s getting married, you&#8217;re dating my sister.</p>
<p>Leonard: Now that Howard&#8217;s getting married, maybe he&#8217;ll inflate one of his old girlfriends for you.</p>
<p>Raj: You know who I blame for my loneliness? The United States of America. Your movies and your TV shows promised streets paved with beautiful blonde women with big bazongas.</p>
<p>Howard: Eat another pie, you&#8217;ll have your own bazongas.</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s cruel. You know it goes straight to my hips.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Gentlemen, please focus. You&#8217;re facing a fire-breathing dragon.</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t know if I want to play anymore.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Because you don&#8217;t have a girlfriend? Well, good Lord, if that becomes a reason not to play Dungeons and Dragons, this game&#8217;s in serious trouble.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Amy: Now, I assume we use this same wax later on to neaten up each other&#8217;s bikini regions.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, my bikini region is fine.</p>
<p>Amy: Who&#8217;s shocked? I&#8217;m not. So, Bernadette, how&#8217;s the wedding planning going? And I&#8217;m not asking as a prospective bridesmaid. Pick me! Pick me!</p>
<p>Bernadette: We went cake-tasting yesterday. Raj came along. He cried and ate half the samples.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, the poor guy&#8217;s so lonely. We should set him up with someone.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You know, I met a really cute girl at work. She&#8217;s married to a guy in one of our drug trials.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, hello? She&#8217;s married.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Yeah, but her husband is in serious congestive heart failure, and a little birdie told me he&#8217;s in the placebo group.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, so future grief-stricken widow is the one to beat.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The Cheesecake Factory.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Are we ready to order?</p>
<p>Sheldon: One moment. I&#8217;m conducting an experiment.</p>
<p>Howard: With Dungeons and Dragons dice?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes. From here on in, I&#8217;ve decided to make all trivial decisions with a throw of the dice, thus freeing up my mind to do what it does best, enlighten and amaze. Page 14, item seven.</p>
<p>Howard: So, what&#8217;s for dinner?</p>
<p>Sheldon: A side of corn succotash. Hmm. Interesting.</p>
<p>Penny: Um, Howard, can I see you for a minute, please?</p>
<p>Howard: Uh, I don&#8217;t want to show any more of your friends how I can fit in the booster seats.</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, no, that&#8217;s not it. Just come with me, please.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Let&#8217;s see what I&#8217;ll be washing that succotash down with. A pitcher of margaritas.</p>
<p>Leonard: Do you really want that?</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s the great thing. It doesn&#8217;t matter. My mind is freed up to think about more important things.</p>
<p>Raj: What&#8217;s it thinking about now?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hamburgers and lemonade.</p>
<p>Penny: Um, Raj, there&#8217;s someone I want you to meet. This is my friend Emily. I know her from my spin class. Raj, relax. She can&#8217;t hear you. She&#8217;s deaf.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(signing)</em>: Emily, this is our friend Raj.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh! Look at you guys just hitting it off. I am so good.</p>
<p>Raj: Hi.</p>
<p>Howard: She says it&#8217;s nice to meet you.</p>
<p>Raj: Does she really mean that or was she signing it sarcastically?</p>
<p>Howard: Raj says it&#8217;s nice to meet you, too. She says she has to go back to her family, but Penny has her number if you want to text her and get together.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, I&#8217;m going to play it cool. Tell her, maybe. Whatever, babe.</p>
<p>Howard: He&#8217;ll text you.</p>
<p>Raj: Ah, look at that. I have a date. I love America again.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And now for dessert, come on, hot fudge sundae, come on, hot fudge sundae. Bam! That&#8217;s what I&#8217;m talking about!</p>
<p><em>Scene: A coffee shop.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Okay, as soon as she gets here, so she knows I&#8217;m cool with it, I&#8217;m going to make a joke about her being deaf. I was thinking, hey, did you hear the one about&#8230;? Oh, no, I bet you didn&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Howard: Maybe we should revisit your lonely fat guy plan.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, she&#8217;s here.</p>
<p>Howard: No joke. Oh, she says she&#8217;s sorry she&#8217;s late.</p>
<p>Raj: Tell her it doesn&#8217;t matter. Tell her, her eyes shimmer like opalescent lilies in the lake of the palace of the celestial maidens.</p>
<p>Howard: Really? That&#8217;s the first thing you want to say?</p>
<p>Raj: I worked on it all night. Use it.</p>
<p>Howard: Look, I don&#8217;t know the sign for opalescent.</p>
<p>Raj: Then spell it.</p>
<p>Howard: I don&#8217;t know how to spell it.</p>
<p>Raj: You&#8217;re blowing this for me!</p>
<p>Howard: He likes your eyes.</p>
<p>Raj: You&#8217;re making me sound like a caveman.</p>
<p>Howard: She says, thank you, you have nice eyes, too.&#8221;</p>
<p>Raj: Really? Ask her how many children she wants, and whatever number she says, say, me too.</p>
<p>Howard: No.</p>
<p>Raj: Fine. Tell her I have a deep, sexy voice like James Earl Jones.</p>
<p>Howard: She doesn&#8217;t know what James Earl Jones sounds like.</p>
<p>Raj: Great. Then she won&#8217;t know I&#8217;m lying.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The same, later.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Let&#8217;s see, what else can I tell you about me that would make you like me? Ooh, I love music. Do you love music?</p>
<p>Howard: You really want to ask her that?</p>
<p>Raj: You&#8217;re right. Everyone loves music.</p>
<p>Howard: She says, do you play an instrument?</p>
<p>Raj: No, but when I was six years old, I tried to start a boy band called Frankie Goes to Bollywood. But I couldn&#8217;t get any other boys to join, so my parents asked the servants to be my backup dancers. Wait, when you sign servants, don&#8217;t sign it like I&#8217;m bragging. Sign it in a way that I sound humble with just a hint of, that&#8217;s right, I had servants.</p>
<p>Howard: Do you hear yourself</p>
<p>Raj: Yes, but she doesn&#8217;t. So get signing, hand monkey.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The same, later.</em></p>
<p>Howard <em>(translating for Emily)</em>: I think I wrote a letter to Santa Claus every day. And then on Christmas morning, under the tree is a little puppy with a red ribbon.</p>
<p>Raj: What are you doing?</p>
<p>Howard: Texting Bernadette that I&#8217;m gonna be late.</p>
<p>Raj: Dude, what is she saying?</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s a funny story about a puppy. Just smile and laugh. Quick, quick, stop smiling.</p>
<p>Raj: What? Why?</p>
<p>Howard: The puppy died, it choked on a doll head. Sad face, sad face!</p>
<p><em>Scene: Outside the coffee shop.</em></p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s a little hard to see with the city lights, but that W-shaped constellation is Cassiopeia. And she was the mother of Andromeda who&#8217;s over there.</p>
<p>Howard: Look, pretty stars. This is her car. She hopes she can see you again sometime.</p>
<p>Raj: Good, good. Oh, boy, help me out here. Does she want me to kiss her or not?</p>
<p>Howard: I speak sign language, I don&#8217;t read minds.</p>
<p>Raj: If you were me, would you kiss her?</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, but I&#8217;m a make out king. <em>(She kisses Raj and gets into car)</em></p>
<p>Raj: I was so smooth on that date.</p>
<p>Howard: You? I made you smooth. You were an idiot.</p>
<p>Raj: Whatever, dude. She kissed me.</p>
<p>Howard: It might&#8217;ve been on your lips, but it was my kiss.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, fine. Let&#8217;s agree she kissed both of us.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. </em></p>
<p>Penny: Mustache is looking good there, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Don&#8217;t thank me. Thank the dice. They told me what percentage of my face to shave.</p>
<p>Howard: Why are you still doing this?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Because it&#8217;s working. In the past few weeks, unburdened by trivial decisions, I&#8217;ve co-authored two papers in notable peer-reviewed journals, and I&#8217;m close to figuring out why the Large Hadron Collider has yet to isolate the Higgs boson particle.</p>
<p>Leonard: You left out, got chafed testicles because you no longer wear underpants.</p>
<p>Sheldon: The dice giveth and the dice taketh away.</p>
<p>Penny: Is Raj out with Emily again?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, every night for the last month.</p>
<p>Penny: Wow, can&#8217;t believe he has a girlfriend.</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(rolls dice)</em>: Me neither.</p>
<p>Howard: Here&#8217;s some other fun news on the Raj/Emily front. He gave her a pair of diamond earrings and leased her a car.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re kidding.</p>
<p>Leonard: You think she&#8217;s taking advantage of him?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, of course not. She wouldn&#8217;t do something like that. She&#8217;s deaf.</p>
<p>Leonard: Deaf women can&#8217;t be gold diggers?</p>
<p>Penny: Handicapped people are nice, Leonard. Everyone knows that.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, I actually have information about Raj that would be helpful with this discussion.</p>
<p>Leonard: Could you tell us?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Let&#8217;s see. <em>(Rolls dice) </em>Snake eyes. Sorry, bud.</p>
<p>Penny: Wait, hang on. Doubles. Roll again.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay, get this. It doesn&#8217;t matter if he&#8217;s showering her with gifts, because the Koothrappalis are vastly wealthy.</p>
<p>Penny: What do you mean, vastly wealthy?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, wealthy means a lot of money, and vastly means even more. I&#8217;m not sure what&#8217;s tripping you up.</p>
<p>Leonard: Look, I know they have money. I don&#8217;t think it&#8217;s that much.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, you&#8217;re wrong. See, as you know, a few years ago, I achieved one of my lesser dreams and became a notary public. Well, from time to time, I notarize banking documents for Raj. The Koothrappalis aren&#8217;t just rich, they&#8217;re Richie Rich rich.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, so how much is that?</p>
<p>Sheldon: About halfway between Bruce Wayne and Scrooge McDuck.</p>
<p>Howard: What the hell? The last time we went to the zoo, that son of a bitch made me buy him a churro.</p>
<p>Leonard: Listen, guys, I&#8217;m sorry, I don&#8217;t mean to be rude, but I need to go call Raj&#8217;s sister, who I love so much. So vastly much.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, so he&#8217;s got money, and it&#8217;s a few gifts and a car.</p>
<p>Howard: And she got him to pay off all her credit cards.</p>
<p>Penny: What? He paid off her credit cards? Damn it, I could&#8217;ve dated Raj for a couple months. But I, I wouldn&#8217;t have, because I&#8217;m not that kind of girl. We should really talk to Raj.</p>
<p>Howard: He&#8217;s not going to listen, he&#8217;s in love.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Can&#8217;t figure out what to do? I remember those days. Now, if you&#8217;ll excuse me, I have to&#8230; <em>(rolls dice) </em>stay right here.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The gym.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, this is a bad idea. We should go.</p>
<p>Penny: No. I&#8217;m the one that introduced him to her. I&#8217;ve got to say something.</p>
<p>Howard: Wow.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re engaged to my friend.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, Bernadette doesn&#8217;t mind where I get my motor running, as long as I park in the right garage.</p>
<p>Penny: I can&#8217;t believe you&#8217;re engaged to my friend. Oh, here she comes.</p>
<p>Howard: Smart. Whisper so the deaf chick can&#8217;t hear you.</p>
<p>Penny: Hi.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(translating)</em>: Oh, hey, hi. Nice to see you.</p>
<p>Penny: Um, can we talk to you about Raj?</p>
<p>Howard: She says, sure, what about him?</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, um, gosh, how do I start? Um, see, Raj is kind of naive. I mean, he hasn&#8217;t dated a whole lot of women. And I&#8217;m concerned that, without meaning to, you might be taking advantage of him. You know, by letting him buy you a bunch of expensive things.And I.. I&#8230; Howard, focus. Tell her what I&#8217;m saying.</p>
<p>Howard: Right. Are you a gold digger or not? Oh, uh, something, something, who the something do you think you are? Mind your own something business and go something yourself. Oh, wait, I got this now.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment. She answers the door.</em></p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m so mad at you!</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, wait&#8230;</p>
<p>Raj: How dare you ambush my girlfriend at the gym!</p>
<p>Penny: We didn&#8217;t mean for it to be an ambush. Just, it&#8217;s kind of impossible not to sneak up on deaf people. And hey, since when are you so chatty?</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m hammered.</p>
<p>Penny: Raj, come here. This girl is trouble. I mean, what kind of relationship is it where you buy her gifts and she gives you sex?</p>
<p>Raj: The best one I ever had!</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, come on. You know you can do better.</p>
<p>Raj: Aha. I see what&#8217;s going on here. You and I had our crazy night together, and now you can&#8217;t stand to see me with another woman.</p>
<p>Penny: Whoa, whoa, whoa, whoa!</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s okay. I can&#8217;t get mad at your feelings.</p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t have feelings.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, that&#8217;s good. Keep telling yourself that. <em>(Storms out)</em></p>
<p>Penny: He is cuter now that I know he&#8217;s rich.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
