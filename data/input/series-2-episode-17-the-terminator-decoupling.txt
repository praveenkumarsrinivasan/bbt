		<h2 class="title">Series 2 Episode 17 &#8211; The Terminator&nbsp;Decoupling</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The Apartment<br />
</em></p>
<p>Howard: Okay, Raj, hand me the number six torque screwdriver.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Stop. We can&#8217;t do this, it&#8217;s not right.</p>
<p>Raj: Sheldon, you have two choices. Either you let him put a bigger hard drive in the TiVo, or you delete stuff before we go out of town.</p>
<p>Sheldon: But once you open the box, you&#8217;ve voided the warranty. The warranty is a sacred covenant we&#8217;ve entered into with the manufacturer. He offers to stand by his equipment, and we in return agree not to violate the integrity of the internal hardware. This little orange sticker is all that stands between us and anarchy.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, then we won&#8217;t touch the hard drive. We&#8217;ll just erase the first season of Battlestar.</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(ripping off sticker)</em>: There. We&#8217;re outlaws.</p>
<p>Penny <em>(entering with a pink suitcase)</em>: Here you go, Leonard. Is this going to be big enough?</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s perfect.</p>
<p>Howard: For taking daffodils to your unicorn.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s just for my notebooks. Thanks, Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, I love San Francisco. I wish I was going with you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I understand your envy. This is a can&#8217;t-miss symposium. There are going to be discussions on bioorganic cellular computer devices, the advancements in multi-threaded task completion, plus a roundtable on the nonequilibrium Green&#8217;s function approach to the photoionization process in atoms.</p>
<p>Penny: When I go, I usually just get hammered and ride the cable cars.</p>
<p>Leonard: This conference is kind of a big thing. The keynote address is being delivered by George Smoot.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, my God, the George Smoot?</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;ve heard of him?</p>
<p>Penny: Of course I haven&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Sheldon: George Smoot is a Nobel Prize-winning physicist, one of the great minds of our time. His work in black body form and anisotropy of the cosmic microwave background radiation cemented our understanding of the origin of the universe.</p>
<p>Penny: It&#8217;s kind of a funny name, though, Smoot.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s like talking to a chimp.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, now that I&#8217;ve been completely insulted, have a good flight.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, I wish.</p>
<p>Sheldon: We&#8217;re not flying, we’re taking the train.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, cool.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, cool. Seven times as long as flying, and costs almost twice as much.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, then why are you doing it?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, we had a vote. Three of us voted for airplane, Sheldon voted for train, so we&#8217;re taking the train.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Don&#8217;t say it like that, Leonard, say it like: we&#8217;re taking the train!</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>Scene: Sheldon’s bedroom.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, we&#8217;re all going over to the Apple store to make fun of the guys at the Genius Bar. You want to come?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I always enjoy that, but I&#8217;m a little busy.</p>
<p>Leonard: What are you doing?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m simplifying the task of packing for our trip. See, by attaching RFID tags to my clothing, it will enable my laptop to read and identify the items with this wand. I will then cross-reference them against destination, anticipated activity spectrum, weather conditions, duration of trip, et cetera.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, that does sound much simpler. How long is this going to take?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Assuming I can keep up this pace, three hours, 11 minutes, and plus however long it takes to conclude this fairly pointless conversation.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wow. Teasing the guys at the Apple store seems a little redundant now.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t follow.</p>
<p>Leonard: I wouldn&#8217;t expect you to. I&#8217;ll see you later.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Socks, one pair, cotton, argyle, blue. Socks, one pair, cotton, argyle, blue.</p>
<p><em>Scene: On the train.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: What on earth are you doing?</p>
<p>Raj: Whatever it is, I&#8217;m guessing we&#8217;re doing it wrong.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Gentlemen, this is the Coast Starlight, one of the great American trains operating on one of the classic American routes. On this side, you&#8217;ll see panoramic ocean vistas inaccessible to any other form of transportation, while on your side, you&#8217;ll be treated to 350 miles of CostCos, Jiffy Lubes, and cinderblock homes with above-ground pools.</p>
<p>Howard: Come on, Raj.</p>
<p>Raj: What&#8217;s wrong with Jiffy Lubes?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why not?</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s over the wheelbase. Are you completely unfamiliar with the suspension characteristics of a pre-1980 Pullman-built Superliner Deluxe passenger coach?</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, we&#8217;ve been on this train 90 seconds, and you&#8217;ve already said a thousand words. Just tell us where to sit and shut up.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Here. I&#8217;m hoping once you reap the endorphic rewards of the steady clickety-clack of steel wheels on polished rails, your sour disposition will abate.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, maybe. Meanwhile back in the 21st century, people are raising their tray tables and putting their seat-backs in an upright position &#8217;cause it&#8217;s time to land in San Francisco.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s not so bad, really. At least these trains have modern plumbing. In India, you squat over a hole in the train and expose your naked buttocks to the chilly air of Rajasthan.</p>
<p>Sheldon: He is referring, of course, to third class on Indian Railways&#8217; magnificent Ranakpur Express and its twelve hundred kilometer journey from Maharashtra to the Bikaner Junction.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, look, now he&#8217;s boring on an international scale.</p>
<p>Raj: Holy crap! Look!</p>
<p>Leonard: Is that who I think it is?</p>
<p>Howard: It can&#8217;t be. What would Summer Glau be doing riding the train?</p>
<p>Leonard: Maybe John Connor&#8217;s aboard and she&#8217;s protecting him from an evil Terminator.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Unlikely. That&#8217;s a television show, Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thank you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Of course, if SkyNet actually did exist in the future, a perfect way to infiltrate and destroy mankind would be to send Terminators back posing as actors who have played Terminators in popular films and television series, lulling us into a false sense of security, i.e., that&#8217;s Summer Glau from The Sarah Connor Chronicles. No, Summer, don&#8217;t kill me! I&#8217;m pro-robot! Ahh!</p>
<p>Leonard: At least he&#8217;s off the train crap.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Whee!</p>
<p>Howard: Sheldon, I owe you an apology. Taking the train was a stroke of brilliance! I&#8217;ve actually got a shot at a Terminator.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, please.When it comes to Terminators, you&#8217;ve got a better shot of scoring with Arnold Schwarzenegger.</p>
<p>Howard: You&#8217;re overlooking something. I have 11 hours with her in a confined space. Unless she&#8217;s willing to jump off a moving train, tuck and roll down the side of a hill, she will eventually succumb to the acquired taste that is Howard Wolowitz.</p>
<p>Leonard: My money&#8217;s on tuck and roll.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m confused. I thought you were involved in some sort of socially intimate pairing with Leslie Winkle.</p>
<p>Howard: Sheldon, let me explain to you how this works.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right.</p>
<p>Howard: That&#8217;s Summer Glau.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes.</p>
<p>Howard: That&#8217;s it.</p>
<p>Raj: Hang on a sec. Why do you get first crack at her?</p>
<p>Howard: Um, well, let&#8217;s see, couple reasons. One, I saw her first.</p>
<p>Raj: No, you didn&#8217;t. I did.</p>
<p>Howard: Fair enough. But then let me move on to number two, unlike you, I can actually talk to women when I&#8217;m sober.</p>
<p>Raj: You fail to take into account that even mute, I am foreign and exotic, while you, on the other hand, are frail and pasty.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, you know the old saying, pasty and frail never fail.</p>
<p>Leonard: Excuse me, but what about me? Why don&#8217;t I get a shot?</p>
<p>Howard: Fine, go ahead. Take a shot.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know, I&#8217;ve already got a gorgeous blonde back home at I can&#8217;t score with. I think I&#8217;ll let you two take this one.</p>
<p>Raj: Sheldon, is there a place on this train to get alcohol?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Interesting that you ask. The Coast Starlight recently added the refurbished Pacific Parlour Car. Built in 1956 and originally known as the Santa Fe Lounge Car, the lower level is a theatre&#8230;</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah-yeah, which way?</p>
<p>Sheldon: and the upper level is a bar that offers wine tastings if you&#8217;re going as far as Portland.</p>
<p>Leonard: So aren&#8217;t you going to go talk to her?</p>
<p>Howard: I will, I&#8217;m just working on my opening line.</p>
<p>Leonard: She&#8217;s probably heard every possible line, Howard. Why don&#8217;t you just try hello?</p>
<p>Howard: No, no, no, that always creeps girls out. I need to come up with something that&#8217;s funny, smart and delicately suggests that my sexual endowment is disproportionate to my physical stature.</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re going to need more than 11 hours.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, no.</p>
<p>Leonard: What&#8217;s the matter?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I forgot my flash drive.</p>
<p>Leonard: So?</p>
<p>Sheldon: So we have to go back.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, Sheldon, I&#8217;m going to say why and your answer cannot be because I forgot my flash drive.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You don&#8217;t understand. My flash drive has my paper on astrophysical probes of M-theory effects in the early universe that I was going to give to George Smoot at the conference.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why do you have to give your paper to George Smoot?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s brilliant. He needs to read it.</p>
<p>Leonard: So you&#8217;ll send him an e-mail when we get back.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Then I won&#8217;t get to see his face light up as he reads it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Right. Of course.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, this is an unmitigated disaster.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, there&#8217;s nothing you can do about it, so relax, sit back, enjoy the clickety-clack of the steel wheels on the polished rails.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You forgot your flash drive, You forgot your flash drive<em> (repeated over and over in time to the sound of the train)</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Only ten hours, 55 minutes to go.</p>
<p><em>Time Shift. </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Howard <em>(steeling himself to talk to Summer Glau, to himself)</em>: It&#8217;s hot in here. Must be Summer. <em>(Walks towards her, then walks straight past. Returns, makes to talk to her, then turns to two nuns over the other side of the corridor) </em>So, where you gals headed?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay, I&#8217;ve found the perfect solution. We get off the train at the next stop in Oxnard. We then take the 1:13 train back to Union Station. We take a cab back to the apartment, get my flash drive, and then race to San Luis Obispo, where, assuming the lights are with us and minimal traffic, we&#8217;ll meet the train.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;ve got a better idea.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Are you going to be sarcastic?</p>
<p>Leonard: Boy, you take all the fun out of it for me.  But look, Penny&#8217;s home. Why don&#8217;t we just call her, have her go in the apartment, get your flash drive and e-mail you the paper?</p>
<p>Sheldon: But the flash drive is in a locked drawer in my desk.</p>
<p>Leonard: So?</p>
<p>Sheldon: The key is hidden in my room.</p>
<p>Leonard: So?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Penny would have to go into my room.</p>
<p>Leonard: So?</p>
<p>Sheldon: People don&#8217;t go in my room!</p>
<p>Leonard: I see. Well, it seems once again, you&#8217;re caught between a rock and a crazy place.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I hate when that happens.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(to self)</em>: It&#8217;s hot in here. It must be Summer. It&#8217;s hot in here. Must be Summer. It&#8217;s hot in here. Must be Summer.</p>
<p>Raj <em>(walking past and straight up to Summer Glau)</em>: It&#8217;s hot in here. Must be Summer.</p>
<p>Summer: That&#8217;s cute.</p>
<p>Raj: Really? I just made it up. Have you seen Slumdog Millionaire?</p>
<p>Summer: Oh, yeah, I loved it.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s loosely based on my life.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny’s apartment. Penny is on the phone.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, we&#8217;re putting the play on for one night in this little 99-seat theatre. Can you come? Oh, great. Do you know 98 other people that might want to come? Oh, hang on. <em>(Switches to another line)</em> Hello?</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(on phone)</em>: Listen carefully. I&#8217;m about to give you a set of instructions, which you must follow to the letter.</p>
<p>Penny: Just a sec. <em>(Switches back to first line) </em>The theatre is above a bowling alley, so it&#8217;s a little noisy, but it might be the only chance I&#8217;ll ever get to play Anne Frank. And the director is brilliant. He uses the bowling sounds as, like, Nazi artillery. Okay, great, I&#8217;ll see you then. <em>(Switches line again) </em>Hello?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay, step four. Do you see that small plastic case on my dresser?</p>
<p>Penny: Your dresser? Who is this?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s Sheldon.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, hey, Sheldon! How is San Francisco?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m not in San Francisco. I&#8217;m on a train. Were you even listening to me?</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, no, I was talking to my friend, but what&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Sheldon: What&#8217;s up? I&#8217;ll tell you what&#8217;s up. I&#8217;m in a crisis situation, and I need you to marshal your powers of concentration, limited as they may be&#8230;</p>
<p>Leonard: Give me the phone. Hi, Penny. It&#8217;s Leonard.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, Leonard. What&#8217;s going on with Dr. Wackadoodle?</p>
<p>Leonard: He&#8217;s calling to ask you a favour. You might be confused because he didn&#8217;t use the words, Penny, Sheldon, please or favour.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay. Enough chitchat. Okay, step one, locate your emergency key to our apartment. Step two, enter our apartment. Step three, enter my bedroom.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, hang on, Sheldon, getting another call.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No! Leonard, let me tell you something. Personal robots cannot get here soon enough.</p>
<p>Raj: And that bright little star peeking her head out early today, that&#8217;s Venus.</p>
<p>Summer: That is so cool. You really know a lot about space.</p>
<p>Raj: Come on. When you were on TV in Firefly, you were actually in space.</p>
<p>Summer: You&#8217;re not one of those guys who really believe that, are you?</p>
<p>Raj: You mean one of the hopeless geeks? No. Those are crazy people. Howard, be a dear and get me another one of these. Now, him, he&#8217;s one of those geeks.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, now, before you enter my bedroom unescorted, I need you to understand that this onetime grant of access does not create a permanent easement. Easement. It&#8217;s a legal right</p>
<p>of access. Good grief. What? No, don&#8217;t put me on hold. Oh!</p>
<p>Howard: Do you believe him? Normally around women, he has the personality of a boiled potato. Put one beer in him, and he&#8217;s M. Night Charmalarmalon.</p>
<p>Leonard: Is that what he&#8217;s drinking? It&#8217;s not even real beer.</p>
<p>Howard: What?</p>
<p>Leonard: Look at it. Non-alcoholic beer.</p>
<p>Howard: What&#8217;s going on?</p>

<p>Sheldon: Okay, now you&#8217;re going to insert the flash drive into the USB port. She calls me Moon Pie because I&#8217;m nummy-nummy and she could just eat me up, now, please put the flash drive in the USB port. The one that looks like a little duck&#8217;s mouth.</p>

<p>Announcer: Next stop: Santa Barbara.</p>

<p>Leonard: Hi, my name&#8217;s Leonard.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;

					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
