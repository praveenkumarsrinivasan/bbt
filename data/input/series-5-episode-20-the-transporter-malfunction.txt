		<h2 class="title">Series 5 Episode 20 &#8211; The Transporter&nbsp;Malfunction</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Oh my God, I love this chicken.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, you know what they say, the best things in life are free.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, you&#8217;re right, I eat your food a lot. How about this, you can raid my fridge any time you want.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, that&#8217;s very kind of you. Next time I have a hankering to wash down a D cell battery with a jar of old pickle juice, I&#8217;ll come a-knocking.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, Raj, you didn&#8217;t send your RSVP in. I&#8217;m supposed to ask you if you&#8217;re bringing someone to the wedding.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;ll let you know.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, can you make it soon. There&#8217;s a battle royale going on over the seating charts. In one corner, Bernadette&#8217;s mom, and the other three, mine.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, I hate wedding receptions. I wish the bride and groom would take a cue from Bilbo Baggins, slip on the ring, disappear and everyone goes home.</p>
<p>Leonard: Mmm, you liked Professor Guyster&#8217;s wedding.</p>
<p>Sheldon: They had a make your own sundae bar. Ooh, that was a night to remember. Do you know, on the one trip, I just had a bowl of nuts.</p>
<p>Howard: Anyway, you gotta let me know if you have a plus one, because if not my mom&#8217;s trying to sneak in the doctor who sucked the fat out of her neck.</p>
<p>Raj: Alright, uh, fine, I&#8217;m coming and I&#8217;m bringing somebody. Uh, Koothrappali plus one.</p>
<p>Leonard: Who you bringing?</p>
<p>Raj: Who are you bringing?</p>
<p>Penny: He&#8217;s bringing me. Who are you bringing?</p>
<p>Raj: Wow, what a bunch of Nosey O&#8217;Donnells.</p>
<p>Howard: Come on, who is it?</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m not telling. I&#8217;m from Asia. I&#8217;m mysterious. Deal with it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Howard, are you having a make your own sundae bar?</p>
<p>Howard: No, uh, I don&#8217;t think so.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well you should. 50% of marriages end in divorce, but 100% of make your own sundae bars end in happiness.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Raj <em>(on skype)</em>: Hello Mummy, Daddy, how are you?</p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali: Pretty good, can&#8217;t complain.</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: Oh I&#8217;m sure you can. Just give it a minute.</p>
<p>Raj: Listen, uh, there&#8217;s something I want to talk to you about. I, I wasn&#8217;t ready until now, but I think it&#8217;s time.</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: It&#8217;s finally happening. You&#8217;re coming out of the closet, aren&#8217;t you?</p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali: We love you, and we accept your alternate lifestyle. Just keep it to yourself.</p>
<p>Raj: No, I&#8217;m not gay. If anything, I&#8217;m metrosexual.</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: What&#8217;s that?</p>
<p>Raj: It means I like women as well as their skincare products.</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: Well, if you&#8217;re not coming out, why did you call us during the cricket semi-finals?</p>
<p>Raj: &#8216;Cause I, I&#8217;m tired of trying to meet someone and, I think I&#8217;d like you to help me find uh, a wife.</p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali: And just to clarify, a female wife?</p>
<p>Raj: Yes.</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: Matchmaking, very smart move son. Much better than marrying for love.</p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali: We married for love.</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: And it&#8217;s been wonderful.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Quantum physics makes me so happy.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, I&#8217;m glad.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s like looking at the universe naked. <em>(Shivers) </em></p>
<p>Penny: Hi, you guys got a minute?</p>
<p>Leonard: Sure, what&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, I was thinking about Sheldon&#8217;s little joke the other night about me eating all your food.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, that was no joke. But I understand your confusion as I am our group&#8217;s resident cut-up.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m sorry, you are our resident cut-up?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes.</p>
<p>Leonard: Prove it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Knock, knock.</p>
<p>Leonard: Who&#8217;s there?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Interrupting physicist.</p>
<p>Leonard: Interrupting physi&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: MUON!</p>
<p>Penny: Anyway, I got a little residual cheque from my commercial and I thought, hey, how about I get the guys a little thankyou to pay them back. So, Sheldon, ta-da!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Ah! A vintage, mint in box, 1975 Mego Star Trek transporter with real transporter action. Hot-darn!</p>
<p>Leonard: Where did you get that?</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, from Stuart at the comic-book store.</p>
<p>Leonard: You went to the comic-book store by yourself?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, it was fun, I walked in and two different guys got asthma attacks. Felt pretty good.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well this calls for an expression of gratitude.</p>
<p>Penny: Ooh, am I about to get a rare Sheldon Cooper hug?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Not this time. Then they wouldn&#8217;t be special. Thanks Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re welcome. Don&#8217;t worry, I didn&#8217;t forget about you. Leonard, I got you a label maker.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wow. No, it&#8217;s great. Also, uh, mint in box.</p>
<p>Penny: Mmm. And, I got you a transporter too!</p>
<p>Leonard: Awesome!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Look, it was actually designed for my vintage Mr Spock action figure.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, that&#8217;s great. Let&#8217;s open it up and put him in there.</p>
<p>Leonard: Ah!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh dear Lord. No!</p>
<p>Penny: Why, they&#8217;re just toys?</p>
<p>Sheldon: They&#8217;re mint in box.</p>
<p>Leonard: They&#8217;re Collectables.</p>
<p>Penny: C&#8217;mon can&#8217;t we just open one up and take a&#8230;</p>
<p>Together: No!</p>
<p>Leonard: Once you open the box it loses its value.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, yeah, my mom gave me the same lecture about my virginity. Gotta tell you, it was a lot more fun taking it out and playing with it.</p>
<p><em>Scene: A restaurant.</em></p>
<p>Bernadette: Are you sure you want us here when you meet this woman?</p>
<p>Raj: Oh yes. In my culture it&#8217;s expected to have a chaparone to oversee a first date. Plus I want to make a good impression and, no offence, but with you guys here I look like six two.</p>
<p>Bernadette: The nuns always chaparoned the dances at my high school. They used to make us leave room between us for the holy spirit.</p>
<p>Howard: Hindus do the same thing. Except they leave room for a cow.</p>
<p>Raj: Listen, I love your charming racist humour, but any chance you could not knock my religion while she&#8217;s here.</p>
<p>Howard: Yesterday you made fun of me for eating lox.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s different. You&#8217;re people don&#8217;t worship lox.</p>
<p>Howard: Well clearly you&#8217;ve never been to brunch with my cousins.</p>
<p>Bernadette: So, arranged marriages. The parents just decide, and then you have to get married?</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, no, no, I get a say in it. But I&#8217;m sure whoever shows up will be better company than a threesome I&#8217;ve been having with Aunt Jemima and Mrs Butterworth.</p>
<p>Girl: Excuse me, are you Rajesh?</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, yes. You must be Lakshmi. Uh, nice to meet you.</p>
<p>Lakshmi: Nice to meet you too.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, uh, these are my, my friends. This is Bernadette.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hello.</p>
<p>Raj: And this is Howard.</p>
<p>Howard: Nice to meet you.</p>
<p>Lakshmi: You too.</p>
<p>Raj: Please have a seat. <em>(To Bernadette) </em>I&#8217;m thinking double wedding!</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Voice of Spock: Dr Cooper. Dr Cooper?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Is someone there?</p>
<p>Spock: Down here, on your desk.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Spock?</p>
<p>Spock: I need to speak with you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Fascinating. The only logical explanation is that this is a dream.</p>
<p>Spock: It is not the only logical explanation. For example, you could be hallucinating after being hit on the head by, say, a coconut.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Was I hit on the head by a coconut.</p>
<p>Spock: I&#8217;m not going to dignify that with a response. Now, to the matter at hand. You need to play with the transporter toy.</p>
<p>Sheldon: But it&#8217;s mint in box.</p>
<p>Spock: Yes, and to open it would destroy it&#8217;s value. But remember, like me, you also have a human half.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, I&#8217;m not going to dignify that with a response.</p>
<p>Spock: Consider this. What is the purpose of a toy?</p>
<p>Sheldon: To be played with.</p>
<p>Spock: Therefore? To not play with it would be?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Illogical. Oh, damn it Spock, you&#8217;re right. I&#8217;ll do it.</p>
<p>Spock: Sheldon, wait. You have to wake up first.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, of course. Set phasers to dumb, right? <em>(Wakes up in bed) </em>Goody, goody, goody. This is wrong. This is wrong. I&#8217;m so excited, but this is wrong. I&#8217;m gonna do it. I&#8217;m doing it. I did it. Oh, that&#8217;s what I always thought 1975 smelled like. One to beam down Mr Scott. Aye, aye, Mr Spock. Energise. <em>(Spins toy). </em>Energise. <em>(Tries to spin toy. It sticks. Tries to get Spock out and toy falls apart.)</em> Don&#8217;t be broken. Please don&#8217;t be broken. <em>(To Spock toy) </em>What did you make me do? Okay, okay, think. <em>(Sees Leonard&#8217;s toy) </em>It&#8217;s only logical.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Lakshmi: My goodness, that was delicious.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, I hope you saved room for chocolate lava cake.</p>
<p>Lakshmi: Impressive. What goes into making something like that.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, you start off by trying to make chocolate souffle, and when it falls you panic, quickly change the name, and voila! Lava cake.</p>
<p>Lakshmi: I bet our parents are dying to know how things are going here.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, let&#8217;s see. Yup, three missed calls.</p>
<p>Lakshmi: Four missed calls, two text messages, and a failed video chat. I win. So, what are we going to tell them.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;d like to tell them things are going well.</p>
<p>Lakshmi: Me too. But before we get their hopes up we should probably make sure we&#8217;re on the same page.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, what page are you on?</p>
<p>Lakshmi: I&#8217;m on the one where I&#8217;m on a lot of pressure from my parents to get married and settle down, and have a family, and I&#8217;m going to do it so they don&#8217;t find out I&#8217;m gay.</p>
<p>Raj: Say again.</p>
<p>Lakshmi: I&#8217;m gay.</p>
<p>Raj: Like, dude on dude, but with women?</p>
<p>Lakshmi: I know a fake marriage isn&#8217;t an honest way to live, but you of all people must know how difficult it is to come out in our culture.</p>
<p>Raj: Why me, of all people?</p>
<p>Lakshmi: Well, there is a rumour back in New Delhi that you&#8217;re, how shall we say, comfortable in a sari.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m not gay.</p>
<p>Lakshmi: Really? The chocolate lava cake? The little soaps in the bathroom? And I&#8217;m sorry, but you&#8217;re wearing more perfume than I am.</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s Unbreakable by Khloe and Lamar. And for your information, it&#8217;s unisex.</p>
<p>Lakshmi: Fill in the blank. I love the nightlife?</p>
<p>Raj: I like to boogie.</p>
<p>Lakshmi: Got you.</p>
<p>Raj: W-w-with women. I like to boogie with women.</p>
<p>Lakshmi: That&#8217;s disappointing. You were exactly the kind of phoney-baloney husband I was looking for.</p>
<p>Raj: Thankyou. And once again, my baloney likes girls. Wait-wait. You don&#8217;t want to put a bit of that in your mouth without trying my homemade chantilly cream. Yeah, okay, that time I heard it.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Sheldon&#8217;s bedroom. Sheldon writhes in bed, then wakes up on an alien planet. </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh dear. Two suns and no sunscreen.</p>
<p>Spock: Hello again, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What is it now, tiny Spock?</p>
<p>Spock: I am very disappointed in you. You broke your toy and switched it with Leonard&#8217;s. You should be ashamed of yourself.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You&#8217;re the one who told me to play with it.</p>
<p>Spock: If I told you to jump off the bridge of the Enterprise, would you do it?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. If I got on the bridge of the Enterprise, I would never, ever leave.</p>
<p>Spock: Trust me, it gets old after a while. You must right your wrong, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why? I got away with it. Leonard has his toy, and he&#8217;s never going to open it, so he won&#8217;t know it&#8217;s broken. And I have a toy that isn&#8217;t broken. Everybody&#8217;s happy.</p>
<p>Spock: Well I am unhappy.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I thought where you come from they don&#8217;t have emotions.</p>
<p>Spock: I come from a factory in Taiwan. Now do the right thing.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You know what you are? Well, you&#8217;re a green blooded buzzkill. Perhaps it&#8217;s time you beam on out of here.</p>
<p>Spock: Fine. I will just use the transporter. Oh, right. You broke it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Very well. Cooper to Enterprise, one to beam up. Energise. <em>(Throws Spock away. Get&#8217;s attacked by a Gorn. Wakes up.) </em>Ah. Tiny Spock. Help.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Howard&#8217;s laboratory. Howard is soldering something.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Ready for lunch?</p>
<p>Howard: Yep, one sec. <em>(Puts thing in mouth.) </em>Ah, good. Ma would have killed me if she&#8217;d found out I&#8217;d broke my retainer.</p>
<p>Raj: Hey, uh, can I run something by you? It&#8217;s about Lakshmi.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah. Sure. How are things going?</p>
<p>Raj: We hit a couple of bumps. She lives over on Manhattan Beach, so it takes over an hour to get there. And she&#8217;s a lesbian.</p>
<p>Howard: What do you mean, she&#8217;s a lesbian?</p>
<p>Raj: Well, you know whenever you and I would try to hit on women in bars, and they&#8217;d blow us off, and then we&#8217;d tell each other they were probably gay. It&#8217;s like that. Except this time it&#8217;s true.</p>
<p>Howard: Then why did she even go out with you?</p>
<p>Raj: She was looking for a husband so she can appear to be straight. And you know, it sounded crazy to me at first, but I&#8217;m actually thinking about doing it.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, well, so the reason that might sound crazy, is &#8217;cause it&#8217;s crazy.</p>
<p>Raj: Look, Howard, you&#8217;re in a relationship. You know you have to make compromises.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, yes. But my compromises are about which bedspread to buy, or whose turn it is to do the laundry, Bernadette&#8217;s or my mom&#8217;s.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s a great deal. We both get our parents off our backs. I don&#8217;t have to come home to an empty apartment every night, plus, once I&#8217;m married, I can finally eat carbs again and let myself go.</p>
<p>Howard: Why don&#8217;t you tell your parents you want to try and find someone else, you know, maybe one who hasn&#8217;t slept with more women than you.</p>
<p>Raj: Because this one wants to marry me. I might never find another one who does.</p>
<p>Howard: So you&#8217;re seriously thinking about marrying someone you&#8217;re never going to have sex with.</p>
<p>Raj: I can&#8217;t believe your attitude. I thought you were in favour of gay people getting married.</p>
<p>Howard: Yes, to other gay people.</p>
<p>Raj: Do you hear how homophobic you sound?</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. Sheldon is working on his laptop. Spock is looking at him judgementally. He switches the two toys.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I don&#8217;t want a broken toy. <em>(Switches them back again. Penny and Leonard enter.) </em>Nothing!</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Nothing. I said nothing.</p>
<p>Penny: That was weird.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really? I don&#8217;t even notice any more.</p>
<p>Penny: I cannot believe you guys aren&#8217;t going to play with these.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well I told you, you don&#8217;t, it&#8217;s mint in box.</p>
<p>Penny: I dunno. I just think it&#8217;s a waste. <em>(Picks up box. Sheldon screams.) </em>Relax. I&#8217;m just looking at the box.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Perhaps you should look with your eyes, and not your muscular Nebraska man-hands.</p>
<p>Penny: What is your problem?</p>
<p>Sheldon: My problem is that I don&#8217;t want you to break Leonard&#8217;s toy, which you probably did by shaking it. She shook it. We all saw her.</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, I bought you this because I wanted you to have fun with it. I don&#8217;t want it to just sit in this box.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know, you&#8217;re right. I mean, it&#8217;s from you, I&#8217;m never going to sell it. I&#8217;m opening it.</p>
<p>Penny: Yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Mmmmm!</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s broken.</p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, nice job, man-hands.</p>
<p>Penny: I didn&#8217;t break it. I, I guess Stuart sold it to me like this.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes. Yes, he did, that is a perfectly satisfying and plausible explanation. Yeah, let&#8217;s all be mad at Stuart.</p>
<p>Penny: You know, I paid a lot for this. Let&#8217;s take it over there and show him.</p>
<p>Leonard: Absolutely.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wait. It was me. I opened your toy, discovered it was broken and didn&#8217;t tell you.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why would you open mine?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I didn&#8217;t. That was a lie. I opened my own toy. And it was already broken so I switched them.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, you should talk to Stuart.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I can&#8217;t because that was a lie. Yours was broken in an earthquake. And that&#8217;s a lie.</p>
<p>Penny: What is the truth?</p>
<p>Sheldon: My Mr Spock doll came to me in a dream and forced me to open it. And when the toy broke I switched it for yours. Later he encouraged me to do the right thing and I defied him. And then I was attacked by a Gorn.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, that I believe.</p>
<p>Penny: Mmm.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, Penny, I just, I want you both to know that I regret my actions toward the two of you. That&#8217;s a lie.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, is that one mine.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, hand it over so I can open it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay. Leonard, even though I don&#8217;t have one any more, I hope you have fun playing with it.</p>
<p>Leonard: And that&#8217;s a lie, right?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Big fat whopper. I hope it breaks.</p>

<p>Raj: So, while I&#8217;m waiting for this mysterious perfect match, who may or may not exist, I&#8217;m supposed to just be alone.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Not necessarily. I think we&#8217;ve found someone for you to cuddle with. <em>(Pulls a puppy out of her bag.)</em></p>
<p>Raj: Oh, my goodness. Aren&#8217;t you the cutest little Yorkie ever? You got him for me?</p>
<p>Howard: Her. We thought you two would hit it off.</p>
<p>Raj: I think we already have. Thank you guys so much. Let&#8217;s see if you fit in my man purse.</p>

					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();
