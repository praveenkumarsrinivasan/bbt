		<h2 class="title">Series 6 Episode 08 &#8211; The 43&nbsp;Peculiarity</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The cafeteria. Sheldon is drawing on a napkin.</em></p>
<p>Raj: What are you drawing over there?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s a hypothetical containment field for a Frisbee-sized wormhole that could serve as a portal to a parallel universe.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, you silly doodlebug.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know, a lot of scientists believe that making contact with other life-forms would probably not end well for us.</p>
<p><span style="font-family:Calibri, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: It&#8217;s a Frisbee-sized wormhole, Leonard. You can block it with a Frisbee. Calm down. </span></span><span style="font-family:Calibri, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><i>(Hands Howard the napkin)</i></span></span></p>
<p>Howard: Do you expect me to build this?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I expect you to wipe the pudding off your chin. Gentlemen.</p>
<p>Howard: Have you guys ever noticed that Sheldon always disappears every day at two forty-five?</p>
<p>Leonard: Really?</p>
<p>Raj: He probably just goes to the bathroom.</p>
<p>Leonard: Actually, no, he goes to the bathroom at eight a.m. with optional follow-ups at one forty-five and seven ten high-fibre Fridays.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s sad that you know that.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, that&#8217;s just the tip of the sadness iceberg.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m looking at his public calendar. Two forty-five to three-oh-five, nothing. Yesterday, two forty-five to three-oh-five, nothing, last week, nothing, last month, nothing. He never has anything booked during that time.</p>
<p>Raj: Twenty minutes a day, completely unaccounted for.</p>
<p>Howard: We should figure out where he goes.</p>
<p>Raj: Ooh, this is exciting. Like one of my classic murder mystery dinner parties.</p>
<p>Leonard: Right, the case of who murdered three Saturday nights of my life?</p>
<p>Howard: Colonel Koothrapali in the kitchen with the olive spread.</p>
<p>Raj: It was tapenade and you guys suck.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment. Penny is texting.</em></p>
<p>Penny: So, how was work today?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, I spent most of the afternoon growing isotopically pure crystals for neutrino detection.</p>
<p>Penny: That sounds like fun.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, it was.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, good, I guessed right.</p>
<p>Leonard: Who you talking to?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, just this guy I met at school.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, great. We&#8217;re still dating, right?</p>
<p>Penny: Relax. He&#8217;s just a friend. We&#8217;re doing an oral report together. He&#8217;s really nice.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m sorry, what was that? I had a little stroke after oral.</p>
<p>Penny: Would you stop? Look, he just moved here from London, okay? He doesn&#8217;t really know anybody.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, good, an English accent, the sexiest accent you can have.</p>
<p>Penny: No. That&#8217;s not true. There&#8217;s French, there&#8217;s Italian. No, you&#8217;re right, it&#8217;s the best.</p>
<p>Leonard: Did you tell him you have a boyfriend?</p>
<p>Penny: It didn&#8217;t come up.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, maybe you should tell him.</p>
<p>Penny: What am I supposed to say?</p>
<p>Leonard: Say, can&#8217;t talk right now, hanging with my boyfriend. England sucks, you suck, USA number one.</p>
<p>Penny: Fine. Hanging with my boyfriend. Talk to you later. Happy?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes. Thank you.</p>
<p>Penny: Mm-hmm. Hmm.</p>
<p>Leonard: What did he say?</p>
<p>Penny: Nothing.</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Penny: Did your boyfriend make you type that?</p>
<p>Leonard: I hate this guy.</p>
<p>Penny: Don&#8217;t be like that.</p>
<p>Leonard: Come on, trust me, he&#8217;s hitting on you.</p>
<p>Penny: No, he&#8217;s not. We&#8217;re just friends. Look, is this gonna be a problem? Because he&#8217;s supposed to come over tomorrow to work.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really? Here?</p>
<p>Penny: If it makes you uncomfortable, I&#8217;ll switch partners, even though the thing&#8217;s due next week and everyone already has a partner and I&#8217;ll probably end up failing the class.</p>
<p>Leonard: That&#8217;d be great. Thank you.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The corridor outside Sheldon&#8217;s office.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Two forty-four, Right on schedule. Hey, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, hello.</p>
<p>Howard: Raj and I are heading over to the genetics lab to pet the glow-in-the-dark bunny. Want to come with us?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, thank you.</p>
<p>Raj: Are you sure? They turn off the lights, and it&#8217;s like a cute little laser show that poops all over the place.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m quite sure. Good day.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, where are you going?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Where are you going?</p>
<p>Raj: We just told you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I just told you.</p>
<p>Howard: No, you didn&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, it&#8217;s your word against mine; see you in court.</p>
<p>Howard: Should we follow him?</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t know, I&#8217;m torn. I want to know where he&#8217;s going, but now I kind of want to play with the bunny.</p>
<p>Alex: Hi, guys.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, Alex, do you know where your boss just went?</p>
<p>Alex: No.</p>
<p>Howard: Don&#8217;t you know his schedule?</p>
<p>Alex: All I know is corduroy makes too much noise and I have to go find quieter pants.</p>
<p>Howard: Come on.</p>
<p>Raj: Boy, what I wouldn&#8217;t give to get her out of those pants and into something a little more stylish.</p>
<p><em>Scene: A corridor in the basement. Sheldon takes out a key, unlocks a door and enters.</em></p>
<p>Howard: This is where he goes? What&#8217;s in there?</p>
<p>Raj: I think it&#8217;s an old storage room.</p>
<p>Howard: What could he be doing in there every day for twenty minutes?</p>
<p>Raj: Well, he&#8217;s not doing twenty-minute abs, because if he were, he would have way better abs. Can you hear anything?</p>
<p>Howard: Not yet. What are you doing?</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m listening.</p>
<p>Howard: Can&#8217;t you face the other way and listen?</p>
<p>Raj: I can&#8217;t do anything right for you, can I?</p>
<p>Howard: What the hell is he up to?</p>
<p>Raj: He is kind of a weirdo. Maybe he&#8217;s got Leonard Nimoy chained up in there. Or Bill Gates. Or Stephen Hawking.</p>
<p>Howard: Why would he chain up Stephen Hawking?</p>
<p>Raj: Howard, please, you can&#8217;t treat the man differently just because he&#8217;s disabled. That&#8217;s not okay.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: If you&#8217;re wondering why I&#8217;ve been staring through the peephole, I&#8217;m trying to get a look at this guy who&#8217;s coming over to Penny&#8217;s.</p>
<p>Sheldon: To be honest, I didn&#8217;t know you were here.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s not a big deal. He&#8217;s just in her history class. They&#8217;re working on a project together. I don&#8217;t even know why I care. You know what, I don&#8217;t care.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You think you don&#8217;t care?</p>
<p>Leonard: This is silly. I have nothing to worry about.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I don&#8217;t know. I mean, statistically speaking, I&#8217;m sure you have something to worry about.</p>
<p>Leonard: What do you mean?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, if we assume your looks are average, yeah, right off the bat, fifty percent of men on Earth are more attractive than you. That&#8217;s one point five billion handsome lads standing by, waiting to rain on your parade.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, yeah, but this isn&#8217;t just about looks. I&#8217;m, I&#8217;m way above average in a lot of other things.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Not height, vision, athleticism, or ability to digest dairy products.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m talking about important things like emotional maturity.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You were just spying on your girlfriend through a peephole.</p>
<p>Leonard: I liked it better when you thought I wasn&#8217;t here.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m not saying you don&#8217;t have attractive qualities. Your choice of friends is impeccable, you&#8217;re a good sleeper, and last but not least, you buy the grapes I like. You&#8217;re a real catch compared to some snoring guy with a fridge full of lousy grapes.</p>
<p>Leonard: None of this matters. I trust that Penny cares about me, and nothing&#8217;s gonna happen with this guy.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, unless of course he&#8217;s a skilled hypnotist.</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: While unlikely, it&#8217;s still a statistical possibility. She might be performing sexual acts with him and not even know it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Now you&#8217;re just being ridiculous.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Am I? The mind&#8217;s a mysterious thing, Leonard. He could be having the time of his life while she thinks she&#8217;s a chicken pecking for corn.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The university basement.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Look at us, sneaking around in the middle of the night like a couple of cat burglars.</p>
<p>Howard: I think we&#8217;re more like ninjas.</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t want to be a ninja, I want to be a cat burglar.</p>
<p>Howard: Fine, I&#8217;ll be a ninja, you be a cat burglar.</p>
<p>Raj: No, we both have to be the same thing. Okay, we&#8217;re ninjas.</p>
<p>Howard: Thank you.</p>
<p>Raj: But next time, we&#8217;ll be cat burglars.</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s locked.</p>
<p>Raj: Are you sure?</p>
<p>Howard: Yes, I&#8217;m sure. Now, you stand guard, I&#8217;m going to&#8230;</p>
<p>Raj<em> (trying door)</em>: It&#8217;s locked.</p>
<p>Howard: Just keep an eye out. I&#8217;ll have this open in a minute.</p>
<p>Raj: When did you learn how to pick locks?</p>
<p>Howard: When I was starting to do magic in junior high, I thought I could be an escape artist like Harry Houdini.</p>
<p>Raj: How did that work out?</p>
<p>Howard: Pretty good. I managed to escape friends, popularity, and every party thrown in a twelve mile radius. There. Ready?</p>
<p>Raj: Hold on, hold on.</p>
<p>Howard: What?</p>
<p>Raj: Sheldon is a very smart man, and he obviously wants to keep this a secret.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, so?</p>
<p>Raj: What if it&#8217;s booby-trapped?</p>
<p>Howard: Don&#8217;t worry, I&#8217;m one step ahead of him.</p>
<p>Raj: Great, what&#8217;s your pl&#8230; <i>(Howard pushes him inside)</i></p>
<p>Howard: Are we good?</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah. Huh. Forty-three? What the hell does that mean?</p>
<p>Howard: I don&#8217;t know. The solution to an equation?</p>
<p>Raj: Maybe. It&#8217;s a prime number. Encryption systems are built on prime numbers.</p>
<p>Howard: What kind of secret does Sheldon have to encrypt?</p>
<p>Raj: He&#8217;s always been very cagey about what he puts in his egg salad to make it so tasty.</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s paprika.</p>
<p>Raj: Really? Well, oh, okay, one mystery solved.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Voice from Outside: Okay, good night.</p>
<p>Penny <i>(off)</i>: Thanks, Cole. See you at school.</p>
<p>Cole <i>(off): </i>See you.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;ll be right back.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I thought you left a long time ago.</p>
<p>Leonard <i>(rushing to catch up with Cole)</i>: &#8216;sup?</p>
<p>Cole: Hey.</p>
<p>Leonard: You, uh, moving into the apartment on the fifth floor?</p>
<p>Cole: No, I was just visiting a friend.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, cool. That cute blonde on four?</p>
<p>Cole: Yeah. You know her?</p>
<p>Leonard: I, well, I see her around. I like to keep my distance because her boyfriend is a pretty scary dude.</p>
<p>Cole: Really?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah. He&#8217;s ganged up.</p>
<p>Cole: She told me he&#8217;s a scientist.</p>
<p>Leonard: That&#8217;s the name of his gang. The Scientists. They are crazy.</p>
<p>Cole: Well, thanks for the tip.</p>
<p>Leonard: No problem, brother. Stay frosty. <i>(Cole leaves. Leonard spots Penny watching.) </i>We&#8217;re still dating, right?</p>
<p><em>Scene: The cafeteria.</em></p>
<p>Alex: Oh, hello, Dr. Hofstadter.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, Alex, and call me Leonard. Dr. Hofstadter is my father. And my mother. And my sister. And our cat. Although, I&#8217;m pretty sure Dr. Boots Hofstadter&#8217;s degree was honorary.</p>
<p>Alex: May I join you, Leonard?</p>
<p>Leonard: Sure.</p>
<p>Alex: Thanks.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, Alex, let me ask you something. My girlfriend knows this guy at school. He&#8217;s got an English accent.</p>
<p>Alex: Ooh, I love English accents.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, you all do. Anyway, I feel like he&#8217;s hitting on her. She says he&#8217;s just being nice and that I should trust her.</p>
<p>Alex: It&#8217;s probably harmless. You know how it is. I&#8217;m sure you get hit on all the time.</p>
<p>Leonard: Right. Because girls are always like, ooh, that guy owns two Star Trek uniforms and gets a lot of ear infections. I got to get me some of that.</p>
<p>Alex: I don&#8217;t know, I bet it happens more than you realize.</p>
<p>Leonard: Trust me, it doesn&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Alex: You sure? You&#8217;re cute, you&#8217;re funny. Maybe you&#8217;re getting hit on, and you don&#8217;t even know it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really?</p>
<p>Alex: Yep, pretty sure.</p>
<p>Leonard <i>(laughs)</i>: Okay, I got to get back to work. Thanks for listening.</p>
<p>Alex: No problem.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hope-hope no girls rip my clothes off on the way.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Raj&#8217;s office.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Come on, we&#8217;re smart guys. We can figure this out.</p>
<p>Howard: Forty-three. What is forty-three? Besides my mom&#8217;s neck size.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s the atomic number for technetium.</p>
<p>Howard: That stuff&#8217;s radioactive.</p>
<p>Raj: Do you think he&#8217;s building a bomb?</p>
<p>Howard: Ah, it took him two years to put together that Lego Death Star, I&#8217;m not worried.</p>
<p>Raj: Ooh, this could be something. forty-three is the number of calories in half a cup of fat-free yoghurt.</p>
<p>Howard: Why would you know that?</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m sorry. We can&#8217;t all eat whatever we want and still stay thin. Wait, in the Hitchhiker&#8217;s Guide to the Galaxy, isn&#8217;t forty-three the answer to the question of life, the universe,and everything?</p>
<p>Howard: That&#8217;s forty-two, dumb-ass.</p>
<p>Raj: Hey, hey. Feelings.</p>
<p>Howard: We should stop. I&#8217;m exhausted.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, me, too. We&#8217;ve got more important things to do. Who cares what stupid forty-three means?</p>
<p>Howard: Not me.</p>
<p>Raj: Let&#8217;s go home. You want to get something to eat?</p>
<p>Howard: Sounds good.</p>
<p>Raj: What the balls is forty-three?</p>
<p>Howard: I have to know!</p>
<p><em>Scene: The stairwell.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Hi.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, shouldn&#8217;t you be out with your gang, spray painting equations on the side of buildings?</p>
<p>Leonard: Come on, I&#8217;m sorry.</p>
<p>Penny: I just can&#8217;t believe you don&#8217;t trust me.</p>
<p>Leonard: I do, of course I do.</p>
<p>Penny: Then why did you embarrass me in front of my friend, who, by the way, knew exactly who you were?</p>
<p>Leonard: Really?</p>
<p>Penny: Your picture&#8217;s on my refrigerator.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh. You know, I&#8217;m really starting to not like this guy.</p>
<p>Penny: What is your problem? Do you use up all your thinking at work and then have none left over for when you get home?</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t know. It-it-it&#8217;s hard sometimes. Everywhere you go, guys hit on you, even if I&#8217;m standing right there. And they&#8217;re all taller than me. Why is everyone taller than me? You know what, this is all in my head. It&#8217;s my problem, not yours.</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, why do you always do this? Listen to me, you&#8217;re the one I&#8217;m with. You know I love you, so will you please relax because you&#8217;re driving me crazy.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know that&#8217;s the first time you ever said that you love me.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah.</p>
<p>Leonard: We&#8217;re just supposed to pretend it&#8217;s not a big deal?</p>
<p>Penny: That&#8217;s exactly what we&#8217;re gonna do because you&#8217;re about to make me cry, and we both know if I start crying, you&#8217;re gonna start crying.</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re right, you should go.</p>
<p>Penny <i>(crying)</i>: All right.</p>
<p>Leonard <i>(crying)</i>: She loves me. <i>(Receives text) </i>Hey, it&#8217;s Alex. Nice having coffee with you. If you want to talk more, I&#8217;m always available. Smiley face, smiley face. What a friendly girl.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The cafeteria.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Okay, picture&#8217;s up. Looks like the camera&#8217;s working.</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s good quality video.</p>
<p>Howard: It better be. It&#8217;s the spare camera for the Mars rover.</p>
<p>Raj: How did you get your hands on that?</p>
<p>Howard: Million dollar camera, ten dollar lock.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, my God, here he comes. This is it.</p>
<p>Howard: What the hell is that thing?</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t know.</p>
<p>Sheldon <i>(on screen)</i>: This is Dr. Sheldon Cooper. Experimental log. Wormhole generator test forty-four.</p>
<p>Howard: Wormhole generator test? <i>(On screen a wormhole appears)</i></p>
<p>Sheldon: The first forty-three parallel universes I&#8217;ve checked proved to be empty. I see no reason to suspect universe number forty-four will be any different. <i>(Puts his head in the wormhole)</i></p>
<p>Howard: Oh, my God!</p>
<p>Raj: Holy crap!</p>
<p>Sheldon<i> (with an alien creature stuck to his face)</i>: Oh! It&#8217;s eating my face!</p>
<p>Raj: Aah! It&#8217;s eating his face!</p>
<p>Sheldon <i>(throwing alien onto the laptop)</i>: I found your webcam and replaced the video feed. You two should be ashamed of yourselves.</p>
<p>Raj: Sheldon, we&#8217;re really sorry.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, really sorry.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Sorry? You may not realize it, but I have difficulty navigating certain aspects of daily life. You know, understanding sarcasm, feigning interest in others, not talking about trains as much as I want to. It&#8217;s exhausting. Which is why, for twenty minutes a day, I like to go down to that room, turn my mind off and do what I need to do to recharge.</p>
<p>Howard: But what are you doing in there?</p>
<p>Raj: And what does forty-three mean?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You don&#8217;t need to know, you don&#8217;t deserve to know, and you will never know.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, well, I know how to make your egg salad now.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {

				}
