		<h2 class="title">Series 2 Episode 23 &#8211; The Monopolar&nbsp;Expedition</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The apartment. Leonard is working on a whiteboard.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, boy.</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I can&#8217;t comment without violating our agreement that I don&#8217;t criticize your work.</p>
<p>Leonard: Then what was oh, boy?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Great restraint on my part.</p>
<p>Leonard: There&#8217;s nothing wrong with the science here.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Perhaps you mean a different thing than I do when you say science.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(making a change)</em>: Okay, how&#8217;s that?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You actually had it right in the first place. Once again, you&#8217;ve fallen for one of my classic pranks. Bazinga! Well, now here&#8217;s a peculiar e-mail. The president of the university wants me to meet him at his office tomorrow morning at 8 a.m.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It doesn&#8217;t say. It must be an emergency. Everyone at the university knows I eat breakfast at eight and move my bowels at 8:20.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes, how did we live before Twitter? I guess you&#8217;ll find out what it is in the morning.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s 14 hours away. For the next 840 minutes, I&#8217;m effectively one of Heisenberg&#8217;s particles, I know where I am or I know how fast I&#8217;m going, but I can&#8217;t know both. Yet how am I supposed to carry on with this huge annoying thing hovering over my head?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, I know the feeling.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: Outside Leonard’s bedroom.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Leonard. <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Leonard. <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, it’s two o’clock in the morning.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why is everybody keep telling me what time it is?</p>
<p>Leonard: Everybody?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You, the president of the university, his wife, their sullen teenage daughter. That entire family is fascinated by what time it is and whether people know it.</p>
<p>Leonard: You went to President Seibert&#8217;s house in the middle of the night?</p>
<p>Sheldon: He didn&#8217;t respond to my e-mail, his phone number is unlisted. Tell me what my other option was.</p>
<p>Leonard: You could have waited until morning. I know, look who I&#8217;m talking to.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Do you remember the grant proposal I submitted to the National Science Foundation to detect slow-moving monopoles at the magnetic North Pole?</p>
<p>Leonard: Hardly a day goes by when I don&#8217;t think about it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Aw, how nice. Well, a space opened up at the last minute on the NSF expedition to the Arctic Circle.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wait a minute. He offered to send you to the North Pole?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes. In fact, he was quite enthusiastic. He said, &#8220;Frankly, if I could send you tonight, I would.&#8221;</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, well, do you want to go?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Of course not. I&#8217;m a theoretical physicist, a career I chose in no small part because it&#8217;s indoors, but if I&#8217;m able to detect slow-moving magnetic monopoles there, I will be the scientist to confirm string theory. People will write books about me. Third-graders will create macaroni-art dioramas depicting scenes from my life.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sure, maybe a tableau of me trying to pummel you to death.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Sarcasm?</p>
<p>Leonard: Maybe.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m on the horns of a dilemma. Can you imagine me, Sheldon Cooper, at the North Pole?</p>
<p>Leonard: Easy peasy, I&#8217;m doing it right now.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m not good with cold, Leonard. How often have we had to leave a movie theatre because I got a headache from drinking the Icee too fast? I can&#8217;t go.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, then don&#8217;t go.</p>
<p>Sheldon: How can you say that? The scientific opportunity of a lifetime presents itself and my best friend says don&#8217;t go.</p>
<p>Leonard: All right, then go.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Listen to you. How can I possibly go?</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, what are the words I can say right now to end this conversation and let me go back to sleep?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Odd, President Seibert posed the exact same question.</p>
<p>Leonard: How was it resolved?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It wasn&#8217;t. His wife set their dogs on me and rendered the question moot.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The stairwell.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Just imagine. If he says yes, we&#8217;ll have an entire summer without Sheldon.</p>
<p>Raj: We could play outside.</p>
<p>Howard: We could sit on the left side of the couch.</p>
<p>Leonard: I could use the bathroom at 8:20.</p>
<p>Raj: Our dreams are very small, aren&#8217;t they?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Good news, gentlemen, I have tentatively accepted&#8230;</p>
<p>All: Yeah! Woo-hoo!</p>
<p>Sheldon: &#8230;the invitation to join the Arctic Expedition.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s not gonna be the same without you.</p>
<p>Howard: Godspeed.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thank you, but your sentiments may be premature.</p>
<p>Raj: Ooh, I don&#8217;t like where this is going.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I would like to propose that the three of you accompany me.</p>
<p>Howard: To the North Pole?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes.</p>
<p>Raj: Is this just so we won&#8217;t touch your stuff while you&#8217;re away?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;ll admit that was a concern. But the fact is, I&#8217;ll need a support team. And the three of you are my first choice.</p>
<p>Howard: Really?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, there are others who might be more qualified, but the thought of interviewing them gave me a stomach ache. Now, I know I&#8217;m proposing an enormous undertaking, so why don&#8217;t you take a few moments to discuss it?</p>
<p>Howard: We&#8217;re not really gonna go to the North Pole with him, are we?</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(voice)</em>: I&#8217;m still within earshot! You may want to wait for my door to close.</p>
<p>Howard: We&#8217;re not really gonna go to the North Pole with him, are we?</p>
<p>Leonard: Hang on. Let&#8217;s talk about it. This is a National Science Foundation expedition. I don&#8217;t know how we can turn it down.</p>
<p>Howard: Easy. Instead of saying, no, we don&#8217;t want to go on an NSF expedition, say, no, we don&#8217;t want to spend &#8220;three months stuck in a cabin in the Arctic Circle with an anal nutbag.&#8221;</p>
<p>Raj: But if we were part of the team that confirmed string theory, we could drink for free in any bar in any college town with a university that has a strong science program.</p>
<p>Leonard: Howard, this is big science. You could be the engineer who builds the equipment that puts us on the cover of magazines.</p>
<p>Howard: I could also be the engineer who builds the crossbow that kills Sheldon.</p>
<p>Raj: You still might get on a magazine.</p>
<p>Howard: So you guys are seriously considering this?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes.</p>
<p>Howard: And you think you can put up with Sheldon?</p>
<p>Raj: Well, I&#8217;m a Hindu. My religion teaches that if we suffer in this life we are rewarded in the next. Three months at the North Pole with Sheldon and I&#8217;m reborn as a well-hung billionaire with wings!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, gentlemen, have you reached a decision?</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m in.</p>
<p>Raj: Me, too.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, damn it. Peer pressure. Fine.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Excellent. And just an FYI, as I am the expedition&#8217;s team leader, protocol dictates that be phrased fine, sir. But don&#8217;t worry, there will be a briefing.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Outside Penny’s door.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Sheldon.<em> </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Sheldon.<em> </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Sheldon.<em> </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Sheldon.<em> </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Sheldon.<em> </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Sheldon.<em> </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: What do you want?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I need access to the Cheesecake Factory&#8217;s walk-in freezer.</p>
<p>Penny: Now, honey, I already told you, the hamburger meat is fresh and stored at a safe temperature.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. This is to train for a three-month expedition to the magnetic North Pole.</p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t know how that sentence could possibly confuse you, but to elaborate, I&#8217;m going to the Arctic Circle with Leonard, Wolowitz and Koothrappali.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re all going?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes.</p>
<p>Penny: For three months?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes.</p>
<p>Penny: Excuse me.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Is that a yes or a no on the freezer? The woman has the attention span of a gnat.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hi.</p>
<p>Penny: Sheldon says you&#8217;re going to the North Pole.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah. Pretty cool, huh?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah. I&#8217;m just a little surprised you didn&#8217;t tell me.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, well, it all happened kind of fast, and we had to get physicals and buy thermal underwear and study up on, you know, snow and stuff. Sorry, I was gonna tell you.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, hey, no, you don&#8217;t have to apologize. There&#8217;s no reason you have to tell me. I was just, you know, surprised.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, yes, you were busy, you were surprised, all very fascinating. Now where do we stand on the freezer?</p>
<p>Penny: Is he serious?</p>
<p>Leonard: Actually, it would help.</p>
<p>Penny: All right, I&#8217;ll see what I can do. So, three months at the North Pole. Wow, that is awesome.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry, but at what point do you put this see what you can do plan into action?</p>
<p>Penny: Just a warning, Sheldon, the freezer locks from the outside.</p>
<p>Leonard: Did she seem upset to you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. Did she seem upset to you?</p>
<p>Leonard: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, good, I got it right. Are you upset?</p>
<p>Leonard: A little bit.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Two for two. I&#8217;m on fire.</p>
<p>Leonard: I mean, I know she&#8217;s not my girlfriend or anything, but wouldn&#8217;t you think she&#8217;d feel a little bad that I&#8217;m going to be gone for the whole summer?</p>
<p>Sheldon: That feels like a bonus question. I&#8217;m going to stop here while I&#8217;m ahead, but I’ve had a great time.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Inside the cheesecake factory freezer.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Alright, now the purpose of this drill is to acclimate us to the use of tools in extreme temperatures such as we will face in the Arctic Circle.</p>
<p>Raj: Where are your tools?</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(pointing at his brain)</em>: Right here. All right, team, open up your practice kits. As the university did not permit me to bring the actual equipment we&#8217;ll be using to the Cheesecake Factory, because apparently I&#8217;m &#8220;ridiculous,&#8221; I&#8217;ve provided substitutes which will exercise your fine motor skills. Leonard, you will be doing a series of complex mathematical problems on a vintage Casio model 1175 calculator watch I received when I won the Earth Science medal in 3rd grade. Treat it with respect. Raj, you will be painting sideburns and a Van Dyke on a six-inch figurine of Legolas the elf. Now, remember, a Van Dyke is a goatee without a moustache. Wolowitz, you will be completing a series of delicate surgical procedures on the classic children&#8217;s game, Operation. To begin with, you will remove funny bone for two hundred dollars.</p>
<p>Howard: For this I went to MIT.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And begin.</p>
<p>Raj: I think I swallowed some paint!</p>
<p>Leonard: I can&#8217;t press any of the buttons with my gloves. Oh, son of a bitch!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Adversity is to be expected. Continue.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, boy, am I gonna get sued.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, I can&#8217;t do this.</p>
<p>Raj: Me either.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Gentlemen, use your imagination. Innovate. Did Han Solo let Luke Skywalker freeze to death on the ice planet of Hoth? No. He cut open a tauntaun and used its internal body heat to warm him up.</p>
<p>Howard: You heard the man. Hold him down and I&#8217;ll cut him open.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hang on, I know I don&#8217;t possess the tools of leadership, but I don&#8217;t understand why we can&#8217;t assemble the equipment inside the hut and then take it outside.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I hadn&#8217;t thought of that. I guess we&#8217;re done here.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment kitchen.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Here, drink slash eat this.</p>
<p>Leonard: What is it?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It’s hot chocolate with a stick of butter.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, why?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Because in the frigid temperatures in the Arctic, we need to consume at least 5,000 calories a day just to maintain our body weight.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, you know I can&#8217;t eat butter. I&#8217;m lactose intolerant.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Way ahead of you, that&#8217;s an &#8220;I Can&#8217;t Believe It&#8217;s Not Butter&#8221; stick.</p>
<p>Raj <em>(on his webcam)</em>: But mummy, all the other guys are going to the North Pole.</p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali: I don&#8217;t care what the other guys are doing. If the other guys jumped in the Bay of Bengal and tried to swim to Sri Lanka, would you follow them?</p>
<p>Raj: If you were standing behind me nagging, I might.</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: Don&#8217;t talk back to your mother. This trip is much too dangerous, Rajesh.</p>
<p>Raj: No, it&#8217;s not. Howard, tell them.</p>
<p>Howard: Doctor and Mrs. Koothrappali, namaste. I understand your concern, but if it&#8217;ll make you feel any better, my mother is fine with me going, and this is a woman who kept a safety rail on my bed until I was 17.</p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali: So, she has no problem with her son being eaten by a walrus?</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s very unlikely, Mrs. Koothrappali. If Raj dies, it&#8217;ll be from frostbite, gangrene, acute sunburn or being ripped to shreds by a fifteen hundred pound polar bear.</p>
<p>Howard: Ma, I&#8217;m putting you on speakerphone with Raj&#8217;s parents. Can you tell them that you&#8217;re okay with me going to the Arctic?</p>
<p>Howard’s Mother: Arctic? I thought you said Arkansas!</p>
<p>Howard: I didn&#8217;t say that. You never listen to me!</p>
<p>Howard’s Mother: He doesn&#8217;t tell me anything! He lives a secret life because he&#8217;s ashamed!</p>
<p>Penny <em>(arriving)</em>: Hey, Leonard, can I talk to you for a sec?</p>
<p>Leonard: Sure, but let&#8217;s go out here where there&#8217;s a little less yelling and guilt. What&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, I got you a little going away present.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, a blanket.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, no, no, no, not just a blanket. See, it has sleeves. Yeah!  So, you can, you know, be all snoodled up while you do your science stuff.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, wow, cool.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, I&#8217;m gonna miss you.<em> (She hugs him for a long time) </em>See you later.</p>
<p>Leonard: Bye. <em> </em></p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali: I told you no. Why don&#8217;t you believe me?</p>
<p>Howard’s Mother: &#8216;Cause it doesn&#8217;t make sense to me! How can it be that in the entire country of India, there isn&#8217;t one Outback Steakhouse?!</p>
<p><em>Scene: Sheldon’s bedroom. Sheldon is asleep. There is a knock on the door.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, Sheldon?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I want a cookie, Meemaw.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, it&#8217;s me.</p>
<p>Sheldon: But Meemaw just made cookies.</p>
<p>Leonard: Listen, I don&#8217;t know if I can go on the expedition.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What?</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t think I can go to the North Pole.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay, Leonard, I know you&#8217;re concerned about disappointing me but I want you to take comfort from the knowledge that my expectations of you are very low.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, that&#8217;s very comforting.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Comforting is a part of leadership. It&#8217;s not a part I care for, but such is my burden.</p>
<p>Leonard: Terrific, it&#8217;s just that I don&#8217;t think Penny wants me to go.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Assuming that&#8217;s a valid reason not to go, which it isn&#8217;t, how do you know this? Did she say it?</p>
<p>Leonard: Not exactly. But she said she was gonna miss me and she gave me this.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What is it?</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s a blanket with sleeves.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, that&#8217;s clever. Let me see if I understand this correctly. Her missing you is an emotional state you find desirable?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes, obviously.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right. Well, given that missing you is predicated on you leaving, logic dictates you must leave.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes, okay, but I&#8217;m gonna be gone for three months. What if she doesn&#8217;t miss me that long and she meets someone else?</p>
<p>Sheldon: She does have a short attention span.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, I can&#8217;t go.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, you may be right. It appears that Penny secretly wants you in her life in a very intimate and carnal fashion.</p>
<p>Leonard: You really think so?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Of course not. Even in my sleep-deprived state, I&#8217;ve managed to pull off another one of my classic pranks. Bazinga!</p>
<p><em>Scene: Outside Penny’s door. Leonard is knocking.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Oh, Leonard, what time is it?</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s 7 a.m. I&#8217;m sorry it&#8217;s early, but we&#8217;re leaving soon, and I needed to talk to you.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay.</p>
<p>Leonard: What did you mean when you said you&#8217;re going to miss me?</p>
<p>Penny: Um, I don&#8217;t know. You&#8217;ll be gone and I&#8217;ll notice.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(indicating the blanket)</em>: Okay, well, um, what about this? What does this mean?</p>
<p>Penny: Wine, credit card and late night television are a bad combination.</p>
<p>Leonard: All right, fine. What about that really long hug? What did that mean?</p>
<p>Penny: That wasn&#8217;t a long hug.</p>
<p>Leonard: It was at least five Mississippis. A standard hug is two Mississippis tops.</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, I don&#8217;t know what to tell you. It was just a hug.</p>
<p>Leonard: Glad we cleared that up.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah.</p>
<p>Leonard: I guess I&#8217;ll see you.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, have a safe trip.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thank you. Bye.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, bye. <em>(Penny closes door. To herself) </em>Means I wish you weren&#8217;t going.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The North Pole.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Damn it.</p>
<p>Howard: What?</p>
<p>Leonard: We&#8217;re out of ice.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, men, we begin initial assembly and deployment of the testing equipment starting tomorrow at 0700 hours, but until then, you are all off duty. I suggest you keep the shenanigans to a minimum as medical help is 18 hours away by dogsled.</p>
<p>Raj: What are you working on?</p>
<p>Howard: Crossbow.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, guys, can I just say something? How about we take a moment to think about where we are right now? This is literally the top of the world. Only a handful of people in all of human history will ever see what we are going to see.</p>
<p>Raj: He&#8217;s right.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, wow.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It is remarkable.</p>
<p>Raj: So, who&#8217;s up for a movie?</p>
<p>Howard: Good idea, what do you think? Ice Station Zebra or John Carpenter&#8217;s The Thing?</p>
<p>Raj: I say double feature.</p>
<p>Leonard: Dinner&#8217;s ready!</p>
<p>Sheldon: What are we having?</p>
<p>Leonard: Reconstituted Thai food.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Did you bring the dehydrated low-sodium soy sauce?</p>
<p>Leonard: Check.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Freeze-dried spicy mustard?</p>
<p>Leonard: Check.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Flash-frozen brown rice, not white?</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, oh, sorry.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Not to worry. I hid it. Bazinga! You&#8217;re in my spot.</p>
<p>Howard: There&#8217;s no time for a crossbow. Find me an icicle.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Three months. This is gonna be great!</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				} else {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='55px' width='100%' frameBorder='0' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).load( function( e ) {
					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );
					$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loaded' );

					JetpackLikespostMessage( { event: 'loadLikeWidget', name: $iframe.attr( 'name' ), width: $iframe.width() }, window.frames[ 'likes-master' ] );

					if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
				setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 250 );
			}
