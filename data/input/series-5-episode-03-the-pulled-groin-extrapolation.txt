<p>Howard: Why would she move out? It&#8217;s her house.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hang on. You seriously think I&#8217;m going to live with your mother?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Howard, I think I can help here. Yes, Bernadette, that&#8217;s exactly what he thinks.</p>

<p>Bernadette: Take me home now.</p>
<p>Howard: Movie theatre.</p>
<p>Raj: Mmmmmm.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay, everybody calm down. There is a simple solution here. Raj, take me to the train store, and then I don&#8217;t care what you people do.</p>

<p>Amy: How was your shower?</p>
<p>Leonard: It was good, good. Just out of curiosity, what time do you usually go to bed?</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, I&#8217;m up all night. I&#8217;m like a possum. Boy, you were not liked in high school, were you?</p>
<p>Leonard: Not really. Is that my yearbook?</p>
<p>Amy: Mm-hmm. Dear Leonard, you&#8217;re really good at science. Maybe one day you&#8217;ll come up with a cure for being a dork.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, it wasn&#8217;t spray-painting a lightning bolt on my briefcase, I can tell you that.</p>
<p>Amy: If it makes you feel any better, the only person who signed my yearbook was my mother.</p>
<p>Leonard: Aw.</p>
<p>Amy: Dear Amy, self-respect and a hymen are better than friends and fun. Love, Mom.</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(entering)</em>: Well, you can add Jerry&#8217;s Junction to the list of train stores Sheldon Cooper will never set foot in again.</p>

<p>Sheldon: You don&#8217;t know the half of it. It was billed as a lively give-and-take on the merits of model train sizes. But it was actually a set-up to intimidate weak-minded spineless rubes into buying H-O starter sets.</p>

<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t want to talk about it. But it&#8217;s not a spine,I&#8217;ll tell you that.</p>

<p>Leonard: Wouldn&#8217;t you rather bring Sheldon?</p>
<p>Amy: I would, but the last wedding we went to was a disaster. He behaved like a child the entire time.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Not my fault. You said there&#8217;d be other scientists there my age.</p>
<p>Amy: Doesn&#8217;t matter. You&#8217;re out, he&#8217;s in. No date to the prom, two dates to a wedding. Hmm, how times change.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Ha-ha, you have to go to a wedding.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Outside Howard&#8217;s house. Amy rings the bell.</em></p>
<p>Howard <em>(off)</em>: I&#8217;ll get it!</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: Could you get it?</p>
<p>Howard <em>(off)</em>: I said I&#8217;m getting it!</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: Fine, I&#8217;ll get it!</p>
<p>Howard: I got it! Oh, hi.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hey. I don&#8217;t want to fight. I was just surprised when you sprung the whole living-with-your-mom stuff on me.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, well, I&#8217;m sorry I didn&#8217;t run it by you first.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: I don&#8217;t know who you&#8217;re talking to, but in or out! We don&#8217;t need bugs!</p>

<p>Howard: Sure she would. Ma, do you mind if Bernadette stays here this weekend?</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: Hey, if she&#8217;s willing to give the milk away for free, who am I to say no?</p>
<p>Howard: See? She&#8217;s good with it.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: Frankly, after all your sleepovers with the little brown boy, a girl is a big relief!</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. Sheldon is playing with his train set.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: All this years, I&#8217;ve been so wrong. The tinier the train, the more concentrated the fun.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re a brain scientist. Can you explain to me why a brilliant man likes playing with toy trains?</p>
<p>Amy: Not without cutting his head open, no. How about making my eyes like Cleopatra?</p>

<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: Oh, I know what that means! Hubba-hubba!</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, God.</p>
<p>Howard: Relax, it&#8217;ll be fine.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Okay.</p>
