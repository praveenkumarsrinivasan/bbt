<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: Why are you reading Pride and Prejudice?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: I&#8217;ll tell you why. Amy ruined Raiders of the Lost Ark for me, so now I&#8217;m trying to find something beloved to her and ruin that.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: Wait, how can, how can anyone ruin Raiders? It&#8217;s perfect.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">All: Aw!</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: Okay, I know you don&#8217;t want to talk about it, but can I just ask you one question about your mom&#8217;s book?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: No.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: I just want to know why a five-year-old boy puts on his mom&#8217;s make-up and wears balloon boobies.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: They weren&#8217;t boobies, they were muscles. And the make-up was green. I was pretending to be The Hulk.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: You were wearing her bra.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: Okay, okay. But you know, as a student of psychology who got a check-plus on her first homework assignment, I think, sometimes, it&#8217;s good to open up about these things.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: Well, why were you so angry?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: Gee, I don&#8217;t know. Maybe it&#8217;s because I was always the subject of her little experiments. <p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: Really? Are you sure about that?</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">Stuart: Is make-up really necessary?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: Well, when someone looks at your dating profile, the first thing they see is your picture. I just want to make sure you look fun and full of life and not like a body they just pulled out of the river. All right, here we go, and smile. Okay, uh-uh, that one&#8217;s in the bank. How about this? Turn away, and then turn back into it. But when you do, imagine the camera is the girl you want to meet.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Stuart: You got it.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: Okay, uh, let&#8217;s try it again. Uh, but this time, pretend the girl you want to meet doesn&#8217;t want to hurt you.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: Almost. Oh, what did you put as the one-word description of yourself?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Stuart: I put unobjectionable. But now that I hear it out loud, it just seems like I&#8217;m being cocky. What did you put for your best feature?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: My parents&#8217; money. Uh, what did you put for your best feature?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Stuart: I put: not applicable.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: Come on, dude, you&#8217;re being too hard on yourself. You&#8217;ve got a lot of good stuff going on.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Stuart: Really? Like what?</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">Stuart: I do have one oddly long testicle.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Oh, it turns out Amy&#8217;s beloved Pride and Prejudice is a flawless masterpiece. He&#8217;s got too much pride, she&#8217;s got too much prejudice, it just works.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: So you&#8217;re looking to ruin something for her in the funny pages?</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: Maybe they fell in love with him as a puppy and didn&#8217;t know how big he was gonna be.</span></p>
