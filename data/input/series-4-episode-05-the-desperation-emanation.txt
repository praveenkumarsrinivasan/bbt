		<h2 class="title">Series 4 Episode 05 &#8211; The Desperation&nbsp;Emanation</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: All I&#8217;m saying is, if they took all the money they spent trying to make a decent Hulk movie, they could probably just make an actual Hulk.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That is apt and amusing. I think I shall share that with Amy Farrah Fowler. She&#8217;ll appreciate the witticism.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thank you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;ll also help improve her initial impression of you.</p>
<p>Leonard: So what&#8217;s going on with you two?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, the status is as it always was. She&#8217;s a girl. She&#8217;s a friend. She is not my, please forgive me for doing this, girlfriend.</p>
<p>Leonard: Right, right. So you&#8217;re still just texting and emailing? You don&#8217;t feel any need to hang out with her, you know, be in the same room?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, you are my best friend. I&#8217;ve known you for seven years, and I can barely tolerate sitting on the couch with you. Imagine my attitude regarding prolonged physical proximity to Amy Farrah Fowler.</p>
<p>Leonard: Got it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I sense judgment on your part.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, no. Maybe a little.</p>
<p>Sheldon: May I suggest that your criticism is based on jealousy.</p>
<p>Leonard: Jealousy? What do I have to be jealous of?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I have a functioning and satisfying relationship with a female. You have none.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, right. That.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Jealousy is an ugly green-eyed monster, not unlike the Hulk, who, by the way, also has a girlfriend. In this iteration, Jennifer Connelly, whom you may recall as the girlfriend of Russell Crowe in A Beautiful Mind, a feel-good romp if there ever was one.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m not jealous.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hu-u-urgh! Leonard not jealous.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence. </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: You know, it just occurred to me, if there are an infinite number of parallel universes, in one of them, there&#8217;s probably a Sheldon who doesn&#8217;t believe parallel universes exist.</p>
<p>Leonard: Probably. What&#8217;s your point?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No point. It&#8217;s just one of the things that makes one of the mes chuckle. What makes you chuckle, Leonard?</p>
<p>Leonard: Hmm, recently? Not much.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Is it because of the conflict that arises from your desperate need to pair-bond with a woman, and the apparent collective decision of all womankind to deny you that opportunity?</p>
<p>Leonard: Um, shut up.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You know, when my grandfather died, and Meemaw was alone, my mom arranged for her to visit a centre three times a week where she could socialize with other lonely people. It&#8217;s very nice. They discuss current events, play bridge, get a hot meal.</p>
<p>Leonard: That sounds lovely.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It is if you like bridge. Do you like bridge, Leonard?</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, I&#8217;m just not dating someone right now. I don&#8217;t need to go to a senior centre.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Meemaw resisted at first, but now she loves it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Fine. If I don&#8217;t meet someone soon, you can put me in a home.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s not a home. It&#8217;s a senior centre.  We&#8217;d never put Meemaw in a home!</p>
<p><em>Scene: The comic book store.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, guys.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey.</p>
<p>Raj: Hey.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, I&#8217;ll bow to social pressure. Hey!</p>
<p>Howard <em>(phone rings)</em>: Excuse me. That&#8217;s my girlfriend, Bernadette. I assigned her her own ringtone, Bernadette by The Four Tops. Hello, Bernadette.</p>
<p>Raj: When I call him, his phone plays Brown Eyed Girl. Which, now that I think about it, is not so good.</p>
<p>Leonard: You realize he&#8217;s just rubbing our noses in the fact that he has a girlfriend, and we don&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Rai: You mean, you don&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Leonard: You have a girlfriend? How could you have a girlfriend? You can&#8217;t even speak to women.</p>
<p>Raj: Two words, deaf chick. It doesn&#8217;t matter if I can&#8217;t talk, because she can&#8217;t hear me.</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s what she said.</p>
<p>Leonard: Great. You have a girlfriend, Howard&#8217;s got a girlfriend, Sheldon&#8217;s got a girl&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, no, no, no!</p>
<p>Leonard: &#8230;who&#8217;s a friend.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thank you.</p>
<p>Leonard: What about you, Stuart? You have a girlfriend yet?</p>
<p>Stuart: Oh, yeah. Yeah. I met her at Comic-Con, the one place in the world where saying I own a comic bookstore is an actual pickup line.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh. Well, good for you.</p>
<p>Stuart: Not really. She&#8217;s horrible. When she wants to have sex, she puts on her plus-size Wonder Woman costume and shouts who wants to take a ride in my invisible plane?</p>
<p>Leonard: Why don&#8217;t you just break up with her?</p>
<p>Stuart: No, no, I can&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why not?</p>
<p>Stuart: &#8216;Cause then I&#8217;d be alone, like you.</p>
<p>Raj: Dude, why don&#8217;t you just invoke your girlfriend pact with Wolowitz?</p>
<p>Leonard: Because I don&#8217;t need his girlfriend to set me up with one of her girlfriends. I&#8217;m perfectly capable of finding a girl on my own.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, Leonard, you remind me of the funny old story about a man who walks into a women&#8217;s correctional institution with a stack of paperwork that will allow the female convicts to go free.</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re saying I couldn&#8217;t get laid in a women&#8217;s prison with a handful of pardons.</p>
<p>Raj: Are you going to let me tell the story or not?</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. Leonard is stretched face down on the sofa.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard? Are you sleeping?</p>
<p>Leonard: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Are you ill?</p>
<p>Leonard: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Are you still depressed because you&#8217;re alone, and no one loves you?</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t know. Maybe.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I want you to know that I&#8217;m genuinely concerned about your well-being.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thank you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You&#8217;re welcome. But it&#8217;s still no reason to have your feet in my spot.</p>
<p>Amy <em>(on webcam)</em>: May I offer an observation?</p>
<p>Leonard: What the hell? How long has she been here?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Since we got home from the comic bookstore.</p>
<p>Leonard: That was two hours ago.</p>
<p>Sheldon: As per your suggestion, we&#8217;re hanging out. Quite frankly, I don&#8217;t see what all the hoopla&#8217;s about. Go on, Amy.</p>
<p>Amy: From a neurobiological standpoint, Leonard, you may be alone because your psychological state is causing an actual pheromone-based stink of desperation.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Did you know that, Leonard?</p>
<p>Leonard: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I did.</p>
<p>Amy: I understand that some people find mates in social gathering places such as bars or taverns. Have you tried a bar or tavern?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, I&#8217;m not gonna try to pick up a woman in a bar.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wise decision. You see, Amy, success at a bar is based on classic male attributes such as height, strength, attractiveness, the ability to hold one&#8217;s liquor and throw darts, separately or together. Leonard has none of these attributes, right, Leonard?</p>
<p>Leonard: Right.</p>
<p>Amy: That&#8217;s not true of all bars. Juice bars, for example.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh! Good point, Amy. Yeah, building on your premise, Leonard could frequent sushi bars, salad bars, oyster bars, the Apple Genius Bar, what are you doing?</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(exiting the apartment)</em>: Keep going. I&#8217;m listening.</p>
<p>Amy: That was rude.</p>
<p>Sheldon: He does it all the time. He&#8217;s a cornucopia of social awkwardness.</p>
<p>Amy: Cornucopia. What a mellifluous word.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Let&#8217;s make that our word of the day.</p>
<p>Amy: Agreed. And we&#8217;ll use mellifluous tomorrow.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, I have to void my bladder. It was nice spending this time with you.</p>
<p>Amy: Wait. I have a request.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes?</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;d like you to meet my mother.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I see. Can I get back to you on that?</p>
<p>Amy: Certainly. Good night.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Good night. <em>(Closing laptop, and running down stairs in panic) L</em>eonard, Leonard, Leonard,</p>
<p>Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard, Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, what?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Amy Farrah Fowler has asked me to meet her mother.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah. So?</p>
<p>Sheldon: What does that mean?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, you know how you&#8217;re always saying that Amy is a girl who&#8217;s your friend, and not your girlfriend?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Uh-huh.</p>
<p>Leonard: You can&#8217;t say that anymore.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wait. What?</p>
<p>Leonard: Look, she obviously wants to take your relationship to the next level.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t want the next level. I like this level. Fix it for me!</p>
<p>Leonard: Me? Well, how am I supposed to fix it?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Simple! You want a girlfriend, Amy wants to be someone&#8217;s girlfriend. Take her off my hands. I give you my blessing.</p>
<p>Leonard: That is insane.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You&#8217;re right. It would never work. Amy finds you tedious.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, good luck.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wait! What am I supposed to do?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, have you considered telling her how you feel?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, I&#8217;m a physicist, not a hippie.</p>
<p>Leonard: All right, well, let me see if I can explain your situation using physics. What would you be if you were attached to another object by an inclined plane wrapped helically around an axis?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Screwed.</p>
<p>Leonard: There you go.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Amy&#8217;s right. He is tedious.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Outside Howard’s house. Leonard rings bell.</em></p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: Howard, get the door!</p>
<p>Howard <em>(off)</em>: Why can&#8217;t you get it?</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: You know I&#8217;m doing a bowel cleanse for my colonoscopy! I&#8217;m like an upside-down volcano here.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, hey. I hope you didn&#8217;t hear that.</p>
<p>Leonard: The volcano thing? No.</p>
<p>Howard: What&#8217;s with the T-shirt? You working at the Apple store now?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, it&#8217;s just something I threw on.</p>
<p>Howard: I know all your shirts. That&#8217;s not one of them. You were pretending to work at the Genius Bar to pick up women, weren&#8217;t you?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah. Turns out, they guard the iPods, but they don&#8217;t guard the shirts.</p>
<p>Howard: So, how&#8217;d it go?</p>
<p>Leonard: It was going well. I was showing this super hot girl how to boot up in Safe Mode. The manager got suspicious, and, well, long story short, they really do have a little jail in the mall.</p>
<p>Howard: Just FYI, don&#8217;t try to go back with a fake moustache. I mean, they may not really be geniuses, but they see right through that.</p>
<p>Leonard: I want to invoke the girlfriend pact, Howard.</p>
<p>Howard: You that desperate?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, I just, I want what you have. You know, I want a woman in my life.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: Holy Moses, how much liquid can be in one tukus?</p>
<p>Leonard: To be clear, I meant like Bernadette, not your mother.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, I know what you mean.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Well, I&#8217;m off to meet Bernadette&#8217;s friend. How do I look?</p>
<p>Sheldon: As if one of the plants from Invasion of the Body Snatchers duplicated you in every way, only with an absurd amount of hair gel.</p>
<p>Leonard: What are you working on?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m removing my digital footprint from the Internet so Amy Farrah Fowler can&#8217;t find me and compel me to meet her mother.</p>
<p>Leonard: Ah, you&#8217;re going off the grid.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Exactly.</p>
<p>Leonard: The old Unabomber approach. Kudos.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thank you. I&#8217;ve also sent Amy a relationship termination notice and changed my cell phone number and e-mail address.</p>
<p>Leonard: What if she just comes over?</p>
<p>Sheldon: She&#8217;ll get lost. We no longer live at 2311 Los Robles. We now live at 311 Los Robles.</p>
<p>Leonard: You changed the address on the building? What about mail?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, no worries, I explained my predicament to our letter carrier. He was sympathetic. His exact words were, Got your back, Jack. Bitches be crazy.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(there is a knock on the door)</em>: Who is it?</p>
<p>Amy <em>(off)</em>: Amy Farrah Fowler.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Darn! She found me!</p>
<p>Leonard: She&#8217;s been here before.</p>
<p>Sheldon: The only flaw in an otherwise perfect plan. Now, get my back, Jack.</p>
<p>Leonard: What do you want to do?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Tell her I&#8217;m not here.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, where are you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t know. You&#8217;ll have to devise a scenario that plausibly explains my absence, keeping in mind that the key to a good lie lies in the details.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hi, Amy. Sheldon&#8217;s not here.</p>
<p>Amy: All right. <em>(Leaves)</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Way to go on the details.</p>
<p><em>Scene: A restaurant.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Thanks again for doing this, Bernadette.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, I take pacts very seriously. One time at my lab, a petri dish of genetically modified super-virus went missing. That day we made a pinky swear never to admit we crossed Ebola with the common cold.</p>
<p>Howard: Why the hell would you cross Ebola with the common cold?</p>
<p>Bernadette: We never did. That would be a terrible, terrible thing.</p>
<p>Girl <em>(arriving)</em>: Hi. Sorry I&#8217;m late, I was at the gym. Spin class. Worked up quite a sweat.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Joy, this is Leonard. Leonard, this is Joy.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hi.</p>
<p>Joy: Hi. You don&#8217;t look like a genius. Go ahead, say something smart.</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh&#8230;</p>
<p>Joy: Aah! Time&#8217;s up. Just kidding. First thing you need to know about me, I&#8217;m hilarious.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah. So, Bernadette tells me she knows you from self-defence classes?</p>
<p>Joy: Yeah. Israeli Krav Maga. Lots of fun. Basically, a hundred different ways to rip a guy&#8217;s nuts off.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wow. Wouldn&#8217;t think there&#8217;d be that many.</p>
<p>Joy: Number 42!</p>
<p>Leonard: Whoa!</p>
<p>Bernadette: Isn&#8217;t she a pip?</p>
<p><em>Scene: The stairwell.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(rhythmically, while walking down the stairs)</em>: Proxima Centauri&#8217;s the nearest star. 					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
