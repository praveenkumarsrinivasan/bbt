		<h2 class="title">Series 6 Episode 11 &#8211; The Santa&nbsp;Simulation</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><i>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment.</i></p>
<p>Penny: Okay, now holding sides of hearth in position, lower mantelpiece assembly onto top tabs.</p>
<p>Leonard: Look at that, I built a fireplace with my own two hands.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re so butch.</p>
<p>Leonard: Aw, I got a little paper cut.</p>
<p>Penny: Of course you did. Your hands are softer than veal.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, before I forget, Saturday I&#8217;m planning a little Dungeons and Dragons night with the guys.</p>
<p>Penny: Really? That&#8217;s how you&#8217;re gonna spend your Saturday night?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, come on, I hardly ever get a chance to play anymore.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, you poor thing. Is having a real-life girlfriend who has sex with you getting in the way of your board games?</p>
<p>Leonard: Little bit, yeah.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Amy: Oh, great! I&#8217;ve always wanted to play Dungeons and Dragons.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, oh, I&#8217;m sorry. I should&#8217;ve mentioned this earlier. You&#8217;re not invited.</p>
<p>Amy: Why not?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Amy, from time to time, we men need to break free from the shackles of civility and get in touch with our primal animalistic selves.</p>
<p>Amy: By rolling dice and playing make-believe with little figurines?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, like a bunch of savages.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Howard and Bernadette&#8217;s apartment. Bernadette is on the webcam.</i></p>
<p>Bernadette: Saturday night? But I&#8217;ve been working late all week. That was gonna be our night.</p>
<p>Howard: But I have to go. We play as a group. If-if I&#8217;m not there, then everyone will blame you. They&#8217;ll be all, Bernadette ruined everything. She&#8217;s the worst. So, you see? I have to play Dungeons and Dragons for the marriage.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You&#8217;re an idiot.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m your idiot. Forever.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment.</i></p>
<p>Raj: So, listen, I know we talked about getting a bite to eat in Silver Lake, and then seeing the Christmas lights in Griffith Park, but Leonard&#8217;s talking about a big D and D game at his place.</p>
<p>Stuart: Saturday night just went from crazy to epic. Woo-hoo!</p>
<p><i>Credits sequence.</i></p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Leonard: All right, Sheldon, to start our quest you need to open this little Christmas gift I got you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: A Christmas gift? You know I don&#8217;t enjoy Christmas.</p>
<p>Stuart: What&#8217;s wrong with Christmas?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, where to begin? Trees indoors. Overuse of the words &#8217;tis and &#8217;twas. And the absurd custom of one stocking. Everyone knows socks belong in pairs. Who uses one sock?</p>
<p>Howard:Pirate with a peg leg?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Actually that helps, thank you.</p>
<p>Leonard: Would you just look inside?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, a scroll. I like scrolls. They&#8217;re my third favourite system of transmitting the written word. After stone tablets and skywriting. You have all been summoned to join a thrilling Dungeons and Dragons adventure. Your quest begins in a secret northern village of elves who have all been massacred. I like where this is heading. Your task is to rescue their leader from a dungeon of ogres. Oh, that&#8217;s a saucy twist. That leader&#8217;s name, Santa Claus. No, no, no.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s actually ho, ho, ho, but you&#8217;ll get the hang of it. Thought it&#8217;d be fun to make a quest with a holiday theme.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Fun? Mixing Dungeons and Dragons with Christmas is like making a sandwich of grape jelly, the best-tasting jelly, and petroleum jelly, the worst-tasting jelly.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, here we go. You find yourselves in the smoking remains of Santa&#8217;s village. Clearly, a great battle has taken place.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, man, the first monster I see, I&#8217;m gonna sneak up behind him, whip out my wand, and shoot my magic all over his ass.</p>
<p>Stuart: Do you hear yourself when you say these things?</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, there are ogre tracks and a trail of broken candy canes in the snow. Sheldon, what do you do?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I signal my contempt for your cruel plan to shove Christmas joy down my throat by making a gesture that says get a load of this guy.</p>
<p>Leonard: Fine. Howard, what do you do?</p>
<p>Howard: I follow the ogre tracks, checking for secret doors along the way.</p>
<p>Leonard: And you discover a secret door leading to a dark corridor.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, guys, let&#8217;s make a plan, spell casters in the back, warriors in the front.</p>
<p>Raj: No, screw that noise, I&#8217;m going in. Hang on, Santa, I&#8217;m coming for you.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, you run into a room full of weapons, hit a trip wire, a cannon blows your face off, you die, you&#8217;re out of the game.</p>
<p>Raj: But, a cannon? Am I really out of the game?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Lucky.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, come on, moving on.</p>
<p>Raj: Wait, doesn&#8217;t anyone have a rod of resurrection? Because if you&#8217;ve got one, I need it bad. Get in here with your rod and give it to me.</p>
<p>Stuart: Okay, you need to say these things in your head before you say them out loud.</p>
<p>Penny <i>(entering)</i>: Hey, guys. I don&#8217;t mean to interrupt your little game, but I thought you&#8217;d like to see what you&#8217;re missing out on. So, Bernadette? Bernadette&#8217;s wearing leopard-print pumps and a rack-tastic red dress from Forever 21. And there&#8217;s Amy, showing all kinds of ankle. In an outfit I&#8217;m assuming is from Forever 63. And I, myself am wearing a little number that got me out of two speeding tickets and jury duty.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I know they&#8217;re making a rhetorical point, I just don&#8217;t know what it is.</p>
<p>Penny: See you, boys. We are going drinking.</p>
<p>Raj: Uh, wait, can I come with you? My character died.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Sorry, Raj, it&#8217;s girls&#8217; night out.</p>
<p>Amy: Maybe another time.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: Come on.</p>
<p>Raj: Ooh! Girls&#8217; night, girls&#8217; night! Ooh! Ooh!</p>
<p>Stuart: How does he not hear that?</p>
<p><i>Scene: A bar.</i></p>
<p>Amy: So, what&#8217;s the plan? Are we gonna teach our fellas a lesson by getting stinking drunk, luring strange men into the bathroom, and turning the toilet stall into a temple of the senses?</p>
<p>Bernadette: No!</p>
<p>Penny: No!</p>
<p>Amy: Geez, who&#8217;s Forever 63 now?</p>
<p>Raj: Can we get another bottle of champagne for the table? Don&#8217;t worry. It&#8217;s my treat.</p>
<p>Amy: Thanks.</p>
<p>Penny: Wow, you should come to girls&#8217; night more often. And not just because if you weren&#8217;t here, this would be a can of Pabst.</p>
<p>Raj: My pleasure, nothing makes me happier than the chance to spoil a lady. Just ask my dog. My vet says if I give her any more foie gras, she&#8217;s going to die of gout.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hey, let&#8217;s help Raj meet a girl tonight.</p>
<p>Raj: No, no, no, I&#8217;m fine.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, wait, are we talking one-night stand or do we want to get him into a relationship?</p>
<p>Amy: Let&#8217;s get him laid!</p>
<p>Raj: Stop it. You&#8217;re ruining girls&#8217; night.</p>
<p>Penny: Raj, you&#8217;re a great guy, you&#8217;re in a bar full of single women, let us help you.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Yeah, you&#8217;re a real catch. I know you&#8217;re shy, but that doesn&#8217;t mean you shouldn&#8217;t have someone wonderful in your life.</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s sweet of you. But what can you do to help?</p>
<p>Bernadette: We&#8217;ll nose around, see if we can find a nice girl, and then introduce you.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay. Well, a couple of things. Don&#8217;t tell them I come from money. I want them to love me for me. They must be insanely hot. Like, nines or tens.</p>
<p>Penny: Nines or tens?</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, an eight is acceptable if she&#8217;s willing to bring another eight to the hot tub.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Bottom line, you&#8217;ll take any woman who&#8217;ll have you, right?</p>
<p>Raj: In a New Delhi minute.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Stuart: I don&#8217;t remember you buying these miniatures in my store.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, uh, yeah. I got &#8216;em on Amazon.</p>
<p>Stuart: Sure. I get it. Why support a friend when you can support a multinational conglomerate that is crushing the life out of that friend?</p>
<p>Leonard: I know, but when I shop online, I can do it on the toilet.</p>
<p>Stuart: Have you seen my store? The whole place is a toilet.</p>
<p>Howard: Can we please move this along?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, sorry. Uh, you come to the end of the tunnel and find a large chest. What do you do? And, Howard, do not say, I feel up the large chest.</p>
<p>Howard: Excuse me, I&#8217;m a married man now. I wasn&#8217;t going to say anything so juvenile.</p>
<p>Leonard: Great. What do you do?</p>
<p>Howard: I walk up to the large chest, bury my face in it and go “blublublublublublublublu”</p>
<p>Sheldon: I open the chest.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s locked, but suddenly the door behind you slams shut and now the walls of the room start closing in.</p>
<p>Stuart: That&#8217;s not good. My character and I both have claustrophobia.</p>
<p>Leonard: Glowing letters appear on the chest that read, If squashed to death you wish not to be, sing of Svaty Vaclav and his victory.</p>
<p>Howard: Who the hell is Svaty Vaclav?</p>
<p>Leonard: Walls are getting closer.</p>
<p>Stuart: Oh, boy, happy place, happy place.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wait, Svaty Vaclav was the Duke of Bohemia.</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re ten seconds away from getting crushed. Nine, eight.</p>
<p>Howard: What are we supposed to do?</p>
<p>Leonard: Seven, six.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wait, wait. Svaty Vaclav is better known as Good King Wenceslas from the beloved Victorian Christmas carol.</p>
<p>Howard: Never heard of it. Must be the one Christmas song not written by a Jewish guy.</p>
<p>Leonard: Three, two.</p>
<p>Stuart: Somebody sing the damn song.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Good King Wenceslas looked out, on the feast of Stephen. When the snow lay &#8217;round about, deep and crisp and even</p>
<p>Leonard: The walls are getting slower.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Brightly shone the moon that night, though the frost was cruel. When a poor man came in sight gathering winter fuel.</p>
<p>Leonard:The walls stop. You&#8217;re safe.</p>
<p>Howard: That was amazing, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Stuart: How did you know that?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It was simple. I combined a well-known historical fact about Bohemia with the memory of my grandparents, Mee-Maw and Pop-Pop, singing Christmas carols while I sat in front of the fire and tried to build a high-energy particle accelerator out of Legos.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, continuing our quest.</p>
<p>Sheldon: W-W-Wait. There&#8217;s still four more verses. You don&#8217;t sing a song and not finish it. Hither, page, and stand by me, if thou know&#8217;st it, telling. Yonder peasant, who is he? Where and what his dwelling? Brightly shone the moon that night, though the frost&#8230;</p>
<p><i>Scene: The bar. </i></p>
<p>Bernadette: Looks like he&#8217;s doing pretty good.</p>
<p>Penny: Of course he is. Look, that girl just got dumped by her boyfriend. She&#8217;s angry, she&#8217;s drunk, and her favorite movie is Slumdog Millionaire. I mean.</p>
<p>Amy: That is some low-hanging fruit.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, here he comes.</p>
<p>Penny: So, how&#8217;d it go?</p>
<p>Raj: Great. I bought her a couple of drinks, and she gave me her e-mail address.</p>
<p>Penny: Ooh!</p>
<p>Bernadette: Jennifer at not-even-if-you-were-the-last-guy-on-earth dot loser.</p>
<p>Raj: What?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;m sorry, Raj.</p>
<p>Raj: Why can&#8217;t I find someone? I&#8217;m smart, I&#8217;ve got a cool job, and my naturally bronzed complexion means I can pull off mustard yellow in a way most guys can&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, honey, it&#8217;s not you, it&#8217;s them.</p>
<p>Raj: No, it&#8217;s not. She was too beautiful for me.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Why would you say that?</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s true. I&#8217;m always attracted to women I can&#8217;t have. I do it all the time. I did the same thing with the two of you.</p>
<p>Amy: The two of them? I don&#8217;t understand.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, uh, there was a time when I had a thing for Penny, and I thought she was into me, too, because she got drunk and naked and climbed into bed with me. But apparently I misread those signals.</p>
<p>Amy: And you liked Bernadette also?</p>
<p>Raj: That was before Penny. I make it a rule to only fall for one of my friends&#8217; girlfriends at a time. I&#8217;m very old-fashioned that way.</p>
<p>Amy: So at, uh, some point, you probably had a thing for me, too.</p>
<p>Raj: No, not really.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, come on, Raj, not even a little bit?</p>
<p>Raj: Not that I can think of.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Think harder.</p>
<p>Raj: Nope. I guess the heart wants what the heart wants. Or in this case, doesn&#8217;t, I mean, at all. What?</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment. Sheldon is still singing.</i></p>
<p>Sheldon: Ye who now will bless the poor, shall yourselves find blessing. Ba-da-bum.</p>
<p>Howard: Done?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I think the word you&#8217;re looking for is bravo.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, you leave the room and find yourself standing before a pool of melted snowmen.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Melted snowmen? Are there carrots and lumps of coal in the water?</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t know. What&#8217;s the difference?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well it&#8217;s a game of the imagination, Leonard. Paint a picture.</p>
<p>Leonard: Fine. You leave the room and find yourself standing before a pool of melted snowmen. There are carrots and lumps of coal in the water.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What happened to the top hats and the corncob pipes?</p>
<p>Leonard: You see those, too.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, it&#8217;s like I&#8217;m really there.</p>
<p>Stuart: I gotta tell you, this, this is the most holiday fun I&#8217;ve had since my therapist changed my anxiety medication and I stopped caring about the blood in my stool.</p>
<p>Howard: Good story.</p>
<p>Leonard: What are you drinking there? A little eggnog?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes. What, is there a problem?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, it&#8217;s nice to see you enjoying a holiday beverage. Pretty Christmassy.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Since when is eggnog a Christmas drink? Eggs are available all year &#8217;round. I&#8217;ve been known to enjoy this poolside.</p>
<p>Leonard: Come on, Sheldon. You know all the Christmas stories, and the carols, you&#8217;ve got an eggnog moustache going on there. Just admit it. You&#8217;re getting a little Yuletide spirit.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, don&#8217;t be silly. Christmas is a bunch of baloney created by the tinsel industry. Why is this so important to you?</p>
<p>Leonard: Honestly, because I had enough crappy Christmases as a kid and I&#8217;m tired of you sucking the joy out of them now.</p>
<p>Stuart: What was so bad about them?</p>
<p>Leonard: I grew up in a house full of crazy academics. Instead of leaving Santa milk and cookies, we had to leave him a research paper. And in the morning, you could tell he&#8217;d been there because the paper would be graded.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No wonder you love Christmas. That sounds amazing.</p>
<p>Leonard: It wasn&#8217;t amazing. I got a C-minus four years in a row.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, I&#8217;m familiar with your work. C-minus was your gift.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The bar.</i></p>
<p>Raj: Amy, I&#8217;m really sorry. I didn&#8217;t mean to upset you.</p>
<p>Amy: It&#8217;s fine. I&#8217;m used to being the girl who never gets looked at twice. I didn&#8217;t have my first kiss till I was 22, and the guy only did it so I&#8217;d give him back his insulin.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Sometimes the pancreas wants what the pancreas wants.</p>
<p>Amy: Forget it. I don&#8217;t expect you guys to understand.</p>
<p>Raj: I understand. In seventh grade, I played Spin the Bottle and it landed on Alina Shankar. She said if I came near her, she would break the bottle and cut me.</p>
<p>Amy: You think that&#8217;s bad? In college, I passed out at a frat party and woke up with more clothes on.</p>
<p>Raj: Sometimes I get so lonely, I sit on my left hand until it goes numb, then I put it in my right hand and pretend I&#8217;m holding hands with another person.</p>
<p>Amy: I do that, too. Sometimes the left hand tries to cop a feel. And I let it.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s kind of nice to talk to someone who understands what it feels like to be alone.</p>
<p>Amy: It is, isn&#8217;t it?</p>
<p>Raj: But you&#8217;re not alone anymore. Now you&#8217;ve got Sheldon.</p>
<p>Amy: I do. And don&#8217;t worry, someday you&#8217;ll have somebody, too.</p>
<p>Raj: Thank you. I hope she&#8217;s half as lovely and amazing as you are.</p>
<p>Amy: Thank you, Rajesh. He wants me, I&#8217;m good. We can go now.</p>
<p>Raj: Damn it.</p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Raj: Now that I know she doesn&#8217;t like me, I&#8217;m kind of into her.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment. Sheldon is writing numbers on a whiteboard.</i></p>
<p>Sheldon: All right. I think I cracked the code to lower the drawbridge.</p>
<p>Howard: Great. Let&#8217;s do it. <i>(They pick up bells and play according to the numbers. The tune is Jingle Bells)</i></p>
<p>Leonard: The drawbridge is lowering.</p>
<p>Stuart: My carpal tunnel&#8217;s acting up.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Play through the pain.</p>
<p>Leonard: You did it. The drawbridge is down. You cross the chasm and find yourself in a small dungeon room. And in the corner, chained to the wall, you see a bloodied and beaten Santa Claus. He says, ho, ho, help me.</p>
<p>Howard: Yes, we found Santa. Christmas is saved. Don&#8217;t ever tell my mother I said that.</p>
<p>Stuart: I take out my skeleton key and run to Santa to unchain him.</p>
<p>Sheldon: But first, I cast a spell of paralysis on Stuart and Howard.</p>
<p>Howard: Wait, what are you doing?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You can&#8217;t talk, you&#8217;re paralyzed. I get right up in Santa&#8217;s big, fat face and say, well, well, well, jolly old Saint Nick, we meet again. Yeah, I believe the last time we spoke was in the Baybrook Mall in Galveston, Texas, when I was five years old, isn&#8217;t that right?</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, okay.</p>
<p>Sheldon: My mother dragged me there and she plopped me down on your lap and you asked me what I wanted for Christmas. And I told you, my Pop-Pop, because that was the year my grandfather died. I missed him and I wanted him back.</p>
<p>Leonard: This is weird, right?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Pop-Pop was the only one in my family who encouraged me to pursue science. But you didn&#8217;t bring him back, did you? No, instead, I got Lincoln Logs. Well, you can build a lot of neat things out of Lincoln Logs, but Pop-Pop ain&#8217;t one of &#8216;em. And now you&#8217;re here asking me for something, to save you. Well, sorry, Mr. Kringle, but today&#8217;s not your day. I&#8217;m leaving you here to rot, and I hope the ogres feast on your bones. I take the skeleton key, and I toss it into the chasm, and on the way out, I give Santa a swift kick in his bowl full of jelly!</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay. So Wolowitz and Stuart are paralyzed, Santa&#8217;s dead, and I picked this over having sex with my girlfriend.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment, late at night. </i></p>
<p>Sheldon: Santa?</p>
<p>Santa: Oh, hello, Sheldon. You should be asleep.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, then, you shouldn&#8217;t have jingle bells on your boots.</p>
<p>Santa: Oh. I&#8217;ve been wanting to talk to you. I&#8217;m sorry I disappointed you when you were a little boy. I can do a lot of magical things, but unfortunately bringing your Pop-Pop back isn&#8217;t one of them.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I understand.</p>
<p>Santa: But I do have something special for you. Close your eyes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I hope it&#8217;s a train.</p>
<p>Santa: Oh, it&#8217;s better than a train.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Two trains?</p>
<p>Santa: Better.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m getting three trains.</p>
<p>Santa: Okay, open &#8216;em. <i>(It&#8217;s a cannon, pointed at Sheldon) </i>This is for leaving me in the dungeon to be eaten alive by ogres!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wait, uh, uh, hang on. In my defense&#8230;</p>
<p>Santa: Ho, ho, ho, ya big dork. <i>(Fires cannon)</i></p>

					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
