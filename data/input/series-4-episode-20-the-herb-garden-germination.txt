		<h2 class="title">Series 4 Episode 20 &#8211; The Herb Garden&nbsp;Germination</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: A bookstore.</em></p>
<p>Dr. Brian Greene: My new book, The Hidden Reality, takes on a grand question. Is our universe the only universe? You see, there&#8217;s a growing belief among scientists like me that ours may only be one among many universes populating a gigantic cosmos. In The Hidden Reality, I explore this possibility without presuming any knowledge of mathematics or physics on the part of the reader.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hysterical.</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;m glad you talked me into this. We work so hard, sometimes, it&#8217;s nice to goof off and do something silly.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Agreed. Wait till you hear how he dumbs down Werner Heisenberg for the crowd. You may actually believe you&#8217;re in a comedy club.</p>
<p>Greene: You can think about Heisenberg&#8217;s Uncertainty Principle much like the special order menu that you find in certain Chinese restaurants, where you have dishes in column A and other dishes in column B, and if you order the first dish in column A, you can&#8217;t order the corresponding dish in column B. That&#8217;s sort of like the Uncertainly Principle.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Ba-dum-bump.</p>
<p>Amy: Say, I heard an interesting tidbit about Howard and Bernadette.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Really, Amy? Gossip? I&#8217;m disappointed in you.</p>
<p>Amy: Now, now. Evolutionary biologist Robin Dunbar has identified gossip as an aid to social bonding in large groups.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Forgive my language, but poppycock.</p>
<p>Amy: What if he&#8217;s right? And by not participating in gossip, society breaks down into small feral bands of tattooed motorcycle riders fighting to the death over the last few cans of tuna fish?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Fine. In the parlance of the urban music scene, what&#8217;s the 4-1-1?</p>
<p>Amy: Bernadette is thinking about breaking up with Howard.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I believe our nation&#8217;s tuna cans are safe. Excuse me. Dr. Greene, question?</p>
<p>Greene: Yes?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You&#8217;ve dedicated your life&#8217;s work to educating the general populace about complex scientific ideas.</p>
<p>Greene: Yes, in part.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Have you ever considered trying to do something useful? Perhaps, reading to the elderly?</p>
<p>Greene: Excuse me?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, but not your books. Something they might enjoy. I kid, of course. Big fan.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. The guys are playing on a Nintendo Wii.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Nice shot.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thank you. My father taught me archery as a child. It&#8217;s odd how the activity brings back the smell of Kmart bourbon.</p>
<p>Leonard: Perfect.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I know. What an elf I would have made. Whoo, what do you think you&#8217;re doing?</p>
<p>Leonard: Shooting at a target?</p>
<p>Sheldon: With what?</p>
<p>Leonard: An arrow.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Really? I didn&#8217;t see you draw one from your quiver.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m not going to do that, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, the people at Nintendo can only go so far in helping us recreate an actual athletic experience. We have to do our part, too. <em>(Leonard mimes drawing an arrow and stabbing Sheldon with it.) </em>That was uncalled for, but I&#8217;ll play along. Ow! I had an unusual experience with Amy last night.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really? How could you tell?</p>
<p>Sheldon: She was attempting to engage me in gossip.</p>
<p>Leonard: You don&#8217;t say?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes. I think prolonged exposure to Penny has turned her into a bit of a Gabby Gertie.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, what&#8217;s the gossip?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, please, I was just pointing it out. I have no desire to engage in the activity.</p>
<p>Leonard: Fine, don&#8217;t tell me.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, get this. Bernadette is thinking about breaking up with Howard.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh. That&#8217;s too bad. I wonder what happened.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Mm, it&#8217;s hard to say. I can only speculate based on the data I collected watching my parents&#8217; marriage implode. In that case, the woman dives into religion, while the man dives into a bottle-blonde bartender who tries to buy my love with action figures. Oop! Out of arrows.</p>
<p>Leonard: Boy, you sure get your money&#8217;s worth out of these games.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The bathroom.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: &#8211; Priya, can I come in?</p>
<p>Priya: Sure.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, God.</p>
<p>Priya: What?</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s okay. You didn&#8217;t know. I&#8217;ll take care of it.</p>
<p>Priya: What, what did I do?</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon doesn&#8217;t allow flossing that close to the mirror.</p>
<p>Priya: You&#8217;re kidding.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s a splatter thing. There&#8217;s a little piece of tape on the floor you&#8217;re supposed to stand behind.</p>
<p>Priya: That&#8217;s madness.</p>
<p>Leonard: I know. Just do it. There&#8217;s a big inspection coming up, and I don&#8217;t want to lose my TV privileges.</p>
<p>Priya: You really need to let me take a look at that roommate agreement one of these days.</p>
<p>Leonard: Mm, I don&#8217;t know. I get a lawyer, he gets a lawyer, it&#8217;s just easier to stand behind the tape.</p>
<p>Priya: Oh, by the way, a fellow at work said I could use his Dodger tickets this weekend. Does that sound like fun?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah! Dodgers are baseball, right?</p>
<p>Priya: You&#8217;ll need to explain the game to me.</p>
<p>Leonard: Mm, it&#8217;s complicated, but as I remember it, the essentials are, get chosen last, get hit by the ball, cry, go home.</p>
<p>Priya: Well, regardless, I&#8217;ve got four tickets, and I was thinking we can bring Bernadette and Howard. She&#8217;s really interesting, and I bet I can get used to him.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, it might not be a great idea to invite those two.</p>
<p>Priya: Why not?</p>
<p>Leonard: Don&#8217;t tell anybody I told you, but I heard she might be breaking up with him.</p>
<p>Priya: Oh, too bad. Although I do know one person for whom that&#8217;s good news.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really? Who?</p>
<p>Priya: My brother. He&#8217;s got a big crush on Bernadette.</p>
<p>Leonard: What? You&#8217;re kidding!</p>
<p>Priya: Mmm. I found poems he wrote about her. Very disturbing. Oh, Bernadette, please play my clarinet.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wow, that&#8217;s hard to believe.</p>
<p>Priya: Yes. And for years, everyone in my family was convinced that he was the clarinet enthusiast. Hmm. What&#8217;s that piece of tape?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, that one doesn&#8217;t apply to you. You sit.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The lobby. </em></p>
<p>Leonard: Hey!</p>
<p>Penny: Hey! How&#8217;s it going?</p>
<p>Leonard: Not too bad. Did you hear about Howard and Bernadette?</p>
<p>Penny: &#8216;Course I heard about it. How did you hear about it?</p>
<p>Leonard: I heard about it from Sheldon. He got it from Amy.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, damn it. I told Amy that in the strictest confidence. Boy, some people are such blabbermouths. Well, whatever, I&#8217;m sure Bernadette can do better.</p>
<p>Leonard: Do you think a sexually ambivalent Indian astrophysicist with selective mutism and alcohol issues is better than a hundred-pound Jewish guy who lives with his mom?</p>
<p>Penny: You are kidding. Raj likes Bernadette?</p>
<p>Leonard: I didn&#8217;t say Raj. Who said Raj?</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, give. How do you know? Did he tell you?</p>
<p>Leonard: No.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, then who?</p>
<p>Leonard: I can&#8217;t say.</p>
<p>Penny: Priya told you. What a little gossip. You know, not an attractive quality in a woman, Leonard. Not judging, just my opinion.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, The point is, if this got out, it would destroy Howard and Raj&#8217;s friendship.</p>
<p>Penny: You don&#8217;t have to worry. Unlike Amy and Priya, I know how to keep a secret.</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re the one who told Amy in the first place.</p>
<p>Penny: In confidence!</p>
<p><em>Scene: Raj’s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Hello, Mummy. Hello, Daddy. How are you?</p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali <em>(on webcam)</em>: We&#8217;re very rich in a very poor country. So, all in all, can&#8217;t complain.</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: So, Priya, how are enjoying staying with your brother?</p>
<p>Priya: It&#8217;s fine. But if I&#8217;m going to stay in Los Angeles much longer, I think I should get my own place.</p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali: Why? Is Rajesh not making you feel comfortable? Rajesh, why aren&#8217;t you making your sister comfortable?</p>
<p>Raj: I am making her comfortable. Besides, she&#8217;s not sleeping here half the time anyway.</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: What? Where are you sleeping, Priya?</p>
<p>Priya: It&#8217;s a figure of speech, Daddy. It means I-I work late.</p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali: Please tell me you&#8217;re not dating an American. I knew this would happen. Rajesh, are you letting your sister date that little Howard boy?</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: Now, hold on. If she is dating an American, that&#8217;s not a bad way to go. He&#8217;s Jewish. Those chaps are very successful, and they don&#8217;t drink a lot.</p>
<p>Raj: It, it doesn&#8217;t matter. Howard has a girlfriend.</p>
<p>Priya: For now.</p>
<p>Raj: What does that mean?</p>
<p>Priya: I&#8217;ll tell you later.</p>
<p>Raj: Is something going on with him and Bernadette?</p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali: Who&#8217;s Bernadette?</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: Doesn&#8217;t sound Jewish.</p>
<p>Mrs Koothrappali: You can&#8217;t tell by that. Winona Ryder is Jewish.</p>
<p>Dr Koothrappali: Okay, we&#8217;re getting off the subject. Rajesh, I want you to try harder to make your sister feel welcome.</p>
<p>Priya: Thank you, Daddy.</p>
<p>Raj: Don&#8217;t worry. Everything I have, I share with her. Including my friend Leon&#8230;</p>
<p>Priya: Good night, Mummy. Good night, Daddy.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, my God, I think you broke my toe!</p>
<p>Priya: Well you should have kept your mouth shut.</p>
<p>Raj: Fair enough. Now, what&#8217;s up with Clarinet? Bernadette!</p>
<p><em>Scene: The cafeteria.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;ve been thinking about Dr. Greene&#8217;s efforts to make science palatable for the masses.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, yeah? What about it?</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s all. I&#8217;ve just been thinking about it. Now, I&#8217;m thinking about fractal equations. Now I&#8217;m thinking about the origin of the phrase train of thought. Now I&#8217;m thinking about trains.</p>
<p>Raj: Are you listening to this guy?</p>
<p>Howard: Hmm? Oh, I&#8217;m sorry. I was somewhere else.</p>
<p>Leonard: Lucky bastard.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Now I&#8217;m thinking about Jell-O.</p>
<p>Raj: So what&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s a Bernadette thing.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, no. I hope everything&#8217;s okay.</p>
<p>Leonard: Whatever it is, we&#8217;re here for you. You can tell us anything.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah. Good or devastating.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m gonna ask her to marry me.</p>
<p>Leonard: Marry you?</p>
<p>Raj: What?</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah. I just need to figure out the right time to pop the question.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, I&#8217;d wait.</p>
<p>Raj: No rush, no rush.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(phone rings)</em>: Oh, great. It&#8217;s my cousin David about the ring. Hey, David, what&#8217;d you find? Oh, sure, a half a carat&#8217;s fine. Her freakishly small hands make anything look big. It&#8217;s one of the reasons I love her.</p>
<p>Leonard: Man, he&#8217;s going to be blindsided.</p>
<p>Raj: I know. It&#8217;ll be awful.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why are you smiling?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, Raj, why?</p>
<p>Raj: Uh, a smile means something different in my country. You know, tears of joy, smile of sadness. India&#8217;s a goofy place.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I&#8217;m back to trains. Woo-woo.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Amy: Did I tell you our lab got a grant to study addiction?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No.</p>
<p>Amy: Fascinating work. I recently trained a capuchin monkey to smoke cigarettes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Have you learned anything?</p>
<p>Amy: Yes. He looks much cooler than the non-smoking monkeys. Although it&#8217;s not much of a contest. The other monkeys just sit around and masturbate.</p>
<p>Sheldon: If you don&#8217;t mind, I&#8217;d like to stop listening to you and start talking.</p>
<p>Amy: By all means.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Howard has announced his intention to propose marriage to Bernadette.</p>
<p>Amy: I don&#8217;t understand. The original piece of gossip indicated an impending breakup.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I know. The group consensus is that his proposal will be met with an humiliating, soul-crushing rejection. Everyone was set a-twitter. Although oddly, no one tweeted.</p>
<p>Amy: It&#8217;s not surprising that the story has captured the attention of our little circle of friends. Are you familiar with meme theory?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m familiar with everything, but go on.</p>
<p>Amy: Meme theory suggests that items of gossip are like living things that seek to reproduce using humans as their host.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m no stranger to memetic epidemiology. At Johnson Elementary School, the phrase Shelly Cooper&#8217;s a smelly pooper spread like wildfire.</p>
<p>Amy: I should think so. That&#8217;s gold.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Your meme hypothesis does intrigue me. How might we examine this more closely?</p>
<p>Amy: Do you have any ethical qualms regarding human experimentation?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s one of the few forms of interaction with people that I don&#8217;t find repellent.</p>
<p>Amy: We need to fabricate a tantalizing piece of gossip.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And a second non-tantalizing piece to use as a control.</p>
<p>Amy: Then we&#8217;ll track its progress through our social group and interpret the results through the competing academic prisms of memetic theory, algebraic gossip and epidemiology.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Look at you, getting me to engage in the social sciences. You&#8217;re a vixen, Amy Farrah Fowler.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny’s door.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Oh, hey, Amy. What&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Amy: Sheldon and I engaged in sexual intercourse. In other news, I&#8217;m thinking of starting an herb garden. Mum&#8217;s the word. Gotta go.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The Cheesecake Factory</em></p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, hey, Raj, what are you doing here?</p>
<p>Raj: I just felt like drinking alone, because I&#8217;m deep and dark.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Uh-huh. So, uh, Howard&#8217;s not here?</p>
<p>Raj: No. It&#8217;s just one of those times when it&#8217;s just you and me. Like when the three of us went to the movies and you and I waited outside the bathroom while Howard threw up Red Vines and Cherry Coke. Do you remember that?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Yeah.</p>
<p>Raj: Me, too. Good times.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, 16 wants to order appetizers.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, thanks.</p>
<p>Raj: This was nice.</p>
<p>Penny: What the hell are you doing? Howard is your best friend, and that is his girlfriend. You should be ashamed of yourself.</p>
<p>Raj: I was when I came in, but it&#8217;s amazing what liquor does to guilt. Besides what you might not know is, Bernadette is planning on breaking up with Howard.</p>

					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}

						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;

				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {

					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
