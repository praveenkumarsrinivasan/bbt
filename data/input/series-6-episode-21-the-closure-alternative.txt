		<h2 class="title">Series 6 Episode 21 &#8211; The Closure&nbsp;Alternative</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, dear. Penny, have you been recording shows on our DVR again?</p>
<p>Penny: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Answer honestly. This is not a trial. That&#8217;ll come later.</p>
<p>Penny: Absolutely not. Help me out here, I can&#8217;t afford another demerit.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah. Uh, maybe we were hacked. You know, the Chinese have been hacking everything lately.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why would the Chinese make our DVR record Paul Blart: Mall Cop?</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t know. It&#8217;s a fat guy on a Segway. That&#8217;s funny everywhere.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m deleting it.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, hang on. Maybe the Chinese haven&#8217;t finished watching it yet.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know, if you&#8217;re trying to make space on the DVR, why don&#8217;t you just get rid of some of the stuff you&#8217;ve already watched? Like, um, Alphas.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, that&#8217;s the season two finale. That was quite the cliffhanger. I&#8217;m going to re-watch it before season three starts.</p>
<p>Leonard: There is no season three. They cancelled that show.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, they can&#8217;t cancel it. It ended on a cliffhanger.</p>
<p>Leonard: They did.</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, Sheldon, there are two dumplings left. Do you want them?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Dumplings? Don&#8217;t you understand what&#8217;s going on here?</p>
<p>Penny: As a rule, no.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That show ended with all the residents of New York either dead or unconscious. Oh, now I&#8217;ll never know what happened.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, why don&#8217;t you make up your own ending?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, sure, what a wonderful idea. And after that, I&#8217;ll make up my own rules of oral hygiene. You know, instead of flossing, I&#8217;ll rub pudding on my gums. I&#8217;m going to get the number of the SyFy Channel and give them what for.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, please, don&#8217;t do that.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. They can&#8217;t just cancel a show like Alphas. You know? They have to help the viewers let go. Firefly did a movie to wrap things up. Buffy the Vampire Slayer continued on as a comic book. Heroes gradually lowered the quality season by season till we were grateful it ended.</p>
<p>Penny: I feel bad for whoever gets that phone call.</p>
<p>Leonard: Ah, don&#8217;t. If they didn&#8217;t want to be yelled at by crazy nerds, they shouldn&#8217;t have started a sci-fi channel.</p>
<p><i>Credits sequence</i></p>
<p><i>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment.</i></p>
<p>Howard: I think you&#8217;re gonna be really happy with this security camera. The optics are great, it&#8217;s got infrared technology, and there&#8217;s even a backup battery when the power goes out.</p>
<p>Raj: Whatever. I can see my little princess while I&#8217;m at work, right?</p>
<p>Howard: Why can&#8217;t you just watch porn like a regular guy? I need your laptop so I can configure the software.</p>
<p>Raj: Wait, hold on. This is weird.</p>
<p>Howard: What?</p>
<p>Raj: I was Googling that girl I&#8217;ve been dating, and I found her blog.</p>
<p>Howard: Cool. Anything juicy?</p>
<p>Raj: She said she recently went on a date with a guy named Roger? And he&#8217;s Indian? And he&#8217;s an astrophysicist, too?</p>
<p>Howard: You know what&#8217;s going on, don&#8217;t you?</p>
<p>Raj: Yes, Mummy was right. American girls are sexually voracious devils.</p>
<p>Howard: I can&#8217;t believe I have to explain this. People change names on blogs to protect their privacy. Roger is Raj.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh. I always thought, if I had a white name, it would be Gavin.</p>
<p>Howard: Keep reading. What does it say?</p>
<p>Raj: No, no, I don&#8217;t know if I should. If she wanted me to know about her blog, she would&#8217;ve told me. It&#8217;s almost like I&#8217;m reading her diary.</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s exactly what it&#8217;s like. Keep reading.</p>
<p>Raj: No, this is creepy.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, I think we were looking at creepy in the rearview mirror when I put up that camera.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s not creepy. Don&#8217;t listen to him. Oh, look who&#8217;s got the sweetest kisses.</p>
<p>Howard: Should I go?</p>
<p><i>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment.</i></p>
<p>Leonard: With all the TV Sheldon was talking about, I had the greatest idea ever. It even blows away my idea for a Star Wars themed coffee shop called Brewbacca&#8217;s. You need to watch Buffy the Vampire Slayer. It is the perfect show for the two of us. It&#8217;s got action and jokes and hot vampires and romance. I cannot oversell this. What do you say?</p>
<p>Penny: It&#8217;s six thirty in the morning.</p>
<p>Leonard: I thought you grew up on a farm.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Sheldon <i>(on phone)</i>: Hello, SyFy Network? Yeah, this is your vice president of programming. Now, we have made a horrible mistake regarding Alphas. Yeah, we need to uncancel it immediately. Well, you just put me through to the people who handle that, you tell them it&#8217;s me, and I don&#8217;t want to answer a lot of questions about if I really am who I say I am, you know? I just. I want this done. No, I am not the person who just called. That man was clearly a cowboy. Yeah, who was plumb concerned about y&#8217;all cancelling his favourite show. Why do they keep hanging up on me?</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;m sorry you&#8217;re upset. You know, Sheldon, sometimes people seek the comfort of physical contact in moments like this.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I am not flying back to Texas just so my mom can give me a hug.</p>
<p>Amy: Okay. Well, what I think is going on here is you have a pathological need for closure.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, that&#8217;s nonsense. I mean, you tell me stories about your day all the time. I don&#8217;t care how they end.</p>
<p>Amy: You know, I might be able to help you with this. There&#8217;s a whole field of behavioural neuroscience that examines ways to retrain your neural pathways so stuff like this bothers you less.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, but I just told you, I don&#8217;t have a problem with closure.</p>
<p>Amy: You sure about that?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, quite sure. <i>(Amy starts a knocking sequence on the table. After staring at her a moment, Sheldon completes it.) </i>That proves nothing.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Raj&#8217;s office.</i></p>
<p>Howard: Ready to go to lunch?</p>
<p>Raj: Do you think I&#8217;m feminine?</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah. Let&#8217;s go.</p>
<p>Raj: Thanks a lot.</p>
<p>Howard: What&#8217;s going on?</p>
<p>Raj: I broke down, and I read Lucy&#8217;s blog, and in one of the entries she said, when we first met, I struck her as a little feminine.</p>
<p>Howard: Just a little? That&#8217;s great.</p>
<p>Raj: I have to talk to her about this.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, geez, why do you girls always want to talk about things? Listen to me, if she&#8217;s writing about your relationship, use it to your advantage. Rig the game.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, that doesn&#8217;t seem fair.</p>
<p>Howard: Is it fair that girls like confident, normal guys more than nervous weirdos?</p>
<p>Raj: No, it&#8217;s not. I&#8217;ve always thought that was unfair.</p>
<p>Howard: So take what&#8217;s in that blog and use it to get her pants off.</p>
<p>Raj: Why do you have to make everything so filthy? Why couldn&#8217;t you just say the blog is like her giving me the key to her heart?</p>
<p>Howard: The key to her heart. That&#8217;s nice. Were you quoting someone or is it tattooed on the small of your back?</p>
<p>Raj: I was quoting a man who knows a thing or two about women, Sir Elton John.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment.</i></p>
<p>Leonard: So, did you love it? Of course you loved it. How could you not love it? Tell me how much you loved it.</p>
<p>Penny: It was cute.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, don&#8217;t say cute. That&#8217;s the worst.</p>
<p>Penny: What&#8217;s wrong with cute?</p>
<p>Leonard: It just makes things seem small. It diminishes them.</p>
<p>Penny: So you want me to stop calling your little tushy cute?</p>
<p>Leonard: You can try, but nobody&#8217;s gonna believe you. I just, I don&#8217;t understand how you can watch a show that great and not be excited by it.</p>
<p>Penny: I liked it. I&#8217;m excited.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, then, tell your face.</p>
<p>Penny: What do you want from me?</p>
<p>Leonard: You know what? Never mind. We gave it a shot. Let&#8217;s just see what else is on.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, come on, don&#8217;t be like that.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m sorry I called it cute. Let&#8217;s watch another one.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, it was fun. Kind of reminded me of my high school. But instead of vampires, we had meth heads. But both came out at night and had messed up teeth.</p>
<p>Leonard: All right, cool. I think you&#8217;ll like the next one better. All the cheerleaders are suffering from an evil curse.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh. Well, that&#8217;s like my high school, too. But instead of a curse, it was crabs.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;ve come up with a series of exercises to help with your compulsive need for closure.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I take issue with the word compulsive.</p>
<p>Amy: All I&#8217;m saying is, we live in a world where closure isn&#8217;t always an op&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: &#8230;tion. Okay. For the sake of argument, let&#8217;s say I have a problem. What would be your plan for addressing it?</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;m going to recondition your brain so that the need for completion isn&#8217;t so overwhelming.</p>
<p>Sheldon: By playing tic-tac-toe?</p>
<p>Amy: Yep. Your turn.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, Amy. And you wonder why people think neuroscience is nothing but a goofy game for diaper babies. Tic-tac-toe can only end in win, lose or draw, none of which will deny me closure. Especially since I&#8217;m about to win. <i>(She wipes the board clean.) </i>B-But we didn&#8217;t finish.</p>
<p>Amy: Exactly. How does that make you feel?</p>
<p>Sheldon: The same way any normal person would. I feel like I want to peel off my own face and tear it in two and then again and again until I have a handful of Sheldon-face confetti.</p>
<p>Amy: And that&#8217;s exactly the feeling we want to address with this course of treatment.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Or you could pitch in, grab a nostril and help me get this face off.</p>
<p>Amy: Come on, you can do this.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You don&#8217;t know what it&#8217;s like to feel completely frustrated. To have a desire build up within you and be denied any opportunity for release.</p>
<p>Amy: Yeah, sounds like a drag.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment.</i></p>
<p>Penny: Okay, help me out here. Why does he love this show so much?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Well, there was action, it was funny. I mean, you do get that usually the monster chases the pretty girl, but this time the pretty girl chases the monsters?</p>
<p>Penny: Yippee, it&#8217;s backwards. I get it.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Why does this bother you so much?</p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t know. It&#8217;s just, he&#8217;s so passionate about so many different things. I just don&#8217;t get that way. Do you?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Well, sure. I&#8217;m pretty passionate about science. I remember the first time I looked through a microscope and saw millions of tiny microorganisms. It was like a whole other universe. If I wanted to, I could wipe it out with my thumb like a god.</p>
<p>Penny: See? I wish I had some of that fire in my life. I mean, I want to care about things and get excited like you guys.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Well, there&#8217;s no reason you can&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Penny: You think?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Absolutely. All we need to do is spend a little time and find something you&#8217;re passionate about.</p>
<p>Penny: Ugh, that sounds like a lot of work.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment. </i></p>
<p>Howard <i>(on </i><i><span style="text-decoration:underline;">webcam)</span></i>: Hey, what&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Raj: Uh, Lucy&#8217;s coming over. I need some advice.</p>
<p>Howard: However long you think the foreplay should be, triple it.</p>
<p>Raj: Just tell me which one you think is more manly. This hockey jersey or this football jersey.</p>
<p>Howard: I don&#8217;t know. Go with hockey.</p>
<p>Raj: Good, black is more slimming. Oh, that&#8217;s her. I got to go be butch. Toodles. Hey.</p>
<p>Lucy: Hi.</p>
<p>Raj: Come in.</p>
<p>Lucy: Oh, look how cute your little doggy is.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, well, I wouldn&#8217;t get too close. If I give the right command in German, she&#8217;ll rip your face off.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment. Montage of scenes.</i></p>
<p>1. Amy <i>(singing)</i>: O&#8217;er the land of the free, and the home of the&#8230; Next.</p>
<p>2. <i>(Sheldon is laying out an intricate pattern of dominoes) </i>Amy: That&#8217;s quite an impressive layout, isn&#8217;t it?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes.</p>
<p>Amy: Let&#8217;s box it up.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Let&#8217;s box it up.</p>
<p>3. <i>(Sheldon is turning the handle on a jack-in-the-box. Just before the end of the tune) </i>Amy<i>: </i>That&#8217;s enough. Sheldon, Sheldon, give it!</p>
<p>4. Amy: Okay, Sheldon, make a wish and blow out the candles. Oops, missed one. Now your wish can&#8217;t come true.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Lucky for you, &#8217;cause I wished you were dead.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment.</i></p>
<p>Lucy: I like your jersey.</p>
<p>Raj: Thanks. I love hockey.</p>
<p>Lucy: Oh, cool. So does my dad. We watched it all the time growing up. Who&#8217;s your favourite player?</p>
<p>Raj: Not Brian Boitano, that&#8217;s for sure. How&#8217;s your burrito?</p>
<p>Lucy: It&#8217;s still a little frozen in the middle.</p>
<p>Raj: Probably because I didn&#8217;t read the instructions. No wrapper&#8217;s going to tell me what to do. Unless his name is Jay-Z.</p>
<p>Lucy: Is something going on?</p>
<p>Raj: What do you mean?</p>
<p>Lucy: Well, you&#8217;re acting all weird, and I&#8217;m pretty weird, so I think I know what I&#8217;m talking about.</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t know. Maybe it&#8217;s all those steroids I&#8217;ve been taking.</p>
<p>Lucy: I think I&#8217;m gonna go. Thank you for the burrito and the pork rinds and the 20-minute lecture on why monster trucks are better than regular trucks.</p>
<p>Raj: Wait, wait. I, I found your blog here you wrote about me.</p>
<p>Lucy: Oh. I kind of just write that for myself. I didn&#8217;t think anybody actually read it.</p>
<p>Raj: No kidding. You didn&#8217;t make it easy to find. I spent hours digging around online. For the record, you have excellent credit scores. And your diabetic aunt seems to be adjusting to her new leg just fine.</p>
<p>Lucy: Why were you acting so strange tonight?</p>
<p>Raj: You called me feminine. And I, I wanted you to think I was more manly.</p>
<p>Lucy: Raj, I didn&#8217;t say feminine was a bad thing. I meant that you&#8217;re sweet and thoughtful and your skin looks so much like caramel, I just want to dip an apple in your face.</p>
<p>Raj: I do have a pretty balls-to-the-wall moisturising regimen.</p>
<p>Lucy: So, are you okay?</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah.</p>
<p>Lucy: Good. Do you even like hockey?</p>
<p>Raj: No. Bought this at the Staples Center when I went to see Taylor Swift.</p>
