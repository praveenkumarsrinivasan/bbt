		<h2 class="title">Series 2 Episode 14 &#8211; The Financial&nbsp;Permeability</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The apartment. The guys are studying a complex chart on the whiteboard.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Hmmm.</p>
<p>Sheldon: The problem appears to be unsolvable.</p>
<p>Raj: Maybe you could run some computer simulations.</p>
<p>Howard: There are too many variables. It would take forever.</p>
<p>Leonard: We&#8217;ve got to be missing something. Let&#8217;s start again. The movie is playing here at 7:20, here at 7:40, here at 8:10 and here at 8:45.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Right, these theatres have to be eliminated.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why? They&#8217;re state-of-the-art digital projection, 20-channel surround sound.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, but they have no Icee machines. Despite my aggressive letter-writing campaign, I might add.</p>
<p>Raj: What about the multiplex here? The seats are terrific.</p>
<p>Sheldon: They have Twizzlers instead of Red Vines. No amount of lumbar support can compensate for that.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, it&#8217;s gonna take at least an hour to eat, and I don&#8217;t see a Sheldon-approved restaurant proximate to a Sheldon-approved theatre.</p>
<p>Raj: We could eat after the movie.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Unacceptable, the delay would result in tomorrow morning&#8217;s bowel movement occurring at work.</p>
<p>Raj: Hang on, hang on. There&#8217;s a 7-Eleven here. We smuggle Slurpies, which are essentially Icees, in under our coats, after having a pleasant meal either here, here or here.</p>
<p>Howard: Wow. I don&#8217;t see how we missed that.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Excuse me, in what universe are Slurpies Icees?</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s how we missed it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, would you be prepared, on a non-precedential basis, to create an emergency ad-hoc Slurpie-Icee equivalency?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, Leonard, you know I can&#8217;t do that.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, I guess we only have one option.</p>
<p>Raj: Yep, I don&#8217;t see any way around it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Bye, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Howard: See ya.</p>
<p>Raj: Later, dude.</p>
<p>Sheldon: They&#8217;re right, it was the only option.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>Scene: The hallway. Sheldon is opening the apartment door. Penny comes running up stairs.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Ooh, ooh, shut the door, shut the door.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why?</p>
<p>Penny: Just do it!<em> (Penny runs in. Sheldon shuts door. Penny opens door again) </em>Get inside and shut the door.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, you didn&#8217;t specify.</p>
<p>Penny: Is Leonard around?</p>
<p>Sheldon: He went to the movies without me. It was the only option. I&#8217;m sorry, I don&#8217;t understand which social situation this is. Could you give me some guidance as to how to proceed?</p>
<p>Penny: The building manager&#8217;s showing an apartment downstairs, and I haven&#8217;t paid my rent.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I see. Penny, I&#8217;m not sure I&#8217;m comfortable harbouring a fugitive from the 2311 North Los Robles Corporation.</p>
<p>Penny: It&#8217;s no big deal. I&#8217;m just a little behind on my bills because they cut back my hours at the restaurant and my car broke down.</p>
<p>Sheldon: If you recall, I pointed out the &#8220;check engine&#8221; light to you several months ago.</p>
<p>Penny: Well the &#8220;check engine&#8221; light is fine. It&#8217;s still blinking away. It&#8217;s the stupid engine that stopped working. It cost me like twelve hundred dollars to fix it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You know, it occurs to me you could solve all your problems by obtaining more money.</p>
<p>Penny: Yes, it occurs to me, too.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hang on a moment. <em>(He opens a jar. Snakes jump out. He then pulls out a large wedge of money) </em>Here. Take some. Pay me back when you can.</p>
<p>Penny: Wow, you got a lot of money in there.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s why it&#8217;s guarded by snakes. Take some.</p>
<p>Penny: Don&#8217;t be silly.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m never silly. Here.</p>
<p>Penny: No, I can&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Don&#8217;t you need money?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, yeah, but&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: This is money I&#8217;m not using.</p>
<p>Penny: But what if you need it?</p>
<p>Sheldon: My expenses account for 46.9% of my after-tax income. The rest is divvied up between a small savings account, this deceptive container of peanut brittle and the hollowed-out buttocks of a superhero action figure who shall remain nameless for his own protection. Or her own protection. Take some.</p>
<p>Penny: Really? I mean, are you sure?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I see no large upcoming expenditures unless they develop an affordable technology to fuse my skeleton with adamantium like Wolverine.</p>
<p>Penny: Are they working on that?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I sincerely hope so.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay. Well, thank you. Oh, God, no, I can&#8217;t. Sheldon honey, I don&#8217;t want things to be weird between us.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Won&#8217;t it also be weird if I have to say hello to you every morning on my way to work and you&#8217;re living in a refrigerator box and washing your hair with rain water?</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;ll pay you back as soon as I can.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Of course you will. It&#8217;s impossible to pay me back sooner than you can. Assuming you subscribe to a linear understanding of time and causality.</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m regretting this already.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Entering the lobby of the building.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: You know, I&#8217;ve given the matter some thought, and I think I&#8217;d be willing to be a house pet to a race of super-intelligent aliens.</p>
<p>Leonard: Interesting.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Ask me why.</p>
<p>Leonard: Do I have to?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Of course. That&#8217;s how you move a conversation forward.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why?</p>
<p>Sheldon: The learning opportunities would be abundant. Additionally, I like having my belly scratched.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, Penny. How was work?</p>
<p>Penny: Great. I hope I&#8217;m a waitress at the Cheesecake Factory for my whole life.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Was that sarcasm?</p>
<p>Penny: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Was that sarcasm?</p>
<p>Penny: Yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Was that sarca..</p>
<p>Leonard: Stop it!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Penny, you appear to have a package here.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, thanks. This must be the beret I ordered. A couple of months ago. It was back-ordered.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Did you know the beret is an example of piece of women&#8217;s fashion adapted from male military uniforms? Another fascinating example is the epaulet.</p>
<p>Leonard: He&#8217;s not lying, he does find that fascinating.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, whatever. It&#8217;s not like I&#8217;m running up and down the streets just buying myself berets. I bought one, like, a month ago, and it was back-ordered, look, it finally arrived, all right?</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, my God, would you just get off my case?</p>
<p>Leonard: Weird.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, good, that was an unusual interaction. I wasn&#8217;t sure.</p>
<p>Leonard: Did you guys have an argument?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, you clearly did something to aggravate her.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m at a loss. If you like, you can review my daily log of social interactions and see if there&#8217;s a blunder I overlooked.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Leonard: Here you go. Oh, you owe me another two dollars. The price of mu-shu pork went up.</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s getting tougher and tougher to be a bad Jew.</p>
<p>Leonard: Here you go, Penny. Shrimp with lobster sauce.</p>
<p>Penny: Thank you, Leonard. What&#8217;s my share?</p>
<p>Leonard: Don&#8217;t worry about it. It&#8217;s my treat.</p>
<p>Penny: No, really, how much?</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s, whatever. Ten, eleven dollars.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, which is it, ten or eleven?</p>
<p>Leonard: Fourteen fifty, but it&#8217;s no biggie, you&#8217;ll get the next one. <em>(Raj whispers something to Howard)</em></p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Howard: He was just wondering if he wore skintight jeans and a tank top if he&#8217;d get his shrimp lo mein for free.</p>
<p>Penny: What are you saying? That I&#8217;m using my body to get dinner? That I&#8217;m some kind of Chinese food prostitute?</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, Raj, what are you saying?</p>
<p>Penny: ‘Cause let me tell you something, buddy, I pay my own way in this world, okay? I don&#8217;t rely on anybody!<em> (Raj runs out of the room) </em>What was that about?</p>
<p>Howard: He has a nervous bladder when he&#8217;s stressed out. Kind of like a puppy.</p>
<p>Penny: Here, Leonard, ten, eleven, twelve, uh, fourteen dollars.</p>
<p>Leonard: It was fourteen fifty, but it&#8217;s okay.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, good. Dinner&#8217;s here.</p>
<p>Penny: Yes, dinner&#8217;s here, and I&#8217;m having some. I&#8217;m having takeout food.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re damn right it&#8217;s okay. I&#8217;ve been having leftovers at the restaurant for like four days, and I wanted something different. So sue me.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Forgive me, Penny, but that would be the very definition of a frivolous lawsuit.</p>
<p>Penny: Sheldon, look, I will pay you back as soon as I can. You just have to give me more time.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, wait, you lent her money?</p>
<p>Penny: She needed money. You seem under pressure. Did I not lend you a sufficient amount? Because I can give you more.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, you know, you would just love that, wouldn&#8217;t you? Yeah. You would just love to open up your little snake can and throw some money at the girl who can&#8217;t pay her bills.</p>
<p>Leonard: Where are you going?</p>
<p>Penny: Going home, where I won&#8217;t be interrogated like a criminal. I forgot my fortune cookie.</p>
<p>Sheldon: In case either of you have larceny in your heart, you should know that I&#8217;ve moved my money out of the snake can.</p>
<p>Leonard: But if you&#8217;re ever short, there&#8217;s always a couple of fifties in Green Lantern&#8217;s ass.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny’s apartment. </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Leonard: Hey.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey.</p>
<p>Leonard: A little mood lighting, huh?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah. When I didn&#8217;t pay my bill, the Department of Water and Power thought I would enjoy the ambience.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, they&#8217;re very considerate that way.</p>
<p>Penny: I used Sheldon&#8217;s money to pay my rent, then I had like fourteen dollars left over.</p>
<p>Leonard: Fourteen dollars, huh?</p>
<p>Penny: Put it back in your pocket, or I&#8217;ll find some other place to put it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Back in the pocket it goes. Look, you do understand that Sheldon really doesn&#8217;t care when he gets the money back. It&#8217;s actually one of the few idiosyncrasies that doesn&#8217;t make you want to, you know, kill him.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, well, that&#8217;s not really my big problem.</p>
<p>Leonard: So you&#8217;re a little behind on your bills. Everybody gets behind on their bills.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, I know, it&#8217;s just, this wasn&#8217;t the plan, it wasn&#8217;t supposed to go this way.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, what was the plan?</p>
<p>Penny: Um, waitress for six months and then become a movie star.</p>
<p>Leonard: Was there a plan B?</p>
<p>Penny: TV star.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s probably not as bad as you think. Let&#8217;s take a look. Maybe we can find some corners to cut. Oh, here&#8217;s something, if you don&#8217;t have electricity, then you probably don&#8217;t need cable. Just a suggestion. 170 dollars for acting classes?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, no, I can&#8217;t give up my acting classes. I&#8217;m a professional actress.</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;ve had an acting job where you got paid?</p>
<p>Penny: That is not the definition of professional.</p>
<p>Leonard: Actually, it kind of&#8230; let&#8217;s keep looking. Whoa, what&#8217;s eighteen hundred dollars to the Los Angeles County Superior Court?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, that&#8217;s nothing.</p>
<p>Leonard: Nothing? It sounds like you got caught speeding going 4,000 miles an hour.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, remember Kurt?</p>
<p>Leonard: Your ex-boyfriend?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah. He got arrested for taking a whiz on a cop car.</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Penny: He was drunk.</p>
<p>Leonard: I would hope so.</p>
<p>Penny: Anyway, he had a bunch of outstanding tickets and a bench warrant, so I, you know, I paid his fines.</p>
<p>Leonard: Did he pay you back?</p>
<p>Penny: No, but he will.</p>
<p>Leonard: And that&#8217;s based on the inherent credit-worthiness of people who get drunk and urinate on police vehicles?</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, I&#8217;m not gonna call up Kurt and ask him for money.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well what are you gonna do?</p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t know, but I may have to find a cheaper place to live.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, no. Oh, you don&#8217;t want to do that.</p>
<p>Penny: Why not?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, moving is a big deal. You have to go to the supermarket and get boxes, and if they&#8217;re not clean, then your books smell like melons, and it&#8217;s just, like&#8230; Why don&#8217;t you just get a roommate and stay here?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, do you know anybody?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, I&#8217;m sure the guy living with Sheldon wouldn&#8217;t mind moving in with you.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, Leonard, honey, if we started living together, I wouldn&#8217;t be able to keep my hands off you.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really?</p>
<p>Penny: And you thought my acting lessons were a waste of money.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Howard: Ugh. This mu-shu pork&#8217;s burning a hole through my duodenum.</p>
<p>Raj: Leviticus 11:3: &#8220;Only that which parteth the hoof and cheweth the cud among the beasts shall ye eat.&#8221;</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, do I mock you with the Bhagavad-Gita every time you scarf down a Whopper?</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, what&#8217;s going on?</p>
<p>Raj: We&#8217;re on a quest through the Valley of Fire to acquire the sacred crown.</p>
<p>Howard: You want the Valley of Fire? It&#8217;s right here.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, you guys want to go on a real live quest?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Outside? I just made cocoa.</p>
<p>Leonard: &#8211; Come on. It&#8217;ll be fun.</p>
<p>Howard: What is it?</p>
<p>Leonard: Do you guys remember Penny&#8217;s ex-boyfriend Kurt?<em> (They all hold their hands above their heads) </em>Yeah, that&#8217;s him. It turns out he owes Penny a lot of money, and I&#8217;m gonna go get it from him.</p>
<p>Who&#8217;s with me?</p>
<p>Howard: Ooh, double sixes.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really? You&#8217;re just gonna let me go by myself?</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, cool, I got a sword.</p>
<p>Leonard: I could use some help.</p>
<p>Raj: Here.</p>
<p>Leonard: You guys are unbelievable, you play a game to simulate adventure, but when there&#8217;s real adventure out there in the real world, you just wimp out.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, do you not recall the last time we visited this gentleman, we returned home without pants?</p>
<p>Leonard: I do.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Are you sure? Because your proposal suggests that you don&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m not afraid of him.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right. Leonard fairly calls the question, who is in favour of abandoning our game and confronting Penny&#8217;s steroid-infused ex-boyfriend in a suicidal mission to extract money from him? Say hi to Kurt for us.</p>

<p>Sheldon: They did.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well?</p>
<p>Sheldon: They had a terrible time of it, Leonard.</p>
<p>Raj: Plus, no one stole their pants.</p>
<p>Leonard: Fine. Just enjoy your little game. I&#8217;ll make this quest on my own.</p>

<p><em> </em></p>

<p>Sheldon: Which means his triumph will be even larger. Minstrels will write songs about him.</p>

<p>Kurt: You do?</p>
<p>Leonard: Feel is a kind of a&#8230; it&#8217;s a strong word. Um, I just think it would be a nice gesture on your part.</p>
<p>Kurt: She&#8217;ll get it when she gets it.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, there you go. Problem solved.</p>
<p>Raj: A successful quest. Now let&#8217;s go find a gas station with a clean bathroom.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, the problem isn&#8217;t solved. He just blew us off.</p>

<p>Howard: Come on, Leonard, let&#8217;s go</p>

<p>Howard: Okay. <em>(They leave) </em></p>

<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t need backup. I have right on my side. And I&#8217;m wearing cargo shorts under my pants.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Leonard’s car.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Congratulations. You may not have succeeded in getting cash, but you did secure a formal acknowledgment of the debt. <em>(Leonard has “I owe Penny $1800, Kurt” written on his forehead.) </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>

<p>Raj: If anybody cares, I still have to pee.</p>

<p>Penny: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Darn. I can&#8217;t seem to get the hang of that.</p>

<p>Penny: Yes, he said he was feeling guilty and wanted to do what was right.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				} else {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='55px' width='100%' frameBorder='0' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).load( function( e ) {
					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );
					$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loaded' );

					JetpackLikespostMessage( { event: 'loadLikeWidget', name: $iframe.attr( 'name' ), width: $iframe.width() }, window.frames[ 'likes-master' ] );

					if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
				setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 250 );
			}
