		<h2 class="title">Series 7 Episode 21 &#8211; The Anything Can Happen&nbsp;Recurrence</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Penny: So, we&#8217;re about to shoot this scene in the movie where the killer ape DNA is slowly taking over my body.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay.</p>
<p>Penny: But I realize they&#8217;re gluing fur everywhere except my cleavage. So, I asked the director why and he says, it&#8217;s important to the story that my boobs be the last things to turn ape.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s sweet that he thinks there&#8217;s a story.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, and there&#8217;s not even a bathroom on set. I have to go to the gas station across the street. I mean, I was dressed like half an ape and still not even close to the most disgusting person in there.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, I could use your assistance.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sure. What&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, now that I&#8217;ve given up string theory, I&#8217;m struggling to find my next area of focus. So, in your professional opinion, which of these areas do you think is the most promising?</p>
<p>Leonard: Huh, well, I think there&#8217;s some really innovative stuff going on in dark matter.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s helpful. Okay. Of these four areas&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: Sheldon, what did we say about being a nicer friend?</p>
<p>Leonard: Thank you.</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, what did we say about being a gullible weenie?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It is just so frustrating.</p>
<p>Penny: &#8216;Cause you&#8217;re trying too hard. You need to do something else, get your mind off it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey. How about we bring back Anything Can Happen Thursdays?</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, that&#8217;s good. Why&#8217;d you guys stop doing that?</p>
<p>Leonard: You made fun of us. Said it was stupid.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah. Sounds like me. Come on, Sheldon. What do you say?</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right. I officially reinstate Anything Can Happen Thursday.</p>
<p>Leonard: Great, what do you want to do?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t know. What do you want to do?</p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t know. What do you want to do?</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m starting to remember the problem with Anything Can Happen Thursdays.</p>
<p><i>Credits sequence.</i></p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Sheldon: Hmm, what can we do that&#8217;s fun?</p>
<p>Leonard: What can we do that&#8217;s different?</p>
<p>Penny: What can we do that&#8217;s free?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, got it. We order a pizza.</p>
<p>Penny: Are you kidding? That&#8217;s what you always do. Think harder.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You&#8217;re right. You&#8217;re right. Got it. We order calzones, cut them open, eat them like pizza. All right, all right, I&#8217;ll shake the brain bush one more time, see what falls out. Got it. There&#8217;s a live-action role-playing group that meets every Thursday night in Griffith Park and re-enacts battles from Lord of the Rings.</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, tell me more about this calzone idea.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know what? Why don&#8217;t we just ease into this. Let, let&#8217;s go for a walk and, and, and see if we find a new restaurant.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Any chance that restaurant is near Griffith Park?</p>
<p>Leonard: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right.</p>
<p>Leonard: Raj and Howard are at Howard&#8217;s mom&#8217;s, but should we call the girls and see if they want to come?</p>
<p>Penny: No. Bernadette&#8217;s working late.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Amy&#8217;s sick.</p>
<p>Leonard: Aw. What&#8217;s wrong with her?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, she talks a lot. She always wants to hold hands.</p>
<p>Leonard: That&#8217;s not what I meant.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, if you were referring to her illness your question should have been, what ails her?</p>
<p>Leonard: What ails her?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh. Who knows?</p>
<p>Penny: Come on, anything can happen. We can push him down the stairs.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Howard&#8217;s mother&#8217;s house.</i></p>
<p>Howard: Hey. Thanks for coming to hang out.</p>
<p>Raj: No problem. How&#8217;s your mom feeling?</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, but she&#8217;d feel better if she took her medicine.</p>
<p>Raj: You know, when Cinnamon won&#8217;t take her medicine, I hide it in a piece of cheese.</p>
<p>Howard: Good idea. We can wrap the pill in cheese, feed it to Cinnamon, and then my mom can eat Cinnamon. So what do you feel like doing?</p>
<p>Raj: I was thinking we could watch a DVD.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, my mom doesn&#8217;t have a lot to choose from. Unless you want to watch the video of her colonoscopy. Spoiler alert, 20 minutes in they find a prune pit.</p>
<p>Raj: Actually, how do you feel about watching House of 1000 Corpses?</p>
<p>Howard: A straight-up gore-fest? You hate this stuff.</p>
<p>Raj: I do, but for some reason, Emily loves it and wants to watch it with me tomorrow, so I thought if I start with you first, then I could act cool about it with her.</p>
<p>Howard: That&#8217;s actually not a bad plan. I can sit through the colonoscopy now, but that first time I was like, oh my God, a prune pit.</p>
<p>Raj: Uh, so, you&#8217;ll watch it?</p>
<p>Howard: Sure.</p>
<p>Raj: You&#8217;re a good friend. I owe you one.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>Howard. Help me get out of the tub.</p>
<p>Raj: Not that one.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The street. </i></p>
<p>Sheldon: So, we&#8217;re just randomly choosing a restaurant without researching it online?</p>
<p>Penny: Yep.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Great. You know, this is how Anything Can Happen Thursday turns into It Won&#8217;t Stop Coming Up Friday.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, how about that Asian fusion place?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Fusion and Asians? I&#8217;m trying not to think about science.</p>
<p>Penny: What the hell? What? She&#8217;s not working late.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And Amy doesn&#8217;t look sick.</p>
<p>Penny: Why would they lie to us?</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s a good question. <i>(Knock, knock, knock) </i>Amy and Bernadette. <i>(Knock, knock, knock) </i>Amy and Bernadette. <i>(Knock, knock, knock) </i>Amy and Bernadette. Why did you lie to us?</p>
<p><i>Scene: Howard&#8217;s mother&#8217;s house.</i></p>
<p>Raj: Hey. Listen to this. Murder, cannibalism and satanic rituals are just a few of the thousand plus horrors that await.</p>
<p>Howard: I just helped my mom out of the tub, so, I&#8217;m one slippery horror ahead of you.</p>
<p>Raj: You&#8217;re a good son. I don&#8217;t know how you do it.</p>
<p>Howard: Beach blankets, my friend, it&#8217;s all about beach blankets.</p>
<p>Raj: All right, let&#8217;s get this over with. Ew, it&#8217;s got someone&#8217;s hair on it.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, yeah, you&#8217;re gonna do great with this movie.</p>
<p><i>Scene: A bar.</i></p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re not working late. Why did you lie to me?</p>
<p>Sheldon: And Amy, you told me you were sick, but you look just as pale and tired as always.</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;m sorry, I just needed a break from hearing you obsess about what to do after string theory.</p>
<p>Bernadette: We kind of just wanted one night where we didn&#8217;t have to hear about how miserable you are making this movie.</p>
<p>Amy: But none of that means we don&#8217;t love you.</p>
<p>Penny: I haven&#8217;t been complaining that much about the movie. Have I?</p>
<p>Leonard: I also love you.</p>
<p>Penny: Well you know what? Maybe I need a break from all of you. Come on, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Where are we going?</p>
<p>Penny: We&#8217;re gonna have Anything Can Happen Thursday, you&#8217;re gonna tell me all about your science stuff and I&#8217;m gonna complain about my movie, and we&#8217;re gonna support each other because that&#8217;s what friends do.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay. &#8216;Cause if I had to pick now, I&#8217;d probably go with dark matter because&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: Shut up.</p>
<p><i>Scene: A restaurant. Sheldon makes an “unsure” noise.</i></p>
<p>Penny: What&#8217;s wrong?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t understand my food. Chinese noodles with Korean barbecue in a taco.</p>
<p>Penny: It&#8217;s fusion.</p>
<p>Sheldon: My mother would lock her car doors if she had to drive through this hodgepodge of ethnicity.</p>
<p>Penny: Think I&#8217;ve been complaining too much about the movie?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Not at all.</p>
<p>Penny: Thank you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: But to be fair, when you talk, most of what you say sounds like, wah, wah, wah, clothes, wah, wah, wah.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, I don&#8217;t understand why you&#8217;re not upset with Amy.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I am. So much so that I&#8217;m gonna bring her here for dinner on our next date night.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Howard&#8217;s mother&#8217;s house.</i></p>
<p>Raj: Okay. So, in the last 20 minutes, we&#8217;ve seen a crazy woman kissing a fetus in a jar. We&#8217;ve seen a guy cut in half and sewn to a fish.</p>
<p>Howard: And the brutal dismemberment of a rotisserie chicken by my mother.</p>
<p>Raj: On the bright side, she didn&#8217;t even notice the pill. Why does Emily like this stuff? Do you think there&#8217;s something psychologically wrong with her?</p>
<p>Howard: What difference does it make?</p>
<p>Raj: What do you mean?</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, come on, she could have a freezer full of ex-boyfriend&#8217;s body parts and you&#8217;d still go out with her.</p>
<p>Raj: I do like that the ex-boyfriend&#8217;s out of the picture.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The bar.</i></p>
<p>Amy: I feel so bad about lying to Sheldon. How am I gonna make it up to him?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;d tell you what I do with Howard, but I don&#8217;t think dressing up like a Catholic schoolgirl is gonna work with Sheldon.</p>
<p>Leonard: He&#8217;d probably give you homework.</p>
<p>Amy: Did you lie to Howard about tonight?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Of course.</p>
<p>Amy: And you don&#8217;t feel guilty about it?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Between Penny&#8217;s gorilla movie and Howard&#8217;s gorilla mother, I had no choice.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thankfully Penny and I have a relationship based on honesty. What? I don&#8217;t lie to her.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, we know you don&#8217;t lie to her.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The restaurant.</i></p>
<p>Penny: Thank you. Hey, maybe the answer to your career question is in one of these.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Penny, there&#8217;s only one cookie with something in the middle that solves life&#8217;s problems, and that&#8217;s an Oreo. Or a Nutter Butter, if you&#8217;re in a pinch.</p>
<p>Penny: Come on, open it. I bet it says something great.</p>
<p>Sheldon: This is Asian fusion. For all you know, there&#8217;s a tiny Chihuahua in here.</p>
<p>Penny: Fine, I&#8217;ll go. Hmm. People turn to you for guidance and wisdom. Yeah, that&#8217;s a good one.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, it&#8217;s not.</p>
<p>Penny: How is that not good?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Turn to you for wisdom? Clearly, that cookie is mocking you. You&#8217;d never hear that kind of sass from a Nutter Butter.</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, since you&#8217;re paying for dinner, I&#8217;ll let that slide. Open yours.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Have you ever paid for a meal?</p>
<p>Penny: Not with money. Read.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Your warm and fun-loving nature delights those around you.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, try again.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Howard&#8217;s mother&#8217;s house. </i></p>
<p>Howard: Let me get this straight. So, he kills this girl&#8217;s father, cuts off the guy&#8217;s face, and is wearing it as a mask while he makes out with her.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m just gonna say it. That&#8217;s not okay. Why can&#8217;t I be in a relationship with a girl who likes The Sound of Music?</p>
<p>Howard: Raj, you are the girl in the relationship who likes The Sound of Music. <i>(Phone rings) </i>Hey, Penny. What&#8217;s up? No, Bernie&#8217;s working late. Really? Thanks for telling me.</p>
<p>Raj: What?</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m having sex with a Catholic schoolgirl tonight.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The street. Outside a psychic&#8217;s shop.</i></p>
<p>Penny: Oh, I think I see our next stop.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You can&#8217;t be serious. If I wanted to waste my time on nonsense, I&#8217;d follow Leonard on Instagram.</p>
<p>Penny: No, come on, tonight we are trying new things.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh. That&#8217;s a lot of incense. Or someone set a hippie on fire.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Outside the bar.</i></p>
<p>Bernadette <i>(on phone)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>Yeah, honey, I&#8217;m still stuck at work. Really? Penny said that? Okay, it&#8217;s true. I&#8217;m sorry. I&#8217;ll see you at home. Yeah, yeah, I&#8217;ll put it on.</p>
<p>Leonard: You and I never just hang out like this. Why is that?</p>
<p>Amy: I know, it&#8217;s weird, right?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah. We should do it more often.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, no. I mean, this is weird right now.</p>
<p>Leonard: <i>(Phone text sound) </i>Ah, it&#8217;s Penny.</p>
<p>Amy: Is she still mad?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, doesn&#8217;t seem like it. She got Sheldon to go to a psychic with her.</p>
<p>Amy: A psychic? He considers them not just mumbo jumbo, but extra-jumbo mumbo jumbo.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, Penny can be very persuasive. She&#8217;s gotten me to do a lot of things I wouldn&#8217;t normally do.</p>
<p>Amy: Because she has sex with you.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, she does.</p>
<p>Amy: Can I confess something? Once in a while, I get a little jealous of how close Penny and Sheldon are.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really?</p>
<p>Amy: I mean, not in a romantic way. It&#8217;s just, she really has some sort of connection with him.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, well they&#8217;ve known each other a long time, and Penny grew up around horses, so she knows how to approach him without making him skittish.</p>
<p>Amy: Don&#8217;t get me wrong, I&#8217;m glad they&#8217;re friends. I just wish he&#8217;d be that comfortable around me already.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, it took him a long time to get comfortable around me, too.</p>
<p>Amy: Really? What did you do?</p>
<p>Leonard: Something terrible in a former life? I don&#8217;t know.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I gotta go. Penny ratted me out. FYI, she&#8217;s getting you a watch for your birthday with money she took out of your wallet.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The psychic shop.</i></p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t mean to be rude or discourteous, but before we begin, I&#8217;d just like to say there is absolutely no scientific evidence to support clairvoyance of any kind. Which means, and again, no insult intended, that you&#8217;re a fraud, your profession is a swindle, and, uh, your livelihood is dependent on the gullibility of stupid people. Again, no offence.</p>
<p>Penny: All right, Sheldon, just ask your question.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay, I just did. What was it?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, for God&#8217;s sake. Look, he&#8217;s a physicist who&#8217;s trying to figure out what his next field of study should be.</p>

<p>Penny: Okay, I can answer that one, I&#8217;ll be bored.</p>

<p>Psychic: Does she have dark hair?</p>
<p>Penny: Yes, yes, your spirit guides are on fire.</p>

<p>Emily: I wonder that, too.</p>
<p>Raj: Then, why do you watch these things?</p>
<p>Emily: Can I tell you something without you judging me?</p>
<p>Raj: Sure.</p>
<p>Emily: They kind of turn me on.</p>
<p>Raj: And play.</p>
