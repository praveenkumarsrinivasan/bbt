			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The university cafeteria.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Your argument is lacking in all scientific merit. It is well established Superman cleans his uniform by flying into Earth&#8217;s yellow sun, which incinerates any contaminate matter and leaves the invulnerable Kryptonian fabric unharmed and daisy fresh.</p>
<p>Howard: What if he gets something Kryptonian on it?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Like what?</p>
<p>Howard: I don&#8217;t know. Kryptonian mustard.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I think we can safely assume that all Kryptonian condiments were destroyed when the planet Krypton exploded.</p>
<p>Raj: Or it turned into mustard Kryptonite, the only way to destroy a rogue Kryptonian hotdog threatening Earth.</p>
<p>Leonard: Raj, please, let&#8217;s stay serious here. Superman&#8217;s body is Kryptonian, therefore his sweat is Kryptonian.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, what about Kryptonian pit stains?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Superman doesn&#8217;t sweat on Earth.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, he&#8217;s invited for dinner in the Bottle City of Kandor. He miniaturizes himself, enters the city where he loses his superpowers. Now, before dinner, his host says, &#8220;who&#8217;s up for a little Kryptonian tetherball?&#8221; Superman says &#8220;sure,&#8221; works up a sweat, comes back to Earth, his uniform now stained with indestructible Kryptonian perspiration.</p>
<p>Raj: Booya.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Superman would have taken his uniform to a Kandorian dry cleaner before he left the Bottle.</p>
<p>Raj: &#8220;Kandorian dry cl&#8230;&#8221; I give up, you can&#8217;t have a rational argument with this man.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, isn&#8217;t that the guy who won the MacArthur genius grant last year? No, not all at once.</p>
<p>Raj: Then how?</p>
<p>Howard: Leonard. Now, Raj. Now, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Raj: I didn&#8217;t get a good look. Can I go again?</p>
<p>Howard: No.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s David Underhill. So what?</p>
<p>Sheldon: So what? His observation of high-energy positrons has provided the first conclusive evidence for the existence of galactic dark matter.</p>
<p>Leonard: I have two words for you. The first is big, the other&#8217;s whoop.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It is a big whoop. It made almost all the work you&#8217;ve done since you&#8217;ve been here completely useless.</p>
<p>Leonard: Did not.</p>
<p>Howard: Did, too.</p>
<p>Leonard: Did&#8230; okay, maybeÂ  some of it, but, look, the guy was just in the right place, at the right time with the right paradigm-shifting reinterpretation of the universe. He got lucky.</p>
<p>Raj: In more ways than one. He&#8217;s a very handsome man.</p>
<p>Howard: Doesn&#8217;t do anything for me. If I was gonna go that way, I&#8217;m more of a Zac Ephron kinda guy.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, yeah, like you have a shot with Zac Ephron.</p>
<p>David <em>(approaching)</em>: Excuse me, Are you Leonard Hofstadter?</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, yeah.</p>
<p>David: I&#8217;m David Underhill.</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, y-yeah.</p>
<p>David: Yeah, Dr. Gablehauser said if I wanted to set something up in the photomultiplier lab, that you&#8217;d be able to give me a hand?</p>
<p>Leonard: You want to work with me?</p>
<p>David: Well, if you have a little time, yeah.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wow, y-yeah, sure. Yeah, no problem. Uh, Here&#8217;s my home number, here&#8217;s my cell, here&#8217;s my office, here&#8217;s my parents&#8217; number up in New Jersey, they always know how to reach me. So&#8230;</p>
<p>David: Okay.</p>
<p>Leonard: Congratulations on the MacArthur Grant, by the way. Big fan.</p>
<p>David: Thanks. I&#8217;ll call you.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay. Bye-bye! What are you looking at?You&#8217;ve never seen a hypocrite before?</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. The guys are playing Wii bowling.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Trailing badly, Wolowitz needs a strike if he has any hopes of catching up with Sheldon Cooper, who is dominating in the ninth frame with a career-best 68.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(entering)</em>: Hey, guys!</p>
<p>Howard: That doesn&#8217;t count. Do over! Do over!</p>
<p>Sheldon: There are no do-overs in Wii bowling.</p>
<p>Howard: There are always do-overs when my people play sports.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Where were you that&#8217;s more important than Wii bowling night?</p>
<p>Leonard: Actually, I was&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s a rhetorical question. There is nothing more important than Wii bowling night.</p>
<p>Leonard: Come on, it&#8217;s just a video game. And we suck at it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Nice motivational speech from the team captain.</p>
<p>Howard: Where were you?</p>
<p>Leonard: I was working with Dave Underhill.</p>
<p>Howard: Ooh, &#8220;Dave.&#8221; Sounds like Leonard&#8217;s got a new BFF.</p>
<p>Leonard: Actually, he&#8217;s pretty cool. I mean, not only is he a brilliant scientist, but it turns out he&#8217;s a Black Diamond skier, he collects vintage motorcycles, he plays in a rock band.</p>
<p>Howard: So? We&#8217;re in a rock band.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, we play Rock Band on our X-Box.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Nice motivational speech from our lead guitarist.</p>
<p>Leonard: He&#8217;s funny, too. He does this hysterical impersonation of Stephen Hawking having phone sex. What are you wearing? That&#8217;s not, he does it better. Anyway, he said he was gonna take me to the gym tomorrow, so I&#8217;m gonna go practice my situps.</p>
<p>Raj: Whoa. Humongous man crush, dude.</p>
<p>Howard: Yep. It&#8217;s officially a bro-mance.</p>
<p>Penny <em>(entering)</em>: Hey, Sheldon, are you and Leonard putting up a Christmas tree?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, because we don&#8217;t celebrate the ancient pagan festival of Saturnalia.</p>
<p>Penny: Saturnalia?</p>
<p>Howard: Gather round, kids, it&#8217;s time for Sheldon&#8217;s beloved Christmas special.</p>
<p>Sheldon: In the pre-Christian era, as the winter solstice approached and the plants died, pagans brought evergreen boughs into their homes as an act of sympathetic magic, intended to guard the life essences of the plants until spring. This custom was later appropriated by Northern Europeans and eventually it becomes the so-called Christmas tree.</p>
<p>Howard: And that, Charlie Brown, is what boredom is all about.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, well, thank you for that, but I got you and Leonard a few silly neighbour gifts, so I&#8217;ll just put them under my tree.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wait! You bought me a present?</p>
<p>Penny: Uh-huh.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why would you do such a thing?</p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t know. &#8216;Cause it&#8217;s Christmas?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, Penny. I know you think you&#8217;re being generous, but the foundation of gift-giving is reciprocity. You haven&#8217;t given me a gift, you&#8217;ve given me an obligation.</p>
<p>Howard: Don&#8217;t feel bad, Penny, it&#8217;s a classic rookie mistake. My first Hanukkah with Sheldon, he yelled at me for eight nights.</p>
<p>Penny: Now, honey, it&#8217;s okay.You don&#8217;t have to get me anything in return.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Of course I do. The essence of the custom is that I now have to go out and purchase for you a gift of commensurate value and representing the same perceived level of friendship as that represented by the gift you&#8217;ve given me. It&#8217;s no wonder suicide rates skyrocket this time of year.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, you know what? Forget it. I&#8217;m not giving you a present.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, it&#8217;s too late. I see it. That elf sticker says to Sheldon. The die has been cast, the moving finger has writ, Hannibal has crossed the Alps.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(to Raj who is whispering in his ear)</em>: I know. It&#8217;s funny when it&#8217;s not happening to us.</p>
<p>Penny: Sheldon, I am very, very sorry.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. No, I brought this on myself by being such an endearing and important part of your life. I&#8217;m going to need a ride to the mall.</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s happening to us.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The stairwell.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Ow! Ow!</p>
<p>David: Are you gonna make it?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, I guess.</p>
<p>David: All right.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thanks for letting me try out your motorcycle.</p>
<p>David: No problem.</p>
<p>Leonard: I had no idea it was so heavy. The thing just fell right over on me, didn&#8217;t it?</p>
<p>David: Yeah. Lucky for you it wasn&#8217;t moving.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, hey, Leonard. Ooh, are you okay?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, yeah. It&#8217;s just a little motorcycle accident.</p>
<p>Penny: My God, how fast were you going?</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t know. It&#8217;s all such a blur.</p>
<p>David: Ha ha ha. Good one. He couldn&#8217;t even get it started. Hi. Dave.</p>
<p>Penny: Hi. Penny. So it&#8217;s your motorcycle?</p>
<p>David: Uh-huh.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, is it okay?</p>
<p>David: It&#8217;s fine.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, lucky for the bike it landed on my leg.</p>
<p>David: You mind giving me a hand with speed racer here?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, yeah. Yeah, sure. So, um, Dave, how do you know Leonard?</p>
<p>David: I&#8217;m a physicist.</p>
<p>Penny: Ha-ha. No, you&#8217;re not.</p>
<p>David: Why is that so surprising?</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, well, it&#8217;s just that the physicists I know are indoorsy and pale.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m not indoorsy. I just wear the appropriate sun block because I don&#8217;t take melanoma lightly.</p>
<p>Penny: So, are you and Leonard working on an experiment together?</p>
<p>David: Yeah, actually we are.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, we&#8217;re examining the radiation levels of photomultiplier tubes for a new dark matter detector.</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, sweetie, sweetie, Dave was talking. You know, I love science.</p>
<p>Leonard: Since when?</p>
<p>Penny: Since always. Call me a geek, but I am just nuts for the whole subatomic particle thing.</p>
<p>David: The last thing I would ever call you is a geek.</p>
<p>Penny: Ha. Well, that&#8217;s what I am, queen of the nerds.</p>
<p>David: Well, if you like, I could show you the lab we&#8217;re working in. We&#8217;ve got some cool toys, you know, lasers and stuff.</p>
<p>Penny: You know, I have always wanted to see a big science lab.</p>
<p>Leonard: Since when?</p>
<p>Penny: Since always.</p>
<p>David: Leonard, are you okay here?</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, yeah. I guess.</p>
<p>David: How &#8217;bout we go see it now? Maybe afterwards we take the bike up the coast, we grab a little bite to eat&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: Um,yeah, yeah, that sounds great, let me just get my jacket.</p>
<p>David: Boy, she&#8217;ll do, huh?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, if you like that type.</p>
<p>David: So, you and her?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, just neighbours.</p>
<p>David: Really. I don&#8217;t know how you live next door to that without doing something about it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Actually, science is my lady.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay. Let&#8217;s go.</p>
<p>David: All right. See you tomorrow, Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: See ya. &#8216;Bye, Penny. Have fun. <em>(Bangs head on door. Sheldon opens it.)</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes? <em>(Leonard falls through) </em>Did you forget your key?</p>
<p><em>Scene: A gift shop.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t see anything in here a woman would want.</p>
<p>Howard: You&#8217;re kidding. You&#8217;ve got lotions and bath oils and soaps, that&#8217;s the estrogen hat trick.</p>
<p>Leonard: What it is is a cacophonous assault of eucalyptus, bayberry, cinnamon and vanilla. It&#8217;s as if my head were trapped in the pyjamas of a sultan.</p>
<p>Raj: Sheldon, if you don&#8217;t like this stuff, let&#8217;s just go next door and build her a bear.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I told you before, bears are terrifying.</p>
<p>Howard: Come on, bath stuff, it&#8217;s perfect! You got a scented candle, a cleansing buff, spearmint and green tea scented bath oil, promotes relaxation.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That presupposes Penny is tense.</p>
<p>Raj: She knows you. She&#8217;s tense. We all are. Buy a basket!</p>
<p>Howard: Excuse me, we&#8217;re ready.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, we&#8217;re not. Let&#8217;s say for a moment that I accept the bath item gift hypothesis, I now lay the following conundrum at your feet, which size?</p>
<p>Howard: This one. Let&#8217;s go.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You put no thought into that.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m sorry. Uh, this one. Let&#8217;s go!</p>
<p>Sheldon: I have insufficient data to proceed. Excuse me, miss?</p>
<p>Assistant: Yes?</p>
<p>Sheldon: If I were to give you this gift basket, based on that action alone and no other data, infer and describe the hypothetical relationship that exists between us.</p>
<p>Assistant: Excuse me?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Here. Now, are we friends? Colleagues? Lovers? Are you my grandmother?</p>
<p>Assistant: I don&#8217;t understand what you&#8217;re talking about and you&#8217;re making me a little uncomfortable.</p>
<p>Howard: See? Sounds just like you and Penny. We&#8217;ll take it.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The university cafeteria.</em></p>
<p>David: Hey, Leonard. Come, join us.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, hey, Dave. And Penny, what a surprise.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, Leonard. Dave was just showing me around the university. You know, this place is unbelievable!</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, I know. I&#8217;ve been offering to show you around for a year and a half. You always said you had yoga.</p>
<p>Penny: I never said that.</p>
<p>Leonard: Maybe I heard you wrong. A lot of words sound like yoga.</p>
<p>David: This is an amazing woman, Leonard. She has a curious and agile mind, not to mention being curious and agile in other respects.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, shut up!</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes, please shut up. So, um, Dave, don&#8217;t you think you and I should get back to the lab? You know, that dark matter isn&#8217;t going to detect itself.</p>
<p>David: Actually, I was thinking about taking the afternoon off so I could work on another experiment with Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: Really? We&#8217;re going to do an experiment?</p>
<p>David: Uh-huh. We&#8217;re going to explore the effects of tequila shots on a gorgeous 22-year-old woman.</p>
<p>Penny: It&#8217;s not an experiment! You saw what happened last night.</p>
<p>David: You ready to go?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah. Oh, can I drive the motorcycle?</p>
<p>David: Yeah, why not? You can&#8217;t do any worse than Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: That&#8217;s funny. By the way, my leg is killing me. Thanks for asking.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Mmm, great news, Leonard, I&#8217;ve solved my Penny gift dilemma.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yippee.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You see, the danger was that I might under or over-reciprocate, but I have devised a foolproof plan. See, I will open her gift to me first and then excuse myself, feigning digestive distress.</p>
<p>Then I&#8217;ll look up the price of her gift online, choose the basket closest to that value, give it to her and then I&#8217;ll return the others for a full refund.</p>
<p>Leonard: Brilliant.</p>

					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
