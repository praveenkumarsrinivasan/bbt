				<p><em>Scene: The cafeteria. Sheldon laughs to himself.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: I wish you could all be inside my head. The conversation is sparkling. <em>(Pause) </em>Fine, I&#8217;ll tell you. A lichen is an organism made up of two separate species, fungi and algae. If you could merge with another species, what species would you pick and why? Hint, there is a right answer. None of you will get it.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, uh, I&#8217;d pick swan because, uh, the resulting hybrid would have the advanced industrial civilization of a human and the long graceful neck I&#8217;ve always dreamed of having.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wrong. Leonard?</p>
<p>Leonard: Horse, but mostly just for the height. A little bit for the genital girth.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wrong, and let&#8217;s keep it clean, shall we?</p>
<p>Howard: Kangaroo, uh, I&#8217;d be a Kanga-Jew. The first of my people to dunk a basketball.</p>
<p>Leonard: Also instead of just living in your mother&#8217;s house, you could actually live inside her body.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Clever, but also wrong. No, the best organism for human beings to merge with is the lichen itself. That way, you&#8217;d be human, fungus, and algae. Triple threat. Like three-bean salad.</p>
<p>Leonard: Give me one circumstance in which that would be useful.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, picture this, a beautiful outdoor concert. Now, as a human, I appreciate Beethoven. As a fungus, I have a terrific view, growing out of a towering maple tree. And no thank you, expensive concessions. Because as an algae, I&#8217;ll just snack on this sunlight.</p>
<p>Raj: He got us again.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, he didn&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Raj: Anyway, if it&#8217;s okay with you, we should talk about Howard&#8217;s bachelor party.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, seems like a bit of a let down after our lichen conversation, but, what do you know, you&#8217;re half swan.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;ve been doing some research on strippers. One agency I spoke to, said I could get us a great price if we&#8217;re flexible on age range and number of limbs.</p>
<p>Howard: Sounds like loads of fun, but I promised Bernadette no strippers.</p>
<p>Raj: You don&#8217;t want strippers? You&#8217;re the king of strippers. The one club in North Hollywood named a pole after you.</p>
<p>Howard: What can I tell ya, I&#8217;m not into that stuff any more.</p>
<p>Leonard: Good for you, Howard. I&#8217;m proud of you. And still, you&#8217;re the first one of us to get married. We have to do something special.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You know Germans have an interesting pre-wedding custom.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, it&#8217;s probably not for me.</p>
<p>Raj: Maybe we can go up to Napa Valley. They&#8217;ve got that wine train.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Boo, wine! But yay, trains. I&#8217;m in.</p>
<p>Raj: Anyway, it&#8217;s a beautiful time of year. Uh, you travel through the vineyards. There&#8217;s a tasting on board. And all the wild flowers are in bloom. It&#8217;s magic.</p>
<p>Leonard: Look at that, in 30 seconds, we went from hiring women to being them.</p>

<p>Penny: Why should I worry?</p>

<p>Penny: Oh, come on Leonard, it&#8217;s you. What&#8217;s going to happen? I mean, even if there was a stripper, all you&#8217;d do is avoid eye-contact and maybe offer to help her kid with his homework.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, I am a young man in his sexual prime. Under the right conditions, I-I-I am capable of just, really crazy stuff.</p>
<p>Penny: Really? What is the craziest thing you&#8217;ve ever done with a woman? And the time you and I had sex in the ocean does not count.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, come on, that&#8217;s got to count. There was a really strong undertow, we could have died.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, have fun tonight.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, I will. There is no telling what might happen.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, there is.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know, there&#8217;s nothing wrong helping some woman&#8217;s kid get through their S.A.T.&#8217;s.</p>
<p><em>Scene: A restaurant.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, I got to hand it to Raj, he found a really nice spot to have a bachelor party.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s not bad. Unless you compare it to a train; then it stinks.</p>
<p>Leonard: Are you drinking whisky?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Indeed. If I&#8217;m to participate in the social convention that is the stag night, then I must embrace all its components, including tobacco, swear words, and yes, alcohol. Jeepers! That&#8217;s yucky.</p>

<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m confused. I thought since our reconciliation, I was your friend in this group.</p>
<p>Wil: Oh, I&#8217;m friends with Howard too.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh. I guess you&#8217;re just friends with anybody. <em>(Drinks) </em>Aagh!</p>
<p>Stuart: Hey, uh, Leonard, things are a little tight at the comic book store. I might need some help covering my share of the check.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, yeah, no worries.</p>
<p>Stuart: And maybe a few bucks for the valet.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, all right.</p>
<p>Stuart: And gas money to get home.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, sure.</p>
<p>Stuart: Great. You know what? This is my grandfather&#8217;s watch.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh.</p>
<p>Stuart: 18-carat gold, got it in Europe during the war.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wow, that&#8217;s very nice.</p>
<p>Stuart: Mm-hmm. A hundred bucks and it&#8217;s yours.</p>
<p>Raj: Hey, everybody! The bachelor boy has arrived! For he&#8217;s a jolly good fellow&#8230;</p>
<p>All <em>(joining in)</em>: For he&#8217;s a jolly good fellow, for he&#8217;s a jolly good fellow, which nobody can deny.</p>

<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Amy <em>(into a mini-cam)</em>: This is Maid of Honour Amy Farrah Fowler, bringing you the wedding activities just weeks out from the big day. Let&#8217;s check in with a beautiful, radiant young woman, and her friend who&#8217;s about to get married. Ladies, can you tell us what you&#8217;re doing?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Um, these are gift bags we&#8217;re going to put in the hotel rooms of our out-of-town guests. This is a map of Pasadena. This is a list of local restaurants. And then, for Howie&#8217;s relatives, we have antihistamines, antacids, and medicine for diarrhoea and constipation.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, we labeled them stop and go.</p>
<p>Amy: All right, pivoting to the big question. Bernadette, on your wedding night you&#8217;ll be consummating your marriage. What do you think your first sexual position will be as husband and wife?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Amy, please.</p>
<p>Amy: Keeping in mind that whoever&#8217;s on top may set the tone for the marriage.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, show&#8217;s over.</p>
<p>Amy: Hey, they may conceive a child on their wedding night. Don&#8217;t you think the kid might get a kick out of knowing how it happened?</p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t care. Ask her things like are you going to take Howard&#8217;s name? Not who&#8217;s going to sit on who?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;ve actually been thinking I&#8217;m going to hyphenate: Bernadette Mary-Ann Rostenkowski-Wolowitz.</p>
<p>Penny: Nice. You know, you should totally get BernadetteMaryAnnRostenkowskiWolowitz.com before someone snaps it up.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howard already took care of it. Plus, he set up our beautiful wedding website with cute little facts about our family histories. Do you know, for a while in Poland, my family and his family were neighbours.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, that&#8217;s cool.</p>
<p>Amy: No, it&#8217;s not. I&#8217;ll explain it to you later.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The restaurant.</em></p>
<p>Raj: May I have your attention, please? We are hear tonight to celebrate the upcoming nuptials of my best friend Howard Wolowitz.</p>
<p>All: Hear! Hear!</p>
<p>Sheldon: And, apparently, Wil Wheaton&#8217;s best friend.</p>
<p>Wil: Sheldon&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: Talk to the hand.</p>
<p>Raj: Does anyone have any words they&#8217;d like to say about our man of the evening?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, I do. <em>(All groan)</em>. As is the tradition, I have prepared a series of disrespectful jokes which generate humour at Howard&#8217;s expense. Prepare to have your ribs tickled. Howard, I always thought you&#8217;d be the last one of us to ever get married, because you are so short and unappealing. Am I right? Let&#8217;s see here. Oh, seriously though, Howard, you&#8217;re actually one of the most intelligent people I know. And that&#8217;s a zinger, because you&#8217;re not. I&#8217;ve always thought that you&#8217;d make someone a fine husband someday. Assuming you&#8217;d be able to get the parts, and develop the engineering skills to assemble them, which I don&#8217;t see as likely. Hacha! Okay, let me see here. Okay, kidding aside, Howard, you are a good friend. And I wish you nothing but happiness. Bazinga, I don&#8217;t!</p>

<p>Sheldon: Doubleazinga! I do! Good luck following that.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, Howard Wolowitz tying the knot. Leaving his crazy bachelor days behind. He was a wild one. Well, I guess we all kind of were. I remember this one time, I was with this girl at the beach. We were in the ocean and we started making out. I know, it was crazy. I wasn&#8217;t even wearing my Aquasocks. Then&#8230;</p>
<p>Kripke: Nobody cares, Hofstadter. Wwap it up.</p>
<p>Leonard: Right. To Howard.</p>
<p>All: To Howard.</p>
<p>Leonard: I totally had sex in the ocean.</p>
<p>Stuart: Okay, I&#8217;ll go. Howard, when I think about you and Bernadette starting this wonderful life together, I can&#8217;t help but get a little choked up. I mean, look at you. You have everything. Look at me. I&#8217;m 37. I sleep in the back of a comic book store, and I have the bone density of an 80-year-old man. To Howard.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, to Howard. Um, uh, who&#8217;s next?</p>
<p>Kripke: I&#8217;ll go. Howard, I&#8217;m gonna say something to you that evewybody&#8217;s thinking but no one has the couwage to say out woud. <p>Raj: Okay, uh, anybody else? Huh? No? Okay, it all comes down to me, the best man. Ooh! This grasshopper is kicking my ass-hopper. Okay, when I first came to this country, I-I didn&#8217;t know how to behave or how to dress, or what was cool, I was pretty lonely. But then I met Howard, and suddenly, my life changed, because we could be lonely together. This man became my whole world.</p>

I told him I&#8217;m addicted to pedicures and he told me he lost his virginity to his cousin.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );

					}
				});
