				<p><i>Sceme: The apartment</i></p>
<p>Bernadette: Raj, your tag&#8217;s sticking out.</p>
<p>Raj: Thank you. That was the closest I&#8217;ve come to sex in, like, two years.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Now I feel a little gross.</p>
<p>Raj: You&#8217;re only making it seem more real for me.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, that&#8217;s my wife. If anyone&#8217;s gonna make her feel gross about sex, it&#8217;s me.</p>
<p>Raj: Can we please talk about something other than my depressing love life?</p>
<p>Sheldon: How about Penny&#8217;s depressing acting career?</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey. I mean, it&#8217;s been a little tough, but Penny&#8217;s following her dreams, and in my book, that is not depressing.</p>
<p>Penny: Aw, thank you.</p>
<p>Howard: Is that book called Lies I Tell to Get Sex?</p>
<p>Raj: Is that a real book? I would totally read that book.</p>
<p>Amy: Can I borrow it when you&#8217;re done?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, I&#8217;ll have you guys know I turned down a part in a movie last week.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why would you do that?</p>
<p>Penny: Because it was crap. It&#8217;s a sequel to that awful killer gorilla movie I was in.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Serial Ape-ist? I thought you died in that.</p>
<p>Sheldon: She does. 42 minutes in.</p>
<p>Raj: While showering topless, 16 minutes after brief side butt during a pillow fight with her sorority sisters.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I have an eidetic memory. I don&#8217;t know what his problem is.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, well, there are no shower scenes in this one. They just try to clone me from my corpse, but my DNA gets mixed with the ape&#8217;s DNA and I end up running around with giant gorilla hands and feet.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Am I missing something, or isn&#8217;t that the part she was born to play?</p>
<p><i>Credits sequence.</i></p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Leonard: So are you really not gonna do this movie?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, I don&#8217;t think it&#8217;s the kind of part that&#8217;s good for my career.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, but don&#8217;t a lot of famous actors get their start doing bad movies?</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, I don&#8217;t think Meryl Streep ever had to say &#8220;Must keep gorilla hands from killing again!&#8221;</p>
<p>Raj: If she did, it would be amazing. That woman can do no wrong.</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t know anything about show business, so if you think it&#8217;s reasonable to turn down paid work and just burn through your savings, I&#8217;m sure you know what you&#8217;re doing.</p>
<p>Amy: I think we&#8217;re gonna go.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Are you sure? We were making fun of failed careers. We didn&#8217;t get to tap the juicy vein that is Howard&#8217;s.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, I work at the same university you do.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, and Hawkeye&#8217;s in the Avengers, but no one ever says help, Hawkeye.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Can I ask you something? Why do you constantly feel the need to put down my husband?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, I&#8217;m sure he does it out of love. The same way my boyfriend makes me feel terrible about my life choices.</p>
<p>Amy: I think we&#8217;re gonna go.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, no, no. This is not a fight. I was just excited that someone offered you a part and a little surprised that you&#8217;d rather sit at home and do nothing than take it. Now it&#8217;s a fight.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, with that sorted out, I&#8217;m happy to answer your question, Bernadette. Howard started it.</p>
<p>Howard: I didn&#8217;t do anything. I was just sitting here.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I wasn&#8217;t referring to this evening. Ten years ago, upon first seeing me, your husband claimed that I looked like C-3PO and Pee-wee Herman. And he called me C-3P-wee Herman.</p>
<p>Raj: Ha-a-a. Still funny.</p>
<p>Bernadette: That was ten years ago.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Nine years, 11 months and three weeks ago, he followed that up by replacing the slides for my lecture with photographs of nude fat women bending over.</p>
<p>Amy: Really?</p>
<p>Howard: The lecture was on cosmic gas clouds. That&#8217;s funny.</p>
<p>Raj: I was there. It was funny.</p>
<p>Sheldon: In any event, that began a decade-long progression of insults, pranks and unwanted magazine subscriptions. To this day, I still get a monthly copy of Granny on Granny. Which, other than its surprisingly fun puzzle page, is complete filth.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The street</i></p>
<p>Amy: Thanks for walking me to my car.</p>
<p>Raj: Actually, it&#8217;s for both of us. Last night, I watched West Side Story, and I&#8217;m a little freaked out by street gangs.</p>
<p>Amy: Why can&#8217;t Raj find a girl? The mystery continues.</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t appreciate your sarcasm, but we&#8217;ve still got two blocks to go, so I&#8217;ll put up with it.</p>
<p>Amy: Whatever happened with your online dating?</p>
<p>Raj: No one was writing me back, so I stopped checking my profile.</p>
<p>Amy: Before I met Sheldon, I was ready to give up, too. Once, I even dropped in on my OBGYN just to get some human contact.</p>
<p>Raj: It has been a while since I got my prostate checked.</p>
<p>Amy: Then I met Sheldon and look at where we are now.</p>
<p>Raj: What? You&#8217;ve kissed, like, once in three years.</p>
<p>Amy: That&#8217;s true. Do whatever you want.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Sheldon&#8217;s office.</i></p>
<p>Howard: Hey.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hello.</p>
<p>Howard: Listen, I was thinking you and I could probably try to be better friends.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Really? I was thinking what we have now is a bit much.</p>
<p>Howard: Seriously, I was talking to Bernadette last night and she made some great points. You and I have known each other a long time, and I didn&#8217;t hear the rest &#8217;cause she took her bra off.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Very well. How do you propose we move forward?</p>
<p>Howard: Uh, for starters, we could stop insulting each other.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s a great idea. And in the spirit of our new arrangement, I will mask my surprise that you came up with it.</p>
<p>Howard: Anyway, I got invited to do a little talk at NASA in Houston this weekend. They gave me two tickets. Bernadette can&#8217;t go. You want to come with me? You can visit your mom, and I&#8217;ll show you around the space centre.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I have one question. Is this a prank where we land in Houston and you&#8217;ve made up wanted posters that have my face with a moustache and a Spanish name and then I get arrested and deported to South America?</p>
<p>Howard: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m glad because I would not have seen that coming.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Amy&#8217;s lab.</i></p>
<p>Raj: Amy, I could use some help.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh. Let me guess. There&#8217;s an undergrad in a leather jacket snapping his fingers by the water fountain.</p>
<p>Raj: I thought about what you said last night, and I went back on the dating Web site and I was looking at this girl&#8217;s profile. She&#8217;s amazing.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, she&#8217;s cute. And smart. Phi Beta Kappa.</p>
<p>Raj: And judging by her lack of Adam&#8217;s apple, she&#8217;s been female her entire life. I like that in a woman.</p>
<p>Amy: Great, so what do you need me for?</p>
<p>Raj: Well, I was up all night trying to write to her, but I wanted to run it by you first.</p>
<p>Amy: All right.</p>
<p>Raj: Emily, your face is like a precious dew-kissed flower.</p>
<p>Amy: Wow.</p>
<p>Raj: I know. Powerful stuff, huh?</p>
<p>Amy: No. You&#8217;re supposed to be yourself, not all desperate and creepy.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, I&#8217;m getting some mixed messages here.</p>
<p>Amy: Just say something normal like, I saw your profile. Looks like we have a lot in common, let&#8217;s get a cup of coffee sometime.&#8221;</p>
<p>Raj: Yes. Where I can be jealous of the cup touching your ruby lips. Or you just write it for me.</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;m not gonna pretend to be you.</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t want you to pretend to be me. You can be like my, uh, online wingman. Like if we met her at a bar and you talked me up.</p>
<p>Amy: Well, what would I say to her?</p>
<p>Raj: Just tell her what I&#8217;m really like. And, if you think it&#8217;ll help, that you saw me changing once and my package reminded you of the muscular tail of a jaguar. Use your own words.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Penny&#8217;s car.</i></p>
<p>Sheldon: Thanks again for taking me to the pharmacy.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, it&#8217;s no problem. Is everything okay?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I&#8217;m fine. It&#8217;s just some, uh, stomach medication for my trip. There&#8217;s the remote yet distinct possibility that I may end up in South America.</p>
<p>Penny: Remember the old days when I would&#8217;ve said something dumb like why? Uh, that doesn&#8217;t sound good.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Remember the old days when I used to point out that your check engine light was on?</p>
<p>Penny: Yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, get ready to stroll down memory lane. Penny, your check engine&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, I know it&#8217;s on, Sheldon. Oh, no, no, no, no, no. I cannot afford this right now.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Maybe it&#8217;s just something minor. Ooh, good news, the light just went out.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment. </i></p>
<p>Leonard: Are we playing individual or teams?</p>
<p>Raj: Teams are fun.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, in that case, I&#8217;d like to be partnered with my good friend Howard.</p>
<p>Raj: But I&#8217;m always on Howard&#8217;s team. We&#8217;re best friends. The kind who finish each others&#8230;</p>
<p>Howard: I really don&#8217;t think we do tha&#8230;</p>
<p>Raj: Do that. See?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, hi.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, how&#8217;d it go at the mechanic?</p>
<p>Penny: Not great. Can I talk to you for a sec?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, sure.</p>
<p>Raj: Maybe I can go with you guys to Houston?</p>
<p>Howard: Isn&#8217;t it a little late to get plane tick&#8230;</p>
<p>Raj: Plane tickets? Yeah.</p>
<p>Howard: Will you please sto&#8230;</p>
<p>Raj: Stop that? Okay.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The stairwell.</i></p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t know what to do. My car threw a rod and it&#8217;s totaled. I can&#8217;t afford a new one, I have no job, and now I can&#8217;t drive to auditions.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m so sorry. I know it&#8217;s a sensitive subject, but can you reconsider that part in the movie?</p>
<p>Penny: I did. I called them. The part&#8217;s gone. They gave it to someone else. Now that girl&#8217;s gonna get discovered and become famous and go on Letterman and talk about how she got her big break on a cheap monkey movie all because some dumb girl thought it was beneath her.</p>
<p>Leonard: At least they talked about you on Letterman. Come on, I can drive you wherever you need to go.</p>
<p>Penny: How? Unlike me, you have a job. I&#8217;m just gonna have to go back to being a waitress, like I will be for the rest of my life.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, would you wrap it up? We&#8217;re waiting on you.</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m sorry, is the fact that my life&#8217;s falling apart interfering with your board game?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It is. You were wrong, friend Howard. She completely understood.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Amy&#8217;s lab.</i></p>
<p>Raj: Hey, you busy?</p>
<p>Amy: No. What&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Raj: Have you heard back from Emily?</p>
<p>Amy: I have.</p>
<p>Raj: Great. And?</p>
<p>Amy: And I&#8217;m afraid she doesn&#8217;t think you&#8217;re right for her.</p>
<p>Raj: I give you one simple thing to do, contact a complete stranger and make her fall in love with me, and you blow it.</p>
<p>Amy: I told her what a good guy you are, but she thought it was a bad sign that you didn&#8217;t write to her yourself. She thought it made you seem too shy and passive.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m not too shy and passive. You write her back and tell her I said that. You know, when you have a second.</p>
<p>Amy: Look, I&#8217;ll see if I can change her mind tomorrow night.</p>
<p>Raj: What&#8217;s happening tomorrow night?</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;m meeting her for coffee.</p>
<p>Raj: What?</p>
<p>Amy: Well, we just, we e-mailed back and forth a bit, and we kind of hit it off.</p>
<p>Raj: I find a girl I like, and you&#8217;re stealing her?</p>
<p>Amy: No. We just ended up having a lot in common. We went to Harvard, we like Chaucer, we&#8217;re both hardcore into quilting.</p>
<p>Raj: Your words fall like acid rain on the wounded petals of my heart.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Bernadette&#8217;s car.</i></p>
<p>Howard: That&#8217;s Sheldon. He says he&#8217;ll be down in a minute.</p>
<p>Bernadette: What are you doing?</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, he&#8217;s not coming out until he sees proof you don&#8217;t have an air freshener in your car.</p>
<p>Bernadette: This is gonna be a long weekend for you.</p>
<p>Howard: You&#8217;re the reason I&#8217;m doing it.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I said to stop insulting each other. I didn&#8217;t tell you to take him on a romantic getaway.</p>
<p>Howard: How do I know what you said? Damn you and your noise-cancelling breasts.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hello.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hi.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, buddy.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You excited for Texas?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, very much so.</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s not every day you get to tour NASA with a real astronaut.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, who&#8217;s the real astronaut?</p>
<p>Howard: Buzz Aldrin.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh. Yay.</p>
<p><i>Scene: An airplane. </i></p>
<p>Sheldon: <i>(Tap, tap, tap) </i>Howard? <i>(Tap, tap, tap) </i>Howard? <i>(Tap, tap, tap) </i>Howard?</p>
<p>Howard: What now?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I have to go to the bathroom.</p>
<p>Howard: You just went to the bathroom.</p>
<p>Sheldon: But I didn&#8217;t use it because it didn&#8217;t seem safe. Despite all my e-mails, the toilet didn&#8217;t have a seatbelt.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, it still doesn&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I realize that, but safety concerns went out the window two apple juices ago.</p>
<p>Howard: Fine.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why are you getting annoyed?</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m trying to be a better friend, but you constantly say and do irritating things.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Like when?</p>
<p>Howard: When? How about in the car? I&#8217;m an astronaut, and you know it. You just don&#8217;t like admitting it, because you&#8217;re jealous.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, truth be told, as a child, I did dream of going to space. Those astronauts were my heroes. And when you got to go, it was hard for me.</p>
<p>Howard: Thank you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Because it made me realize they&#8217;ll just send anyone up there. Aren&#8217;t you going to let me out?</p>
<p>Howard: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: But I still need to use the bathroom.</p>
<p>Howard: Here you go. Be creative. What&#8217;s the matter? Scared of a little turbulence?</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {

					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );

				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );

				});
