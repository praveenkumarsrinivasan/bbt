		<h2 class="title">Series 3 Episode 09 &#8211; The Vengeance&nbsp;Formulation</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: Outside an apartment building.</em></p>
<p>Howard: So two years later, there&#8217;s a knock on the door, guy opens it, and there on his porch is the snail, who says, &#8220;What the heck was all that about?&#8221;</p>
<p>Bernadette: I don&#8217;t really get it.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, see, it took two years for the snail to&#8230; <em>(she kisses him) </em>not important.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Can I ask you a question?</p>
<p>Howard: Sure.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Where do you think this is going?</p>
<p>Howard: To be honest, I was hoping at least second base.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You&#8217;re so funny. You&#8217;re like a stand-up comedian.</p>
<p>Howard: A Jewish stand-up comedian, that&#8217;d be new.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Actually, I think a lot of them are Jewish.</p>
<p>Howard: No, I was just&#8230; never mind.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Look, Howard, this is our third date and we both know what that means.</p>
<p>Howard: We do?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Sex.</p>
<p>Howard: You&#8217;re kidding.</p>
<p>Bernadette: But I need to know whether you&#8217;re looking for a relationship or a one-night stand.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, just to be clear, there&#8217;s only one correct answer, right? It&#8217;s not like chicken or fish on an airplane?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Maybe you need to think about it a little.</p>
<p>Howard: You know, it&#8217;s not unheard of for a one-night stand to turn into a relationship.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Call me when you figure it out.</p>
<p>Howard: Three dates means sex? Who knew?</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Greetings, homies, homette.</p>
<p>Penny: Why are you back from your date so early?</p>
<p>Howard: In romance, as in show business, always leave them wanting more.</p>
<p>Penny: What exactly does that mean?</p>
<p>Leonard: He struck out.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence. </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>Scene: The university cafeteria.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Hey, did either of you guys know that three dates with the same woman is the threshold for sex?</p>
<p>Raj: Actually, I&#8217;ve never had three dates with the same woman.</p>
<p>Leonard: With Penny and me, it took two years. Now that I think about it, that was three dates.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, well, before you and Penny hooked up, did she ask for any kind of commitment?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, she was pretty clear about wanting to keep her options open.</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(arriving)</em>: I have something to announce, but out of respect for convention, I will wait for you to finish your current conversation. What are you talking about?</p>
<p>Leonard: The cultural paradigm in which people have sex after three dates.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I see. Now, are we talking date, the social interaction, or date, the dried fruit?</p>
<p>Leonard: Never mind, what&#8217;s your announcement?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, good, my turn. Well, this is very exciting and I wanted you to be among the first to know&#8230;</p>
<p>Kripke: Hey, Cooper, I hear you&#8217;re going to be on the wadio with Ira Fwatow from Science Fwiday next week.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thank you, Kripke, for depriving me of the opportunity to share my news with my friends.</p>
<p>Kripke: My pweasure.</p>
<p>Sheldon: My thank you was not sincere.</p>
<p>Kripke: But my pweasure is. Let me ask you a question, at what point did National Public Wadio have to start scwaping the bottom of the bawwel for its guests? Eh, don&#8217;t answer, it&#8217;s wetowical.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why are you such a stupid head? That is also rhetorical. I&#8217;m sorry you had to hear that.</p>
<p>Leonard: Are you really going to be on NPR?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, they&#8217;re interviewing me by phone from my office, regarding the recent so-called discovery of magnetic monopoles in spin-ices. It&#8217;s pledge week and they&#8217;re trying to goose the ratings with a little controversy.</p>
<p>Leonard: Very cool, congratulations.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thank you. My mother is very excited. She&#8217;s convening her Bible study group to listen in, and then pray for my soul.</p>
<p>Raj: I was on the radio once. I called in to Fever 104 FM New Delhi and was the fourth person to say the phrase that pays, &#8220;Fever 104, बहुत अच्छा संगीत के मौजूदा घर.” That means: &#8220;Fever 104, home of the really good current music.&#8221; It&#8217;s much catchier in Hindi.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: All right. These are the talking points for my NPR interview tomorrow. I need to make sure that they&#8217;re simple enough for the less educated in the audience to understand. Howard, look this over and tell me what&#8217;s unclear to you.</p>
<p>Howard: Excuse me, I have a master&#8217;s degree in engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. It required the completion of 144 units of grad work and an original thesis.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes. Look this over and tell me what&#8217;s unclear to you.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know, when Sheldon gives you homework, you don&#8217;t have to do it.</p>
<p>Raj: In fact, it&#8217;s better if you don&#8217;t, otherwise it makes the rest of us look bad.</p>
<p>Penny: Hi, guys.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hello.</p>
<p>Penny: Yo, Raj, talk to me. I&#8217;m sorry, just screwing with you. Hey, Howard, why haven&#8217;t you called Bernadette?</p>
<p>Howard: Did she say something?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, she said she hasn&#8217;t heard from you in a week. I thought you liked her?</p>
<p>Howard: I do, yeah, but she wants a commitment and I&#8217;m not sure she&#8217;s my type.</p>
<p>Penny: She agreed to go out with you for free. What more do you need?</p>
<p>Howard: Look, Bernadette is really nice. I just always thought when I finally settle down into a relationship, it would be with someone, you know, different.</p>
<p>Penny: Different how?</p>
<p>Howard: Well, you know, more like Megan Fox from Transformers, or Katee Sackhoff from Battlestar Galactica.</p>
<p>Penny: Are you high?</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;d have a better shot with the three-breasted Martian hooker from Total Recall.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, now you&#8217;re just being unrealistic. Anyway, that movie was like 20 years ago, imagine how saggy those things would be.</p>
<p>Penny: Howard, you&#8217;re going to throw away a great girl like Bernadette because you&#8217;re holding out for some ridiculous fantasy?</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, just because you settled doesn&#8217;t mean I have to.</p>
<p>Leonard: Excuse me, I&#8217;m sitting here.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, I did not settle for Leonard. I mean, obviously, he isn&#8217;t the kind of guy I usually go out with, you know, physically.</p>
<p>Leonard: Again, I&#8217;m right here.</p>
<p>Penny: My point is, I do not judge a book by its cover. I am interested in the person underneath.</p>
<p>Leonard: I am here, right? You see me.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, I&#8217;m interested in what&#8217;s inside people, too, but why is it wrong to want those insides wrapped up in, say, the delicious caramel that is Halle Berry? <em>(To Raj) </em>Yes, you&#8217;re delicious caramel, too.</p>
<p>Penny: All right, you know what, I will tell you why it&#8217;s wrong&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: Excuse me, may I interject?</p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Biologically speaking, Howard is perfectly justified in seeking out the optimum mate for the propagation of his genetic line.</p>
<p>Howard: Thank you, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Now, whether that propagation is in the interest of humanity is, of course, an entirely different question.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Kripke is in a storage cupboard of some kind. He has tubes rigged up through the roof attached to helium tanks which he is turning on. The radio is playing. </em></p>
<p>Radio: This is Ira Flatow and you&#8217;re listening to NPR&#8217;s Science Friday. Joining us today by phone from his office in Pasadena, California is Dr. Sheldon Cooper.</p>
<p>Kripke: Oh, this is going to be a wiot.</p>
<p><em>Scene switches to Sheldon’s office, where the end of the tubing can be seen coming through the wall. Hissing can be heard.</em></p>
<p>Radio: Thanks for being with us today, Dr. Cooper.</p>
<p>Sheldon: My pleasure, Ira.</p>
<p>Ira: Now, let&#8217;s talk about magnetic monopoles. Can you explain to our audience just what a monopole is?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Of course. First, consider an ordinary magnet which has, <em>(the pitch of his voice begins to rise) </em>as even the most uneducated in your audience must know, two poles, <em>(pitch rises further) </em>a<em> </em>north and south pole. If you cut that in half, you have two smaller magnets, each with its own north and south pole.</p>
<p>Ira: Uh, Dr. Cooper, I think there might be something wrong with our connection.</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(even higher pitch)</em>: No, I hear you fine. As I was saying, an ordinary magnet has two poles. The primary characteristic of a monopole is that it has only one pole, hence, monopole.</p>
<p><em>Scene cuts to university cafeteria where Kripke is holding up the radio for all to listen. </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: A requirement for string theory, or M-theory, if you will, is the existence of such monopoles. I, myself, led an expedition to the Arctic Circle in search of said particles. Kripke, I found the nozzle! I&#8217;m going to kill you!</p>
<p><em>Scene: Howard’s bathroom. He is in the bath.</em></p>
<p>Howard: So nice you could join me this evening. You&#8217;re looking lovely as always.</p>
<p>Katee Sackhoff: Thanks, Howard. Always nice to be part of your masturbatory fantasies.</p>
<p>Howard: Come on, Katee, don&#8217;t make it sound so cheap.</p>
<p>Katee: I&#8217;m sorry, fiddling with yourself in the bathtub is a real class act.</p>
<p>Howard: Thank you. So, shall we get started?</p>
<p>Katee: Sure. But can I ask you a question first?</p>
<p>Howard: You want to play Cylon and colonist?</p>
<p>Katee: No. I want to know why you&#8217;re playing make-believe with me when you could be out with a real woman tonight.</p>
<p>Howard: You mean, Bernadette?</p>
<p>Katee: No, I mean Princess Leia. Of course I mean Bernadette. She&#8217;s a wonderful girl and she really likes you.</p>
<p>Howard: I know, but she&#8217;s not you.</p>
<p>Katee: I&#8217;m not me. The real me is in Beverly Hills going out with a tall, handsome, rich guy.</p>
<p>Howard: Really? Tall?</p>
<p>Katee: Six-four.</p>
<p>Howard: Ouch.</p>
<p>Katee: The point is, you&#8217;ve got a wonderful girl in your life, and you&#8217;re ignoring her in order to spend your nights in the bathtub with a mental image and a wash cloth.</p>
<p>Howard’s Mother: Howard! What are you doing in there?</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m taking a bath!</p>
<p>Howard’s Mother: I hope that&#8217;s all you&#8217;re doing! We share that tub!</p>
<p>Howard: Don&#8217;t remind me!<em> (Katee has vanished)</em> Oh, man. All soaped up and no place to go.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Sheldon’s bedroom. </em></p>
<p>Leonard: How ya doin&#8217;, buddy?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I was humiliated on national radio. How do you think I&#8217;m doing?</p>
<p>Leonard: Come on, it wasn&#8217;t that bad.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What do you want?</p>
<p>Raj <em>(in a munchkin voice)</em>: We represent the Lollipop Guild, and we want you.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, so Kripke played a joke on you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It wasn&#8217;t funny.</p>
<p>Raj: I thought it was funny.</p>
<p>Leonard: Raj.</p>
<p>Raj: You laughed.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Did you laugh?</p>
<p>Leonard: I fell on the floor. All right, he got you, you can get him back.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I refuse to sink to his level.</p>
<p>Raj: You can&#8217;t sink. With all that helium in you, you&#8217;re lucky you don&#8217;t float away.</p>
<p>Leonard: Are you really admitting defeat?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I never admit defeat.</p>
<p>Leonard: Good.</p>
<p>Sheldon: However, on an unrelated topic, I am never getting out of this bed again.</p>
<p>Leonard: What if you could make Kripke look even sillier than he made you look?</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t think that&#8217;s possible, dude.</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re not helping.</p>
<p>Raj: I didn&#8217;t come to help, I came to mock.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, what you need to do is figure out a way to exact vengeance on Kripke, like, uh, like, how the Joker got back at Batman for putting him in the Arkham Asylum for the Criminally Insane.</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s true. He didn&#8217;t just stay there and talk about his feelings with the other psychotic villains. He, he broke out and poisoned Gotham&#8217;s water supply.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, I suppose I could poison Kripke.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, no.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s a simple matter. There are several toxic chemicals that&#8217;d be untraceable in an autopsy.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, uh, that&#8217;s the spirit, but, um, let&#8217;s dial it back to a non-lethal form of vengeance.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh! How about we put awhoopee cushion on his office chair? He&#8217;ll sit down, it&#8217;ll sound like he&#8217;s flatulent, even though he&#8217;s not.</p>
<p>Leonard: Let&#8217;s keep thinking.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The Cheesecake Factory. Howard enters.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Oh, gee, you&#8217;re too late. Scarlett Johansson and Wonder Woman were just in here trolling around for neurotic, little weasels.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, I came to talk to Bernadette. She&#8217;s working today, right?</p>
<p>Penny: Yes, but I don&#8217;t think she wants to see you.</p>
<p>Howard: Why not?</p>
<p>Penny: Come on, Howard. You hurt her feelings by not calling her all week. Plus, I&#8217;ve kind of been talkin&#8217; some smack about ya.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hello, Howard.</p>
<p>Howard: Wait, Bernadette, I need to talk to you.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I can&#8217;t now, I&#8217;m working.</p>
<p>Howard: This will only take a second. You asked me to think about where our relationship was going, and I did. Bernadette? Will you marry me?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Is this more comedy that I don&#8217;t understand?</p>
<p>Howard: No. I&#8217;m serious. I&#8217;m never going to find another girl like you who likes me and is, you know, real.</p>
<p>Bernadette: So, this isn&#8217;t a joke?</p>
<p>Howard: No.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Then you&#8217;re insane.</p>
<p>Howard: I prefer to think of myself as quirky.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howard, we&#8217;ve only been on three dates. We haven&#8217;t even had sex yet.</p>
<p>Howard: Fair enough. When&#8217;s your break?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Wow. <em>(Walks away)</em></p>
<p>Howard: Don&#8217;t you just hate when this happens?</p>
<p>Penny: Wow.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment kitchen.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, how&#8217;s this for revenge? A solution of hydrogen peroxide, and one of saturated potassium iodide.</p>
<p>Raj: What&#8217;s this?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Mountain Dew. Ah, refreshing. Now, we&#8217;re going to combine these chemicals with ordinary dish soap, creating a little exothermic release of oxygen.</p>
<p>Raj: Foamy vengeance.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, exactly.</p>
<p>Leonard: This is brilliant, Sheldon. How are we going to deploy it in Kripke&#8217;s office?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Already taken care of. Observe. This is a live shot of Kripke&#8217;s lab via a mini webcam I was able to install, thanks to a dollar bill discreetly placed in the night janitor&#8217;s shirt pocket.
