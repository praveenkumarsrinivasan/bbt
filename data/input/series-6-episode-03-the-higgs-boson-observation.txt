		<h2 class="title">Series 6 Episode 03 &#8211; The Higgs Boson&nbsp;Observation</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: Webcam conversation between Bernadette&#8217;s Office and the International Space Station</em></p>
<p>Howard: Hey, Bernie.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hey, how&#8217;s my little astronautie hottie?</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, I guess. It&#8217;s just, being cooped up in this tin can for weeks on end is starting to get to me.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Well, hang in there. You just have a couple more days to go, and then you&#8217;ll be home.</p>
<p>Howard: I know.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I got to get back to work. I love you.</p>
<p>Howard: Love you too. Hey, Bernie? Before you go, can you do something for me?</p>
<p>Bernadette: What do you want me to do?</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, here it is. I really miss gravity. Can you drop something so I can watch it fall?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Really, you&#8217;re serious? Okay. <i>(Drops a pencil)</i></p>
<p>Howard: Oh, baby, you&#8217;re killing me.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. Penny enters, carrying a box.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Ugh. Hey, Sheldon? Hi. This came for you today. It&#8217;s from your mom.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, thank you, Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: M-hmm.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, wait, here. For your troubles.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, boy, a whole dollar. Now, I can quit my paper route. So, what&#8217;s in it?</p>
<p>Leonard: Mmm, doesn&#8217;t matter. Half the time, he just ends up playing with the box.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, it&#8217;s journals and research papers I wrote as a child.</p>
<p>Penny: Aw, how cute. Is this like a diary?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, that&#8217;s my potty training journal.</p>
<p>Penny: Really, your potty training journal?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah. And forgive my crude penmanship. I didn&#8217;t start typing until I was six.</p>
<p>Penny: August 7, 8:42 a.m. This is humiliating. What was wrong with diapers?</p>
<p>Sheldon: There are some charts in the back where I kept track of shape, colour and consistency.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, disgusting.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, what&#8217;s disgusting is he&#8217;s still keeping track.</p>
<p>Penny: Why do you need all this stuff?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, no, no, no.</p>
<p>Penny: Sorry!</p>
<p>Sheldon: I am glad you asked. Are you familiar with the Higgs boson?</p>
<p>Penny: Of course, it is, it&#8217;s been in the news. And it&#8217;s a very famous boson.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Nice try. Now, in 1964, Dr. Peter Higgs, accomplished self-promoter and physicist, he wrote a paper postulating the existence of a subatomic particle called the Higgs boson. Now, initially the paper was rejected, but recently, he was proven right, and now he&#8217;s on the fast track to win a Nobel prize.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, that&#8217;s basically what I said.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, the point is Higgs is being celebrated for work he did 50 years ago, so that got me thinking, perhaps I&#8217;ve already hit upon the idea that will win me my Nobel prize.</p>
<p>Leonard: I didn&#8217;t know they gave Nobel prizes for making boom-boom in the potty.</p>
<p>Penny: You really think there&#8217;s some kind of scientific discovery in here?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, this box only covers my work through nursery school. There&#8217;s a good deal more to come. I didn&#8217;t really hit my academic stride until I cut out that time-suck known as playing outdoors.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, you&#8217;re going to spend hours and hours combing through all this stuff?</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s a good point. My time is much too valuable. You know, perhaps I should find someone to do it for me. You know, someone with a rudimentary understanding of science, but whose real talents lie in menial labour.</p>
<p>Leonard: Not gonna happen.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, if I didn&#8217;t think you could handle it, I wouldn&#8217;t be asking.</p>
<p>Leonard: If you want help, just hire a grad student.</p>
<p>Penny: Maybe I could do it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You, really? You can assess the quality of my work? Okay, um, here. I wrote this when I was five years old.</p>
<p>Penny: A proof that algebraic topology can never have a non self-contradictory set of abelion groups. I&#8217;m just a blonde monkey to you, aren&#8217;t I?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You said it, not me.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: Sheldon&#8217;s office.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, Ms. Jenson. Uh, before we begin, may I offer you a refreshment? Water, coffee, tea, a marijuana cigarette?</p>
<p>Ms Jenson: No, thanks. I&#8217;m fine.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Now, are you sure? Everyone&#8217;s smoking them. I think they&#8217;re the best.</p>
<p>Ms Jenson: I don&#8217;t do drugs.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Excellent, yeah. That was a ruse. They&#8217;re not the best. Physics is the best. And by the way, coffee was also an unacceptable choice. All right, so I see here you&#8217;re from Des Moines, Iowa. Uh, you&#8217;re summa cum laude in theoretical physics from Stanford University. Oh, and two years ago, you had a persistent ear infection. I hope that didn&#8217;t cause any hearing loss.</p>
<p>Ms Jenson: No, of course not. How did you know about that?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I did a comprehensive background check. Medical records, credit reports, criminal history. <i>(Hiding mouth behind book) </i> I trust you paid off those parking tickets.</p>
<p>Ms Jenson: Yes, I did.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hearing unimpaired, good.</p>
<p>Ms Jenson: Look, Dr. Cooper, I really want this position. It would be an incredible honour to work for a man of your brilliance.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Flattery will not get you this job, Ms. Jenson.</p>
<p>Ms Jenson: It&#8217;s not flattery if it&#8217;s the truth.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, well, thank you. Welcome aboard.</p>
<p><em>Scene: A University corridor.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: You talk to Howard lately?</p>
<p>Raj: Uh, yeah, last night. He kept making me drop pencils for him. I got uncomfortable. <i>(Entering Sheldon&#8217;s office) </i> Hey, Sheldon, hope you&#8217;re hungry, they&#8217;re serving macaroni and&#8230; <i>(spots Ms Jenson) </i> che-ee-ee-ese.</p>
<p>Leonard: Smooth. Hi.</p>
<p>Ms Jenson: Hello.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, aren&#8217;t you going to introduce us?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. I have people for that now. You&#8217;re up.</p>
<p>Ms Jenson: I&#8217;m Alex, Dr. Cooper&#8217;s new assistant.</p>
<p>Leonard: Ah, congratulations and may God have mercy on your soul. I&#8217;m Leonard. This is Raj.</p>
<p>Alex: It&#8217;s nice to meet you. I&#8217;m so excited to be working with Dr&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, please reserve chitchat for your breaks, Ms. Jenson. FYI, there will be no breaks.</p>
<p>Alex: I should probably get to work.</p>
<p>Leonard: She seems nice.</p>
<p>Raj: Hey, you already got a girlfriend. I call dibs.</p>
<p>Leonard: All I said was she seems nice.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, well, I love her.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Webcam conversation between Bernadette&#8217;s Office and the International Space Station</em></p>
<p>Bernadette: Hey, I wasn&#8217;t expecting to hear from you today. Everything okay?</p>
<p>Howard: Uh, no, not really.</p>
<p>Bernadette: What&#8217;s wrong?</p>
<p>Howard: Well, remember the Soyuz capsule they were sending to bring us home?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Uh-huh.</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s delayed. We&#8217;re gonna be here at least another week. Maybe ten days. It&#8217;s the Russians, so you don&#8217;t know. They left dogs up here in the sixties.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Come on, Howard. No one&#8217;s leaving you up there.</p>
<p>Howard: I don&#8217;t know how much longer I can take this. I can&#8217;t sleep, and zero gravity is giving me crazy acid reflux. I&#8217;m down to my last three Tums.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You&#8217;re going to be fine. You survived that Weight Watchers cruise with your mom. And they ran out of low-fat ice cream on day two. Just calm down and take a few deep breaths.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, okay. What am I doing? I&#8217;m using up all the oxygen. If I die, promise you&#8217;ll never have sex with another man.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment. Amy is applying very red lip gloss.</em></p>
<p>Amy: Oh, yeah. I&#8217;m a man-eater now.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, for the final touch, this is an eyelash curler. You just place it on your lashes and squeeze it closed.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, I don&#8217;t know. Looks like something used by Tinkerbell&#8217;s gynecologist.</p>
<p>Penny: Who I hope for her sake is not Captain Hook, so&#8230; who are you calling?</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;m going to video-chat Sheldon. If my new look leads to phone sex, I&#8217;m going to have to ask you to leave the room.</p>
<p>Alex: Dr. Cooper&#8217;s office.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, hello. Who are you?</p>
<p>Alex: I&#8217;m Alex, Dr. Cooper&#8217;s assistant. Can I help you?</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;d like to speak to Sheldon.</p>
<p>Alex: I&#8217;m sorry. He&#8217;s asked me to hold all calls unless you&#8217;re Stephen Hawking, his mother or himself from the future.</p>
<p>Amy: All right. Well, tell him Amy called.</p>
<p>Alex: Last name?</p>
<p>Amy: He knows my last name. I&#8217;m his girlfriend. We have a contract and everything. I&#8217;ll send you a PDF.</p>
<p>Alex: All right, I will tell him you called.</p>
<p>Amy: Hmm, that&#8217;s weird.</p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Amy: Sheldon told me he had a new assistant named Alex. He didn&#8217;t mention that Alex was a girl.</p>
<p>Penny: Maybe he didn&#8217;t notice.</p>
<p>Amy: You&#8217;re right. I don&#8217;t have to worry about Sheldon.</p>
<p>Penny: No, you do not.</p>
<p>Amy: It&#8217;s her I have to worry about.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, Amy, really? She is not going to come on to Sheldon.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, really? Look at this face <i>(Sheldon&#8217;s picture on phone)</i> How can any woman spend eight hours a day alone with this face and not fall in love with it?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, for starters, at some point, that face starts talking.</p>
<p><em>Scene: University corridor.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Amy, this is crazy. You have nothing to be suspicious about.</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;m not suspicious. I just want to stop in, say hi to my boyfriend and meet his new assistant. <i>(Throwing open Sheldon&#8217;s office door) </i>Aha!</p>
<p>Penny: Can we go now?</p>
<p>Amy: Hang on. <i>(Rubs Sheldon&#8217;s telephone receiver under her armpit)</i></p>
<p>Penny: What are you doing?</p>
<p>Amy: Isn&#8217;t it obvious? I&#8217;m spreading my scent to mark my territory.</p>
<p>Penny: Come on, Amy. That is not gonna work.</p>
<p>Amy: Really? Because just before you became my best friend, I did this all over your apartment.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The cafeteria.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Has your, uh, assistant said anything about me?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, in fact, she has. Uh, her exact words were, what is that guy&#8217;s problem?</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m in her head. Let the dance begin.</p>
<p>Alex: Here&#8217;s your frozen yoghurt, Dr. Cooper.</p>
<p>Leonard: This should be fun.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Half chocolate, half vanilla, side by side, not swirled?</p>
<p>Alex: Yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Half a teaspoon of sprinkles?</p>
<p>Alex: Rainbow, not chocolate.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Two cherries?</p>
<p>Alex: One on top, one on the bottom.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Stems removed?</p>
<p>Alex: Um, top one, yes. I didn&#8217;t check the one on the bottom.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh!</p>
<p>Alex: I&#8217;m so sorry, Dr. Cooper.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s all right, Alex. I&#8217;m not mad at you, I&#8217;m just disappointed.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wait, Alex. Do you want to join us?</p>
<p>Alex: Um&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: Uh, Alex, a moment. I need a word with Dr. Hofstadter. Do you think it&#8217;s appropriate for her to sit with us, given her station?</p>
<p>Leonard: Given her what?</p>
<p>Sheldon: If I&#8217;ve learned anything from British television shows on PBS, it&#8217;s that servants dine downstairs with their own kind.</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s a kindness, Leonard. Otherwise, you&#8217;re cruelly tempting them with a glimpse of a world that&#8217;s just beyond their soot-stained fingertips.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, sure. Please join us.</p>
<p>Alex: Oh, okay. Thank you.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, Alex, what&#8217;s the topic of your dissertation?</p>
<p>Alex: I&#8217;m looking for Trojan asteroids at Earth&#8217;s L-Five Lagrange point.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, that happens to be Dr. Koothrappali&#8217;s field of expertise. You two have a lot to talk about.</p>
<p>Alex: Is that true? Is he all right?</p>
<p>Leonard: No. But compared to your boss, he&#8217;s the poster boy for sanity.</p>
<p>Alex: It&#8217;s okay, I&#8217;ve been around scientists all my life. My dad&#8217;s an astronomer at SETI.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, SETI, the Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence. You should introduce him to Sheldon. The search is over.</p>
<p>Alex: So, what kind of research are you doing?</p>
<p>Leonard: High-energy lasers.</p>
<p>Alex: Ooh. Military?</p>
<p>Leonard: Not yet, but I can remove unwanted hair from two miles away.</p>
<p>Amy <i>(to Penny, on other side of room)</i>: You were right. I had nothing to worry about. That skank&#8217;s your problem, not mine.</p>
<p>Alex: You&#8217;re very funny, Dr. Hofstadter.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thank you.</p>
<p>Sheldon <i>(choking)</i>: Cherry stem! Cherry stem!</p>
<p><em>Scene: Webcam conversation between Bernadette&#8217;s Office and the International Space Station</em></p>
<p>Bernadette: How you doing, Howie? You feeling a little better?</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, a lot better, thanks. One sec. Listen close, I don&#8217;t have a lot of time. I need you to go to my house. In my bedroom, you&#8217;ll find a model rocket. I want you to take it and bring it back to your place.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Okay.</p>
<p>Howard: Step two, build a version roughly fourteen stories high. Fill it full of rocket fuel and come get me. I&#8217;ll leave the door unlocked.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howie, honey, maybe you should talk to someone, let them know you&#8217;re having a little anxiety.</p>
<p>Howard: No, no, I&#8217;m fine. No anxiety. We should probably talk in code. From now on, frog is me, sandwich means you and lemon means rocket. So, come on, sandwich, build me a lemon &#8217;cause froggy wants to come home.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s car.</em></p>
<p>Amy: Look at you, putting on a brave face.</p>
<p>Penny: There&#8217;s nothing to be brave about. Everything&#8217;s fine.</p>
<p>Amy: Really? I don&#8217;t know how much you know about primate behaviour, but Sheldon&#8217;s assistant was clearly courting Leonard. Were she a mandrill, she would have bent over and displayed her brightly coloured hindquarters like a big red welcome mat. By the way, you try that at the junior prom, you get kicked out.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, maybe she was flirting with him, but who cares? Look, I don&#8217;t even know where my relationship is with Leonard is right now.</p>
<p>Amy: So says your prefrontal cortex. But meanwhile, the limbic system of your brain is calculating that if another woman is attracted to Leonard, it must be because he&#8217;s desirable.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, of course he&#8217;s desirable. I mean, he&#8217;s great. He&#8217;s smart, he&#8217;s sweet, and, ooh, in the bedroom, whew, let me tell you, he really tries.</p>

<p>Penny: Wow. Anything else?</p>

<p>Leonard: Magnets: What Do They Stick To? If the answer is metal, it&#8217;s not exactly groundbreaking.</p>
<p>Sheldon: The original title was &#8220;A Rederivation of Maxwell&#8217;s Equations Regarding Electromagnetism&#8221;&#8221; I dumbed it down because some of the more religious people in town were starting to say I was a witch.</p>
<p>Alex: Sheldon thinks the approach in this paper might change the way we calculate ferromagnetic hysteresis.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, it&#8217;s about time. I hated the old way. Hi. I&#8217;m Penny.</p>
<p>Alex: Alex. Uh, do you work with Dr. Hofstadter?</p>
<p>Penny: In a way. We&#8217;ve kind of been involved in a five-year experiment.</p>
<p>Alex: Oh. Well, you&#8217;re lucky. He seems very talented. And I&#8217;m sure a lot of people want to work with him.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, a lot of people can&#8217;t. Uh, hey.</p>
<p>Leonard: Where are we going?</p>
<p>Penny: My limbic system wants to take your pants off.</p>
<p>Alex: She seems nice.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Notice people on your own time. We&#8217;re working.</p>
