		<h2 class="title">Series 7 Episode 24 &#8211; The Status Quo&nbsp;Combustion</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><i>Scene: The Apartment.</i></p>
<p>Leonard: How&#8217;s your mom holding up?</p>
<p>Howard: She&#8217;s doing okay, but we just lost another nurse.</p>
<p>Amy: How many is that now?</p>
<p>Howard: Two, and I know what you&#8217;re thinking, she&#8217;s eating them.</p>
<p>Bernadette: She&#8217;s just so impossible, they keep quitting.</p>
<p>Sheldon: So, who&#8217;s watching her now?</p>
<p>Howard: A bowl full of M&amp;M&#8217;s with a few Ambien tossed in.</p>
<p>Raj: What up, guys?</p>
<p>All: Hello, hey!</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay. Well, now that everyone&#8217;s here, Penny and I have some big news. We&#8217;re engaged.</p>
<p><i>General cheering and congratulations.</i></p>
<p>Raj: And I thought me having sex with Emily was gonna be the big news.</p>
<p><i>General cheering and congratulations.</i></p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, hey, what the hell?</p>
<p>Bernadette: You guys propose all the time. This never happens.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re right! Yay!</p>
<p><i>Credits sequence.</i></p>
<p><i>Scene: The cafeteria.</i></p>
<p>Raj: Boy, I&#8217;m so hungry today. I wonder why?</p>
<p>Howard: Because you had sex the other night?</p>
<p>Raj: You know what? That may be it. By the way, it isn&#8217;t like riding a bike. Like, I fell off a few times.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, buddy.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hello.</p>
<p>Leonard: You okay?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I just got called into President Siebert&#8217;s office. The university won&#8217;t let me switch my field of study to inflationary cosmology. They&#8217;re forcing me to continue with string theory.</p>
<p>Howard: Why?</p>
<p>Sheldon: He said it&#8217;s why they hired me, it&#8217;s, it&#8217;s what my grant was designated for, and that everybody has to do things they don&#8217;t want to do. He then gave an example of something he had to do, even though he didn&#8217;t want to, which was look at my stupid face.</p>
<p>Leonard: That&#8217;s a rude thing to say, out loud.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s an outrage. Honestly, I&#8217;m tempted to leave the university.</p>
<p>Howard: You know, if you&#8217;re really serious about that, I hear there are some exciting opportunities in home care for the old and fat.</p>
<p>Leonard: Whatever you do, just don&#8217;t make any rash decisions.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t know. I am really aggravated.</p>
<p>Raj: When I&#8217;m feeling low, I have sex with a girl. But that&#8217;s just me.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Mrs Wolowitz&#8217;s house.</i></p>
<p>Howard: Oh, come on, give her a chance.</p>
<p>Nurse: No. Life, it is too short.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I know you&#8217;ve only been here a day and a half, but you&#8217;re like part of the family. I don&#8217;t think the service is gonna send any more people.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, maybe it&#8217;s time we just release Ma back into the sea.</p>
<p>Bernadette: That&#8217;s not helpful.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, then, we may need to get used to the idea that we&#8217;re gonna be living here the next few months.</p>
<p>Bernadette: But we have jobs. We can&#8217;t baby-sit her 24 hours a day.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, what if we use our vacation time?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I wanted to go to Hawaii, not hell.</p>
<p>Howard: I don&#8217;t know what else we can do.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howie, I love you, and as your wife, your mother is every bit as much my problem as she is yours, so, I want a divorce.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Mrs Hofstadter <i>(on webcam)</i>: Hello, Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hi, Mom. I have some exciting news.</p>
<p>Mrs Hofstadter: I&#8217;m listening.</p>
<p>Leonard: Before I tell you, will you promise to try and be happy for me and keep any concerns you have to yourself?</p>
<p>Mrs Hofstadter: No. Let me save you all of your hemming and hawing. Sheldon already told me that you and Penny are engaged.</p>
<p>Leonard: I hate that you talk to him more than you talk to me.</p>
<p>Mrs Hofstadter: Would you like for you and me to talk more?</p>
<p>Leonard: You know what? It&#8217;s probably fine.</p>
<p>Mrs Hofstadter: In any event, while I&#8217;ve had my misgivings about Penny, Sheldon spoke very fondly of her, and if she is good enough for him, then she&#8217;s good enough for me.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m your son. What about the fact that she&#8217;s good enough for me?</p>
<p>Mrs Hofstadter: Sure.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thanks, Mom.</p>
<p>Mrs Hofstadter: Leonard, would it make you feel better to hear that your mother approved of your life choices?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes, it would.</p>
<p>Mrs Hofstadter: Yeah. Well, you should work on that.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment.</i></p>
<p>Penny <i>(on phone)</i>: No, Mom, it&#8217;s the same guy I&#8217;ve been going out with for the past two years. Yeah, the scientist. Well, it&#8217;s complicated. I mean, he works with lasers and atomic magnets. No, I did not see it coming. No, we did not set a date. No, I am not pregnant. Yeah, this is a first for our family. All right, tell Dad I love him. I gotta go. All right, bye.</p>
<p>Amy: Atomic magnets?</p>
<p>Penny: Shut up.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Sorry I&#8217;m late. The leaf blower broke, so I had to hand-dry my mother-in-law.</p>
<p>Penny: You want some wine?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Thanks. Little warning before you jump into this marriage business. You&#8217;re not just marrying him, you&#8217;re marrying his family.</p>
<p>Penny: I think Leonard&#8217;s mom&#8217;s okay with me.</p>
<p>Bernadette: It doesn&#8217;t matter if she&#8217;s okay with you. The question is, can she go to the bathroom by herself? Hit me again.</p>
<p>Amy: So, what are the living arrangements gonna be?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, haven&#8217;t really talked about it yet, but I figure at some point, I&#8217;ll move in with him, or he&#8217;ll move in with me.</p>
<p>Amy: Well, with you not working, that makes financial sense.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You&#8217;re not working. How would you like a job in home health care?</p>
<p>Penny: Not a chance.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Please. I&#8217;m desperate.</p>
<p>Penny: No.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;ll pay you anything you want.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, then, yeah.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Mrs Wolowitz&#8217;s house.</i></p>
<p>Penny: No, keep your money.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I could&#8217;ve ridden a bull longer than that.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment. </i></p>
<p>Leonard: What you working on?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m writing an appeal to the Faculty Senate, so that I can move on from string theory.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh. How&#8217;s it going?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You tell me. Dear Esteemed Colleagues, as you may know, I have requested to change my field of study. My decision to do so is, I believe, in the best interest of science. At your convenience, I&#8217;d be happy to explain it to you in words you&#8217;ll understand.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s nice that you called them esteemed.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You&#8217;re right. I&#8217;ll take that out.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, listen, there was something I was hoping to float past you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Mm.</p>
<p>Leonard: Now that Penny and I are engaged, I thought we might want to talk about our living arrangements.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Of course. She&#8217;s spent many nights here, and you&#8217;re worried about preserving the myth of her virginity before the wedding.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m not.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Good, because not only has that ship sailed, if it hit an iceberg, countless men would perish.</p>
<p>Leonard: Actually, this is about where she and I are going to live.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What do you mean?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, well, we might want to live together.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, yeah, well, I&#8217;ve already given this some thought, and I&#8217;m willing to let Penny live with us one day a week for a trial period. Now, obviously, not when she&#8217;s made cranky by the shedding of her uterine lining.</p>
<p>Leonard: That&#8217;s very sweet. But we were thinking more of us maybe living together with&#8230; not you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t understand. How could we all live together if I&#8217;m not there?</p>
<p>Leonard: Look, I, I know this is, this is change, and that sounds scary.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Where are you going to go?</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t know. We just started to think about this. Maybe I&#8217;ll move in with Penny, or maybe she and I&#8217;ll take this place, and you can move across the hall.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Move across the hall? Did you take a marijuana?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, I did not.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Did you get hit on the head with a coconut?</p>
<p>Leonard: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, then, I&#8217;m all out of guesses. What? Me move across the hall. Why would you even suggest such a thing?</p>
<p>Leonard: Because I love Penny, and want to give her the life she deserves.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I see. You&#8217;re putting your future bride&#8217;s happiness above mine.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, yeah.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wow.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Amy&#8217;s apartment. </i></p>
<p>Sheldon: How dare the university force me to go back to string theory?</p>
<p>Amy: They just don&#8217;t appreciate you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, and on top of that, Leonard has the audacity to suggest that now that he and Penny are engaged, he may not want to live with me any more.</p>
<p>Amy: Here, I made you some Strawberry Quik.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I have real problems here, Amy. I can&#8217;t be mollified with a beverage designed for children. Mmm, yummy.</p>
<p>Amy: You know, this might work out for the best. I mean, you&#8217;re always complaining about what a terrible roommate Leonard is. Like how he turns up the thermostat when you&#8217;re not there.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Ugh, it&#8217;s like walking into the Amazon. And not the good Amazon with one-day shipping. The awful one with birds and snakes.</p>
<p>Amy: You hate the sound of all those keys on his key chain.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Four keys! Who does he think he is, a warden?</p>
<p>Amy: See? Maybe you&#8217;ll love living alone.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t know. Perhaps.</p>
<p>Amy: And if it turns out you don&#8217;t, you and I could live together.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You and&#8230; oh, sure, and while we&#8217;re at it, why don&#8217;t we get engaged, too? Why don&#8217;t we get a little house, start a family? Enjoy our sunset years together? Do you hear yourself, woman?</p>
<p>Amy: Sheldon, it was just a thought.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. Here&#8217;s a thought. You&#8217;re not moving in, Leonard&#8217;s not moving out, everything stays exactly the way it is. And by the way, I saw you make this Strawberry Quik with syrup, you&#8217;re supposed to use the powder.</p>
<p>Amy: It tastes the same.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. The syrup tastes better and I don&#8217;t like it.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment. </i></p>
<p>Penny: This is so sweet. You never cook for me.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, you cook for me all the time and, ugh.</p>
<p>Penny: If you don&#8217;t like my cooking, why haven&#8217;t you ever said anything?</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s hard to talk with so much heavy chewing to do.</p>
<p>Penny: Sorry. I&#8217;ll get better.</p>
<p>Leonard: I know you&#8217;ll try. So, should we talk about setting a date?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, I&#8217;d like to pick one that works with my brother&#8217;s schedule.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay. And when would that be?</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, 12 to 18 months from now, depending on good behaviour.</p>
<p>Amy: Hi, is Sheldon here?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, I thought he was with you.</p>
<p>Amy: He was, but he stormed off and now he isn&#8217;t answering his phone.</p>
<p>Leonard: What happened?</p>
<p>Amy: He was really angry that you suggested he move out.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh.</p>
<p>Amy: I also mentioned that he and I could live together but he was too mad at you to realize what a great idea that is.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, he&#8217;s been having a couple of tough days. I&#8217;m sure he&#8217;s fine. He probably just needs a little alone time to decompress.</p>
<p>Amy: You&#8217;re probably right. So, what are you guys doing?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, Leonard cooked for me and now we&#8217;re just having a nice dinner, you know, as a newly engaged couple.</p>
<p>Amy: That&#8217;s nice.</p>
<p>Leonard: Anyway, as I was saying, Sheldon probably just needs a little alone time. &#8216;Cause that&#8217;s important. Not just for him, but for most anybody, really.</p>
<p>Amy: Don&#8217;t I know it. What is that, polenta?</p>
<p>Penny: Amy, get out.</p>
<p>Amy: Right.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The comic book store.</i></p>
<p>Sheldon: No.</p>
<p>Stuart: Hey, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What happened?</p>
<p>Stuart: I was cooking in the back room last night and the hot plate caught on fire.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And you couldn&#8217;t put it out?</p>
<p>Stuart: I was across the street at the do-it-yourself car wash, taking a shower.</p>
<p>Sheldon: So when will you reopen?</p>
<p>Stuart: Um, I don&#8217;t know. I&#8217;m waiting to hear back from the insurance company.</p>
<p>Sheldon: So, tomorrow?</p>
<p>Stuart: I don&#8217;t mean to be rude, Sheldon, but, uh, my life is kind of falling apart right now.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Your life? The university is making me do string theory and my girlfriend loves me so much she wants to live with me. And now, the place I need to go when I&#8217;m sad is damp and smells funny.</p>
<p>Stuart: Well, sorry I let you down.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. I do not accept this. Everything is changing and I hate it. It stops now. I&#8217;m helping you get back on your feet. I would like to purchase this comic book, please.</p>
<p>Stuart: That&#8217;ll be $2.99.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Really? It&#8217;s soaking wet.</p>
<p>Stuart: Fine, a dollar.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Can you break a twenty?</p>
<p>Stuart: No, I only have hundreds.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You know what? I don&#8217;t always recognize sarcasm, but I do right now, and I don&#8217;t appreciate it. I&#8217;m sorry for your loss. But you&#8217;re not the only one whose day has been a disaster.</p>
<p>Stuart: <i>(Light falls on ground just where he was standing a moment before) </i>That could have killed me. I can&#8217;t catch a break.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment. </i></p>
<p>Leonard: I haven&#8217;t heard from Sheldon in a while. You think he&#8217;s okay?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, I&#8217;m sure he&#8217;s fine.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m gonna see where he is.</p>
<p>Penny: How?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, I know his password, so I can track his phone.</p>
<p>Penny: You do that?</p>
<p>Leonard: Not always, but ever since he wandered off at the swap meet chasing a balloon, I get worried.</p>
<p>Penny: He can take care of himself. Look, we went over stranger danger and gave him that whistle.</p>

<p>Penny: It&#8217;s sweet how you look out for him. You&#8217;re a good guy.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s not just that. My mother would kill me if I let something happen to him.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The comic book store.</i></p>
<p>Raj: Dude, I&#8217;m so sorry.</p>
<p>Howard: Don&#8217;t take this the wrong way, but did you do this for the insurance money?</p>

<p>Howard: Hang on. I know a place you can stay and earn some money at the same time.</p>
<p>Stuart: Great.</p>
<p>Howard: I just have to warn you, it&#8217;ll involve humiliation, degradation and verbal abuse.</p>
<p>Stuart: So what&#8217;s the catch?</p>
<p><i>Scene: The train station.</i></p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You tracked my phone?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Boy, you chase one balloon for three miles.</p>

<p>Sheldon: Don&#8217;t be melodramatic. I&#8217;m just getting on a train and leaving forever.</p>

<p>Sheldon: My plan is to stop at malls and buy what I need. It&#8217;s called living off the land.</p>

<p>Sheldon: No. I&#8217;ve reached my breaking point. I need to leave. Now.</p>
<p>Penny: And go where?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It doesn&#8217;t matter.</p>

<p>Sheldon: I suppose it is. Except I have a credit card. And I refuse to carry my laptop at the end of a stick. And I&#8217;d sooner die than eat beans out of a can.</p>
<p>Leonard: Come on, come home with us and tomorrow I&#8217;ll, I&#8217;ll take you to Legoland.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Legoland is not the solution to everything. And it&#8217;s too much of a scene since that movie came out.</p>
<p>Leonard: Then what can I do?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, I am overwhelmed. Everything is changing and it&#8217;s simply too much. I need to get away and think.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, come on, you know you&#8217;re overreacting.</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, hang on. Just come here. Maybe we need to let him go.</p>
<p>Leonard: What? Why?</p>
<p>Penny: It might be good for him.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know he can&#8217;t take a trip like this by himself.</p>
<p>Penny: He&#8217;s a grown man.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, he looks like a grown man. You&#8217;ve seen Freaky Friday, sometimes little kids end up in big person bodies.</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, we can&#8217;t protect him forever.</p>
<p>Leonard: I know, but&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: He&#8217;ll be okay. You taught him well, Padawan.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Good Lord. Padawan&#8217;s the student, not the teacher.</p>
<p>Penny: Seriously, let him go.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, if you really need to do this, I&#8217;m not gonna stand in your way.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I do.</p>

<p>Penny: Be safe and call us.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I will.</p>

<p>Sheldon: Yes?</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m gonna miss you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Of course you are.</p>
<p>Leonard: He just made that easier.</p>
<p>Penny: Mm.</p>

<p>Bernadette: Yep.</p>

<p>Amy <i>(on phone)</i>: Okay, Sheldon, thanks for checking in. Talk to you tomorrow? Oh, okay, bye.</p>
<p>Leonard: He&#8217;s okay?</p>
<p>Amy: Actually sounds like he&#8217;s doing pretty well.</p>
<p>Penny: I really think this is gonna be for the best.</p>
<p>Leonard: Me, too. And he was able to take a sabbatical from&#8230;</p>
<p>Amy: How could you let him go?</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				} else {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='55px' width='100%' frameBorder='0' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).load( function( e ) {
					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );
					$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loaded' );

					JetpackLikespostMessage( { event: 'loadLikeWidget', name: $iframe.attr( 'name' ), width: $iframe.width() }, window.frames[ 'likes-master' ] );

					if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
				setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 250 );
			}
