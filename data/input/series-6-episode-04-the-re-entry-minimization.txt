			<div class="entrytext">
				<p class="western"><em>Scene: The Apartment.</em></p>
<p class="western">Raj: Howard&#8217;s capsule should be re-entering the atmosphere any minute.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: It&#8217;ll be good to have him back.</p>
<p class="western">Raj: The Fantastic Four reunited.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Yeah, you had a good run, Fake Wolowitz. We&#8217;ll remember you with nostalgic fondness, the way we do the dial-up modem, the VHS tape, or, or Leonard&#8217;s gym membership.</p>
<p class="western">Raj: We&#8217;re not kicking him out. Stuart and I have become good friends.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Okay, one vote for, one vote against. Leonard, you&#8217;re the tiebreaker.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: I don&#8217;t have a problem with Stuart. Besides, he gives us a twenty percent discount at his comic book store.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Well, I don&#8217;t sell my friendship that cheaply.</p>
<p class="western">Stuart: I can go thirty.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Welcome aboard, old chum.</p>
<p class="western">Raj: Okay, the NASA Web site says Howard&#8217;s final descent has begun.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: He left a boy, he returns a boy-sized hero.</p>
<p class="western"><em>Scene: The Soyuz Capsule. Howard is screaming.</em></p>
<p class="western">Dimitri: Loops, calm down!</p>
<p class="western">Howard <i>(singing)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>Baruch atah, Adonai, Eloheinu. Melech haolam, hamotzi. Lechem min haaretz</p>
<p class="western">Mike: What&#8217;s that?</p>
<p class="western">Howard: The Jewish prayer for eating bread! We don&#8217;t have one for falling out of space! We did it! We&#8217;re on the ground! We survived!</p>
<p class="western">Mike: That was just the parachute. We still have another six miles to go. <i>(Howard screams again)</i></p>
<p class="western"><i>Credits sequence.</i></p>
<p class="western"><em>Scene: The Airport Arrivals Hall.</em></p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: Waiting for my husband.</p>
<p class="western">Man in Suit: That&#8217;s nice.</p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: He&#8217;s coming back from outer space.</p>
<p class="western">Man in Suit: I think this flight&#8217;s coming from Houston.</p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: No, I mean, he&#8230; Never mind. There he is! Howie!</p>
<p class="western"><span style="font-style:normal;">Various others </span><i>(all at once)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>Howie! Howie!</p>
<p class="western">Howard: Whoa, thank you. Great to be back on Earth.</p>
<p class="western">Howie Mandel: Uh, I think they&#8217;re here for me, Ringo.</p>
<p class="western">Man in Suit: Mr. Mandel, I&#8217;m your driver.</p>
<p class="western">Howie Mandel: Oh, thanks. That nut job was telling everybody on the plane he&#8217;s an astronaut.</p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: I missed you so much.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: I missed you, too. Where are the guys?</p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: Oh, it&#8217;s just me.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: Oh, I get it. They&#8217;re waiting back home with a big surprise party. Don&#8217;t worry, I can act surprised. <i>(Does)</i></p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: No, I told them they could have you tomorrow night. Tonight, you belong to me. <i>(Sneezes) </i></p>
<p class="western">Howard: Gesundheit. You okay?</p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: I am now that you&#8217;re back. Come on, let&#8217;s go home so I can tear off those little pants.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: Great. Just keep in mind astronauts lose a lot of muscle tone in space, so you might have to do most of the heavy lifting.</p>
<p class="western"><em>Scene: The apartment. </em></p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: You know, I am very disappointed that I won&#8217;t be able to celebrate Howard&#8217;s accomplishment tonight.</p>
<p class="western">Amy: Me, too. But we&#8217;ll see him tomorrow.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Yes, it&#8217;s just that in all the years I&#8217;ve known him, he&#8217;s never had the opportunity to receive my admiration. I was excited to see the look on his face when it finally happened.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: You&#8217;re unbelievable.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: I know.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: All right, Pictionary. What are the teams?</p>
<p class="western">Penny: How about boys versus girls?</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Oh, that hardly seems fair. But I guess any team that I&#8217;m not on has a decided disadvantage.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Once again, unbelievable.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Yeah, once again, I know.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: All right, round one. Here.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Got it.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Okay. Ready, set, go.</p>
<p class="western">Amy: Uh, box? Uh, window?</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: Batman. Batman and Robin. Uh, Wonder Twins plus the monkey. Wonder Twins plus the monkey and Batman.</p>
<p class="western">Amy: Uh, gift? Uh, Present!</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Present! Yeah!</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Oh, Leonard. Now, how can you not get that?</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: In what universe is that a present?</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: It&#8217;s not a present, it&#8217;s the present. Look. There&#8217;s you and me. There&#8217;s Penny and Amy. We&#8217;re playing Pictionary. In the present.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Oh, my God, we&#8217;re gonna kill them.</p>
<p class="western"><em>Later</em></p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: It&#8217;s a quark-gluon plasma.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: No.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: It&#8217;s asymptotically free partons inside a quark-gluon plasma.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: Nothing with quarks.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: It&#8217;s an observational rebuttal of the Lambda-CDM model of the universe.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: No.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: It&#8217;s a chocolate chip cookie.</p>
<p class="western">Amy: Yes.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: How could you miss that?</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Hey, if you want someone to guess chocolate chip cookie, you draw a glass of milk next to it.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: Penny got it.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Yeah, only after I eliminated all the obvious answers. You&#8217;re welcome.</p>
<p class="western"><i>Later</i></p>
<p class="western">Leonard: Oh, uh, uh, uh, sausage. Uh, uh, uh, uh, bratwurst? Oh, oh, a hot dog.</p>
<p class="western">Amy: Penny, aren&#8217;t you gonna draw something?</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Relax, we got time, this is so fun.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: There.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: A solar system. Uh, uh, uh, uh, um, unidentified flying liverwurst? I don&#8217;t&#8230;</p>
<p class="western">Amy: Now?</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Soon.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Come on, Leonard, I am spoon-feeding this to you.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: I don&#8217;t know, uh, Casper the alcoholic ghost?</p>
<p class="western">Penny: All right, that&#8217;s enough. <i>(Draws)</i></p>
<p class="western">Amy: Uh, hand. Uh, nail, polish?</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Yep!</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Wait, no, no. No. The word is Polish. See, look. Polish sausage. And the, the model of the solar system developed by Nicolaus Copernicus, a Polish astronomer. And then, finally, if that wasn&#8217;t enough, which it should have been, this is Madame Curie killing herself by discovering radium, who, although she was a naturalized French citizen, was Polish by birth.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Excuse me, the word is polish. See? Small P.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Ah. So it is. I guess we both share blame on this one.</p>
<p class="western"><em>Scene: Howard and Bernadette&#8217;s Bedroom.</em></p>
<p class="western">Howard: Bernie, you okay?</p>
<p class="western"><span style="font-style:normal;">Bernadette </span><i>(off):</i> Yeah, I&#8217;ll be right there. <i>(Hacking and coughing noises)</i></p>
<p class="western">Howard: Did you get a sea lion while I was gone?</p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: It&#8217;s just allergies.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: Okay.</p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: Took some Benadryl. I&#8217;ll be fine. So, did you miss me?</p>
<p class="western">Howard: Oh. Are you kidding? Every minute of every&#8230;</p>
<p class="western"><span style="font-style:normal;">Bernadette</span><i> (has a coughing fit)</i>: Ew. Excuse me.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: Sweetie, we don&#8217;t have to do this now.</p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: Yes, we do. You left right after we got married. This is like our honeymoon. Now, hold on to your hat. Oh. Uh-oh.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: You okay?</p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: A little dizzy. Must be the Benadryl. Switch places with me.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: Okay.</p>
<p class="western">Bernadette: Now, kiss me. <i>(He does. She starts snoring)</i></p>
<p class="western">Howard: Bernadette? Bernie? Bernie?</p>
<p class="western">Bernadette <i>(waking suddenly)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>That was amazing. You made me feel things I never&#8230; <i>(starts snoring again)</i></p>
<p class="western">Howard: Well, that was quick, and a little gross. Now, I know how she feels.</p>
<p class="western"><em>Scene: The apartment. </em></p>
<p class="western">Penny: Hey, Sheldon, you&#8217;re always full of fun little facts. Where did the expression got your ass handed to you come from?</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Don&#8217;t know.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: I wonder if it&#8217;s from, like, ancient Rome where they&#8217;d actually chop somebody&#8217;s ass off and then go, here. You know, to appease Loseroneous, the god of losers.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: I am not a loser. Pictionary is not a true test of any real intelligence or skill.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: Mmm, in all fairness, Pictionary has verbal skills, visual skills. It&#8217;s a pretty well-rounded game.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Lot of big talk from a man who can&#8217;t draw a chocolate chip cookie.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: All right, fine, pick another game. Amy and I will beat you at anything.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: All right. Let&#8217;s play Physics Fiesta.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Oh, come on, what is that?</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: It&#8217;s a game that I just invented, in which the participants answer physics questions in remedial Spanish. Um, ¿Dónde está el boson de Higgs?</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: En el acelerador de particulares.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Bueno, mi amigo.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: No, no, we&#8217;re not playing some dumb made-up game.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: All games are made up. They&#8217;re not found in nature. You don&#8217;t just dig in the ground, come across a rich vein of Rock&#8217;em Sock&#8217;em Robots.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: Just pick another game.</p>
<p class="western">Amy: Why don&#8217;t we do something athletic like go over to the university pool and play water polo?</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: No good, Sheldon doesn&#8217;t float.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: That is true. I have a higher than normal body density. If I run too deep a bath, I drown.</p>
<p class="western">Amy: All right, let&#8217;s keep it simple. How about darts?</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: No, that&#8217;s not fair either.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Why not?</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Darts is a bar game. You&#8217;ve been frequenting drinking establishments since you were of legal age.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: Yeah, that&#8217;s when it started.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Well, regardless, she has a distinct advantage in all tavern-based competitions. Pool, beer pong, wet T-shirt contests, they&#8217;re all out.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Okay. Just for the record, I have never entered a wet T-shirt contest. I&#8217;ve won a few, but that&#8217;s just because I spill when I&#8217;m drunk, so&#8230;</p>
<p class="western">Amy: We could go to the cadaver lab at UCLA and play real-life Operation.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: No.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: Uh-uh.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: I don&#8217;t think so.</p>
<p class="western">Amy: Are you sure? I mean, the nose doesn&#8217;t light up, but if the corpse is fresh enough, sometimes you can get the leg to jerk.</p>
<p class="western"><em>Scene: Mrs Wolowitz&#8217;s House.</em></p>
<p class="western">Howard: Hey, Ma, twinkle, twinkle, your little star is home. Ma, the chain&#8217;s on the door.</p>
<p class="western">Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>Howard? I thought I wasn&#8217;t going to see you till tomorrow.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: Yeah, well, Bernie&#8217;s not feeling well, so I thought I&#8217;d stop by, tell you about the greatest adventure of my life, see if you can make me feel bad about it.</p>
<p class="western">Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>Uh, uh, okay, hold on, I&#8217;m not decent.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: All right. Woman hasn&#8217;t tied her robe in 20 years. Suddenly she&#8217;s not decent?</p>
<p class="western">Man&#8217;s Voice <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>You want me to hide in the closet or go out the back?</p>
<p class="western">Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>Shh, you need to whisper.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: Ma, who is in there?</p>
<p class="western">Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>Uh, nobody. The TV is on.</p>
<p class="western">Man&#8217;s Voice <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>I only got one sock. Where&#8217;s my other sock?</p>
<p class="western">Howard: Who is that?</p>
<p class="western">Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>I told you, it&#8217;s the TV. Jay Leno lost a sock, it&#8217;s hilarious.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: If you&#8217;re busy, I can come back.</p>
<p class="western">Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>Just give me a second. Go, go, go, go.</p>
<p class="western">Howard <i>(to man climbing out window)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>Dr. Schneider?</p>
<p class="western">Dr Schneider: Oh, hello, Howard.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: What are you doing here?</p>
<p class="western">Dr Schneider: Um, house call.</p>
<p class="western">Howard: You&#8217;re a dentist.</p>
<p class="western">Dr Schneider: Yes, yes, I am. I think he&#8217;s on to us.</p>
<p class="western"><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p class="western">Penny: Okay, glasses off. Find Waldo.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: Hurry up, find him, find him.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: I&#8217;m trying. Don&#8217;t yell at me.</p>
<p class="western">Sheldon: For goodness&#8217; sake, he&#8217;s wearing a hat, glasses, and a red striped shirt.</p>
<p class="western">Leonard: I know what he looks like.</p>
<p class="western">Amy: Oh, there he is, I got him.</p>
<p class="western">Penny: Yes, we win again.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				} else {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='55px' width='100%' frameBorder='0' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).load( function( e ) {
					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );
					$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loaded' );

					JetpackLikespostMessage( { event: 'loadLikeWidget', name: $iframe.attr( 'name' ), width: $iframe.width() }, window.frames[ 'likes-master' ] );

					if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
				setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 250 );
			}
