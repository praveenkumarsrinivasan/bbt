		<h2 class="title">Series 6 Episode 09 &#8211; The Parking Spot&nbsp;Escalation</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The cafeteria.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: They are not.</p>
<p>Raj: They are, too.</p>
<p>Leonard: Your hypothesis is completely disconfirmed by all the data. You&#8217;re just clinging to it out of intellectual stubbornness.</p>
<p>Raj: No, you&#8217;re displaying a shocking ignorance of the subject matter. Mummies and zombies are the exact same thing.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, yeah? Mummies are wrapped in bandages.</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s called a fashion choice.</p>
<p>Leonard: All right, you brought this on yourself. Sheldon, get him.</p>
<p>Sheldon: If a zombie bites you, you turn into a zombie. However, if a mummy bites you, all you turn into is some schmo with a mummy bite. So, like a zombie, that&#8217;s been eaten from the waist down, you, sir, have no leg to stand on.</p>
<p>Leonard: Good boy. Here&#8217;s a cookie.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, thank you.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, fellas, what&#8217;s going on?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, mummies and zombies again.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, they&#8217;re not the same thing.</p>
<p>Leonard: You get a cookie, too.</p>
<p>Howard: Thanks. Guess who picked up his new car this morning?</p>
<p>Raj: Congratulations. Does it have that new car smell?</p>
<p>Howard: Yep. For as long as I can keep my mother out of it. If you want to check it out later, it&#8217;s parked right out front, space 294.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry, 294?</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s my parking spot.</p>
<p>Raj: Why do you have a parking spot? You don&#8217;t have a car. You don&#8217;t even drive.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It doesn&#8217;t matter. That&#8217;s my spot.</p>
<p>Leonard: Maybe they reassigned it because you never use it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, I&#8217;m not using my nipples, either. Maybe they should reassign those.</p>
<p>Howard: Sheldon, someday, if you get a car, I&#8217;m sure they&#8217;ll give you another parking space.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t want another parking space. I want my parking space. It&#8217;s perfect. It&#8217;s a corner spot, cutting the risk of door-dings in half. It&#8217;s a mere 28 steps from the building entrance. The nearby tree provides shade on hot days and is also home to a delightful squirrel. Which is fortuitous because most squirrels are real jerks.</p>
<p>Howard: Fun story. Meanwhile, you still don&#8217;t have a car.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Don&#8217;t try to change the subject. This is about a parking space. It has nothing to do with cars.</p>
<p>Howard: Are you listening to yourself?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I always listen to myself. It&#8217;s one of the great joys of my life. Now, get your car out of my spot.</p>
<p>Howard: Nope.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Very well. You leave me no choice.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The parking spot. Sheldon is trying to push Howard&#8217;s car out of the spot.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: What are you looking at, you stupid squirrel?</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon <i>(on phone):</i> President Siebert, listen to reason. Yeah, I understand I don&#8217;t use the parking spot, but that&#8217;s not the point. I&#8230; Yes, I&#8217;m aware you told me not to call you at home, but you didn&#8217;t answer the door. And I know you were there, because I saw you through the mail slot. Yeah, well, that&#8217;s some salty language. May I remind you that you&#8217;re the president of a major university, not the president of the Potty Mouth Club. There it is again. Do you kiss your mother with that mouth? Oh, well, I&#8217;m sorry for your loss. Good night, sir. Unbelievable. He says Wolowitz deserves my spot because he&#8217;s a high-profile asset to the university.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, he&#8217;s not wrong. Howard did go to the International Space Station.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, that was five weeks ago. How much longer is he gonna milk that cow?</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, let it go. It&#8217;s not a big deal.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, no, this is a slippery slope, Leonard. It starts with a parking space, where does it end? It&#8217;s like my dad always said, first they say you can&#8217;t drink and drive, next thing you know, you can&#8217;t let your 10-year-old take the wheel while you sleep one off in the backseat.</p>
<p>Leonard: All that story does is make me feel bad for your mother.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, you&#8217;re my best friend. Why don&#8217;t you ever take my side?</p>
<p>Leonard: Because I can never understand your side.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Sheldon&#8217;s office.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Give it back.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry, but could you be more specific?</p>
<p>Howard: My Iron Man helmet. Koothrappali saw you take it. Give it back.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, that. Well, see, I wanted it, and you weren&#8217;t using it. Apparently, those are the rules we live by now. Payback, it truly is the B word, isn&#8217;t it?</p>
<p>Howard: Sheldon, that is a five hundred dollar limited edition collectible, and I want it back.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;d love to help you out, but unfortunately <i>(puts helmet on)</i>, I&#8217;m using it.</p>
<p>Howard: Fine. I&#8217;m taking your diploma.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Go ahead. That&#8217;s the only doctorate you&#8217;ll ever get. It smells funny in here.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment stairwell.</em></p>
<p>Bernadette: We&#8217;re so proud of you, Amy. Your first bikini wax.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah. So, how you doing?</p>
<p>Amy: A little sensitive, but not bad. Does it always take that long?</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, no, they usually don&#8217;t have to go out and get more wax.</p>
<p>Amy: I feel like I&#8217;m five pounds lighter.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Really? Only five?</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, anybody want a drink?</p>
<p>Amy: Sure.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Okay. So, did you spend last night hearing about this silly parking space fight, too?</p>
<p>Amy: For hours. Fortunately, I couldn&#8217;t understand most of it &#8217;cause Sheldon was wearing that stupid robot mask.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howard was so angry I had to mash up Benadryl in his ice cream to get him to fall asleep.</p>
<p>Amy: I guess this is what we get for being with two testosterone-fueled alpha males. At some point, they&#8217;re bound to lock horns.</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m assuming these are some kind of horns they bought at Comic-Con?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;m really sorry they took Sheldon&#8217;s spot away. He shouldn&#8217;t have to suffer just because Howard&#8217;s such a big deal now.</p>
<p>Amy: I know, Sheldon should just let Howard have his little moment in the sun.</p>
<p>Bernadette: What&#8217;s that supposed to mean?</p>
<p>Amy: Well, I mean, Howard&#8217;s never gonna go to space again, but Sheldon will always be a genius.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You&#8217;re right. And I&#8217;m sure Sheldon will get a fancy parking spot again, if and when he makes a worthwhile contribution to science.</p>
<p>Amy: If and when?</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, maybe we should change the subject. Um, Amy, how are your lady parts? Still chilly down there?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hang on. None of Sheldon&#8217;s theories have ever been definitively proven. My husband actually went to outer space.</p>
<p>Amy: That&#8217;s an impressive accomplishment. He&#8217;s now an inspiration to millions of Americans who know you don&#8217;t have to be special or even qualified to go into space.</p>
<p>Penny: You know, I remember the first time I got a bikini wax. My sister did it with melted Crayolas and duct tape. It&#8217;s a bad idea.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Gosh, Amy. I&#8217;m sensing a little hostility. Is it maybe because, like Sheldon&#8217;s work, your sex life is also theoretical?</p>
<p>Penny: Damn.</p>
<p>Amy: Yeah, well, at least when we do make love, Sheldon won&#8217;t be thinking about his mother. And yes, that is a cleverly veiled reference to Howard&#8217;s lifelong obsession to crawl back into her spacious womb.</p>
<p>Penny: Anyway, to this day, I still can&#8217;t see a box of crayons without crossing my legs.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I don&#8217;t have to take this. I&#8217;m gonna go home and have sex with my husband right now. Maybe I&#8217;ll let him do it to me in the parking spot. Which sounds dirty, but I didn&#8217;t mean it that way,</p>
<p><em>Scene: The stairwell.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Okay, here&#8217;s another one: If a zombie bites a vampire, and the vampire bites a human, does the human become a vampire or a zombie? Or a zompire?</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Cookie.</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t have any.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, I&#8217;m not giving it away. <i>(Enters apartment. Howard is sitting naked in his spot with a laptop on his lap)</i></p>
<p>Howard: Hi Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: He&#8217;s in my spot. Leonard, make him stop being naked in my spot.</p>
<p>Leonard: Howard, what are you doing?</p>
<p>Howard: He wasn&#8217;t using it. And I needed a nice cool piece of leather to wiggle my naked ass on.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Get off there.</p>
<p>Howard: Give me back my Iron Man helmet.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Give me back my parking space.</p>
<p>Howard: You don&#8217;t need a parking space. You don&#8217;t have a car.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You don&#8217;t need an Iron Man helmet. You&#8217;re not Iron Man.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, we appear to have reached an impasse. And you know, I have to say, I thought you&#8217;d be more upset that your laptop is sitting on my junk.</p>
<p>Raj: I didn&#8217;t pick up on that. That&#8217;s a nice touch.</p>
<p>Leonard: Mmm.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The parking spot. Sheldon is spraypainting out Howard&#8217;s name and replacing it with his own.</em></p>
<p>Amy: This is so exciting. I&#8217;m feeling all tingly. Although that could just be my newly defoliated bikini zone.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Keep a lookout. This place is swarming with campus security. They will not hesitate to scold us.</p>
<p>Amy: Freaking pigs.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, yeah. Okay, now, pull your car into the spot and let&#8217;s get out of here.</p>
<p>Amy: Wait, I&#8217;m leaving my car here?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, and be sure and put on the emergency brake. Really makes these things tough to budge.</p>
<p>Amy: Before I park, come in the back seat. I want to show you something I had done today.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, colour me intrigued.</p>
<p>Amy: What do you think?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I think you&#8217;re high on paint fumes. And boy, that&#8217;s a lot of Band-Aids.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Howard and Bernadette&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Okay, Howard&#8217;s mother is in, like, every one of your wedding photos.</p>
<p>Bernadette: What can I tell ya? She&#8217;s a big girl. Wherever you look, there she is. More coffee?</p>
<p>Penny: No, Leonard&#8217;s taking me to a physics lecture, and coffee&#8217;ll just keep me awake. <i>(Knock on door) </i>Oh, I&#8217;ll get it.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, looks like someone&#8217;s on Team Bernadette. Where&#8217;s Howard?</p>
<p>Bernadette: He&#8217;s not here. What&#8217;s wrong?</p>
<p>Amy: He had my car towed. It cost me two hundred dollars to get it back.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, no. Where was it parked?</p>
<p>Amy: In Sheldon&#8217;s spot.</p>
<p>Bernadette: That doesn&#8217;t make sense. Sheldon doesn&#8217;t have a spot. Was it maybe in Howard&#8217;s spot?</p>
<p>Amy: Don&#8217;t play dumb with me, sister. You tell your husband he owes me two hundred dollars.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Well, that doesn&#8217;t make sense, either.</p>
<p>Amy: Why not?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Because I&#8217;m the one who had it towed.</p>
<p>Amy: You?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Didn&#8217;t see that one coming, did ya?</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, yeah? Well, you&#8217;re not gonna see this coming. <i>(Swings handbag. Bernadette ducks. Penny is hit in the face)</i></p>
<p>Penny: Ow! Ow!</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, my God,</p>
<p>Bernadette: Are you okay?!</p>
<p>Penny: You idiot, what the hell do you have in there?!</p>
<p>Amy: Just my wallet, keys and a coffee can full of change I&#8217;ve been meaning to take to the bank.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Don&#8217;t move. I&#8217;ll get some ice.</p>
<p>Amy: Are you okay?</p>
<p>Penny: Get away from me or I swear to God I will rip out what&#8217;s left of your pubes!</p>
<p>Bernadette: Here.</p>
<p>Penny: Thanks. Ah!</p>
<p>Bernadette: Amy, you think maybe this has gotten a little out of hand?</p>
<p>Penny: Gee, you think?</p>
<p>Amy: I do. Penny, Bernadette and I are sorry.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You hit her! What did I do?</p>
<p>Amy: You had my car towed.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You were parked in Howard&#8217;s spot.</p>
<p>Amy: I was parked in Sheldon&#8217;s spot.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Sheldon doesn&#8217;t have a spot.</p>
<p>Penny: Guys, I think I need to go to the emergency room.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Okay, let&#8217;s go.</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;ll drive. You can see where the tow truck scratched my car.</p>
<p>Bernadette: The tow truck didn&#8217;t scratch your car.</p>
<p>Amy: How do you know?</p>
<p>Bernadette: &#8216;Cause I did it!</p>
<p><em>Scene: The parking spot. Sheldon has set up his office in it.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Morning, Professor Stevens. Don&#8217;t look at that whiteboard. That&#8217;s my math, not your math. Keep walking, nosey.</p>
<p>Howard: What the hell are you doing?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh. You said I&#8217;m not using my space, so I&#8217;m using it.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, you need to move now.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, I don&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Howard: You can&#8217;t stay there forever.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Actually, I have a plastic baggy strapped to my leg that says I can. Give up, Wolowitz. You&#8217;ve chosen to tangle with a superior intellect you can&#8217;t defeat. There is nothing you could possibly do to&#8230; <i>(Howard starts sounding his horn. Sheldon puts on earphones)</i></p>
<p>Howard: Those aren&#8217;t gonna help you, Sheldon,</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, yes, they are. I mean, what?</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m warning you, Sheldon!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Your threats are empty, nothing can move me. <i>(Howard starts pushing his chair with the front of the car) </i>Stop that.</p>
<p>Howard: Get out of my spot.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. That&#8217;s it. I am calling campus security. You prepare for the scolding of your life.</p>
<p>Leonard: What are you idiots doing?</p>
<p>Sheldon: He&#8217;s trying to kill me, Leonard. Video games and rock music have desensitized him to violence.</p>
<p>Howard: Would you please talk some sense into your lunatic roommate?</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re both acting like lunatics. It&#8217;s just a parking spot.</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s not just a parking spot. He can&#8217;t handle the fact that I&#8217;m a bigger deal than he is now.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, preposterous. I have been solely responsible for this university&#8217;s six loop quantum gravity calculations, I have changed the way we think about Bose-Einstein condensates, and I am also the one who got Nutter Butters in the cafeteria vending machine. 						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;

						return;

					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
