		<h2 class="title">Series 3 Episode 22 &#8211; The Staircase&nbsp;Implementation</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: Penny’s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Okay, Babydoll Pink, let&#8217;s see if you can cover up the fact that I got my dad&#8217;s feet.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(voice off)</em>: It&#8217;s just two degrees, Sheldon. I just want to turn up the thermostat two degrees!</p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(voice off)</em>: Let me point out that two degrees can be the difference between water and steam.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(voice off)</em>: Yes, if we lived in a teakettle.</p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(voice off)</em>: This is the temperature you agreed to in the roommate agreement.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(voice off)</em>: Aw, screw the roommate agreement!</p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(voice off)</em>: No, you don&#8217;t screw the roommate agreement. The roommate agreement screws you.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(voice off)</em>: You know what, go to Hell and set their thermostat.</p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(voice off)</em>: I don&#8217;t have to go to Hell. At 73 degrees, I&#8217;m there already!</p>
<p>Penny <em>(after a knock on the door)</em>: Who is it?</p>
<p>Leonard: Leonard.</p>
<p>Penny: Hang on.</p>
<p>Leonard: Can I sleep on your couch tonight?</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, well, you can try, but the people across the hall are being very noisy.</p>
<p>Leonard: You heard that, huh?</p>
<p>Penny: Apparently, the one fella tried to adjust the thermostat, then the other fella went bat-crap crazy.</p>
<p>Leonard: So you agree, he&#8217;s nuts.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, not as nuts as the guy who chooses to live with him.</p>
<p>Leonard: Believe it or not, he was worse when I met him.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, I do not believe that.</p>
<p>Leonard: You are so naive. Just like I was seven years ago. <em>(Flasback: The lobby) </em>I&#8217;d just started at the university.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Excuse me, I&#8217;m looking for Sheldon Cooper&#8217;s apartment.</p>
<p>Man with a box: Oh, I bet you&#8217;re here to check out the room for rent.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Yeah.</p>
<p>Man: Run away, dude.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Man: Run fast, run far. <em>(End of flashback.)</em></p>
<p>Leonard: That should have been my first clue.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Moments later.</em></p>
<p>Penny: So Sheldon&#8217;s last roommate tried to warn you off?</p>
<p>Leonard: For all I knew, he was the crazy one. He had this really deranged look.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, yeah, he&#8217;d been living with Sheldon.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sure, it makes sense now. <em>(Flashback. Past Leonard is getting out of the lift). </em>Anyway, I went upstairs and knocked on the door.</p>
<p>Large Black Transvestite: Yeah?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Dr. Cooper?</p>
<p>Transvestite: No, you want the crazy guy across the hall.</p>
<p>Leonard: In retrospect, that was clue number two.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Yes?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: I&#8217;m Leonard Hofstadter. I called you about the apartment. You said&#8230;</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: I know what I said. I know what you said. I know what my mother said on March 5, 1992. What is the sixth noble gas?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: You said you&#8217;re a scientist. What is the sixth noble gas?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Uh, radon?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Are you asking me or telling me?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Telling you? Telling you.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: All right, next question. Kirk or Picard?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Oh, uh, well, that&#8217;s tricky. Um, well, uh, Original Series over Next Generation, but Picard over Kirk.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Correct. You&#8217;ve passed the first barrier to roommate-hood. You may enter.</p>
<p>Past Leonard <em>(Enters apartment. It is bare except for two lawn chairs, a television and some whiteboards)</em>: Oh, this is pretty nice. Uh, the bedrooms are back there?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: That depends.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: I don&#8217;t understand, their, their existence is conditional?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: No, but your ability to perceive their existence is conditional on you passing the second and third barriers.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: There&#8217;s three?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Each more daunting than the last. Have a seat.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Okay.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: No! That&#8217;s where I sit!</p>
<p>Past Leonard: What&#8217;s the difference?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: This seat is ideally located both in relation to the heat source in the winter and a cross breeze in the summer. It also faces the television at a direct angle allowing me to immerse myself in entertainment or game play without being subjected to conversation. As a result, I&#8217;ve placed it in a state of eternal dibs.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Can you do that?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Cathedra mea, regulae meae. That&#8217;s Latin for my chair, my rules. Now, you said on the phone that your area of study is physics.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Uh, yeah, experimental physics.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Hmm.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: What is that?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Doesn&#8217;t concern you. You&#8217;ll be going to the university every day?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Yes.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: And you have a vehicle?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: A car, yeah.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: And you&#8217;ll be willing to drive me?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Well, can&#8217;t you drive?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: I can. I choose not to.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Okay, I suppose I could drive you. Well that&#8217;s a point in my favour, right?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Why don&#8217;t you let me do this.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Come on, I just asked.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Last question. In a post-apocalyptic world, which task would you assign the highest priority? Locating a sustainable food source, re-establishing a functioning government, procreating, or preserving the knowledge of mankind?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Uh, I&#8217;m gonna go with preserving the knowledge.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: That&#8217;s correct. FYI, I would have accepted any answer other than procreating. Come, I&#8217;ll show you the rest of the apartment.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Oh, good. I passed the barriers.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: The second barrier. Don&#8217;t get cocky. This is the bathroom. Are you fairly regular?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Uh, I guess.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: This isn&#8217;t going to work if you&#8217;re guessing. When do you evacuate your bowels?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: When I have to.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: When you have to? I&#8217;m sorry, I don&#8217;t rent to hippies.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: I, I&#8217;m sorry, uh, in the morning. Around eight.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: I can&#8217;t give you eight. I can give you seven thirty.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Fine. I&#8217;ll take it.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Third barrier passed. You have won the right to see your room. Huzzah!</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Is this it?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: No, this is my room. People don&#8217;t go in my room.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: So where do you sleep?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: I don&#8217;t understand.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: If people don&#8217;t go in there, and you&#8217;re people, and&#8230; You are people, aren&#8217;t you? Making a joke.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Do you do this often?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: On occasion.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Your room. You may want to repaint. <em>(End of Flashback)</em></p>
<p>Penny: Okay, and after all that, you just moved in?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, I didn&#8217;t just move in. First we had to iron out a few details. <em>(Flashback: The apartment)</em></p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Roommates agree that Friday nights shall be reserved for watching Joss Whedon&#8217;s brilliant new series Firefly.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Does that really need to be in the agreement?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: We might as well settle it now, it&#8217;s going to be on for years. Initial here. All right, that&#8217;s television and movies. Section nine, miscellany. The apartment&#8217;s flag is a gold lion rampant on a field of azure.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: We have a flag?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Never fly it upside down unless the apartment&#8217;s in distress. And next, if either of us ever invents time travel, we agree our first stop will be this meeting today in precisely five seconds.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Okay.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Well that&#8217;s disappointing. <em>(End of flashback.)</em></p>
<p>Penny: Why on earth did you agree to all that?</p>
<p>Leonard: It was the best apartment I&#8217;d seen, the rent was very reasonable and after you&#8217;ve passed the first three barriers you kind of want to take it all the way.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, I&#8217;m sorry, Leonard, it&#8217;s very hard to feel sympathy for you.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, how about this? Let me tell you about the first time I brought a girl over. <em>(Flashback to Leonard’s bedroom.)</em></p>
<p>Past Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Leonard?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Shh. Just pretend we&#8217;re not here.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Leonard?Leonard?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: I&#8217;m sure he&#8217;ll go away.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: I&#8217;m just going to keep knocking till you answer. <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Leonard? <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Leonard? <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Leonard?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: What do you want? I didn&#8217;t say come in!</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: You asked what I wanted. I wanted to come in. I&#8217;m here because you violated our roommate agreement. Specifically, section eight, visitors, subsection C, females, paragraph four, coitus. Roommates shall give each other 12 hours notice of impending coitus.&#8221;</p>
<p>Past Leonard: I didn&#8217;t even know her 12 hours ago.</p>
<p>Joyce Kim: That&#8217;s it! I&#8217;m out of here!</p>
<p>Past Leonard: But, Joyce, come on.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: 12 hours? <em>(End of flashback.)</em></p>
<p>Penny: Oh, my God.</p>
<p>Leonard: Do I get some sympathy now?</p>
<p>Penny: A little bit. Okay. Let me get this straight. You move in with this guy, he makes you sign a ridiculous roommate agreement, then he walks into your bedroom while you&#8217;re doing this Joyce Kim, and you still stay?</p>
<p>Leonard: Actually, I couldn&#8217;t get too mad at him about Joyce Kim.</p>
<p>Penny: Why not?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, I was doing some government research at the time, you know, military, rocket fuel. It&#8217;s kind of secret.</p>
<p>Penny: What does that have to do with Joyce Kim?</p>
<p>Leonard: As it turns out, she was a North Korean spy. Luckily, Sheldon drove her out before I revealed anything important. Which I&#8217;m not saying I would have.</p>
<p>Penny: So, what, that&#8217;s it? You&#8217;ve stayed with Sheldon all this time because he kept you from going to federal prison?</p>
<p>Leonard: That was part of it. The other part is what happened with the elevator.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, yeah, I&#8217;m wondering about that. You said it was working when you moved in.</p>
<p>Leonard: It was, but one night, Sheldon came home from work&#8230; <em>(Flashback, the apartment. The settee has now appeared. Leonard, Howard and Raj are playing a video game.)</em></p>
<p>Past Sheldon: What is going on here?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Hey, Sheldon. This is Howard and Raj. They work at the university, too.</p>
<p>Past Howard: Hey.</p>
<p>Past Raj: Hey.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: I&#8217;ll get to you later. What are you sitting on?</p>
<p>Past Howard: I can&#8217;t speak for these guys, but I&#8217;m sitting on my tushie. It&#8217;s a joke.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Yeah, not a good idea.</p>
<p>Past Raj: Tushie is buttocks, right?</p>
<p>Past Howard: Right.</p>
<p>Past Raj: Hilarious.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Explain the couch.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Oh, well, there were some people on the first floor moving out, and they sold it to me for a hundred dollars. Howard and Raj helped me bring it up.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: But what&#8217;s wrong with the furniture we have?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: They&#8217;re lawn chairs. And there was no place for company.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Did it occur to you that was by design?</p>
<p>Past Leonard: According to the roommate agreement, I&#8217;m entitled to allocate 50% of the cubic footage of the common areas.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: But you didn&#8217;t notify me by e-mail, so this is still a breach.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: I did notify you.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Oh, you did, did you? Oh, drat! Hoisted by my own spam filter.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: What am I doing in your spam folder?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: I put you there after you forwarded me a picture of a cat playing the piano, entitled this is funny.</p>
<p>Past Raj: Oh, yeah, I saw that. That was hilarious. <em>(End of flashback.)</em></p>
<p>Penny: Okay, what does all this have to do with the elevator?</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m getting to it. <em>(Back to flashback.)</em></p>
<p>Past Sheldon: I assure you, you&#8217;ll be sorry you wasted your money on an iPod when Microsoft comes out with theirs.</p>
<p>Past Raj: Okay, do you have an opinion about everything?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Yes.</p>
<p>Past Howard: And you just assume you&#8217;re always right?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: It&#8217;s not an assumption. Change seats with me.</p>
<p>Past Raj: Why?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: I don&#8217;t like this spot. I have to keep turning my head.</p>
<p>Past Raj: Fine.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Ooh, it&#8217;s time for Babylon 5!</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: We don&#8217;t watch Babylon 5 in this apartment.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: Why not?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Because no one likes Babylon 5.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: I like it.</p>
<p>Past Raj: Me, too.</p>
<p>Past Howard: So do I.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: There you go&#8211; three against one.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: They don&#8217;t get a vote. It&#8217;s one against one. And according to the roommate agreement, all ties will be settled by me.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: But I said no to that.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: And I said yes. And I settle all ties. Change seats with me.</p>
<p>Past Howard: Why?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: There&#8217;s a draft on my neck over here.</p>
<p>Past Howard: So, I get the draft?</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: You&#8217;re protected by your turtleneck.</p>
<p>Past Howard: Fine. And it&#8217;s a dickey.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Hmm, I&#8217;m still not comfortable. Of course. There&#8217;s too many people here.</p>
<p>Past Leonard: We can fix that. Let&#8217;s leave.</p>
<p>Past Howard: Yeah, we can go over to my place.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Wait. Let me get my jacket.</p>
<p>Past Howard: You&#8217;re not going with us.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Why not?</p>
<p>Past Raj: You&#8217;re the guy we&#8217;re trying to get away from.</p>
<p>Past Sheldon: Oh. Well, in that case, I don&#8217;t need my jacket. And for the record, the correct syntax is I&#8217;m the guy from whom you&#8217;re trying to get away. Oh, yes, this is definitely going to be my spot. <em>(End of flashback.)</em></p>
<p>Penny: Okay, how do you know he said that? You left the room.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, do you want me to finish working on your man feet or not?</p>
<p>Penny: Fine. Go ahead. <em>(Flashback to Howard’s bedroom.)</em></p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz: Howard, are you having a playdate?</p>
<p>Past Howard: I don&#8217;t have playdates! I have colleagues!</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz: Do their parents know they&#8217;re here?</p>

					return;

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
