<p>Sheldon: There is ominous music playing, and there is an afghan over my head. I don&#8217;t know where you&#8217;re from, but where I&#8217;m from, that means I&#8217;m not doing okay.</p>
<p>Leonard: Want me to make you some tea?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Tea is for when I&#8217;m upset. I&#8217;m not upset. The university&#8217;s forcing me to work with Kripke. I&#8217;m outraged.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, cocoa?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, cocoa. Do you have any idea what it&#8217;s like to be paired with someone who&#8217;s so incredibly annoying?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, teacher! Me! Me!</p>
<p>Sheldon: See, I did all this great work, and now he&#8217;s just going to come along and ruin it. I am angrier than ever and filled with despair.</p>
<p>Penny: What beverage do you make for that?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, no, no, no, I know this. Uh, hot apple cider with cinnamon sticks?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, hot apple cider with cinnamon sticks!</p>

<p>Kripke: If this one&#8217;s bwank, too, I&#8217;m going to be fuwious.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Fine.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Howard&#8217;s lab.</em></p>
<p>Raj: You! Always bet on black.</p>
<p>Howard: Get that waste of money out of my face.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s only a waste of money if we don&#8217;t play with them. <i>(As doll) </i>He&#8217;s right, dawg.</p>
<p>Howard: Please, I&#8217;m working.</p>
<p>Raj: You know, there is a way we can get action figures to look exactly like us.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, yeah? How&#8217;s that?</p>
<p>Raj: Two words, 3-D printer. Wait, maybe it&#8217;s three words. No, hang on. Okay, one word, a letter and a number and maybe a hyphen. 3-D printer.</p>
<p>Howard: I have always wanted a 3-D printer.</p>
<p>Raj: Of course you have. They&#8217;re an engineer&#8217;s dream. Anything you can design, a 3-D printer can make out of plastic.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, but they&#8217;re so expensive.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, come on. You deserve one. You&#8217;ve worked hard to find a woman who makes a lot of money.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, the prices have been coming down.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, true. They&#8217;re practically giving them away. You know, in exchange for money.</p>
<p>Howard: And we can make stuff we need for work with it. Prototypes of my CAD/CAM designs, specialized tools&#8230;</p>

<p>Sheldon: I feel like I&#8217;m being strangled by a boa constrictor. Why&#8217;d you stop?</p>

<p>Leonard: Sheldon, Kripke&#8217;s not smarter than you. You just got stuck on a wrong path. Happened to Einstein. He got stuck on the unified field theory for decades.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, don&#8217;t play the Einstein card. His great breakthroughs happened when no one knew anything. So everything was a great breakthrough.</p>
