		<h2 class="title">Series 3 Episode 20 &#8211; The Spaghetti&nbsp;Catalyst</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The lobby.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Oh, damn, they cancelled my Visa. Oh, yay, a new MasterCard!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Uh-oh.</p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I was going to get my mail.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay. Are, are you hoping to get it telepathically?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I think you mean telekinetically. And no, I just wasn&#8217;t sure of the proper protocol now that you and Leonard are no longer having coitus.</p>
<p>Penny: God, can we please just say no longer seeing each other?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, we could if it were true. But as you live in the same building, you see each other all the time. The variable which has changed is the coitus.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, here&#8217;s the protocol, you and I are still friends, and you stop saying coitus.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Good, good. I&#8217;m glad we&#8217;re still friends.</p>
<p>Penny: Really?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, yes. It was a lot of work to accommodate you in my life. I&#8217;d hate for that effort to have been in vain.</p>
<p>Penny: Right.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Just to be clear do I have to stop saying coitus with everyone or just you?</p>
<p>Penny: Everyone.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Harsh terms. But all right, I&#8217;ll just substitute intercourse.</p>
<p>Penny: Great.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Or fornication. Yeah. But that has judgmental overtones, so I&#8217;ll hold that in reserve.</p>
<p>Penny: So, how you been?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, my existence is a continuum, so I&#8217;ve been what I am at each point in the implied time period.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re just coitusing with me, aren&#8217;t you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Bazinga.</p>
<p>Penny: Mmm. How&#8217;s Leonard doing?</p>
<p>Sheldon: He seems all right. Although he does spend a disturbing amount of time looking at photographs of you and smelling the pillow you slept on. Oh, but now that I think of it, he asked me not to tell you that.</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;ll pretend I didn&#8217;t hear it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;d rather you pretend I didn&#8217;t say it. I see you bought Mama Italia marinara spaghetti sauce.</p>
<p>Penny: Yep.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s the sauce my mother uses. She likes cooking Italian because according to her, that&#8217;s what the Romans made Jesus eat.</p>
<p>Penny: Interesting. I&#8217;ll have to have you over for spaghetti some night.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m hungry now.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh. Um, okay. Why don&#8217;t you give me an hour and come over?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Will you cut up hot dogs into little chunks and mix them in with the sauce?</p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t have hot dogs.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, it&#8217;s all right, I do. Oh! You&#8217;re in for what my mother calls a real Eye-talian treat. <em>(Enters apartment).</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, where you been?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I was talking with Penny.</p>
<p>Howard: What&#8217;s wrong with you? You can&#8217;t hang out with your roommate&#8217;s ex. That&#8217;s totally uncool.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, no, it&#8217;s fine. I don&#8217;t care. I&#8217;m over it.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, he&#8217;s over it, that&#8217;s why he&#8217;s been whining all day about trying to invent that memory-wiper gizmo from Men in Black.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Is he making any progress? Because I&#8217;d like to erase Ben Affleck as Daredevil.</p>
<p>Howard: So would Ben Affleck. The point is, in a situation like this you got to pick sides. You&#8217;re either on Team Leonard or Team Penny.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Which one picks last?</p>
<p>Howard: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, usually I&#8217;m on the team that picks last. Unless there&#8217;s a kid in a wheelchair.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, I got you your tangerine chicken. I hope you&#8217;re hungry.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, of course I&#8217;m hungry. And as I have no plans to eat with any other team, I will consume my tangerine chicken with the appropriate gusto. Mm, mm, mm!</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Just out of curiosity, do we still have hot dogs?</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t know. Why?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Just making dinner conversation. Go, Team Leonard!</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>Scene: A few moments later.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Oh, God, this is good.</p>
<p>Raj: Let me ask you a question. Do you believe you&#8217;re going to go to hell for eating sweet and sour pork?</p>
<p>Howard: Jews don&#8217;t have hell. We have acid reflux.</p>
<p>Leonard: Do you want the last dumpling, Sheldon?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Certainly. It&#8217;s not like I have to moderate my food intake because I&#8217;m planning on eating again very shortly. Mm, mm, mm!</p>
<p>Leonard: So, you guys want to do something tonight?</p>
<p>Howard: Nah, I can&#8217;t. I got to pick up my mom from her water aerobics class. 18 overweight women flapping their arm fat in a swimming pool. Looks like the manatee tank at Sea World.</p>
<p>Leonard: What about you, Raj?</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, there it is, now that you don&#8217;t have a girlfriend, you want to hang out with me again.</p>
<p>Leonard: I never stopped hanging out with you.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, please, we all know I&#8217;m the friend you call when you have no other options. If we were the Justice League, I&#8217;d be Aquaman.</p>
<p>Howard: I wish you were Aquaman. Then I could send you to scoop my mom out of the old lady tank.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Excuse me, I&#8217;m thirsty, so I&#8217;m going to go to the refrigerator and get myself a refreshing beverage.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know what? I&#8217;ll just spend the evening alone.</p>
<p>Raj: What, suddenly I&#8217;m not good enough for you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Ah, I do so love beverages. Now I think I&#8217;ll take my after-dinner walk.</p>
<p>Leonard: Since when do you take after-dinner walks?</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, since when do you take walks?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I read a study online that walking after a meal not only aids in digestion, but increases serotonin, and you know me, if there&#8217;s one thing I like more than a refreshing beverage, it&#8217;s serotonin. Bye-bye.</p>
<p>Howard: Hold on. I&#8217;ll walk down with you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, that&#8217;s not necessary. You can go first.</p>
<p>Howard: Or we could go together.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I can&#8217;t think of a reason why not.</p>
<p>Howard: Let&#8217;s go.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hold on. Nope, no reason.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;ve missed you.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The lobby.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, say hello to your mother for me.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What?</p>
<p>Howard: You said you were going for a walk.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I didn&#8217;t say outside.</p>
<p>Howard: So what, you&#8217;re just gonna walk up and down the stairs?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, of course not. That would be odd and suspicious behavior.</p>
<p>Woman’s voice: Here Bubbles. Here boy.</p>
<p>Howard: Which way are you going?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Which way are you going?</p>
<p>Howard: I parked my scooter down the block.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m going the other way. Bye.</p>
<p>Howard: Bye. Actually, I&#8217;m this way. Do I smell hot dogs?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. I mean, I have no idea what you smell.</p>
<p>Howard: I definitely smell raw hot dog.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Perhaps you&#8217;re getting a brain tumour.</p>
<p>Howard: All right, have a nice walk.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I shall. Have a nice scoot.</p>
<p>Howard: You might want to stand back. I&#8217;m sitting on top of 13 horses here.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh. Hello, doggie. Nice doggie. I bet you think you smell hot dogs. Look, a cat!</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny’s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny, <em>(knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny.<em> (Long pause, knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny. <em>(Penny opens door. A dishevelled Sheldon is holding up one hot dog.) </em>Here. I had to trade the others for my life.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Hey, Leonard?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah?</p>
<p>Raj: I haven&#8217;t had sex in a year.</p>
<p>Leonard: Where you going with this, Raj?</p>
<p>Raj: Don&#8217;t flatter yourself, dude. I want to go out and meet a woman.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, go.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, I need a wingman. I don&#8217;t want to come off like a lonely loser.</p>
<p>Leonard: And you think my presence will help with that?</p>
<p>Raj: Well, I do. Next to you, I&#8217;ll look like a catch.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m not going out tonight, Raj.</p>
<p>Raj: All right. Would you mind if I went to your room and downloaded some Asian pornography?</p>
<p>Leonard: Very much.</p>
<p>Raj: Doesn&#8217;t have to be Asian.</p>
<p>Leonard: Don&#8217;t worry. You&#8217;ll meet a girl someday.</p>
<p>Raj: No, I won&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes, you will, and she&#8217;ll be beautiful, and kind and sexy and funny and everything you ever wanted in a woman.</p>
<p>Raj: You really think so?</p>
<p>Leonard: I do, and you&#8217;ll fall hopelessly in love and give her your heart. And she&#8217;ll take it and grind it into pathetic, little pieces.</p>
<p>Raj: But we&#8217;ll have sex first, right?</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny’s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Mmm, mmm, mmm. That&#8217;s Eye-talian.</p>
<p>Penny: So, um, was Leonard okay with you coming over?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, yes. In fact, he said, I&#8217;m fine, I don&#8217;t care. And he in no way said it in a manner which would lead one to believe that he was covering up feelings of anguish and betrayal.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, good.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m also pleased to report that he&#8217;s all cried out over you.</p>
<p>Penny: He&#8217;s been crying?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I believe that was something else I wasn&#8217;t supposed to mention.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, God, I feel terrible.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Do you have a stomach ache, too?</p>
<p>Penny: No. Why, do you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No.</p>
<p>Penny: Why did you ask if I had one, too?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Just making polite dinner conversation. Your turn.</p>
<p>Penny: All right. So, what&#8217;s new in your life?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, my new shoes are not made for running.</p>
<p>Penny: Have you been running?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. It&#8217;s just a suspicion I have. Mmm, mmm, mmm.</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m so glad you like it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I do. Leonard never cooks for me.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, maybe that&#8217;s &#8217;cause Leonard can&#8217;t cook.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You can&#8217;t cook and you made me this.</p>
<p>Penny: Whatever. Ooh, I&#8217;m gonna get the cheesecake out of the fridge.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, Lord, I&#8217;m in Jewish hell.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Look at this. Do you think she&#8217;s really doing that or is it PhotoShop?</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m pretty sure Martha Stewart never got naked with a room full of big, fat Japanese guys.</p>
<p>Raj: You don&#8217;t know that. Prison changes people.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, where you been?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I told you, walking.</p>
<p>Leonard: For an hour and a half?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I got lost.</p>
<p>Leonard: How could you get lost? Your phone has GPS.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Satellites are down. Solar flares.</p>
<p>Raj: There are no solar flares right now.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, there are.</p>
<p>Raj: Dude, I&#8217;m an astrophysicist. If there were solar flares, I&#8217;d be all up in it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry. I misspoke. What I meant to say was my battery died.</p>
<p>Leonard: What the hell was that about?</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t know. Do you think this is really Hillary Clinton doing it with Oprah?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, we really need to get you a girl.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Leonard’s bedroom. Leonard is asleep.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Leonard, <em>(knock, knock, knock) </em>Leonard,<em> (knock, knock, knock) </em>Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, just come in!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thanks for seeing me on such short notice.</p>
<p>Leonard: What do you want, Sheldon?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Maybe this isn&#8217;t a good time.</p>
<p>Leonard: Tell me why you woke me up or I swear to God I will kill you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Do you really think death threats are an appropriate way to begin this conversation? Sometimes your lack of social skills astonishes me.</p>
<p>Leonard: What do you want?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You may want to sit down.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m in bed!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Point taken. You may want to sit up.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon!</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;ve been seeing Penny behind your back.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, when you say seeing Penny, what exactly does that mean?</p>
<p>Sheldon: We had dinner last night. She made me spaghetti with little hot dogs cut up in it. Well, little hot dog. I gave up the other five hot dogs to a real dog. A real, big dog. A hell hound. Tangential to the primary story. How about I circle back to it?</p>
<p>Leonard: Fine. Why did you have dinner with Penny?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I told you, she made spaghetti with little hot dogs. I like spaghetti with little hot dogs.</p>
<p>Leonard: Then why did you have Chinese food with us?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I didn&#8217;t want to upset you. Howard made it very clear that my allegiance should be to male comrades before women who sell their bodies for money.</p>
<p>Leonard: Is it possible he said Bros before Hos?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, but I rephrased it to avoid offending the hos.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, I don&#8217;t care if you want to be friends with Penny.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh. Well, so the emotional turmoil that&#8217;s been keeping me from achieving REM sleep was entirely unjustified?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well then as my meemaw would say, looks like we butchered a pig, but nobody wanted bacon.</p>
<p>Leonard: I guess not.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And now, as promised, the tangent. Sheldon and the Hell Hound, or How I Lost My Hot Dogs.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The laundry room.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Oh, um, I, I can come back.</p>
<p>Leonard: D-don&#8217;t be silly. We&#8217;re neighbours, we&#8217;re going to run into each other, may as well get used to it.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, I guess you&#8217;re right.</p>
<p>Leonard: You used to it yet?</p>
<p>Penny: Nope.</p>
<p>Leonard: Me neither. Oh, Sheldon seemed think that I would be upset about you hanging out with him. But I just want you to know it&#8217;s fine.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, oh, good, because, um, his mother called me.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );

				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );

						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
