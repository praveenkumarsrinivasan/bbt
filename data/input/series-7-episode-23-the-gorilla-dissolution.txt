		<h2 class="title">Series 7 Episode 23 &#8211; The Gorilla&nbsp;Dissolution</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><i>Scene: The upstairs landing of Mrs Wolowitz&#8217;s house.</i></p>
<p>Bernadette: Almost there. You&#8217;re doing great.</p>
<p>Howard: Thanks for lifting my spirits. Next time, try lifting the box.</p>
<p>Raj: Please hurry.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>I don&#8217;t need a treadmill.</p>
<p>Howard: The doctor says you need to get exercise.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>I get plenty of exercise.</p>
<p>Howard: Crushing my will to live isn&#8217;t exercise.</p>
<p>Raj: If she isn&#8217;t gonna use it, then why are we doing this?</p>
<p>Howard: She&#8217;ll use it. All I need is to rig it with a fishing pole and a HoneyBaked Ham.</p>
<p>Raj: All right. Now what?</p>
<p>Bernadette: We set it up in Howie&#8217;s old room.</p>
<p>Raj: Do you know how to set it up?</p>
<p>Howard: Please, I&#8217;m an MIT-trained engineer. I think I can handle&#8230; Ma, look out.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: Aaaaah! </span>I told you this thing would kill me.</p>
<p><i>Credits sequence.</i></p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Raj: So she&#8217;s gonna be laid up for at least six weeks.</p>
<p>Leonard: Poor Mrs. Wolowitz.</p>
<p>Amy: Should we do something for her?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I know. Let&#8217;s go see the new Spider-Man movie.</p>
<p>Amy: Sheldon, we&#8217;re talking about your friend&#8217;s mother. She got hurt.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I thought that subject had run its course, so I changed it. It&#8217;s called reading the room, Amy.</p>
<p>Penny: Hi.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, how were things on the set?</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, pretty good, actually.</p>
<p>Raj: So the movie&#8217;s not as bad as you thought?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, no, it is, but I decided instead of complaining about it, I&#8217;m just gonna go in every day and give it my all.</p>
<p>Amy: Good for you.</p>
<p>Penny: Thanks. There&#8217;s no reason why I shouldn&#8217;t be the best bisexual go-go dancer slowly transforming into a killer gorilla anyone&#8217;s ever seen.</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t know. The bisexual gorilla go-go dancer in Schindler&#8217;s List is tough to beat.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Ah, very good. Because a gorilla go-go dancer of any sexual preference would be out of place in a film about the Holocaust.</p>
<p>Leonard: It only gets funnier when you explain it, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I know.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Mrs Wolowitz&#8217;s house.</i></p>
<p>Howard: Okay, she&#8217;s all settled in the guest room.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Maybe we should get one of those machines to help her up the stairs.</p>
<p>Howard: You mean a forklift?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howie.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m sorry. I just can&#8217;t deal with this right now.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Taking care of your own mother? How can you say that?</p>
<p>Howard: Bernie, she&#8217;s gonna be off her feet for six to eight weeks. Are you prepared to feed her, wash her and take her to the toilet?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I would do it for my mother.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, of course you would, you&#8217;re a loving person. I&#8217;m what my people would call a putz.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Look, I&#8217;m not crazy about the idea, but what other choice do we have?</p>
<p>Howard: We get a nurse. Preferably someone from a third world country who&#8217;s used to suffering and unpleasant smells.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You&#8217;d hire a total stranger to take care of the woman who raised you? That&#8217;s so cruel.</p>
<p>Howard: Not if we pay them well and let them listen to the music of their homeland.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>I need to tinkle.</p>
<p>Howard: Sounds like a job for a loving person. Would you like me to play some Polish music while you carry her to the toilet?</p>
<p>Bernadette: You are a putz.</p>
<p>Howard: As advertised.</p>
<p><i>Scene: A cinema.</i></p>
<p>Sheldon: Thanks for coming with me.</p>
<p>Raj: Thanks for inviting me after everyone else said no. Aren&#8217;t you gonna get 3-D glasses?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I brought my own. No sense in risking bridge-of-nose herpes.</p>
<p>Raj: Is that a real thing?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, until they invent nose condoms, I&#8217;m not finding out.</p>
<p>Raj: Emily, Hey.</p>
<p>Emily: Oh, hey, Raj.</p>
<p>Raj: Uh, this is my friend, Sheldon. Sheldon, this is Emily.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, yes, you&#8217;re the dermatologist. I went for a walk yesterday without sunscreen. Do you see anything on my forehead that I should be concerned about?</p>
<p>Emily: Um, you know what? I better go. My movie&#8217;s about to start.</p>
<p>Raj: Are you here alone?</p>
<p>Emily: No, not really.</p>
<p>Raj: What do you mean, not really?</p>
<p>Man: Hey, should we get our seats?</p>
<p>Emily: Yeah. It was nice seeing you. Um, I&#8217;ll call you later.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, okay.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That was awkward, right?</p>
<p>Raj: Uh-huh.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Is it because she&#8217;s dating you but was out with that other fellow?</p>
<p>Raj: Yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Good. I thought she saw something on my forehead.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The movie set. </i></p>
<p>Director: And action.</p>
<p>Wil Wheaton: Please don&#8217;t shut me out.</p>
<p>Penny: Go away. Just go away.</p>
<p>Wil: I swear, I will find a way to turn you back.</p>
<p>Penny: What gave you the right to mix my DNA with that of a killer gorilla?</p>
<p>Wil: I was trying to save your life.</p>
<p>Penny: Life? What life? Look at me, I&#8217;m a monster. And now I have blood on my hands, or paws. I don&#8217;t know.</p>
<p>Wil: You can&#8217;t give up. I love you.</p>
<p>Penny: I love you, too. But I&#8217;m afraid I love killing more. Like, one day, I might actually try and kill you.</p>
<p>Director: And cut. All right. All right, let&#8217;s set up for the next scene.</p>
<p>Penny: Actually, you know what? Can we do one more? I think I could do it better.</p>
<p>Director: Let&#8217;s just move on. No one cares.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, I care. I mean, look, if we&#8217;re gonna do this, why not try and make it something we&#8217;re actually proud of?</p>
<p>Director: Look, sweetie, this movie is garbage, but you should be happy about that, &#8217;cause if it was a good movie, you wouldn&#8217;t be in it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Whoa, whoa, hang on. There&#8217;s no need to insult her.</p>
<p>Director: And who are you?</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m her boyfriend.</p>
<p>Director: Isn&#8217;t she too hot for you?</p>
<p>Leonard: A little, yeah.</p>
<p>Director: Well, boyfriend, get off my set.</p>
<p>Penny: You can&#8217;t do that. He&#8217;s with me.</p>
<p>Director: You know what? You can get off my set, too. You&#8217;re fired.</p>
<p>Penny: What? You can&#8217;t fire me. I&#8217;m the star. I&#8217;m the girl that goes bananas. It says so on the poster.</p>
<p>Director: Yeah, but we just shot the last scene where we see your face. So from now own, the star of the movie is whoever wears this.</p>
<p>Wil: Hey, if you&#8217;re gonna fire her, then you have to fire me, too.</p>
<p><i>Scene: A bar.</i></p>
<p>Wil: Wow, that fell apart really fast.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Raj: Thanks for skipping the movie. I couldn&#8217;t sit in that theatre for two hours wondering about Emily and that guy.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, quite all right. After my forehead melanoma scare I&#8217;ve learned not to sweat the small stuff. Well, sorry, I don&#8217;t have all the ingredients to make chai tea.</p>
<p>Raj: You don&#8217;t have to make me anything.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, I do. You&#8217;re upset about Emily and you&#8217;re Indian. I need to make you chai tea. Now, I have all the ingredients except cardamom seeds. Do you happen to have any on you?</p>
<p>Raj: Sorry, I left them in my turban.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I&#8217;ll make English breakfast tea. They destroyed your culture. That&#8217;s close enough. You know, I&#8217;m curious, why are you so upset about seeing Emily with another man?</p>
<p>Raj: Wouldn&#8217;t you be upset if you saw Amy out with someone else?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Can&#8217;t happen. We have an ironclad relationship agreement which precludes her from physical contact with anyone other than me.</p>
<p>Raj: But you don&#8217;t have sex with her, either.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Slick, huh?</p>
<p>Raj: To be truthful, Emily and I haven&#8217;t dated that long, and we never agreed to be exclusive to each other.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Have you had intercourse?</p>
<p>Raj: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, stick to your guns. There will be a lot of pressure.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Mrs Wolowitz&#8217;s house.</i></p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>I&#8217;m hungry again.</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s like the world&#8217;s fattest cuckoo clock.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You know, you&#8217;re always talking about having a baby someday. This is exactly what it&#8217;s gonna be like.</p>
<p>Howard: No, it&#8217;s not.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Come on. The constant fussing, eating, pooping, burping, drooling. We&#8217;re even waiting for the day when she can finally walk on her own.</p>
<p>Howard: Maybe you&#8217;re right. Anything she finds on the floor goes right in her mouth.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;m just telling you now, if we do have kids, don&#8217;t expect me to do all the work.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, I&#8217;m a very paternal person. I&#8217;d be excellent at taking care of a baby.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>I&#8217;m still hungry.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m coming, you big baby.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The bar.</i></p>
<p>Penny: You know, the only thing worse than doing a movie where they glue monkey hair to your ass is getting fired from a movie where they glue monkey hair to your ass.</p>
<p>Wil: Forget it, man, it&#8217;s crap. You just move on to the next thing.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, well, it&#8217;s easy for you to say. You used to be famous.</p>
<p>Wil: Hey. I just lost a job for you.</p>
<p>Penny: All right, I&#8217;m sorry, you&#8217;re famous.</p>
<p>Wil: Penny, it&#8217;s not about being famous. It&#8217;s about the art. It&#8217;s about the passion we have for our craft. <i>(Text message tone)</i> I have an audition for Sharknado 2. If I book this, I am totally gonna pay you back for this beer.</p>
<p>Penny: God, what am I doing with my life?</p>
<p>Leonard: You having second thoughts about acting?</p>
<p>Penny: You were on set, you saw what it was like.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, but it&#8217;s not always that bad.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, really?</p>
<p>Leonard: What about when you did, uh, Anne Frank at that cute little theater?</p>
<p>Penny: It was over a bowling alley.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, but there was ample parking.</p>
<p>Penny: Are you done?</p>
<p>Leonard: And you were so good in the TV commercial.</p>
<p>Penny: It was for haemorrhoid cream.</p>
<p>Leonard: And I got itchy and swollen just watching you.</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, you are really not cheering me up.</p>
<p>Leonard: Come on. How can you be sad when you&#8217;re going home with all five-foot-six of this?</p>
<p>Penny: Hmm. You think you&#8217;re five-foot-six, that&#8217;s funny.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t understand it. I&#8217;m a nice guy, I have a great job, I&#8217;m well-educated, come from a good family. Why don&#8217;t women want to be with me?</p>
<p>Sheldon: An interesting question. Well, good night.</p>
<p>Raj: What? Don&#8217;t send me home. I can&#8217;t be alone right now.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s your problem. You can&#8217;t be alone.</p>
<p>Raj: What do you mean?</p>
<p>Sheldon: How many women have you had dates with?</p>
<p>Raj: Eleven.</p>
<p>Sheldon: How many of those women did you think would become your perfect companion?</p>
<p>Raj: Eleven. Wait. Do I count the 200-pound Sailor Moon girl that Howard and I had a threesome with at Comic Con?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Sure.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;ll stick with eleven. She liked Howard better.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, now do you see the problem?</p>
<p>Raj: Maybe. I, I don&#8217;t know.It&#8217;s late, I should, I should go. Look, I do get what you&#8217;re saying. Instead of desperately clinging to any woman who will go out with me, I need to work on my fear of being alone.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I was trying to suggest chemical castration, but it&#8217;s my bedtime, so whatever gets you out the door. Good night.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Mrs Wolowitz&#8217;s house.</i></p>
<p>Bernadette: Howie, I&#8217;m back!</p>
<p>Howard: Shh, I just got her to sleep.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Sorry.</p>
<p>Howard: What took you so long? The grocery store is a few blocks away.</p>
<p>Bernadette: They only had regular yoghurt. I had to go to a different store to get the extra-fat kind your mom likes.</p>
<p>Howard: Then why do I smell coffee on your breath?</p>
<p>Bernadette: So what? After two days of taking care of her, excuse me for stopping to get a mocha.</p>
<p>Howard: A mocha? Well, it must be nice to be queen.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Queen? I&#8217;ve been killing myself here.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, whose fault is that? I wanted to get a nurse, but you were all, I&#8217;m nice, I want to take care of people.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;m glad I got that mocha. And you know what else I&#8217;m glad about? I bought you a brownie and I ate it in the car.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <i>(off)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: </span>Howard.</p>
<p>Howard: Thanks a lot. Now I have to go rub her belly again.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment.</i></p>
<p>Raj: Hey.</p>
<p>Emily: Thanks for letting me come over.</p>
<p>Raj: Of course. Please, come in.</p>
<p>Emily: I just wanted to say how sorry I am about tonight, and I want to make sure that we&#8217;re okay.</p>
<p>Raj: Uh, look, you and I haven&#8217;t made any commitments to each other.</p>
<p>Emily: I know. I just felt like I needed to explain. The guy I was with did my last tattoo, and he&#8217;s been asking me out for months. I finally said yes just to get it over with.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s okay.</p>
<p>Emily: Really?</p>
<p>Raj: Well, I mean, yeah, it freaked me out a little, but that&#8217;s my issue, not yours.</p>
<p>Emily: Wow. If I saw you out with another woman, I&#8217;d be pretty upset.</p>
<p>Raj: Thank you. Not just for being upset, but for believing that could happen.</p>
<p>Emily: Just so you know, I&#8217;m not seeing anyone else.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, me, neither.</p>
<p>Emily: Okay.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {

					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {

				}
