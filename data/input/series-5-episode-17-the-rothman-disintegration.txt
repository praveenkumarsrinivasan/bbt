<p>Penny: You&#8217;re right. You&#8217;re absolutely right. I should have been honest with you and told you the gift was too much.</p>
<p>Amy: Yes, too much. Because our friendship is fundamentally asymmetrical. I clearly like you more than you like me.</p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t think you can put a number on how much one person likes another.</p>
<p>Amy: I bought you a painting that&#8217;s 12 square feet in area. There&#8217;s a number.</p>
<p>Penny: Amy, come on&#8230;</p>
<p>Amy: If you don&#8217;t like feet, you can try dollars. The painting set me back three grand.</p>
<p>Penny: Three gr&#8230; Oh, my God! Look, look, Amy, all you need to know is, you are my friend and I don&#8217;t want to lose you out of my life.</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;m sorry, I&#8217;m just having trouble believing you right now.</p>
<p>Penny: Grab your hammer.</p>
<p>Amy: No. Damage is done.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, look, I didn&#8217;t want to say this, but the real reason I took the painting down was because it made Bernadette very jealous.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, my goodness, how could I have not seen that? The painting is a constant reminder that of the three of us, she is the least cool.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, th-th-th-that&#8217;s what it is, so&#8230;</p>
<p>Amy: You have such a good heart.</p>
<p>Penny: Huh, I try.</p>
<p>Amy: Come on.</p>
<p>Penny: Well where are we going?</p>
<p>Amy: We&#8217;re going to go put this painting back up in your apartment.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, what about Bernadette?</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, screw her. She&#8217;s just lucky we let her hang out with us.</p>

<p>Leonard: What are you doing?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m trying to raise the temperature in here before my nipples tear through my shirt.</p>
<p>Howard: Why don&#8217;t you just turn up the thermostat?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Aha! Good question. It turns out the thermostat for my new office isn&#8217;t in my new office. No. It&#8217;s next door, in Professor Davenport&#8217;s office, who is currently enjoying the hot flashes associated with menopause.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why is there a hole here?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why is there a hole in my new office? I&#8217;ve narrowed it down to two possibilities: There was something in the wall that someone outside the wall wanted, or the more disturbing, there was something in the wall that wanted out.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, at least you finally got a window that opens. That&#8217;s nice.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Is it? Listen.</p>
<p>Raj: What, you don&#8217;t like wind chimes?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, I hate them, but it gets worse. There it is.</p>
<p>Howard: The bird?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, It&#8217;s completely out of tune with the wind chimes.</p>
<p>Raj: So?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You don&#8217;t get it, do you? That&#8217;s a mockingbird. Mockingbirds can change their song, which means he&#8217;s out of tune on purpose. He&#8217;s mocking me. Oh dear. There it is again. Do you feel it?</p>
<p>Howard: The growing realization that you are one wacky bastard? Yep.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. The vibration. We are directly underneath the geology lab, and they&#8217;re running their confounded sieve shakers again. Hey, gravel monkeys, if you need to shake rocks, try jiggling your heads around!</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, relax.</p>
<p>Sheldon: How can I relax? My nervous system is being stretched out like the strings of a harp and plucked by holes and birds and wind and the low-hanging scrotum of the difficult-to-evict Professor Rothman.</p>
<p>Leonard: If you&#8217;re not happy, why don&#8217;t you just let Kripke have the office?</p>
<p>Sheldon: What, and let him win? Do I look crazy to you? We&#8217;re trying to think down here, you geode-loving feldspar jockeys! <em>(To the bird) </em>And you, the notes are C, D, E, G and A. You pick one or I am chopping down that tree!</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Penny: How&#8217;s that?</p>
<p>Amy: Little higher on the right.</p>
<p>Penny: Now?</p>
<p>Amy: Little more. No. That&#8217;s got it.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, okay. That&#8217;s, uh, that&#8217;s good.</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;ll let you in on a little secret.</p>
<p>Penny: Mm.</p>
<p>Amy: Originally we were painted nude. But I had him add clothes, &#8217;cause I thought it was an unnecessary challenge to our heterosexuality.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, good call.</p>
<p>Amy: But if you ever change your mind, all it would take is some warm, soapy water and a couple of sponges.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re talking about the painting, right?</p>
<p>Amy: Sure.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Rothman&#8217;s office. Sheldon has his head stuck in the hole in the wall.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Help! Somebody help!</p>
<p>Leonard: What happened?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I was trying to see what was in here and my head got stuck.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why would you do that?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s called scientific curiosity. Now go get butter.</p>

					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();
