		<h2 class="title">Series 6 Episode 19 &#8211; The Closet&nbsp;Reconfiguration</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: Howard and Bernadette&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Bernadette: What you doing?</p>
<p>Howard: You said clean up. I&#8217;m cleaning up.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You can&#8217;t just throw everything in the closet.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, you can tell me what to do or you can tell me how to do it, but you can&#8217;t do both. This isn&#8217;t sex.</p>
<p>Bernadette: What if someone looks in there?</p>
<p>Howard: They&#8217;re just coming over for dinner. No one&#8217;s gonna look in the closet.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Well, you don&#8217;t know that. What if someone&#8217;s looking for the bathroom and they open that door?</p>
<p>Howard: Could work out. For all we know there&#8217;s a toilet in there somewhere.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Fine. But after tonight we need to get a handle on this mess.</p>
<p>Howard: You know what we should do? We should show the closet to Sheldon.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Why?</p>
<p>Howard: Are you kidding? He&#8217;s like a savant at organizing. Everything in his apartment has a label on it. Including his label maker, which has a label that says label maker. And if you look really close at that label maker label, you&#8217;ll see a label that says label.</p>
<p>Bernadette: He&#8217;s our guest, we can&#8217;t just ask him to straighten our closet.</p>
<p>Howard: No, we wouldn&#8217;t ask him. We&#8217;d just show him the closet and let the goblins in his head take it from there.</p>
<p><em>Later.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Hey, guys, come on in.</p>
<p>All: Hi.</p>
<p>Penny: Ooh, it smells good.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Thanks. And, Sheldon, I know tonight&#8217;s the night you eat Thai food, so I went to the Asian market, got all the ingredients and made it from scratch.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, you shouldn&#8217;t have.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, it&#8217;s my pleasure.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, you really shouldn&#8217;t have. I brought my own.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You stopped and got him takeout?</p>
<p>Leonard: I had no choice. He kept kicking the back of my seat.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Sheldon, I&#8217;ve been cooking all day.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, now don&#8217;t you feel silly.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Show him the closet.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: Howard and Bernadette&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Raj: These spring rolls are amazing. Good job, Bernadette.</p>
<p>Bernadette: That&#8217;s the takeout that Sheldon brought.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, well, I&#8217;m sure they wouldn&#8217;t have tasted nearly as good if I hadn&#8217;t tried your food first.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Howard, did you want your clothes arranged seasonally or by colour?</p>
<p>Howard: Colour&#8217;s fine.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wrong, they&#8217;ll be arranged seasonally.</p>
<p>Penny: Sheldon, aren&#8217;t you gonna spend a little time with Amy?</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, it&#8217;s okay, I&#8217;m used to it. The other day at Whole Foods, he spent an hour optimizing the cheese aisle.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, and some thanks I got. The assistant manager chased me out with an artisanal salami.</p>
<p>Amy: His quirks just make you love him more. Someone please agree with me.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The closet.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, come on. It&#8217;s getting late. Time to go.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, five more minutes.</p>
<p>Leonard: That&#8217;s what you said five minutes ago. Amy and Penny are already in the car. Let&#8217;s move it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: How come I never get to do anything I want to do?</p>
<p>Howard: You know, if he really wants to stay and finish, I can give him a ride home.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Please, Leonard, he said it&#8217;s okay.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, it&#8217;s, wait, I can go home without you? Bye.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Howard, I have a few questions. I found three bowling pins. Now, do you juggle these, or are you missing seven?</p>
<p>Howard: Juggle.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You health nuts kill me.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, my God, it&#8217;s beautiful. Look, he found the juggling pins I hid.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Uh, just a couple more items. Howard, I found this letter from your dad in a box. Now, based on the content, it could either be filed&#8230;</p>
<p>Howard: Whoa, you opened this?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, I had to find out if it was personal correspondence or memorabilia. Now, as I was saying, based on the content&#8230;</p>
<p>Howard: I couldn&#8217;t be less interested. Now, come on, I&#8217;ll take you home.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howard, don&#8217;t you want to know what&#8217;s in the letter?</p>
<p>Howard: If I wanted to know, I would&#8217;ve opened it years ago. The closet looks great. Let&#8217;s get out of here.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wait, can I bring this box of extra shirt buttons to sort on the ride?</p>
<p>Howard: Do whatever you want.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thanks. Oh. Great party.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The stairwell.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: You know, when I first met Howard, he would pull his scrotum out of his shorts and say, aw, I sat in gum.</p>
<p>Penny: What is your point?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, it&#8217;s just kind of weird how grown up he is now. Happily married guy throwing dinner parties.</p>
<p>Penny: Really? You couldn&#8217;t just say that? You had to tell the scrotum story?</p>
<p>Leonard: Trying to paint a picture.</p>
<p>Penny: It was a nice change of pace not eating takeout around a coffee table.</p>
<p>Leonard: Mm, you know, we could throw a dinner party, too. Maybe even ask everyone to get dressed up.</p>
<p>Penny: Sure. Just, when you say dressed up, you mean nice clothes, right? Not, like, capes and tights and crap?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah. Although&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: No.</p>
<p><i>Scene: Howard and Bernadette&#8217;s apartment.</i></p>
<p>Bernadette: Howie, you okay?</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, I just couldn&#8217;t sleep.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Told you you shouldn&#8217;t have espresso after dinner. I know the little cups make you feel big, but it&#8217;s not worth it.</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s this stupid letter.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Did you read it?</p>
<p>Howard: No.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You must be curious.</p>
<p>Howard: Of course I&#8217;m curious. I haven&#8217;t seen the man since, oh, I was a little kid. And a letter shows up on my 18th birthday? What&#8217;s that about?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Why don&#8217;t you read it? Maybe he apologizes or explains why he left.</p>
<p>Howard: He abandoned me and my mother. Why does he deserve a chance to explain anything?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I get that. So, what do you want to do with it?</p>
<p>Howard: Something I should have done a long time ago. <i>(Sets fire to it)</i></p>
<p>Bernadette: Really? Are you sure?</p>
<p>Howard: Yep.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Feel better?</p>
<p>Howard: I do. <i>(Smoke alarm goes off) </i>Great. Neither one of us is tall enough to reach that.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Amy: I can&#8217;t believe he set it on fire.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Yeah, just seeing that letter really freaked him out. And he was already having a tough day &#8217;cause he accidentally wore my pants to work. I don&#8217;t know why he was upset. They were bigger on him than me.</p>
<p>Amy: Boy, I&#8217;m really curious what was in that letter.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Me, too, but I guess now we&#8217;ll never know.</p>
<p>Amy: Well, you said Sheldon read it. Why not ask him?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I can&#8217;t do that. What kind of wife would I be if I didn&#8217;t respect my husband&#8217;s privacy?</p>
<p>Penny: What if I ask Sheldon, you just happen to be in the room?</p>
<p>Bermadette: That works.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The laundry room.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Bleuch. Like cleaning out the entire building&#8217;s belly button.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, hello. What can I do for you ladies?</p>
<p>Amy: You have something we want.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, dear. My mother warned me this is what happens to pretty boys in the big city.</p>
<p>Penny: No, we just want information.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh. Oh, I&#8217;ve got that in spades. Ravage me.</p>
<p>Penny: We heard you read the letter from Howard&#8217;s father.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I did.</p>
<p>Penny: What did it say?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I can&#8217;t tell you that. I&#8217;m bound by closet organizer/organizee confidentiality.</p>
<p>Amy: Sheldon, that&#8217;s not a real thing.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, neither is the rule that you have to hold your girlfriend&#8217;s hand at the movies. You know. That doesn&#8217;t stop you from pawing at me like you&#8217;re a bear and I&#8217;m a trash can full of sweets.</p>
<p>Penny: Why do you even care? Just tell us what it says.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Control over the information contained in that letter belongs to Howard. By happenstance, I came to know it. That doesn&#8217;t give me the right to disseminate it freely.</p>
<p>Penny: Come on. Look, the letter was found in Bernadette&#8217;s closet. Doesn&#8217;t that count for something?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Are you pointing out that California is a community property state, and since Howard and Bernadette are married, the intellectual property contained in that letter is jointly owned by the two spouses?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, obviously.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well played. Sometimes I don&#8217;t give you enough credit, Penny.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Dude, you made the right choice coming to me for help with this party.</p>
<p>Leonard: Actually, all I did was invite you.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, put your mind at ease. I&#8217;m here to make sure your dinner party kicks Howard&#8217;s dinner party&#8217;s ass. Now, the first thing we need is a theme. I&#8217;m thinking, ah, turn-of-the-century Moulin Rouge.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m thinking you need a testosterone patch. Penny and I just want to do something low-key. You know, cocktails, light jazz music, hors d&#8217;oeuvres.</p>
<p>Raj: So your theme is I saw a rerun of Mad Men and bought some crab puffs from Trader Joe&#8217;s? Hate to miss that.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, where have you been?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, Leonard. If I was prone to sarcasm, I&#8217;d say I was pulling off a major heist at the museum of laundry baskets.</p>
<p>Leonard: One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten. I meant, golly, Sheldon, you&#8217;ve been gone a long time.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh. Yeah, well, I was waylaid by Penny, Bernadette and Amy. They made me reveal confidential information about Howard&#8217;s father.</p>
<p>Leonard: What information?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I can&#8217;t tell you that. I am bound by closet organizer/organizee confidentiality.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, come on, we won&#8217;t tell anyone.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Sorry, badgering me won&#8217;t work. What you should have said is, It&#8217;s pointless to keep this a secret because Penny will tell us.</p>
<p>Leonard: Fine, then that.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, I&#8217;ll tell you. My goodness, everyone&#8217;s on their game today.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment kitchen.</em></p>
<p>Amy: This is really fun.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, it&#8217;s nice to get dressed up once in a while.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, and the hors d&#8217;oeuvres are delightful.</p>
<p>Leonard: As is the company.</p>
<p>Penny: Aw.</p>
<p>Sheldon: My shirt is itchy and I wish I were dead.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, uh, listen, everybody. Before Howard gets here, et&#8217;s all just agree to not bring up the letter from his father.</p>
<p>Raj: Of course.</p>
<p>Penny: Sure.</p>
<p>Amy: Absolutely.</p>
<p>Sheldon: If I say yes, can we turn off that Latin orgy music?</p>
<p><em>Scene: The stairwell.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Ridiculous that we still have to walk up all these flights of stairs.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Yeah, try doing it in heels.</p>
<p>Howard: I am.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Wait. There&#8217;s something I have to tell you.</p>
<p>Howard: What?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I know what was in your dad&#8217;s letter.</p>
<p>Howard: Sheldon, I swear to God, I&#8217;m gonna kill you.</p>
<p>Amy: Hey.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I made him tell us.</p>
<p>Howard: What? Us? Who else knows?</p>
<p>Penny: I know.</p>
<p>Amy: Me, too.</p>
<p>Leonard: Same here.</p>
<p>Raj: Shame on all of you.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know, too.</p>
<p>Raj: Couldn&#8217;t leave him one friend, could you?</p>
<p>Howard: So everybody knows what&#8217;s in that letter except for me?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, it&#8217;s six against one. Stand down, sir.</p>
<p>Howard: How could you do this?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;m sorry.</p>
<p>Raj: If you want, we could tell you.</p>
<p>Howard: No, I don&#8217;t want to know. I mean, I do, but&#8230; I got to go.</p>
<p>Amy: Use me as a human shield!</p>
<p>Sheldon: I panicked. He looked taller than usual.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The closet.</i></p>
<p>Bernadette <i>(off)</i>: Howard?</p>
<p>Howard: In here.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;m sorry. I should have left it alone.</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s okay. Sorry I ran off like that.</p>
<p>Bernadette: What are you looking at?</p>
<p>Howard: Uh, pictures of my dad and me when I was a kid.</p>
<p>Bernadette: That&#8217;s nice.</p>
<p>Howard: I got to tell you, as angry as I am at Sheldon for blabbing, he did a hell of a job organizing this closet. Look at this. Photos of Wolowitz family before father left forever. Check out nine-year-old Howie with cornrows. Neither race was happy to see me with those.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Think you could take a break?</p>
<p>Howard: Why?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Got a little surprise for you. Come on.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, honey, I am in no mood to have sex tonight. I&#8217;ll lay there if you absolutely have to have it, but&#8230; Oh. What are you guys doing here?</p>
<p>Leonard: When you left, you weren&#8217;t sure whether or not you wanted to know what was in your dad&#8217;s letter, so we came up with kind of a cool solution.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, yeah, what&#8217;s that?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s simple, really. It occurred to me that knowing and not knowing can be achieved by creating a macroscopic example of quantum superposition. The, the principle that a physical system exists partially in all its possible states at once.</p>
<p>Penny: We were all thinking it, really. It was kind of the elephant in the room, so&#8230;</p>
<p>Sheldon: Anyway, um, I realized that if we each present you with an account of what your father wrote to you, only one of which is true, and then we don&#8217;t tell you which one it is, you will forever be in a state of epistemic ambivalence.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah. And I said if it wasn&#8217;t epistemic, we might as well not do it.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Sit down, honey.</p>
<p>Leonard: Raj, you&#8217;re up.</p>

				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				} else {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='55px' width='100%' frameBorder='0' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).load( function( e ) {
					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );
					$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loaded' );

					JetpackLikespostMessage( { event: 'loadLikeWidget', name: $iframe.attr( 'name' ), width: $iframe.width() }, window.frames[ 'likes-master' ] );

					if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
				setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 250 );
			}
