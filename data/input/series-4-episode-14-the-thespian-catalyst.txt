		<h2 class="title">Series 4 Episode 14 &#8211; The Thespian&nbsp;Catalyst</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: A lecture hall.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Good evening. I&#8217;m your guest lecturer, Dr. Sheldon Cooper. I was expecting applause, but I suppose stunned silence is equally appropriate. I agreed to speak to you this evening, because I was told that you&#8217;re the best and the brightest of this university&#8217;s doctoral candidates. Hmm. Of course, that&#8217;s like saying you are the most important electron in a hydrogen atom. &#8216;Cause, you see, there&#8217;s only one electron in a hydrogen atom. Best and brightest, my sweet patootie. All right, let&#8217;s begin. Show of hands, who here is familiar with the concept of topological insulators? Don&#8217;t kid yourselves.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: I found another tweet from a student at Sheldon&#8217;s lecture. Dr. Cooper has taken a relatively boring subject and managed to make it completely insufferable. Plus, he looks like a giant insect.</p>
<p>Howard: Look. Listen to this one. Does Einstein&#8217;s theory explain why time flies when you&#8217;re having fun, but when you&#8217;re listening to Dr. Cooper, it falls out of the sky, dead?</p>
<p>Raj: Ooh, somebody took pictures and uploaded them to their Flickr account.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wow. How do you get an entire lecture hall to flip you off at the same time?</p>
<p>Howard: Apparently, if you&#8217;re Sheldon, all you need to do is turn your back.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, Leonard, is your wi-fi down? I can&#8217;t get on.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, Sheldon changed the password. It&#8217;s now Penny is a freeloader. No spaces.</p>
<p>Penny: Thanks. What are you guys doing?</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon gave a lecture at the university tonight. We&#8217;re reading the reviews.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh. How&#8217;d he do?</p>
<p>Howard: Well, picture the Hindenburg meets Chernobyl meets Three Mile Island meets Tron 2.</p>
<p>Penny: That bad, huh?</p>
<p>Leonard: Read this woman&#8217;s tweet.</p>
<p>Penny: Listening to Dr. Cooper has made me want to start cutting myself again</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Good evening, Leonard, Howard, Raj, freeloader.</p>
<p>Howard: So, how&#8217;d the lecture go?</p>
<p>Leonard: In a word, triumphant.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really? Triumphant?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, yes, you should have seen those young people. Thirsty for knowledge, drinking in my wisdom. I may have changed a few lives today.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, please let me tell him.</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t know, I kind of promised Howard.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Tell me what?</p>
<p>Howard: Actually, we should all share the moment. Raj, if you would.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, tweets about my lecture. Hmm. That&#8217;s rather unfair. That&#8217;s downright cruel. Plus, insects have six legs. Yeah, I&#8217;m not familiar with the acronym KMN.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, uh, from the context, we think it means kill me now.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, I suppose everyone&#8217;s entitled to their own opinion. I think I&#8217;ll turn in. I didn&#8217;t want to teach those poopy heads, anyway.</p>
<p>Howard: FYI, I think that&#8217;s what Darth Vader said just before he started building the Death Star.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The Cheesecake Factory</em></p>
<p>Howard: Sheldon still moping?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, it&#8217;s weird. Even though he didn&#8217;t want to give the lecture in the first place, being rejected by those students really hit him hard.</p>
<p>Raj: Mmm, I know the feeling. It&#8217;s like accidentally walking into a gay bar and then having no one hit on you. It-It happened to a friend of mine.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Are you guys doing okay?</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, Leonard and I are fine, but I think Raj needs to meet a girl really soon.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Well, that shouldn&#8217;t be too hard. He&#8217;s such a cutie.</p>
<p>Raj: Thank you, but cute is for bunnies. I want to be something with sex appeal, like, like a labradoodle.</p>
<p>Howard: Labradoodle?</p>
<p>Leonard: We might be starting to zero in on your problem.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Don&#8217;t you listen to them. You&#8217;ve got plenty of sex appeal.</p>
<p>Raj: You really think so?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Yeah. You&#8217;re a hottie.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, thanks, Bernadette. And just for the record, labradoodles are hypo-allergenic, which is a very sexy quality to those troubled by animal dander.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Amy <em>(on webcam, with an undersea background)</em>: So, what do you think?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s a charming illusion, but it does not cheer me up.</p>
<p>Amy: Not even when I do this? <em>(Pretends to be a fish)</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: No.</p>
<p>Amy: Well, that was the last arrow in my quiver of whimsy.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Do you realize that teaching is the first thing I&#8217;ve failed at since my ill-fated attempt to complete a chin-up in March of 1989?</p>
<p>Amy: If this humiliating experience is really troubling you, there are things we could do about it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: For instance?</p>
<p>Amy: Well, the first thing that comes to mind is isolating the part of your brain where the memory is stored and destroying it with a laser.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hmm, no. One slip of the hand, and suddenly I&#8217;m sitting in the Engineering Department, building doodads with Wolowitz.</p>
<p>Amy: All right. Have you considered improving your socialization skills, thus allowing you to communicate more effectively with other people?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Isn&#8217;t that their burden? I&#8217;m the one with something interesting to say.</p>
<p>Amy: Fair enough, but in its essence, teaching is a performance art. In the classroom paradigm, the teacher has the responsibility to communicate, as well as entertain and engage.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I sense that you&#8217;re trying to slow-walk me to an epiphany. Would you mind very much jumping to it?</p>
<p>Amy: Perhaps you should consider taking acting lessons.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Acting lessons. Interesting. It might help if I could act as though I care about my students and whether or not they learn.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny’s apartment door.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny. <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny. <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You&#8217;re an <em>(finger quotes) </em>actress, correct?</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m not an <em>(finger quotes)</em>actress. I&#8217;m an actress.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right. You&#8217;re an actress. I need you to teach me.</p>
<p>Penny: You want an acting lesson?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Perhaps two. I&#8217;d like to master the craft.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, where is this coming from?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It has been suggested to me that acting techniques could improve my lecturing, at which, if certain tweets and blogs are to be believed, I suck the big one.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, I saw those. They were funny. I printed a few out and put &#8216;em on my fridge.</p>
<p>Sheldon: So, when could we start?</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, just to be clear. You are asking me for help because I know something that the brilliant Dr. Sheldon Cooper doesn&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I suppose that&#8217;s one way to look at it.</p>
<p>Penny: I think it&#8217;s the only way to look at it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Are you going to help me or not?</p>
<p>Penny: Probably. I&#8217;m just enjoying the foreplay. Does this mean you are done mocking my acting career?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I&#8217;m sorry, I thought making the transition from actor to acting teacher was the signal that one&#8217;s career had reached the end of the road.</p>
<p>Penny: Forget it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;ll pay you 40 dollars.</p>
<p>Penny: Saturday, nine a.m. Bring cash.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The university cafeteria</em></p>
<p>Howard: Raj, I have amazing news!</p>
<p>Raj: What?</p>
<p>Howard: I just got offered a fellowship at the Weitzmann Institute in Israel.</p>
<p>Raj: Dude, that&#8217;s incredible!</p>
<p>Howard: I know. The only thing is, I&#8217;m gonna be gone for two years.</p>
<p>Raj: Aw, I&#8217;m gonna miss you. Are you going with him?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I have to stay here for school.</p>
<p>Howard: That&#8217;s what we&#8217;re here to talk to you about. You see, Bernadette has needs.</p>
<p>Raj: What kind of needs?</p>
<p>Howard: Sexual needs.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Most of them regular, some of them kind of messed up.</p>
<p>Howard: So, while I&#8217;m gone, you&#8217;re going to have to satisfy her.</p>
<p>Bernadette: What do you say?</p>
<p>Raj: I say okey-dokey.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(to the real Raj who is daydreaming)</em>: What ya thinking so hard about?</p>
<p>Raj: Just that I&#8217;m definitely not gay.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny’s apartment. </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny. <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny. <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Penny.</p>
<p>Penny: What&#8217;s wrong?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Nothing. I was acting.</p>
<p>Penny: You were acting?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes. In preparation for today&#8217;s studies, I read Stanislavski&#8217;s An Actor Prepares, Stella Adler&#8217;s</p>
<p>The Technique of Acting, Uta Hagen&#8217;s Respect for Acting, and Henry Winkler&#8217;s Heyyy, I&#8217;m an Actor.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, good for you. Come on in.</p>
<p>Sheldon: How shall we begin?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, I thought we&#8217;d start with some basic movement exercises. You know, get our bodies warmed up a little.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right.</p>
<p>Penny: So I just want you to relax and kind of move around in the space. You know, just do whatever feels natural. Sheldon?</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(doing nothing)</em>: You said to do whatever feels natural. This feels natural. Certainly more natural than what you&#8217;re doing.</p>
<p>Penny: Come on, you got to work with me. We need to get connected with our bodies.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Penny, my body and I have a relationship that works best when we maintain a cool, wary distance from each other.</p>
<p>Penny: All right, let&#8217;s just say we&#8217;ve warmed up.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You&#8217;re the teacher.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay. One of the things that might help you in connecting with your students is being a little more spontaneous. So why don&#8217;t we try some improvisation?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why not? It seems like you&#8217;re improvising your entire curriculum.</p>
<p>Penny: This is all about listening and responding.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Gotcha.</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m going to create a character and a situation, and you just jump in when you feel it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right.</p>
<p>Penny: All right.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Action.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, it&#8217;s not a movie. It&#8217;s improv. So no one calls action.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hey, you taught me something. Who would have thought it?</p>
<p>Penny: Okay. Uh, no, the shipment has not arrived, and I really need those shoes. They are my biggest seller. Yes, ladies sizes six through ten. Thank you. Oh, sorry, I have to go, I have a customer. Bye-bye. Hi. Can I help you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;d like a frozen yogurt, please.</p>
<p>Penny: Yogurt?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah.</p>
<p>Penny: Um, okay, sure. Luckily, we sell both shoes and yogurt here.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You do?</p>
<p>Penny: Yes. Look up at the sign, and remember, improv is always about saying yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right. Yes. I see a sign. It says Camarillo State Mental Hospital.</p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s the only explanation I can come up with for why you think you sell shoes and yogurt.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, you know what? Let&#8217;s just try a different improv. Uh, oh, this time we will be two winos living under a freeway overpass.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, and we&#8217;re going to use props?</p>
<p>Penny: You bet. I had dreams, you know. I was gonna be famous. Show everybody back home I could be someone. Now look at me. Want some?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You have any frozen yogurt?</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m still not adjusted to how the SyFy channel spells their name now. S-Y-F-Y. That&#8217;s siffy.</p>
<p>Raj: Uh-huh.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(phone rings)</em>: Hello? Oh, my God! Is he okay?</p>
<p>Raj: What happened?</p>
<p>Leonard: Hang on. Hang on. Uh-huh. Okay, thank you. Howard was on his scooter, and got hit by a truck. He&#8217;s in critical condition.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, no!</p>
<p>Bernadette: Did you hear? Isn&#8217;t it terrible?</p>
<p>Leonard: Have you seen him?</p>
<p>Bernadette: They wouldn&#8217;t let me in. Oh my Howie.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;ll be okay. It&#8217;ll be okay.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;ll be okay. It&#8217;ll be okay. <em>(Phone rings) </em>It&#8217;s Howard. Howard, hello.</p>
<p>Howard: Raj, is that you?</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, I&#8217;m right here, buddy. How are you?</p>
<p>Howard: Shh, shh. Listen to me. I&#8217;m not gonna make it.</p>
<p>Raj: No, no, no, don&#8217;t say that. You&#8217;re going to be all right.</p>
<p>Howard: Raj, I don&#8217;t have time. Now, pay attention. My last wish is that you look after Bernadette.</p>
<p>Raj: Of course, of course. Now when you say look after, you mean&#8230;</p>
<p>Bernadette: Sexually.</p>
<p>Raj: Excuse me, Bernadette. I have to hear it from him.</p>
<p>Howard: Sexually.</p>
<p>Raj: Got it. Take care. I guess I have no choice but to make sweet, guilt-free love to you over and over again for the rest of my life.</p>
<p>Bernadette: That&#8217;s how I heard it.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(out of Raj’s daydream)</em>: Or it could be sy-fee.</p>
<p>Raj: What?</p>
<p>Leonard: S-Y-F-Y. Sy-fee.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, right. Good one.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny’s apartment. </em></p>
<p>Penny: So, did you get a chance to go over the scene I gave you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes. I didn&#8217;t care for it.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, Cat on a Hot Tin Roof is an American classic.</p>
<p>Sheldon: So is the McRib sandwich. I don&#8217;t care for that, either.</p>
<p>Penny: Fine. What would you rather do as a scene study?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m glad you asked. I took the liberty of adapting a Star Trek fan fiction novella I wrote when I was ten into a one-act play.</p>
<p>Penny: And you think it&#8217;s better than Tennessee Williams?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why don&#8217;t we leave that for future generations to decide?</p>
<p>Penny: Where No Sheldon Has Gone Before.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s the story of a young boy who is transported from the ignorant backwoods of East Texas to the 23rd Century, where his genius is not only appreciated, but celebrated.</p>
<p>Penny: KMN.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Now, in this pivotal scene, Sheldon&#8217;s mother, played by you, argues with an emissary of the United Federation of Planets, Mr. Spock, the role I will bring to life.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, that&#8217;s fine, but let&#8217;s try and get you out of your comfort zone.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why would we want to do that? It&#8217;s called the comfort zone for a reason.</p>

					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();
