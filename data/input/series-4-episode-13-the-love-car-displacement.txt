<p>Penny: The what?</p>

<p>Penny: Oh. Oh, okay, well, you know, like I said, I have plans, so.</p>
<p>Amy: Shame. Since you&#8217;re my best friend, I thought it would be a good bonding opportunity.</p>

<p>Penny: Oh, don&#8217;t feel bad. I never read Leonard&#8217;s, and I used to sleep with him.</p>

<p>Leonard: I think her weekend just opened up.</p>
<p>Penny: Wait, wait, just to be clear, when you guys say spa, does that mean the same thing as when regular people say it?</p>
<p>Leonard: Pretty much. Except we keep our shirts on in the sauna.</p>
<p>Penny: You know, it is going to be difficult, but I&#8217;m going to cancel my plans so I can do this for my bestie.</p>
<p>Amy: Please don&#8217;t touch my breasts.</p>
<p>Penny: I, I wasn&#8217;t going to.</p>
<p>Amy: All right. I just want to establish boundaries.</p>
<p>Penny: Boy, this is great. I haven&#8217;t had a vacation in ages.</p>
<p>Sheldon: In order to take a vacation, one first has to work.</p>
<p>Penny: You know, for a smart guy, you really seem to have a hard time grasping the concept, don&#8217;t piss off the people who handle the things you eat.</p>
<p>Amy: That does seem to be a valid principle.</p>

<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Good morning. If I could have everyone&#8217;s attention, please? I know we&#8217;re all eager to get on the road to Big Sur, so if we focus, I&#8217;m sure we can get through this orientation in under a half an hour. Then it&#8217;s just Q&amp;A, quiz, safety drills, pose for commemorative group photo and we&#8217;re off.</p>

<p>Sheldon: Raj? What are you doing? I don&#8217;t think so. You&#8217;ve had your allotted six ounces. The first bathroom break isn&#8217;t until the Denny&#8217;s located near Bakersfield, which is approximately two and half hours away. Remember, people, we&#8217;re only as strong as our weakest bladder.</p>

<p>Sheldon: All right. We have seven people, and two cars. In the lead car, driven by Leonard, will be myself, Amy Farrah Fowler and Penny.</p>
<p>Amy: Yes! He had you in the other car, but I got you upgraded.</p>
<p>Penny: Yay!</p>
<p>Sheldon: She made the case that if we break down in the middle of nowhere, your Nebraska backwoods skills and brawny hands will give us the best chance to survive in the wild.</p>
<p>Penny: Brawny?</p>
<p>Leonard: They&#8217;re bigger than mine.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Leonard’s car. </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Red Leader to Red Five, come in. Red Leader to Red Five, come in. Howard, you promised.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(voice)</em>: Fine.  Red Five to Red Leader. What do you want now?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It’s eleven fifteen. I&#8217;m requesting your quarter-hourly location update.</p>
<p>Howard: Still right behind you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Copy that, Red Five. Radio contact is sufficient. No need to extend your middle finger.</p>
<p>Penny: So, Amy, I&#8217;ve been wondering, are you and Sheldon going to be sharing a room?</p>
<p>Amy: No, we discussed it. We decided we didn&#8217;t want to jeopardize our relationship by getting to know each other too well.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Indeed. Nothing sours a friendship more than over-familiarity with someone&#8217;s toilet routine.</p>
<p>Leonard: I can vouch for that.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey.</p>
<p>Leonard: Not you, him.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh. Thanks.</p>
<p>Leonard: Although yours was an eye-opener.</p>
<p>Amy: Don&#8217;t worry, Penny. You&#8217;re my plus-one. You&#8217;ll bunk with me. And FYI, travel makes me constipated, so I&#8217;m the ideal hotel roommate.</p>
<p>Penny: Terrific. Are we there yet?</p>
<p><em>Scene: Bernadette’s car. </em></p>
<p>Bernadette: I hope we get there in time to see the keynote address.</p>
<p>Howard: Really? You want to see the keynote?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Yeah, it sounds fun. Super bacteria: global apocalypse or exciting research opportunity?</p>
<p>Howard: Actually, I was thinking we could go straight to the room and take a nap.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Really? And miss the keynote?</p>
<p>Howard: We can watch it later on C-SPAN. Besides, I was really looking forward to you and me napping together in the hotel.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Well honey, if you&#8217;re that tired, why don&#8217;t you just take a nap here in the car?</p>
<p>Howard: No, see, it&#8217;s not&#8230;</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hang on. It&#8217;s Leonard. Hi, Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(voice)</em>: Yeah, hi. Listen, I just got a text from Raj. He wanted me to tell you that when Howard says nap, he means sex.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh. Thank you, Raj.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Leonard’s car. </em></p>
<p>Leonard: Are you an element in the actinoid series?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. Amy?</p>
<p>Amy: Are you usually radioactive when found in nature?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No.</p>
<p>Amy: Are you in the lanthanoid series?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Amy, it&#8217;s Penny&#8217;s turn. Penny?</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, I don&#8217;t know. Are you food?</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s not apropos. We&#8217;ve already established I&#8217;m found in the periodic table.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, it&#8217;s a table, right? I mean, why can&#8217;t there be food on it?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I knew she wasn&#8217;t lead car material.</p>
<p>Penny: Who elected you Road Trip God?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: It was a late-night vote. We were all exhausted, and he was threatening to filibuster. It&#8217;s not technically Road Trip God, it&#8217;s Travel Supervisor.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Although Road Trip God does have a certain ring to it.</p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t understand why you people just let him bully you like this. Someone should stand up to him. What&#8217;s he going to do?</p>
<p><em>Scene: Bernadette’s car. </em></p>
<p>Penny: I can&#8217;t believe you let him kick me out of the car.</p>
<p>Howard: What could we do? He&#8217;s the Travel Supervisor.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Don&#8217;t worry, Penny. This is a better car anyway.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah. It&#8217;s the Love Car.</p>
<p>Penny: Should I ask?</p>
<p>Bernadette <em>(singing)</em>: They say we&#8217;re young and we don&#8217;t know, we won&#8217;t find out until we grow.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(singing)</em>: Well, I don&#8217;t know if all that&#8217;s true, &#8217;cause you got me, and, baby, I got you.</p>
<p>Together <em>(singing)</em>: Babe, mm da-da, da-da, da-da, I got you, babe, da-da, da-da, da-da, I got you, babe&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: Red Leader, I&#8217;m really sorry.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The hotel. </em></p>
<p>Howard and Bernadette <em>(singing)</em>: I got you, babe.</p>
<p>Amy: I missed you.</p>
<p>Penny: You know what? I missed you, too.</p>
<p>Very tall and powerfully built man: Bernadette?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, my God, Glenn!</p>
<p>Glenn: Great to see you!</p>
<p>Bernadette: Are you here for the conference?</p>
<p>Glenn: Yeah, I&#8217;m doing a global warming panel.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, good for you. Uh, Glenn, this is my boyfriend Howard.</p>
<p>Glenn: Oh. Nice to meet you.</p>
<p>Howard: Hi. Ow. Hi.</p>
<p>Glenn: You&#8217;re a lucky man. Bernie&#8217;s a great gal.</p>
<p>Howard: Yes. Bernie sure is.</p>
<p>Glenn: Well, I got to run. The panel&#8217;s tomorrow morning. It&#8217;s called, Remembering Snow: A Look Back.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;ll try to catch it.</p>
<p>Glenn: Oh, great. Bye.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Bye.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, Bernie?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Yeah?</p>
<p>Howard: Please tell me he&#8217;s your gay cousin.</p>
<p>Bernadette: No. He was one of my professors in college.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh! That&#8217;s a relief.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Then we went out for a year. Come on, let&#8217;s check in, so we can take that nap.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(after Raj whispers to him)</em>: No, it&#8217;s not necessarily proportional. Shut up!</p>
<p><em>Scene: Howard’s hotel room.</em></p>
<p>Howard: That green bag is Dr. Cooper&#8217;s. Here&#8217;s an extra five. Make him wait. Oh. Right to the nap, huh? Okay? Uh, so, this Glenn guy. You say you went out with him for, like, a year.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Do we really need to talk about my old boyfriend now?</p>
<p>Howard: No. I guess not. What is he, like, six-four, six-five?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Six-seven.</p>
<p>Howard: Probably has a hard time finding a suit that fits.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Is something bothering you?</p>
<p>Howard: No. It&#8217;s just&#8230;</p>
<p>Bernadette: What?</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m just thinking. If you had sex with that guy, I mean, there&#8217;s nothing I can do here that will make any kind of impact.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howard, it&#8217;s not a contest. I love you. I want to be with you.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, great, love you, too, but, if it were a contest, I wouldn&#8217;t have a chance, right?</p>
<p>Bernadette: You can&#8217;t think that way.</p>
<p>Howard: Yep. Loser.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howard, stop it.</p>
<p>Howard: Sorry. I just never figured that a guy like me going out with a girl like you would ever have to compete with a guy like that.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Wait a minute, a girl like me? What&#8217;s that mean?</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m&#8230; I&#8230;</p>
<p>Bernadette: Are you saying you don&#8217;t think I&#8217;m hot enough to go out with a guy like Glenn?</p>
<p>Howard: No! No, I&#8217;m saying exactly the opposite.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;m too hot to go out with a guy like Glenn?</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, let&#8217;s go with that.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Amy’s hotel room.</em></p>
<p>Amy <em>(exiting bathroom)</em>: Still nothing. Remind me to try again in an hour.</p>
<p>Penny: Will do.</p>
<p>Amy: So, girl talk?</p>
<p>Penny: Um, sure. What do you, what do you got in mind?</p>
<p>Amy: Do you subscribe to the Freudian theory of penis envy?</p>
<p>Penny: Um, I never really thought about it. Why?</p>
<p>Amy: Sometimes I think it might be nice to have one.</p>
<p>Penny: Really?</p>
<p>Amy: Not for sex, for convenience. You can&#8217;t deny that, by comparison, our internal plumbing is extremely high maintenance.</p>
<p>Penny: Again, I&#8217;ve never given it much thought.</p>
<p>Amy: We have time now. Think about it.</p>
<p>Penny <em>(knock on door)</em>: Oh, good. Hey.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Can I stay here tonight?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah. Why, what happened?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howard&#8217;s a complete and total ass.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, yeah, that. Come on in.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Thanks. I&#8217;ll sleep on the floor.</p>
<p>Amy: Not necessary. Penny and I are perfectly comfortable sharing a bed.</p>
<p>Penny: We are?</p>
<p>Amy: Of course, we&#8217;re best friends.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, right, right, the blog.</p>
<p>Amy: Word of warning, though. I&#8217;m prone to night terrors, so if I wake up kicking and screaming, don&#8217;t panic. Just pin me down and stroke my hair, and I&#8217;ll be fine.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Leonard’s room. </em></p>
<p>Penny: Hey, can I stay here tonight?</p>
<p>Leonard: Shh-shh-shh-shh. Sheldon&#8217;s asleep. What&#8217;s going on?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, Howard&#8217;s a complete and total ass, Bernadette&#8217;s in my bed, and no matter how much you stroke Amy&#8217;s hair, she bites.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {

					return;

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
