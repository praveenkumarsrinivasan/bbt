		<h2 class="title">Series 5 Episode 13 &#8211; The Recombination&nbsp;Hypothesis</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The stairwell.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: You feel like trying something new for dinner? Maybe Indian, Tex-Mex?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You ever wonder how humans would be different if they evolved from lizards instead of mammals?</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, let&#8217;s talk about that.</p>
<p>Sheldon: As you know, lizards, cold-blooded animals, lack the ability to sense temperature. But they do move more sluggishly when it&#8217;s cold. So, lizard weathermen would say things like, bring a sweater, it&#8217;s slow outside. I love my mind.</p>
<p>Leonard: We all do. Now, how about dinner?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I would assume we&#8217;d enjoy insects or smaller lizards. We could also pull each other&#8217;s tails off and grill them, they&#8217;ll just grow back. Oh! My life-size cardboard Mr. Spock is here! I know he wouldn&#8217;t care for an outburst of human emotion, but, oh goodie, oh goodie, oh goodie. Commander Spock requesting permission to be unfolded.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(seeing Penny&#8217;s door open and the girls inside)</em>: Excuse me.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Permission granted, Commander.</p>
<p>Amy: Which is why the more intelligent the monkey, the more faeces they fling.</p>
<p>Leonard: Excuse me, Amy. Penny, do you have plans for dinner tonight?</p>
<p>Penny: Why, you guys going somewhere?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, I mean just you and me.</p>
<p>Penny: You mean, like a date?</p>
<p>Leonard: Not like a date, a date.</p>
<p>Amy: Ooh!</p>
<p>Bernadette: Ooh!</p>
<p>Penny: Um, o-okay. Sure.</p>
<p>Amy: Ooh!</p>
<p>Bernadette: Ooh!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, no! They sent the wrong Spock! Live long and suck it, Zachary Quinto.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s bedroom.</em></p>
<p>Bernadette: This is so exciting. If Leonard and Penny get back together, Howard and I can go on double dates with them.</p>
<p>Amy: How come you never invite Sheldon and me on a double date?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Mm, uh&#8230; How&#8217;s it going in there, Penny?</p>
<p>Penny: Just a sec. <em>(Emerges in revealing slinky green dress) </em>Too much?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Yes.</p>
<p>Amy: No.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, just hang on.</p>
<p>Amy: You just can&#8217;t handle her raw sexuality, can you?</p>
<p>Bernadette: When did you and Leonard break up?</p>
<p>Penny: Uh, about two years ago.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Why do you think he asked you out again?</p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t know.</p>
<p>Amy: Maybe he&#8217;s dying. That would be so romantic.</p>
<p>Penny: He&#8217;s not dying.</p>
<p>Amy: Too bad. If he were, she could just throw him in bed and ride him right up until he flatlines.</p>
<p>Penny: How about this?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Maybe.</p>
<p>Amy: Come on.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, okay, no.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Do you think you&#8217;ll sleep with him tonight?</p>
<p>Penny: Absolutely not. Look, we&#8217;re just gonna have dinner and, you know, see how it goes.</p>
<p>Amy: If he were dying, would you sleep with him?</p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Amy: Assuming he were dying of something that couldn&#8217;t be sexually transmitted. You know, like a spear wound to the head.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, he is not dying.</p>
<p>Amy: How do you know? Are you a doctor?</p>
<p>Bernadette: You can make-believe, though. Sometimes Howard and I pretend that his arrhythmia is acting up and I&#8217;m a sexy cardiologist. And the naughty part is I&#8217;m not in his HMO network.</p>
<p>Penny: Screw it. I&#8217;m not gonna make a big deal out of this. It&#8217;s just dinner.</p>
<p>Amy: With a dead man.</p>
<p>Penny: Amy, stop it. Oh, God, I&#8217;m so nervous.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Relax. You know Leonard&#8217;s always been crazy about you. It&#8217;s gonna be great.</p>
<p>Penny: I know, but we&#8217;ve finally gotten to a place where we can hang out without it being weird. And what if something goes wrong? Then what?</p>
<p>Amy: Guess it&#8217;ll just be Sheldon and me going on a double date with Howard and Bernadette.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Sure, we&#8217;d love that. Change your clothes, we got a lot riding on this.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. The guys are playing a board game.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Mm. I want to build a road, but I need wood. Do either of you fellows have wood? <em>(Raj and Howard snigger) </em>I don&#8217;t understand the laughter. The object of Settlers of Catan is to build roads and settlements. To do so requires wood. Now, I have sheep; I need wood. Who has wood for my sheep?</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, how do I look?</p>
<p>Howard: Well, more to the point, why are you doing this?</p>
<p>Leonard: What are you talking about?</p>
<p>Raj: Did you forget what Penny did to you? It took you two years and defiling my sister to turn that frown upside down.</p>
<p>Leonard: I didn&#8217;t defile your sister, we had a relationship.</p>
<p>Raj: I heard you call her Brown Sugar. In my book, that&#8217;s defilement.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You want to know my opinion?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, boy, do I?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Sarcasm?</p>
<p>Howard: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, then. The reason you&#8217;re fixated on a good-natured simpleton like Penny is that she&#8217;s the exact opposite of your first romantic attachment, your brilliant yet intimidating mother.</p>
<p>Leonard: Where on earth did you get that from?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s in her book, Needy Baby, Greedy Baby.</p>
<p>Leonard: That doesn&#8217;t make it true.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s called non-fiction for a reason, Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: See you later.</p>
<p>Raj: If they ever make a movie version of that book, you know who should play Leonard&#8217;s mother? Sandra Bullock.</p>
<p>Howard: Why?</p>
<p>Raj: Because she&#8217;s great in everything.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Now, where were we? Oh, yes. Does anyone have any wood? Oh, come on! I just want wood. Why are you making it so hard?</p>
<p><em>Scene: A restaurant.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: So, do they have a name for a first date with someone you used to go out with?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, that&#8217;s a good question. How about awkward?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, that sounds right.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah?</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, how about if we pretend we&#8217;re actually on a first date? See how that goes.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, Polly, tell me about yourself.</p>
<p>Penny: It&#8217;s Penny.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, sorry, yeah. Awkward.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, uh, let&#8217;s see. I&#8217;m from Nebraska, and ever since I was a little girl, I dreamed of moving to L.A. and becoming a movie star. Anyway, after four years of acting lessons and waiting tables, I&#8217;ve done a haemorrhoid commercial and a production of Anne Frank above a bowling alley. So, you know, dreams do come true. Your turn.</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, let&#8217;s see. Uh, I am an experimental physicist at Cal-Tech, most of my research is with high-powered lasers, and, oh, I&#8217;ve just gotten a big government grant to see if they can be used to knock out incoming ballistic missiles.</p>
<p>Penny: Wow. Can they?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, God, no. The money&#8217;s pretty good. And I used the equipment to make my own Bat-signal.</p>
<p>Penny: Bat-signal? What are you, some kind of nerd?</p>
<p>Leonard: Not some kind of nerd, I am the king of the nerds.</p>
<p>Penny: What does that mean?</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, it means if anyone displeases me, I don&#8217;t help them set up their printer.</p>
<p>Penny: You are so funny.</p>
<p>Leonard: Good. Remember that when I take my shirt off.</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, this is nice. I&#8217;m so glad we did this.</p>
<p>Leonard: Me, too. So what do you think? Are we gonna get back together?</p>
<p>Penny: Whoa. Not so fast.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m sorry, what did I say?</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, you know I will always have feelings for you.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, God.</p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Leonard: You said always. You&#8217;ll always have feelings for me.</p>
<p>Penny: So?</p>
<p>Leonard: So, that sounds more like something you&#8217;d say if you didn&#8217;t want a relationship with someone. This isn&#8217;t working out, but I&#8217;ll always have feelings for you. I&#8217;m sorry I slept with your best friend, but I&#8217;ll always have feelings for you. Here&#8217;s the thing, Lisa, I&#8217;m into dudes now, but I&#8217;ll always have feelings for you.</p>
<p>Penny: How would you say it?</p>
<p>Leonard: I have feelings for you.</p>
<p>Penny: It&#8217;s the same thing.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, it&#8217;s not. Always made it worse.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re overthinking this.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, I&#8217;m not.</p>
<p>Penny: Yes, you are. You always overthink things.</p>
<p>Leonard: Ah now th-th-there you go. Always made it worse.</p>
<p>Penny: See, this is where everything goes wrong, when we talk.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, I don&#8217;t know how you have a relationship without talking.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, I went out with this guy TJ for eight months, we never talked. To this day, I don&#8217;t even know what TJ stands for.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wait, if you guys didn&#8217;t talk, what did you&#8230; never mind, stupid question.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: And now that I have some wood, I&#8217;m going to begin the erection of my settlement.</p>
<p>Raj: He&#8217;s got to be doing this on purpose.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(as Leonard enters)</em>: Ooh, eight thirty. You and Penny decide to go out and paint the town beige?</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re thirty years old and you live with your mother.</p>
<p>Raj: I guess it didn&#8217;t go well.</p>
<p>Sheldon: We don&#8217;t know that. Not to a certainty. All we know is that Leonard is home.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(as Leonard groans loudly in his room)</em>: How about now?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Again, not enough evidence. For all we know, he&#8217;s being murdered. Now, back to our game.</p>
<p>Raj: You were in the middle of an erection.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, of course. It&#8217;s right here in my hand.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Leonard&#8217;s bedroom. Leonard&#8217;s phone text signal sounds.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: What does she want from me now? <em>(Goes to front door, where Penny is waiting) </em>What&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Penny: Do not overthink this. <em>(Kisses him, then leads him to her apartment.) </em></p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t understand.</p>
<p>Penny: Bup-bup-bup-bup-bup-bup-bup.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, but earlier it seemed like&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: No talking.</p>
<p>Leonard: Even during? &#8216;Cause sometimes I have questions.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s bedroom. They are in bed.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Well, that was fun.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, except for when I got the foot cramp.</p>
<p>Penny: You hung in there, though.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, there was a lot at stake.</p>
<p>Penny: So what do you want to do now?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, I want to go get my asthma inhaler, but it might ruin the moment. Just, just help me out here. How does a miserable date end in sex?</p>
<p>Penny: I don&#8217;t know, it&#8217;s complicated.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, I&#8217;m a pretty smart guy, and right now my brain has dibs on the blood supply, so give it a go.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, it&#8217;s just, at the restaurant when you said you wanted us to be together again, it got very real very fast and I panicked.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why? What are you afraid of?</p>
<p>Penny: Well, what if we do go out and I do something stupid and dump you again?</p>
<p>Leonard: What if I dump you?</p>
<p>Penny: Come on, be serious.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, how about if we don&#8217;t think about this as a relationship? It could be more like a new version of software. Penny and Leonard 2.0. We can test it internally, shake out the bugs, and if we both feel it&#8217;s solid, then we&#8217;ll roll it out to the public.</p>
<p>Penny: So we don&#8217;t tell people we&#8217;re back together?</p>
<p>Leonard: Exactly. We pretend like our date went badly.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, we don&#8217;t really have to pretend.</p>
<p>Leonard: And we let them think that we decided to just be friends and that everything&#8217;s cool.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, great. Hey, I just remembered. I still got one of your inhalers.</p>
<p>Leonard: I can&#8217;t believe you kept this.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, I was gonna throw it away, but I just couldn&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Leonard: That is so sweet. In 25 to 30 minutes I&#8217;m gonna show you how much this means to me.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. Leonard is sneaking back in.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, hi, hi. How&#8217;s it going?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Uh, can&#8217;t complain. Thanks for asking. Well, what were you doing out at three o&#8217;clock in the morning?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, uh, uh, what are you doing up?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I was using the bathroom.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, well, so was I.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Really? I didn&#8217;t see you in there.</p>
<p>Leonard: Obviously, when I saw that you were in ours, I went and used another one.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Where?</p>
<p>Leonard: The, the gas station across the street.</p>
<p>Sheldon: In your pyjamas?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Without shoes?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: On a cold winter&#8217;s night?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Seems unlikely. Did you bring your asthma inhaler?</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, uh, uh, yes, I did.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, then, I guess it&#8217;s plausible. Hang on a second, mister.</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: After you used the facilities at the gas station, did you make a purchase?</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s customary, when using the rest room at a retail establishment, to make a small purchase. Did you?</p>
<p>Leonard: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Here&#8217;s two dollars. Go buy some beef jerky.</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t want beef jerky.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s not about you. It&#8217;s about a poor immigrant from Pakistan trying to make his way in America by working the graveyard shift at the Colorado Boulevard Chevron.</p>
<p>Leonard: Fine.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Needy Baby, Greedy Baby indeed.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The Cheesecake Factory.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard will be here in a moment. He&#8217;s looking for a different parking space.</p>
<p>Howard: Why?</p>
<p>Sheldon: We were next to a car with an ask me about my grandchildren bumper sticker, and I was afraid if we ran into them on the way out, I&#8217;d be obligated to do so.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;m so disappointed it didn&#8217;t work with him and Penny.</p>
<p>Amy: Me, too. Out of deference to them, let&#8217;s not flaunt our happy relationship.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You have a keen insight into the human heart, Amy Farrah Fowler.</p>

					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
