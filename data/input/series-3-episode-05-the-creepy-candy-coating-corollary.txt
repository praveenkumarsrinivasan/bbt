		<h2 class="title">Series 3 Episode 05 &#8211; The Creepy Candy Coating&nbsp;Corollary</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Howard: All right, Raj has played his phantom warlord card, and I am going to back him up with my strangling vines. Choke on that, sucka.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, well, then I&#8217;ll just cut your vines with my ruby sword. That&#8217;s right, I did it. I cut &#8216;em.</p>
<p>Penny: Um, I have a question.</p>
<p>Leonard: Warlord beats troll, troll beats elf, elf beats water sprite, and basically everything beats enchanted bunny.</p>
<p>Howard: Unless you have the carrot power.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, I&#8217;ve got another question. When does this get fun?</p>
<p>Howard: Are we going to talk or are we going to play Mystic Warlords of Ka-ah?</p>
<p>Leonard: Just play a potion card.</p>
<p>Penny: Which one?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It doesn&#8217;t matter. You can&#8217;t possibly win.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, don&#8217;t ruin the game.</p>
<p>Penny: How could he ruin the game?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Given the cards that have already been played, Penny can only be holding necromancer potions, which are only effective against wraiths and day-walkers, and there are no more of either left to be drawn. The cards remaining in the undrawn stack are four fire weapons, a troll, two ogres and the jewel of Osiris.</p>
<p>Leonard: See? Ruined.</p>
<p>Penny: Sheldon, that is incredible.</p>
<p>Sheldon: From your vantage point, it certainly must seem so.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon has kind of a photographic memory.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Photographic is a misnomer. I have an eidetic memory, as I&#8217;ve told you many times. Most recently last year during lunch on the afternoon of may seventh. You had turkey and complained it was dry.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, I guess game&#8217;s over.</p>
<p>Penny: Really? Oh, great. I mean, aw. Okay, I gotta go.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why?</p>
<p>Penny: Because the last me I didn&#8217;t go, I ended up playing Mystic Warlords of Ka.</p>
<p>Howard: Not Ka. Ka-ah.</p>
<p>Penny: Ba-eye.</p>
<p>Leonard: See ya. Still can&#8217;t believe she&#8217;s going out with me.</p>
<p>Raj: Nobody can.</p>
<p>Howard: That reminds me, I have a bone to pick with you.</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Howard: You and I made a pact that if either of us ever got a hot girlfriend, that person would have his girlfriend hook the other guy up with one of her girlfriends.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, I don&#8217;t remember that.</p>
<p>Sheldon: June 30th, 2004. Opening day of Spider-man 2 at the AMC Pasadena. They only had red icees, no blue.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, yeah.</p>
<p>Howard: So you&#8217;ve been with Penny for like a month and a half now. Where&#8217;s my shorty, Morty?</p>
<p>Leonard: Howard, you can&#8217;t hold me to that.</p>
<p>Howard: Why not?</p>
<p>Leonard: Because when I made that agreement, I didn&#8217;t think I&#8217;d ever have a hot girlfriend. And I was positive you never would.</p>
<p>Raj: Hey, how come I wasn&#8217;t part of this deal?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You had left the refreshment stand in order to indulge in your customary preemptive pre-show urination.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, so that&#8217;s how it works? I have a teeny bladder and now I don&#8217;t get a hot girlfriend?</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, Raj, that&#8217;s how it works.</p>
<p>Raj: Damn.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>Scene: The university cafeteria.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, damn it. Can I have a napkin?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry, no.</p>
<p>Leonard: But you have a whole bunch of &#8216;em.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, I&#8217;ve moved to a four-napkin system. Lap, hands, face and personal emergencies. If you like, starting tomorrow, I&#8217;ll add a guest napkin, but I&#8217;m afraid there&#8217;s nothing I can do for you today. <em>(Leonard steals a napkin) </em>Good luck. That&#8217;s the face napkin.</p>
<p>Howard: So, have you talked to Penny yet?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, I haven&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Howard: Why not?</p>
<p>Leonard: Because I&#8217;ve been busy, because I haven&#8217;t figured out a way to bring it up, and mostly, and I can&#8217;t stress how key this is, because I don&#8217;t want to.</p>
<p>Howard: Leonard, a pact is a pact. You have to get Penny to fix me up.</p>
<p>Leonard: It&#8217;s not that simple. What am I supposed to say? Penny, do you have any friends you&#8217;d like to never hear from again?</p>
<p>Howard: Come on, I&#8217;m smart, I have good job and I have only three percent body fat.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s true. I&#8217;ve seen him at the beach, he&#8217;s like a human chicken wing.</p>
<p>Howard: Leonard, come on.</p>
<p>Leonard: Fine. I&#8217;ll ask if she has a friend for you.</p>
<p>Howard: A hot friend.</p>
<p>Leonard: Right.</p>
<p>Howard: And tall. I want our kids to be able to ride space mountain before they&#8217;re 20.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;ll see what I can do.</p>
<p>Raj: Hey, Sheldon, guess what I heard today?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;d imagine you heard any number of things today. When you arrived at work you undoubtedly heard, hello, Raj, how are you, Raj? Given that you&#8217;re wearing a new sweater-vest, you may have heard new sweater-vest? And possibly, though far less likely, nice sweater-vest.</p>
<p>Raj: Why don&#8217;t I just tell you what I heard today.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That would probably save us some time.</p>
<p>Raj: Saturday night at the comic book store, they&#8217;re having a Mystic Warlords of Ka-ah tournament. First prize is five hundred dollars. If we team up, we&#8217;d be unstoppable.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry, Raj, but I have no interest in playing a game in which I find no challenge.</p>
<p>Raj: What about the money?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I have money.</p>
<p>Raj: But this is other money.</p>
<p>Sheldon: How does it different from the money I have?</p>
<p>Raj: Half of it will be mine.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Do you need it to buy a less disturbing sweater-vest?</p>
<p>Raj: Leonard, help.</p>
<p>Leonard: Are you kidding? I couldn&#8217;t even talk him into giving me one of his freakin&#8217; napkins.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Leonard and Penny are in bed.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Wow, you really are a genius.</p>
<p>Leonard: Not really. I googled how to do that. So, listen, have you ever made a pact with someone?</p>
<p>Penny: You mean, like a pinky swear?</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, fine, like a pinky swear.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, in the first grade, my friend Rosie and I made a pact to marry Bert and Ernie. You know, from Sesame Street?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, I&#8217;m familiar with Bert and Ernie.</p>
<p>Penny: Then we found out we both wanted Ernie. We didn&#8217;t speak again until middle school.</p>
<p>Leonard: Over puppets?</p>
<p>Penny: The heart wants what the heart wants, Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay. Speaking of what the heart wants, um, a long time ago, I made a pact with Wolowitz that kind of involves you.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, I don&#8217;t know where you&#8217;re going with this, but tread carefully because it may be the last conversation we ever have.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, no, nothing like that. The deal was that if either of us ever got a girlfriend, we’d have her fix the other one up with one of her friends.</p>
<p>Penny: And you thought a good time to bring this up be right after sex.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, I sure as hell wasn’t going to bring it up before sex, and during, I was trying to remember what I read on google, so&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m not hooking Wolowitz up with one of my friends.</p>
<p>Leonard: Come on, it doesn&#8217;t have to be a good friend. And you know that deep down inside, Howard&#8217;s a really nice guy.</p>
<p>Penny: The problem isn&#8217;t what&#8217;s on the inside. It&#8217;s the creepy candy coating.</p>
<p>Leonard: Will you at least think about it? Just as a favour to me?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, great thing about Ernie, was he never asked me for anything. He just gave.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The comic book store.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Got it. Got it. Got it. Got it. Got it. Got it. Betty and Veronica? Ugh.</p>
<p>Stuart: Hey, Sheldon, the new Green Lantern figurine&#8217;s coming in tomorrow. Want me to set one aside for you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thank you. You just robbed me of the opportunity to stumble upon the figurine and make the oh-so-satisfying journey from discovery to desire to possession.</p>
<p>Stuart: All right, I won&#8217;t set one aside for you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: But I must have it.</p>
<p>Stuart: Okay, I&#8217;ll set one aside for you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thank you. You know, I can buy all these things online. I come here for the personal service.</p>
<p>Raj: Hey, Stuart, is the Wil Wheaton signed up for the mystic warlords tournament THE Wil Wheaton from Star Trek?</p>
<p>Stuart: Yeah, he lives around here. Big gamer.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Excuse me. Are you saying that Wil Wheaton aka Ensign Wesley Crusher on Star Trek:</p>
<p>The Next Generation is going to be participating in your tournament?</p>
<p>Stuart: Oh, I&#8217;m sorry, did I rob you of the opportunity to stumble onto that for yourself?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You don&#8217;t understand. Growing up, I idolized Wil Wheaton? Wesley Crusher had an eidetic memory just like me.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, what a coincidence. Maybe you can discuss that with him while you’re playing in the tournament. Sign here.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I was such a fan that in 1995, I travelled ten hours by bus to a sci-fi convention in Jackson, Mississippi, wearing my Star Fleet Academy cadet uniform in order to meet Wil Wheaton and get him to autograph my mint in-package Wesley Crusher action figure.</p>
<p>Raj: Ooh, it&#8217;ll be like a reunion then. Sign here.</p>
<p>Sheldon: My arduous journey, however, was for naught. Although advertised to appear, he did not show up. It was at that moment, I vowed eternal hatred for Wil Wheaton.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, great, you can tell him you hate him. Sign here.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It might also interest you to know that Wil Wheaton currently ranks sixth on my all-time enemies list, right between director Joel Schumacher, who nearly destroyed the Batman movie franchise, and Billy Sparks, who lived down the street from me and put dog poop on the handles of my bicycle.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, I get it, he&#8217;s a bad guy. Sign here.</p>
<p>Sheldon: In the words of Khan Noonien Singh in the immortal wrath of Khan, he tasks me, he tasks me and I shall have him.</p>
<p>Raj: No doubt, sign here.</p>
<p>Sheldon: From hell&#8217;s heart, I stab at thee.</p>
<p>Stuart: All right, Raj, looks like you&#8217;re teamed up with die, Wil Wheaton, die.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Leonard’s car.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Howard: So, tell me more about the future mother of my children.</p>
<p>Penny: She&#8217;s adorable, Howard. I think you&#8217;ll like her.</p>
<p>Howard: Great. So what did you tell her about me? Did you mention the body fat?</p>
<p>Penny: No, I thought that would be a nice surprise for her.</p>
<p>Howard: Good, good.</p>
<p>Penny: I just told her you&#8217;re an aerospace engineer, you speak five languages&#8230;</p>
<p>Howard: Six if you count Klingon.</p>
<p>Leonard: Girls don&#8217;t count Klingon, Howard. Right?</p>
<p>Penny: Right. Oh, and I told her you have an unhealthy attachment to your mother.</p>
<p>Howard: What?</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m kidding.</p>
<p>Leonard: Another delightful surprise for her.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The comic book store.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Sheldon, it&#8217;s your play. Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: My enchanted troll bludgeons your screaming harpy with a cursed mace. Game.</p>
<p>Raj: Sorry, boys. Say hi to your mother when she picks you up. Or I could just tell her later tonight! We pwned them, dude! Up top!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Look at him. Wil Wheaton, my old friend, I have chased you round the moons of Nibia and round the Antares maelstrom and round perdition&#8217;s flames!</p>
<p>Raj: You know you keep quoting Wrath of Khan, but he was in next generation. It&#8217;s a totally different set of characters.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Silence! How much longer must I wait for my revenge?</p>
<p>Raj: Well, if Wil Wheaton and Stuart win their match and we beat lonely Larry and Captain Sweatpants in the next round, we&#8217;ll face off for the championship.</p>
<p>Sheldon: So, my path to satisfaction is blocked by lonely Larry and Captain Sweatpants. Very well. They must be destroyed.</p>
<p>Raj: Dude, you have to stop talking like that. It&#8217;s really lame.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Silence!</p>
<p><em>Scene: Leonard’s car.</em></p>
<p>Howard: So, Penny tells me you&#8217;re working as a waitress to put yourself through grad school. That&#8217;s pretty great, what are you studying?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Microbiology.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, cool. So you could study me.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I don&#8217;t understand.</p>
<p>Howard: Microbiology is the study of tiny living things.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I know, I&#8217;m studying it.</p>
<p>Howard: And I said you could study me &#8217;cause I&#8217;m a tiny living thing. It&#8217;s a joke.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Are you sure?</p>
<p>Howard: Do you like science-fiction?</p>
<p>Bernadette: No.</p>
<p>Howard: Role-playing games?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Like in the bedroom or like Dungeons and Dragons?</p>
<p>Howard: Either.</p>
<p>Bernadette: No.</p>
<p>Leonard: Gonna be a long night.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, well, it&#8217;s your fault.</p>
<p>Leonard: I had to ask. You didn&#8217;t have to say yes.</p>
<p>Howard: You like magic?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Not really.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay.</p>
<p>Leonard: Long, long night.</p>
<p>S<em>cene: The comic book store.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: I play my endless serpent. Raj will play ecstatic frenzy, which means Larry will have to play chaos overlord, then Captain Sweatpants, molten river. I play nightshade dryad, game, set and match. Now fetch me Wil Wheaton! bortaS bIr jablu&#8217;DI&#8217; reH QaQqu&#8217; nay&#8217;</p>
<p>Wil Wheaton: Did that guy just say revenge is a dish best served cold in Klingon?</p>
<p>Stuart: I believe so.</p>
<p>Wil Wheaton: What is wrong with him?</p>
<p>Stuart: Everyone has a different theory.</p>
<p><em>Scene: A restaurant.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Howard: How about computers? Do you like computers?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I use them. I don&#8217;t like them.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay&#8230; Puppies? Where do you stand on puppies?</p>
<p>Bernadette: A puppy once bit my face.</p>
<p>Howard: Of course it did.</p>
<p>Leonard: How about that? Einstein was wrong.</p>
<p>Penny: What?</p>
<p>Leonard: Approaching the speed of light doesn&#8217;t slow down time. Approaching them does.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(phone rings)</em>: Excuse me. Oh, damn. It&#8217;s my mother.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Are you going to answer it?</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m torn. She might be dying, and, you know, I wouldn&#8217;t want to miss that. On the other hand, if I let it go to voicemail, I could play it over and over.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I know how you feel. My mother makes me crazy.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );

				}
