		<h2 class="title">Series 4 Episode 22 &#8211; The Wildebeest&nbsp;Implementation</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: A public washroom. Amy and Penny are in cubicles, Bernadette is washing her hands.</em></p>
<p>Amy: I must say, Penny, this is great fun.</p>
<p>Penny: Glad you&#8217;re enjoying yourself.</p>
<p>Amy: Until I met you and Bernadette, my trips to the bathroom had been entirely focused on elimination. Now they have a delightful social aspect.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Amy, you must&#8217;ve been in the bathroom with other women before.</p>
<p>Amy: Of course I have. But they were strangers and seemed off-put when I engaged in friendly stall-to-stall chit-chat.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Some women don&#8217;t like to get chummy when their panties are down.</p>
<p>Amy: You okay in there, bestie?</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m fine.</p>
<p>Amy: The reason I ask is that many people experience bladder shyness, the inability to pass urine</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, I said I&#8217;m fine. Stop talking to me.</p>
<p>Amy: She always this crabby when she urinates?</p>
<p>Bernadette: We&#8217;re really not that close.</p>
<p>Penny: Screw it. I&#8217;ll go later.</p>
<p>Amy: And I&#8217;ll be right by your side.</p>
<p><em>Scene: A shoe shop. </em></p>
<p>Bernadette: Did I tell you Priya invited me and Howard to have dinner with her and Leonard?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, that&#8217;s nice.</p>
<p>Amy: No, it&#8217;s not. It&#8217;s a strategic manoeuvre. Leonard&#8217;s new girlfriend is testing Bernadette&#8217;s loyalty to you and the group. That bitch is crafty.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You think?</p>
<p>Amy: Of course. How does the cheetah attack a herd of wildebeests? By going after its weakest member.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Well, what makes me the weakest member?</p>
<p>Amy: Your trusting nature coupled with your teeny-tiny body. You wouldn&#8217;t last a minute on the Serengeti.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, Amy, you&#8217;re being silly. I am not concerned about who hangs out with who. And I certainly don&#8217;t have a problem with Leonard&#8217;s new girlfriend who wears way too much makeup. Ooh, these are cute. Of course if I buy them, I&#8217;ll have to rent my womb out to a gay couple.</p>
<p>Bernadette: It doesn&#8217;t matter. I&#8217;m going to tell her we can&#8217;t make it.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, no. You have to go.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I don&#8217;t understand. I thought I was a teeny-tiny wildebeest.</p>
<p>Amy: You are, with hair that smells like strawberries. And we&#8217;re going to use that to our advantage.</p>
<p>Penny: Wait. What are you talking about?</p>
<p>Amy: By accepting the invitation, Bernadette becomes a double agent inside the enemy camp. She could ferret out Priya&#8217;s tactics, identify her weaknesses and feed her false information, such as, Leonard&#8217;s no stranger to back-alley cockfights.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I don&#8217;t know. I&#8217;m not a very good liar. They kind of whup that out of you in Catholic school.</p>
<p>Amy: Don&#8217;t worry. I&#8217;ll teach you. I did two years of Cub Scouts before they found out I was a girl.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, I don&#8217;t know you people. I&#8217;m just an innocent woman wondering if this shoe store will take my Texaco card.</p>
<p>Amy: How do you feel about concealing a recording device in the cleavage of your ample bosom?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I don&#8217;t want anything in my ample bosom.</p>
<p>Amy: Come on, Strawberries. Take one for the team.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence </em></p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, see you later.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Good news. I finally have a handle on my idea for three-person chess.</p>
<p>Leonard: That is good news. Bye.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Uh, do you know how I solved the balanced centre combat-area problem? Five words, transitional quadrilateral to triangular tessellation.</p>
<p>Leonard: That, that&#8217;s brilliant.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s what I do. But wait, there&#8217;s more. I also invented two new chess pieces. The serpent, and the old woman.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, now I have to ask. What do they do?</p>
<p>Sheldon: When the serpent slithers to an opposing player&#8217;s piece, that piece is considered poisoned and will die after two more moves. Ugh.</p>
<p>Leonard: All right.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Unless, it gets to the old woman in time, in which case she sucks out the poison, turning her into the Grand Empress, a piece combining the power of the knight, queen and serpent.</p>
<p>Leonard: Elegant.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s because it&#8217;s simple.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, well, I look forward to playing with you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And&#8230;</p>
<p>Leonard: And what?</p>
<p>Sheldon: And a third person. It&#8217;s three-person chess. I must say, ever since you started having regular intercourse, your mind has lost its keen edge. You should reflect on that.</p>
<p>Leonard: Excuse me, but Einstein had a pretty busy sex life.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, and he never unified gravity with the other forces. If he hadn&#8217;t been such a hound dog, we&#8217;d all have time machines.</p>
<p>Leonard: Got it. Bye.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You know I&#8217;m right.</p>
<p>Leonard <em>(outside apartment)</em>: I think he&#8217;s getting worse.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, my metatarsal are barking.</p>
<p>Leonard: You okay?</p>
<p>Amy: Yeah, yeah. I&#8217;m just breaking in some new shoes.</p>
<p>Leonard: Very pretty.</p>
<p>Amy: Thank you. Did you know that women wear high heels to make the buttocks and breasts more prominent?</p>
<p>Leonard: Hadn&#8217;t really thought about it.</p>
<p>Amy: Look.</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, sure. Very&#8230; prominent.</p>
<p>Amy: Please, Leonard, don&#8217;t leer, you have a girlfriend.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sorry.</p>
<p>Amy: So, are you off to dinner with Priya, Howard and Bernadette?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah. How did you know?</p>
<p>Amy: I heard it at the mall, when I was shopping with my girlfriends, &#8217;cause, you know, that&#8217;s kind of my life now. Have a good night. Try not to ogle my caboose as I walk away.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Priya: Hey, you.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hi. Hey, Raj, will you be joining us for dinner?</p>
<p>Raj: The lonely guy and the two happy couples? I&#8217;d rather get a prostate exam from a leper who walks away with nine fingers.</p>
<p>Priya: Oh, would you please stop feeling sorry for yourself?</p>
<p>Raj: I have to feel sorry for myself. I&#8217;m the only one who cares. Just like I&#8217;m the only one who&#8217;ll have sex with me.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really? In front of your sister?</p>
<p>Priya: We shared a room growing up. This is not news to me.</p>
<p>Raj: Excuse me. I&#8217;m going to go wander the streets alone. Invisible, unwanted and unloved, a pathetic shadow in a city with no heart. <em>(Leaves, then returns a moment later) </em>I forgot my windbreaker. It&#8217;s chilly.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment. </em></p>
<p>Amy: I don&#8217;t understand. Is this a way to break in new shoes?</p>
<p>Penny: No. Once these puppies touch the ground they&#8217;re mine, and I&#8217;ll have to wear them walking up and down Hollywood Boulevard in order to pay them off. Pretty, pretty, pretty.</p>
<p>Amy: Good news: the wildebeest is in the curry.</p>
<p>Penny: The what?</p>
<p>Amy: Bernadette is with Priya and Leonard. Message received. Commence operation &#8220;Priya Wouldn&#8217;t Wanna Be-ya.&#8221;</p>
<p>Penny: Hey. Seriously? Didn&#8217;t you get enough of this cliquey crap in high school?</p>
<p>Amy: I wish. A clique requires friends. I didn&#8217;t have any.</p>
<p>Penny: None?</p>
<p>Amy: I used to take my lunch down to the maintenance room and eat with the janitor. It was nice until his wife called me a puta and made me stop. Hang on. It&#8217;s the wildebeest. Priya just made a snide comment about your acting career.</p>
<p>Penny: Wh&#8230; What the hell did she say?</p>
<p>Amy: She thinks it&#8217;s cool you&#8217;re following your dream, no matter what.</p>
<p>Penny: That bitch!</p>
<p>Amy: How do you want to handle it?</p>
<p>Penny: Um, okay. Tell Bernadette to tell Priya that I&#8217;m on my way to Prague to shoot a movie with Angelina Jolie.</p>
<p>Amy: Got it. Is it going to be in 3-D?</p>
<p>Penny: What? I don&#8217;t know. It doesn&#8217;t matter.</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;m gonna say 3-D. That&#8217;ll let her know the studio has faith in it.</p>
<p>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment.</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re kidding. 3-D?</p>
<p>Bernadette: That&#8217;s what I hear.</p>
<p>Howard: Then the studio must have real faith in it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wonder why she didn&#8217;t tell me.</p>
<p>Priya: Have you been spending time with your ex-girlfriend?</p>
<p>Leonard: No.</p>
<p>Priya: Then why are you surprised she didn&#8217;t tell you?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, it&#8217;s not as much surprised as, uh, uh, you know, uh. th-th-th-the other thing.</p>
<p>Priya: What other thing?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, you know, if you, if, if, if you, uh, I don&#8217;t, oh, what&#8217;s the word I&#8217;m looking for?</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m not going to help you. This is hilarious.</p>
<p>Bernadette: She&#8217;s also dating an astronaut.</p>
<p>Priya: Wow. That&#8217;s very impressive.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Yeah. But Leonard&#8217;s impressive, too.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thank you. Befuddled. The word I was looking for was befuddled.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. Sheldon is playing three person chess by himself. </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Knight to old woman six-and-a-third. Brilliant move. Thank you. <em>(Knock on door) </em>Will the two of you excuse me?</p>
<p>Raj: I need a hug.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Sorry, I have company.</p>
<p>Raj: Come on, Sheldon, open the door.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t want to hug you.</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t want to hug you, either. I was just feeling blue.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Blue, as in depressed.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, not so much depressed as lonely.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t know what colour lonely is.</p>
<p>Raj: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Red is angry, yellow is frightened, green is jealous and blue is depressed. Perhaps we can assign a colour to lonely.</p>
<p>Raj: Nothing rhymes with orange. It&#8217;s probably lonely.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right. Come in. You look positively orange with loneliness. No, I don&#8217;t see that catching on at all.</p>
<p>Raj: What are you doing?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m working on my three-person chess game.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, cool. Can I play?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s three-person chess. Did you bring a friend?</p>
<p>Raj: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Then as a mental exercise I invite you to figure out why the two of us can&#8217;t play three-person chess. Can you believe this guy? Social protocol does, however, require me to bring you a hot beverage in your time of need.</p>
<p>Raj: No, thank you. I&#8217;m fine.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s not optional. We&#8217;re out of tea. I hope you like bouillon.</p>
<p>Raj: I, uh, I guess you&#8217;re probably wondering what&#8217;s got me down.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Actually, I was wondering if I could add a third new chess piece. How do you think people would feel about Prince Joey, the king&#8217;s feeble-minded but well-meaning cousin?</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m going to be 30 years old, and I have no one in my life to love me. I&#8217;m such a basket case, I can&#8217;t even talk to a woman without having alcohol in my body.</p>
<p>Sheldon: The fun thing about Prince Joey is every time he moves, there&#8217;s a one-in-five chance he&#8217;ll kill himself.</p>
<p>Raj: Sheldon, listen to me. I have a big decision to make, and I&#8217;m scared.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yellow. Go ahead.</p>
<p>Raj: A friend at the School of Pharmacology gave me these new pills they&#8217;re testing. He says it&#8217;s the next big thing for social anxiety disorder.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Fascinating. What&#8217;s in it?</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m not sure. Some sort of beta-blocker attached to a molecule extracted from the urine of cows.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I like cows.</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s not the point.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It was its own point. Go on.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m a scientist. My ability to think is my bread and butter. I&#8217;m afraid if I take this, I might lose that special, unique something that makes me so successful in my field.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Rajesh, I&#8217;ve had the privilege of working alongside you for many years. My recommendation is that you gobble these up like Tic Tacs.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment. </em></p>
<p>Howard: Okay, I&#8217;m still trying to work this out. How did Penny meet an astronaut?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I don&#8217;t know. The regular way people meet astronauts.</p>
<p>Howard: Most of those guys live in Texas.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Obviously, this one doesn&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Leonard lives here. Priya&#8217;s from India. People meet, Howard. God!</p>
<p>Howard: Fine.</p>
<p>Bernadette: You&#8217;ve met lots of astronauts, and I&#8217;ve never grilled you about that. I&#8217;d thank you to extend me the same courtesy.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m not grilling you, I was just curious.</p>
<p>Leonard: I still can&#8217;t get over the fact that she got a big movie part. Not that I care what my ex-girlfriend&#8217;s up to, &#8217;cause I don&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Maybe that&#8217;s where she met the astronaut, all right?</p>
<p>Priya: I&#8217;m sorry. What would an astronaut be doing working on a movie?</p>
<p>Bernadette: He&#8217;s a consultant.</p>
<p>Leonard: I thought the movie was about 18th-century Vienna?</p>
<p>Bernadette: He can&#8217;t have a hobby? Excuse me, I have to pee. Or is that implausible, as well?</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s nice to have another couple to hang with, isn&#8217;t it?</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment. </em></p>
<p>Penny <em>(to the shoes)</em>: All right, guys, you have to go back. I can&#8217;t afford you. “No, don&#8217;t send</p>
<p>us away, we love you.” I love you, too, but you cost more than my rent. “But, Penny, you look so good in us.” Damn it, the shoes are right.</p>
<p>Amy: Good golly, Penny, your whimsy is boundless. <em>(Phone rings) </em>What do you got for me, wildebeest?</p>
<p>Bernadette <em>(in Raj&#8217;s bathroom)</em>: I think they&#8217;re on to me. The story&#8217;s starting to fall apart.</p>
<p>Amy: Calm down. Everything&#8217;s going to be okay. <em>(To Penny) </em>We may have to kill her. Bernadette, I&#8217;m putting you on speakerphone. Where are you now?</p>
<p>Bernadette: In the bathroom.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, look at this, another one of our classic bathroom gabfests. What&#8217;s the problem?</p>
<p>Bernadette: They&#8217;re just asking me all sorts of questions I can&#8217;t answer.</p>
<p>Amy: Just change the subject.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				} else {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='55px' width='100%' frameBorder='0' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).load( function( e ) {
					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );
					$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loaded' );

					JetpackLikespostMessage( { event: 'loadLikeWidget', name: $iframe.attr( 'name' ), width: $iframe.width() }, window.frames[ 'likes-master' ] );

					if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
				setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 250 );
			}
