<p>Sheldon: Sheldon is not going.</p>
<p>Leonard: Really? What do we tell Siebert?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Tell him Dr. Cooper feels that the best use of his time is to employ his rare and precious mental faculties to tear the mask off nature and stare at the face of God.</p>

<p>Sheldon: Don&#8217;t tell him that, tell him the mask thing.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The party.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Hey, put your tie back in your pants.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thanks.</p>
<p>Raj: Nice place. Reminds me of my parents&#8217; house back in New Delhi.</p>
<p>Howard: You&#8217;re kidding.</p>
<p>Raj: No. We are very wealthy. But the only difference is, we have more servants.</p>
<p>Leonard: More than this?</p>
<p>Raj: More than we can use. You see, in India, we don&#8217;t make the mistake of letting our poor people have dreams.</p>
<p>Seibert: Ah. There&#8217;s my band of brainiacs. Where&#8217;s Dr. Cooper?</p>
<p>Leonard: He&#8217;s tearing the mask off nature to look at the face of God.</p>
<p>Seibert: The board of directors insists he has a beautiful mind. I think he&#8217;s just bananas. Come on, let me introduce you to one of the university&#8217;s leading donors.</p>
<p>Raj: I think we were misled about the cute girls.</p>
<p>Seibert: Mrs. Latham, I&#8217;d like you to meet three of our outstanding young researchers. This is Dr. Leonard Hofstadter, Dr. Rajesh Koothrappali and Howard Wolowitz.</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: Well, what happened to you, Wolowitz, couldn&#8217;t stick with it long enough to get your PhD?</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m an engineer. Most engineers don&#8217;t bother with a PhD. But you may be interested to know I designed the zero-gravity waste-disposal system for NASA.</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: Got it, you&#8217;re a space plumber.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m gonna go hit the bar.</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: Tell me about these two.</p>
<p>Raj: Do him first.</p>
<p>Seibert: Dr. Hofstadter is representing our experimental physics program tonight. I think you&#8217;ll really enjoy hearing about his fascinating work.</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: Right. Fascinate me.</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh.. b.. d.. uh.. uh..</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: They&#8217;re cute when they&#8217;re about to wet themselves, aren&#8217;t they? I&#8217;ll make it easy for you. When you arrive at the lab in the morning, what sort of machine do you turn on?</p>
<p>Leonard: Coffee maker?</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: All right, Dr. Kooth&#8230; uh, whatever it is, you&#8217;re up.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s Koothrappali. I have to tinkle.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: And so, instead of bowing to pressure, and going to that pointless soiree, I stayed right here and did a load of whites.</p>
<p>Amy <em>(on webcam)</em>: Well, normally I respect your macho rebellious attitude toward The Man, but, in this case, I think you&#8217;ve made a foolish mistake.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Unlikely. But make your case. Keeping in mind that your critical attitude is ruining our Saturday night together, and I&#8217;m not above minimizing your window.</p>
<p>Amy: Sheldon, like it or not, until you manage to upload your intelligence into a self-sustaining orbiting satellite, equipped with high-speed Internet and a cloaking device, you will be dependent on other members of the human race.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s it. Prepare to be minimized.</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;m not finished. All scientists have to fund-raise, Sheldon. How do you think I paid for my lab?</p>
<p>I went to Saudi Arabia and met with a prince who had an interest in neurobiology.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Your lab is funded by some Middle-Eastern dilettante?</p>
<p>Amy: Technically, Faisal is my fiance. But I do have a state-of-the-art two-photon microscope and a place to stay in Riyadh for the winter.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, that explains those puzzling camel race photos on your Facebook page.</p>
<p>Amy: And consider this, without you to make the case for the physics department, the task will fall to people like Leonard and Rajesh.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Are you trying to scare me? &#8216;Cause you&#8217;re succeeding.</p>
<p>Amy: Well, then prepare to be terrified. If your friends are unconvincing, this year&#8217;s donations might go to, say, the geology department.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, dear,not, not the dirt people!</p>
<p>Amy: Or worse, it could go to the liberal arts.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No!</p>
<p>Amy: Millions of dollars being showered on poets, literary theorists and students of gender studies.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, the humanities!</p>
<p><em>Scene: The party.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: On the bright side, I don&#8217;t think President Siebert will be making us go to any more fund-raisers.</p>
<p>Howard: It was so much easier at my bar mitzvah. The old people just came up to you, pinched your cheek and handed you a savings bond.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, don&#8217;t be such gloomy Gusses. Look at the size of these shrimp! At what point do we start calling them lobsters?</p>
<p>Leonard: Face it, Raj, we crashed and burned tonight.</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: Oh, you didn&#8217;t do that badly.</p>
<p>Leonard: Mrs. Latham, the first machine I turn on in the morning is the helium-neon laser, &#8217;cause it needs to warm up.</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: I no longer care, dear. But don&#8217;t worry, I really enjoyed meeting you this evening.</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re kidding. That was good for you? &#8216;Cause I was sweating through my T-shirt.</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: Excellent! There&#8217;s nothing I like better than making smart people feel ill at ease.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why?</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: Oh, I don&#8217;t know, it&#8217;s one of the fun things you get to do when you have lots of money. Watch. Hey! Who said you could eat that shrimp? See? Fun.</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(behind them)</em>: No, no, no, I&#8217;m just here for your money. I don&#8217;t want to shake anyone&#8217;s germy hands. Explain it to them, Siebert.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. </em></p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(on phone)</em>: I must confess I don&#8217;t understand you, President Siebert. First you say you want me to appear at your fund-raisers, but now you say you never want me to go anywhere near your fund-raisers. Forgive me, but that sounds like a mixed message. Here we go again. If there&#8217;s simply no talking to me, why did you call? I&#8217;m sorry, someone&#8217;s on the other line. Why don&#8217;t you see if you can organize your thoughts, and we&#8217;ll try again later. Cooper-Hofstadter residence. Go for Cooper.</p>
<p>Good morning, Mrs. Latham. Well, yes, of course I remember you. A woman well past her prime seeking to augment her social status by doling out her late husband&#8217;s ill-gotten gains. So, how much money are you going to give me? I&#8217;m not crazy, my mother had me tested. Well, if you&#8217;re not going to give me money, then why are you calling? She wants to talk to you. Who&#8217;s crazy now?</p>
<p>Leonard: Hello, Mrs. Latham. Yes, I live with him. I don&#8217;t, I, I really don&#8217;t know why. Tonight? Sure, that&#8217;d be great. Okay, I’ll, I&#8217;ll see you then. Bye. She wants to have dinner and talk about my research.</p>
<p>Sheldon: An entire dinner to talk about your research? Where you going, the drive-thru at Jack in the Box?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, wherever we&#8217;re going, she&#8217;s sending a car to pick me up.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay, I see what&#8217;s happening.</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: My stature intimidates her, so she&#8217;s using you to get to me. Crafty old gal.</p>
<p>Leonard: Excuse me, but you are not the only distinguished scientist in this apartment. I&#8217;ve been published in peer-reviewed journals, I received a Dissertation of the Year award for experimental particle physics.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, that can&#8217;t be it. And since you seem to have forgotten, the reason we live together is we&#8217;re best friends. And I got your back, Jack.</p>
<p><em>Secne: Mrs Latham’s car.</em></p>

<p>Mrs Latham: I&#8217;m glad you enjoyed it.</p>
<p>Leonard: The only time I eat this well is when my mom&#8217;s in town and she takes me out to dinner.</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: Is that so?</p>
<p>Leonard: You kind of remind me of her. She enjoys making people uncomfortable, too.</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: Well, you remind me of a boy I dated in college.</p>
<p>Leonard: No kidding.</p>
<p>Mrs Latham: Sweet boy. Very smart. If only he&#8217;d had money.</p>

<p>Mrs Latham: Well, I must say, you make a very persuasive case for it.</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );

				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).load( function( e ) {
					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );

						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
