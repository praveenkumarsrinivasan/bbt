<p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: Awkward silence, Sheldon on his phone, no touching. Somebody&#8217;s having date night.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Oh, no, I got bored with that. I&#8217;m just browsing cuticle scissors on Amazon.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: How do you not tear off his clothes and take him right here on this table?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: If you do that, I&#8217;ll scream.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Sheldon, I have some exciting news to tell you.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: That makes two of us. My new cuticle scissors will be here in one to two business days. Come on, one.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: I&#8217;ve been invited to consult on an experiment at your university for a few months. Isn&#8217;t that great? We could have lunch together. We could carpool. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: You know, riding with Leonard has gotten a little tedious lately. The only car game he ever wants to play is the Quiet Game. And he&#8217;s terrible at it, I always win.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: So, you&#8217;re okay with this?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Well, why wouldn&#8217;t I be?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Well, this project would have us working in close proximity to one another. And there&#8217;s the vulgar adage that one should not defecate where one eats.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: My father used to say that all the time. That and, um, who does one have to orally gratify to get a drink around here? But what does that have to do with you working at the university?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Sheldon, don&#8217;t defecate where you eat means don&#8217;t have a romantic relationship in the workplace.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Really?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Yes.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Huh. Yeah, I always took it literally. That&#8217;s why I have never once moved my bowels in this or any restaurant. Hmm.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: I&#8217;m relieved that you don&#8217;t have a problem with us working together.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Not as relieved as I&#8217;m about to be. It&#8217;s a brave new world, little lady.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: Are you crazy? You don&#8217;t want your girlfriend at work with you. Hell Clam.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Why not? Hairy Fairy.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: I think it could be romantic. You know, my parents met at his place of work. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: Your father&#8217;s a gynaecologist.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: I know. What started as a pap smear turned into a date. Which turned into her working there, which turned into marriage, which then turned into hatred, which continues to this day. Two-Eyed Cyclops.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: Would you please tell him this isn&#8217;t a good idea?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: No, no, I think it&#8217;ll be great. Maybe next time he gets conjunctivitis at work, she can hold his head and try to put the drops in his eyes. Giant baby.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: That&#8217;s a Raging Ogre.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: Yeah, I know.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: I&#8217;m just saying, I&#8217;d never want to work with Bernadette. Can you imagine seeing someone all day long and then you&#8217;re supposed to hang out with them after work, too?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: Hold on. We do that all the time. You and I work together and play together.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: Yeah, I know, and it drove me into the arms of another woman. Bipolar Bear.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Well, I appreciate your concern, but I won&#8217;t be seeing any more of Amy than I already do. I assume we&#8217;ll deduct any extra time we spend together at work from our weekly quota.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: Please let me be there when you tell her that.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Why? So you can see the look on Amy&#8217;s face when she hears my top-notch idea?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: Please, oh, please, just let me be there.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;"><i>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment.</i></span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: So, Amy, what are you gonna be working on at Caltech?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: I&#8217;m leading a study to see if deficiency of the monoamine oxidase enzyme leads to paralyzing fear in monkeys.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: If they&#8217;re anything like humans, the answer&#8217;s yes.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Wait, you&#8217;ve, you&#8217;ve done this experiment on humans?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: You mean like death row inmates with nothing to lose? No, that would be unethical.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: You know, not a lot of people know this, but the monoamine oxidase enzyme was discovered by a woman, Mary Bernheim. That&#8217;s right, my phone is just as smart as you guys.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon <i>(off)</i>: <i>(Knock, knock, knock) </i>Amy, Bernadette, Penny. <i>(Knock, knock, knock) </i>Amy, Bernadette, Penny. <i>(Knock, knock, knock) </i>Amy, Bernadette, Penny.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: He&#8217;s never gonna stop doing that, is he?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: I don&#8217;t mind. I&#8217;m hoping to put his love of repetition to good use someday.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Hi. Um, I&#8217;ve reconsidered. Uh, you can&#8217;t work where I work. Enjoy the rest of your evening.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Sheldon. I already signed the contract. I cashed a cheque. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Mmm, you are not going to come out of this looking good.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: Sheldon, I don&#8217;t understand. I thought you said you were fine with it.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Well, I was. But that was before Howard explained to me how awful it would be if he had to work with his significant other.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: He said what?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Now, don&#8217;t be insulted. He just thinks too much of you would be mind-numbingly tedious. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: Excuse me, I need to have a chat with my husband.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Yeah, well, now, well, keep it short. From what I gather, brevity is your friend. So, are we good here?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: No, we&#8217;re not good here. I am working on this project.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: Yeah, and you can&#8217;t tell her what she can and cannot do.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Last week, you told Leonard he couldn&#8217;t wear his Wookie jacket out in public.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Penny: That&#8217;s different. I&#8217;m not going to the mall with someone dressed like a dumb space bear.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Sheldon, you don&#8217;t have to worry about me bothering you. I&#8217;ll be in a different building. And we don&#8217;t even have to have lunch together.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Really?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Yes. Before all things, I&#8217;m a scientist. I&#8217;m just there to do my work and, with a little luck, scare the living crap out of some monkeys.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Hmm. You sure your mothlike personality won&#8217;t be drawn to this blazing fire that is myself?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: More and more sure.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Well, then, you have my permission.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: I didn&#8217;t ask for your permission.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Too late. No backsies.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: No, no, listen to me. Sheldon misunderstood. What I meant was, if we worked together, there&#8217;d be too much of me for you, not the other way around.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Howard, if you&#8217;re going to lie to your wife, don&#8217;t start the sentence with Sheldon misunderstood. That&#8217;s a dead giveaway.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: Well?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: Okay, fine. I did say that, and I think it&#8217;s true. I think if we worked together and lived together, we&#8217;d get sick of each other.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Yeah, but to be fair, he only said the part about him getting sick of you.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: For the love of God, why?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: What exactly do you think you&#8217;d get sick of?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: His only options here are to fake a heart attack or have a real one.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: It&#8217;s nothing in particular. I&#8230;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: Is it my voice? Am I too bossy? What?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: My arm is feeling numb.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: Nailed it.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: That&#8217;s the wrong arm for a heart attack, doofus. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: My point is, I&#8217;m sure there are things about me that would drive you crazy if you had to deal with them all day long.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: Like looking me in the eye and lying to me?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: Oh, well, come on, where am I supposed to look when I lie to you?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Bernadette: Find somewhere else to sleep tonight.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: Bernie&#8230;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: If it makes you feel any better, Amy and I are fine. I mean, really good.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;"><i>Scene: The cafeteria.</i></span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: So, boys, how was the pyjama party? You guys jump on the bed and sing into hair brushes?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: It wasn&#8217;t a pyjama party. It was just a couple of bros hanging out, giggling, eating cookie dough and watching Princess Bride.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: Please, stop talking.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: As you wish.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: I mean, you know, Sheldon, none of this would&#8217;ve happened if I hadn&#8217;t tried to help you.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Uh, it also wouldn&#8217;t have happened if, in the early universe, hydrogen was a little more common or a little less common. This is fun. Your turn.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Gentlemen.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: Amy.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: Hey.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Hello.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: That was kind of icy. You two okay?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Oh, we&#8217;re fine. As Howard advised, she&#8217;s merely respecting our professional boundaries.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Leonard: Smart. Take relationship advice from a man who spent last night braiding Raj&#8217;s hair.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Yeah, you make a good point. It would appear I was worried for nothing. Look at her, desperately wishing she was over here at the cool table. Don&#8217;t worry, little moth. The flame will come to you.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: Oh, uh, on the off chance that Bernadette doesn&#8217;t call back and apologize, how do you feel about Mexican food for dinner tonight?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: I don&#8217;t know. Sounds kind of heavy.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Raj: That&#8217;s &#8217;cause you always fill up on chips.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: And this is Dr. Gunderson from Stockholm.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Ah, Sweden. Yeah, home of my favourite Muppet and, uh, second favourite meatball. Okay, the Nordic reputation for lack of humour is well-founded. Boy, is his name Gunderson or No-Funderson?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Where are we going with this, Dr. Cooper?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Oh, please, I&#8217;m your boyfriend. You call me Sheldon. That&#8217;s right, I am in a boy-girl relationship with this cute little lump of wool.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Sheldon.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: It&#8217;s a physical relationship, too. Hand-holding, hugging, even on hot days. Ow. Okay, here&#8217;s a new one. Apparently now we kick each other in the shin under the table. How do you like it when I do it to you? Not so much, huh?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;"><i>Scene: Amy&#8217;s lab.</i></span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Test subject D7, aka Betsy, fear response study. Image number one, Frenchman on bicycle carrying baguettes. No visible reaction. Image number two, sousaphone. Still no reaction. Okay. Let&#8217;s kick things up a notch. Image number three, crocodile with a mouthful of monkeys. Okay, now we&#8217;re talking.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Dr. Fowler?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: What do you want, Sheldon?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Well, I&#8217;m done with work, so&#8230; ugh!</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Amy: Oh, sorry, sorry, sorry. Sorry, uh, better?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;">Sheldon: Oh, baguettes. Yes, I like baguettes. What exactly are you doing?</span></p>

					}
				}

				pm( {
					target: target,
					type: 'likesMessage',
					data: message,
					origin: '*'
				} );
			}

			function JetpackLikesBatchHandler() {
				var requests = [];
				jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).each( function( i ) {
					if ( jetpackLikesWidgetBatch.indexOf( this.id ) > -1 )
						return;
					jetpackLikesWidgetBatch.push( this.id );
					var regex = /like-(post|comment)-wrapper-(\d+)-(\d+)-(\w+)/;
					var match = regex.exec( this.id );
					if ( ! match || match.length != 5 )
						return;

					var info = {
						blog_id: match[2],
						width:   this.width
					};

					if ( 'post' == match[1] ) {
						info.post_id = match[3];
					} else if ( 'comment' == match[1] ) {
						info.comment_id = match[3];
					}

					info.obj_id = match[4];

					requests.push( info );
				});

				if ( requests.length > 0 ) {
					JetpackLikespostMessage( { event: 'initialBatch', requests: requests }, window.frames['likes-master'] );
				}
			}

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				} else {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='55px' width='100%' frameBorder='0' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).load( function( e ) {
					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );
					$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loaded' );

					JetpackLikespostMessage( { event: 'loadLikeWidget', name: $iframe.attr( 'name' ), width: $iframe.width() }, window.frames[ 'likes-master' ] );

					if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
				setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 250 );
			}
