			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Following a “Previously on The Big Bang Theory” section</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: A Soyuz Spaceship capsule.</em></p>
<p>Mission Control <em>(in Russian)</em>: Launch step twelve reading okay.</p>
<p>Cosmonaut <em>(in Russian)</em>: Launch step twelve acknowledged.</p>
<p><span style="font-size:small;"><span style="font-size:small;">Howard: Hey Mike?</span></span></p>
<p>Mike Massimino: Yeah.</p>
<p>Howard: I changed my mind. I don&#8217;t want to do this.</p>
<p>Mike: Good one.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, I&#8217;m a funny guy. I also have a hysterical bit planned for later where I pretend to cry through the whole launch.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, what channel is NASA TV?</p>
<p>Sheldon: 289, right between the Game Show Network at 288 and the East coast feed of the Disney Channel on 290.</p>
<p>Amy: I love his eidetic memory, it&#8217;s so sexy. Sheldon, what are the ingredients in Pringles?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Dried potatoes, vegetable oil, corn flower, wheat starch, maltodextrin, salt, and my favourite ingredient of all, uniformity.</p>
<p>Amy: The uterus quivers, does it not?</p>
<p>Leonard: There&#8217;s Howard&#8217;s rocket, live from Kazakhstan.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, God, I&#8217;m so nervous. I don&#8217;t think I can watch.</p>
<p>Raj: You&#8217;re nervous? I&#8217;ve been stress-eating for four days. Look at me. I&#8217;m wearing my fat pants.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Remarkable. In just under a half hour, 200 metric tons of fuel will ignite in a controlled explosion right beneath Howard&#8217;s keister. And all from a country whose entire contribution to the global economy, has been Tetris and mail-order brides.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Give me those damn Pringles.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Soyuz capsule.</em></p>
<p>Cosmonaut <em>(in Russian)</em>: Are all of the systems of the ship ready?</p>
<p>Mission Control: Da.</p>
<p>Mike: Okay, we&#8217;re in the final countdown. How you doing over there?</p>
<p>Howard: Good! Good! Oh, quick question, I missed it in the briefing. How much urine do these suits hold?</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: Soyuz capsule.</em></p>
<p>Mike: Hey, Froot Loops, want to hit your fan switch?</p>
<p>Howard: Check.</p>
<p>Cosmonaut: He calls you Froot Loops because of your very gay haircut?</p>
<p>Howard: No, it&#8217;s &#8217;cause I live with my mom and she makes me Froot Loops.</p>
<p>Cosmonaut: Go with gay story, people are more accepting of that.</p>
<p>Mike: Actually, Froot Loops just got married to a girl.</p>
<p>Cosmonaut: Congratulations.</p>
<p>Howard: Thanks, we decided to do it before the launch.</p>
<p>Cosmonaut: You and Mrs. Loops have a big wedding?</p>
<p>Howard: Not exactly. Listen, if you don&#8217;t mind, I&#8217;m not really up for chatting. I&#8217;m just going to sit here quietly and let my life flash before my eyes. That went really quick. Let me try it again.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Howard&#8217;s bedroom.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Close your eyes. Put out your hand. I got you something special.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Come on, Howard. I&#8217;m not falling for that again.</p>
<p>Howard: No, here.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, Howie. A little star, it&#8217;s beautiful. Put it on me.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, but I&#8217;m going to have to get it back from you so I can take it to the International Space Station. That way, when I come home, you will have a star that was actually in space.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, my God.</p>
<p>Howard: Take that, every guy who&#8217;s ever bought you anything.</p>
<p>Bernadette: This is the most amazing gift I&#8217;ve ever gotten.</p>
<p>Howard: Really? Well, if you like it that much, then close your eyes and put out your hand.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: &#8216;m going to the supermarket to buy snacks for your trip. Do you want me to get those little boxes of Froot Loops you like?</p>
<p>Howard: No! When I eat Froot Loops, the other astronauts make fun of me!</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howard, I don&#8217;t want to wait until you&#8217;re back to get married.</p>
<p>Howard: What?</p>
<p>Bernadette: I want to be married to you before you get in that rocket.</p>
<p>Howard: But I&#8217;m leaving in two days.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: What about Apple Jacks?</p>
<p>Howard: I don&#8217;t need to take cereal.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: What kind of breakfast do you think they&#8217;re going to give you in Russia?</p>
<p>Howard: They invented blintzes. I&#8217;ll be fine.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: They invented the lightbulb in New Jersey. It doesn&#8217;t mean they hand them out to you when you go.</p>
<p>Bernadette: We&#8217;ll have a quick little ceremony with just our friends, and we&#8217;ll still have the big reception with everyone when you get back.</p>
<p>Howard: Wow. Okay. Let&#8217;s get married.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: You know what, I&#8217;ll buy you All-Bran in case you get stopped up in outer space.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Bernadette: So anyway, we decided to go down to City Hall this afternoon get married, and then have the reception when Howard gets back.</p>
<p>Leonard: That&#8217;s so great. <em>(Others also make encouraging sounds)</em></p>
<p>Howard: I mean, we know it&#8217;s short notice, but we&#8217;d love you all to come with us.</p>
<p>Amy: No, no, no, this is not the wedding I wanted! I want to wear my maid of honour dress and walk down the aisle with a hundred eyes on me, while a string quartet plays The Way You Look Tonight.</p>
<p>Bernadette: That wasn&#8217;t going to be our processional music.</p>
<p>Amy: Well, it was going to be mine.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thank you for the invitation, but I have to decline because it doesn&#8217;t sound like something I&#8217;ll enjoy.</p>
<p>Leonard: Come on, Sheldon, it&#8217;ll be fun.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s what you said about The Green Lantern movie. You were 114 minutes of wrong.</p>
<p>Bernadette: So, what do you say, Amy?</p>
<p>Amy: Can I wear my maid of honour dress?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Seriously? You&#8217;re going to wear that thing to City Hall?</p>
<p>Amy: It&#8217;s all I have left. You&#8217;re going to take that from me, too?</p>
<p><em>Scene: City Hall.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Amy, you look great.</p>
<p>Amy: I know.</p>
<p>Leonard: Where&#8217;d you get a beer?</p>
<p>Raj: From that happy young couple over there with all the tattoos. Beautiful story, they&#8217;re in rival drug gangs, and they&#8217;re getting married. Shh, no one can know.</p>
<p>Amy: Look at all these people in love. It kind of gets you thinking, doesn&#8217;t it?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It does, indeed. Leonard, is it awkward being here with Penny given that you recently proposed to her?</p>
<p>Howard: You proposed to Penny?</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t want to talk about it.</p>
<p>Raj: Where did he pop the question? What did you say?</p>
<p>Leonard: She said no, can we drop it now?</p>
<p>Penny: It wasn&#8217;t a real proposal.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Why wasn&#8217;t it a real proposal?</p>
<p>Sheldon: He asked her during coitus.</p>
<p>Howard: Did you get down on one knee or were you already there?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howard, don&#8217;t talk like that on your wedding day.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m sorry, Ma&#8230; Bernadette. Ma&#8230; Burna&#8230; You&#8217;re ma Bernadette.</p>
<p>Penny: Good move telling Sheldon.</p>
<p>Leonard: What, I can&#8217;t propose? I can&#8217;t talk to my friends? Is there anything else I&#8217;m not allowed to do?</p>
<p>Amy: All right, that&#8217;s enough. Today is not about you two. Today is about Howard and Bernadette and me.</p>
<p>Registrar: Folks, can I have your attention. It&#8217;s five o&#8217;clock, we&#8217;re going to be able to take three more couples. The rest of you will have to come back on Monday.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, no.</p>
<p>Howard: I got this. Excuse me, but is there any way you could squeeze us in? See, I&#8217;m an astronaut and I&#8217;m leaving for Russia on Sunday so I can take a Soyuz rocket to the International Space Station.</p>
<p>Registrar: Yeah, me, too. I&#8217;ll see you there.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I can&#8217;t believe we&#8217;re not going to get married.</p>
<p>Amy: Excuse me, I&#8217;m going to go see if the couple at the front of the line needs a maid of honour.</p>
<p>Scene: Soyuz capsule.</p>
<p>Cosmonaut: So, I tell my wife, get a dog, don&#8217;t get a dog, I&#8217;m not walking it, I&#8217;m not feeding it, I&#8217;m not picking up after it.</p>
<p>Mike: You know you&#8217;re going to wind up walking it.</p>
<p>Cosmonaut: I know.</p>
<p>Howard: Uh, shouldn&#8217;t you guys be talking about space stuff instead of dogs?</p>
<p>Mike: Dimitri, Froot Loops would be more comfortable if we talked about space stuff.</p>
<p>Dimitri: Okay, I&#8217;m going into space, and when I come back, I have to pick up a poodle crap.</p>
<p>Mike: Is that better?</p>
<p>Howard: Thanks.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The Cheesecake Factory bar.</em></p>
<p>Howard: You know, we could always drive to Vegas and get married.</p>
<p>Bernadette: No, isn&#8217;t that kind of tacky?</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, I know tons of people who got married in Vegas.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Are any of them still married?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, I mean, not to the same people but&#8230;</p>
<p>Bernadette: There&#8217;s got to be some place special we could do it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard, where did you envision marrying Penny?</p>
<p>Leonard: Will you shut up?</p>
<p>Raj: Well, I know how to make it special.</p>
<p>Howard: I told you we are not recreating the wedding from The Sound of Music.</p>
<p>Raj: Yes, you made that brutally clear to me. What I was going to suggest is if that you&#8217;re willing to wait until Sunday morning, the Google satellite will be over Pasadena. You can have a wedding photograph from space.</p>
<p>All: Oh!</p>
<p>Leonard: That&#8217;s so cool.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, wait to go, Raj.</p>
<p>Raj: I keep telling you, if I wasn&#8217;t an astrophysicist, I would have been a party planner. It was always a coin flip.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, so we know we&#8217;re going to do it Sunday morning. Now we need to find a good place for the satellite to see us.</p>
<p>Leonard: How about our roof?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, I like that.</p>
<p>Howard: That&#8217;s great.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, my gosh. I can&#8217;t believe my maid of honour dress is going to be on Google Earth.</p>
<p>Howard: So, we have the where and the when, but we still need to figure out who&#8217;s going to do the ceremony.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, that&#8217;s easy. Anyone can go online and get ordained as a minister. I know a piercing parlour where, for a hundred bucks they&#8217;ll marry you and stick a wedding ring through any body part you want.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Great, well, who&#8217;s it going to be?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;ll do it, provided I can perform the ceremony in Klingon.</p>
<p>Bernadette: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What do you see in her?</p>
<p><em>Scene: Soyuz capsule.</em></p>
<p>Dimitri <em>(in Russian)</em>: Mission Control, say again, how fast is it leaking?</p>
<p>Howard: Leaking? What&#8217;s leaking?</p>
<p>Dimitri: Fuel. Shh.</p>
<p>Mission Control <em>(in Russian)</em>: Not bad. We feel okay to go.</p>
<p>Dimitri <em>(in Russian)</em>: Okay, thanks Mission Control.</p>
<p>Howard: There&#8217;s fuel leaking and we&#8217;re still going to go?</p>
<p>Mike: Don&#8217;t lose your Froot Loops, Froot Loops.</p>
<p>Dimitri: This happens a lot. Nine times out of ten, no problem.</p>
<p>Howard: What happens on the tenth time?</p>
<p>Dimitri: Problem.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Hey, guys, before I forget, I got you a little groomsman present.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, thanks, man.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You didn&#8217;t have to do that.</p>
<p>Howard: Fantastic Four, annual number three from 1965, in mint condition. The one where Mr. Fantastic and the Invisible Girl get married.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, wow.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, dear. I was afraid of this.</p>
<p>Howard: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: While a thoughtful gift, this comic book in this condition is worth at least a hundred dollars.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, so?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I bought you and Bernadette a gravy boat worth eighty-eight dollars. Which places me in your debt and I can&#8217;t be in your debt because someday you might ask me to help you move, or to kill a man.</p>
<p>Leonard: I doubt he&#8217;ll ask you to kill a man.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, what if it&#8217;s his only way out? I can&#8217;t risk it. Here is twelve dollars. Now, we&#8217;re even. Wait, wait, wait, I bought a card. Give me two dollars. And for the record, this is why I hate gift-giving.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s door. Howard knocks.</em></p>
<p>Amy: Who is it?</p>
<p>Howard: It&#8217;s the groom.</p>
<p>Amy: Can&#8217;t come in. Bad luck to see the bride.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, uh, fine. Bernie, I have to go pick up my mother. I&#8217;ll be right back.</p>
<p>Bernadette <em>(off)</em>: Why can&#8217;t she drive herself?</p>
<p>Howard: She doesn&#8217;t want to sit in her dress and wrinkle it so I&#8217;m going to lay her down in the back of my neighbour&#8217;s van.</p>
<p>Bernadette <em>(off)</em>: All right, just hurry!</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, I&#8217;ll see you later, Ma&#8230; uh&#8230; lovely bride-to-be. I really got to watch that.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The roof.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Come on, Raj, we&#8217;re ready to start.</p>
<p>Raj: What, we&#8217;re ready when I say we&#8217;re ready. Uh-du-du-du, ooh, okay, now we&#8217;re ready.</p>
<p>Howard: Ma, you want to move your chair over here so you can see?</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: I&#8217;m fine where I am. I don&#8217;t want to fall off the roof.</p>
<p>Howard: You&#8217;ll fall through the roof before you fall off it.</p>
<p>Raj: Penny. <em>(She starts music playing) </em></p>
<p>Mr Rostenkowski: Your new mother-in-law&#8217;s a piece of work.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Not now, Dad.</p>
<p>Mr Rostenkowski: She&#8217;s got a bigger mustache than me. Here you go.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Here you go? What am I, a football?</p>
<p>Mr Rostenkowski: Like that guy could catch a football.</p>
<p>Raj: Howard and Bernadette, the five of us stand before you as your friends and newly ordained ministers.</p>
<p>Mrs Wolowitz <em>(off)</em>: Louder!</p>

				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				} else {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='55px' width='100%' frameBorder='0' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).load( function( e ) {
					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );
					$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loaded' );

					JetpackLikespostMessage( { event: 'loadLikeWidget', name: $iframe.attr( 'name' ), width: $iframe.width() }, window.frames[ 'likes-master' ] );

					if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
				setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 250 );
			}
