		<h2 class="title">Series 5 Episode 01 &#8211; The Skank Reflex&nbsp;Analysis</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The Cafeteria</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s not what it looks like. It&#8217;s not what it looks like.</p>
<p>Leonard: What are you grinding about?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Penny&#8217;s brain teaser this morning. She and Koothrappali emerge from your bedroom. She is dishevelled, and Raj is dressed only in a sheet. The sole clue, it&#8217;s not what it looks like.</p>
<p>Leonard: Just let it go, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: If I could, I would, but I can&#8217;t, so I shan&#8217;t. Now, knowing Penny, the obvious answer is, they engaged in coitus. But, since that&#8217;s what it looked like, we can rule that out. Let&#8217;s put on our thinking cap, shall we? <em>(Mimes doing so)</em> Raj is from India, a tropical country. Third World hygiene. Parasitic infections are common, such as pinworms. Mm-hmm. The procedure for diagnosing pinworms is to wait until the subject is asleep, and the worms crawl out of the rectum for air. <em>(Leonard spits out food)</em> Yes, just like that. Penny could have been inspecting Raj&#8217;s anal region for parasites. Oh, boy. That&#8217;s a true blue friend.</p>
<p>Leonard: They slept together, Sherlock.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, you weren&#8217;t listening. She said, it&#8217;s not what it looks like.</p>
<p>Leonard: She lied.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh. Well don&#8217;t I look silly sitting here wearing this?</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: The Cafeteria</em></p>
<p>Howard: Hey.</p>
<p>Raj: Hey.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hey. Leonard, is it awkward for you knowing that one of your dear friends had sexual intercourse with a woman you used to love in the very place you lay your head?</p>
<p>Leonard: No, I&#8217;m fine with it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That sounds like sarcasm, but I&#8217;m going to disregard it, because I have an agenda. Paintball. Specifically, the interdepartmental tournament this weekend. Now, in order to function better as a fighting unit, I thought we should establish a chain of command. Now, it goes without saying that I would outrank the three of you, but the question remains, by how much? Now, I don&#8217;t see me as some four-star general, back at HQ riding a desk and playing golf with the Secretary of Defence. But I also can&#8217;t be Sergeant Cooper, because that might lead you to think of me as just a regular Joe. This might take some thought. As you were.</p>
<p>Leonard: What the hell is wrong with you?</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, how could you do that?</p>
<p>Raj: What is it to you?</p>
<p>Howard: I got his back.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, right. You&#8217;re just jealous because it turns out I&#8217;m Penny&#8217;s number two choice after Leonard.</p>
<p>Howard: Hey, if I wasn&#8217;t engaged to Bernadette, that totally could have been me.</p>
<p>Leonard: Please. Sheldon would have been before you, and he might not even have genitals.</p>
<p>Raj: Why do you care so much? You&#8217;re dating my sister, and Penny and I are in love.</p>
<p>Leonard and Howard together: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Gentlemen, if I may interject, I&#8217;ve decided my rank will be captain. If it&#8217;s good enough for Kirk, Crunch and Kangaroo, it&#8217;s good enough for me.</p>
<p>Howard: You&#8217;re not in love with Penny.</p>
<p>Raj: Yes, I am. The god Kamadeva has shot us with his flowery arrows of love.</p>
<p>Howard: Who?</p>
<p>Raj: He&#8217;s the Hindu version of Cupid, but way better, because he rides a giant parrot.</p>
<p>Leonard: Raj, come on. You fall in love with any girl who smiles at you. A month ago, you were writing poems about his fiancee.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m sorry. What?</p>
<p>Raj: Rubbish. He&#8217;s talking rubbish.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, Bernadette, please play my clarinet.</p>
<p>Raj: That could have been about anyone. Besides, you have nothing to worry about, because now I&#8217;m the dusky half of Koothrapenny.</p>
<p>Sheldon: For the record, I do have genitals. They&#8217;re functional and aesthetically pleasing.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment. Penny has a bottle of wine. She can&#8217;t find a glass. Finds a measuring jug and uses that instead. There is a knock on the door.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Coming. Yup, that&#8217;s good. Wine glasses should have handles.</p>
<p>Amy: Keeping accurate track of your alcohol intake. Smart idea considering how trampy you get when you&#8217;ve had a few.</p>
<p>Penny: You heard what I did?</p>
<p>Amy: Well, I heard who you did.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, my God, I screwed up everything. I hurt Leonard, I hurt Raj, I mean, what is wrong with me? I feel like two totally different people, Dr. Jekyll and Mrs. Whore.</p>
<p>Amy: Don&#8217;t be so hard on yourself. Do you know the story of Catherine the Great?</p>
<p>Penny: No.</p>
<p>Amy: She ruled Russia in the late seventeen hundreds, and one night, when she was feeling particularly randy, she used an intricate system of pulleys to have intimate relations with a horse.</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m&#8230; I&#8217;m sorry. What does this have to do with me?</p>
<p>Amy: She engaged in interspecies hanky-panky, and people still call her great. I&#8217;m sure your reputation can survive you shagging a little Indian boy.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment. There is a knock on the door. He opens it.</em></p>
<p>Bernadette: You jerk face! What did you tell Howard? Did you say there was something going on between us? Because he thinks there is. He&#8217;s completely freaking out!</p>
<p>Raj: Please, come in.</p>
<p>Bernadette: What the hell is wrong with you?</p>
<p>Raj: Well, you were always so nice to me, I thought maybe you liked me.</p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;m nice to everyone.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m sorry.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Damn right, you&#8217;re sorry. And you tell Howard there&#8217;s never been anything between us.</p>
<p>Raj: I will. Hey, Bernadette?</p>
<p>Bernadette: What?!</p>
<p>Raj: Do you think I have a shot with Penny?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Of course you do. You&#8217;re a cutie pie. Any girl would be lucky to have you.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment.</em></p>
<p>Penny: You know, I&#8217;ve done this before. In kindergarten, I was supposed to marry Jason Sorensen at recess, but by the time my class got out there, he was already engaged to Chelsea Himmelfarb. So what did I do? Hung upside down from the monkey bars, let all the boys see my underpants.</p>
<p>Amy: You can&#8217;t blame yourself. When your prefrontal cortex fails to make you happy, promiscuity rewards you with the needed flood of dopamine. We neurobiologists refer to this as the skank reflex.</p>
<p>Penny: You know what? Let&#8217;s get out of here.</p>
<p>Amy: Where are we going?</p>
<p>Penny: Somewhere where no one&#8217;s seen me naked. We may have to drive awhile. <em>(Opens door. Sees Leonard and Sheldon. Slams it shut again.)</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Subtlety isn&#8217;t her strong suit, is it?</p>
<p>Penny: Can I stay at your place for a few nights?</p>
<p>Amy: Really? A best friend sleepover? Yay.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, sure. Yay!</p>
<p>Amy: We&#8217;ll make popcorn, stay up all night and I&#8217;ll teach you my secret language, Op.</p>
<p>Penny: Sounds great.</p>
<p>Amy: Nope. Sounds Gop Rop E A Top.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, I&#8217;m gonna go pack a bag.</p>
<p>Amy: No, you&#8217;re not. You&#8217;re gonna Pop A Cop Kop A Bop A Gop.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Do we really have to wear this camouflage crap to play paintball?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Who said that? Leonard, I can hear your voice, but I can&#8217;t see you.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m not in the mood, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, there you are. Leonard, I know you&#8217;re upset about recent events, and I have someone here to help. <em>(Showing laptop screen)</em></p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t want to talk to Amy.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, it&#8217;s not Amy.</p>
<p>Dr Hofstadter: Hello, dear.</p>
<p>Leonard: You called my mother?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, Leonard, is it really necessary to caption the obvious?</p>
<p>Dr Hofstadter: He&#8217;s been like that since he was a toddler. Look, Mommy, a butterfly. Maddening.</p>
<p>Leonard: What&#8217;s going on? What do you want?</p>
<p>Dr Hofstadter: Sheldon informed me that you&#8217;re experiencing an emotional upheaval, and I&#8217;m here to help.</p>
<p>Leonard: That&#8217;s so nice.</p>
<p>Dr Hofstadter: And we&#8217;re back to the obvious. Now, what&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, uh, okay, um, I don&#8217;t want to get back together with Penny. We tried it, it was crazy, it didn&#8217;t work, but I can&#8217;t deal with the fact that she slept with my friend Raj. And then I find out that Raj&#8217;s sister Priya, who I&#8217;ve been going out with for eight months, is moving back to India. So I&#8217;m just completely confused and alone.</p>
<p>Dr Hofstadter: I understand.</p>
<p>Leonard: Got any advice?</p>
<p>Dr Hofstadter: Yes. Buck up.</p>
<p>Leonard: Excuse me. You&#8217;re a world-renowned expert in parenting and child development, and all you&#8217;ve got is buck up?</p>
<p>Dr Hofstadter: Sorry. Buck up, sissy pants.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thanks, Mother. I feel much better.</p>
<p>Dr Hofstadter: If you need any more help from me, my books are available on Amazon. Logging off.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Amy&#8217;s apartment. Amy is brushing Penny&#8217;s hair.</em></p>
<p>Amy: Ninety-nine, one hundred. It&#8217;s like a waterfall of liquid gold. My turn.</p>
<p>Penny: You know, I don&#8217;t even know what the point of me staying in L.A. is. I haven&#8217;t gotten a single acting job since I moved out here. The closest I came was last month, I got a callback for a hemorrhoid commercial.</p>
<p>Amy: Oh, I could so see you being the face of haemorrhoids.</p>
<p>Penny: I know, right? Maybe I should just move back to Nebraska.</p>
<p>Amy: No, I can&#8217;t let you do that.</p>
<p>Penny: Why not? <em>(There is a knock on the door)</em></p>
<p>Amy: For the first time ever, I have a thriving social life. And no pressure, but it kind of lives and dies with you.</p>
<p>Raj: Hi, Amy. Can I talk to Penny?</p>
<p>Amy: A guest in my trundle bed and a boy at my door? I wish I could tell 13-year-old me it does get better.</p>
<p>Penny: How did you know I was here?</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s all over her Facebook page.</p>
<p>Amy: I&#8217;ll take your stuff to the bedroom and clear out a drawer.</p>
<p>Penny: Thanks.</p>
<p>Amy: No problem. Try and keep it in your pants, okay?</p>
<p>Penny: So, hi. What&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Raj: I was wondering if you&#8217;re free Friday. They&#8217;re having a Totally &#8217;80s Night at the Greek. Hall &amp; Oates, Katrina and the Waves and three-fifths of Kajagoogoo.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh. Gee, that&#8217;s really sweet, but the thing is&#8230;</p>
<p>Raj: Aw, there&#8217;s a thing.</p>
<p>Penny: Look, honey, I was really drunk and made a huge mistake last night. We should&#8217;ve never slept together. It&#8217;s what ruins friendships.</p>
<p>Raj: You can&#8217;t ruin a friendship with sex. That&#8217;s like trying to ruin ice cream with chocolate sprinkles.</p>
<p>Penny: Come here. Just listen to me. I want to go back to the way we were before. You know, friends. No sprinkles.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh. All right.</p>
<p>Penny: Thank you.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, uh, as your friend, you might like to know that, um, we didn&#8217;t have sex in the conventional sense.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, God. Did you pull some weird Indian crap on me?</p>
<p>Raj: No, no. After we got undressed and jumped in bed, you, you asked if I had protection.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, you did, didn&#8217;t you?</p>
<p>Raj: Of course. I&#8217;m always packing. Anyway, um, I had trouble putting it on and you tried to help and, that was all she wrote.</p>
<p>Penny: So, we didn&#8217;t actually&#8230;</p>
<p>Raj: I did. It was beautiful.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh!</p>
<p>Raj: Penny, please, please promise me you won&#8217;t tell anybody about this.</p>
<p>Penny: Of course I won&#8217;t. No, I won&#8217;t.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, good. Um, can I tell people that our love burned too bright and too quickly? Kind of a Candle in the Wind deal?</p>
<p>Penny: Sure.</p>
<p>Raj: Cool. Can I say it fell apart because you were all, I want to have your babies, and I was like, I&#8217;m too rock and roll to be tied down?</p>
<p>Penny: No.</p>
<p>Raj: Can I say I ruined you for white men?</p>
<p>Penny: Also no.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, just the candle thing.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah.</p>
<p>Raj: Cool. All right friend.</p>
<p>Penny: Hmm.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;ll see you around.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay. Raj, wait. Thank you for being my friend. <em>(She hugs him.)</em></p>
<p>Raj: Penny?</p>
<p>Penny: Mm-hmm?</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s getting beautiful again.</p>
<p><em>Scene: A shed on the paintball range.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, this is a Google Earth view of the field of battle.</p>
<p>Howard: I don&#8217;t see anything.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Give it a second to load. Whenever you&#8217;re ready, AT&amp;T! Okay, here we go. This is us here. To the south is Professor Loomis and the Geology Department. According to their Twitter feed, they&#8217;re out of sunblock, which means they&#8217;ll have to hug the tree line or risk melanoma. That&#8217;s our edge. All we have to do is move quickly over this ridge, the rock-worshipping pasty-faced bastards won&#8217;t know what hit them. All right, let&#8217;s move out.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hang on, Sheldon. How could you not tell me your sister was moving back to India?</p>
<p>Howard: Maybe he was too busy writing clumsy penis metaphors about my fiancee.</p>
<p>Raj: Screw you. That was a beautifully written penis metaphor.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know what, guys, I&#8217;m not in the mood for paintball. What do you say we just bag it?</p>
<p>Howard: Fine with me.</p>
<p>Raj: Sure, whatever.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You can&#8217;t quit. That&#8217;s a court-martial offence. That&#8217;s punishable by&#8230; You can&#8217;t quit.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sorry, Sheldon, it&#8217;s just not a good time for playing games.</p>
<p>Sheldon: This is a game to you? Uh, was the Battle of Antietam a game? Huh? Was the sack of Rome a game?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes, no and no.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wait. I just want you all to know that I forgive you. This mutiny isn&#8217;t your fault, it&#8217;s mine. I haven&#8217;t earned these bars. Although what I lack in leadership, apparently I more than make up for in sewing.</p>
<p>Howard: Let it go, Sheldon. I&#8217;ll get you a Jamba Juice on the way home.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. Jamba Juice is for heroes. And that&#8217;s what we&#8217;re going to be.</p>
<p>Leonard: What are you doing?</p>

<p>Penny: Oh, hell with Nebraska. I&#8217;m gonna be a star!</p>

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );

				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
