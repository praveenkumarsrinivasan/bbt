		<h2 class="title">Series 5 Episode 07 &#8211; The Good Guy&nbsp;Fluctuation</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: Sheldon&#8217;s office.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: And reverse the spin on the antiproton, and gamma becomes alpha, multiplied by a matrix of negative I comma zero, and there we have it. Conclusive proof that I am absolutely worthless after nine o&#8217;clock.</p>
<p>Ghostly voice: Sheldon, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hmm. <em>(Looks outside office door. Corridor lamp sparks and goes out. Corridor is bathed in an eerie glow) </em>All right, all right. I see what&#8217;s going on. A little pre-Halloween hijinkery. A ghostly moan, a rattling of chains, a witch&#8217;s cackle. The trifecta of haunted house clich√©s. Instead of eek, I say yawn.</p>
<p>Ghostly voice: Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, the walls are dripping blood, which looks nothing like a phenolphthalein indicator exposed to a sodium carbonate solution. <em>(Reading message on wall) </em>See you in hell Sheldon. The most frightening thing about that is the missing comma. <em>(A luminous skeleton rushes towards him) </em>Ah. Okay, all right. That one was clever. Skeleton with phosphorous on a zip line. Come on out, merry pranksters. Take a bow.</p>
<p>Raj: You should&#8217;ve seen your face.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, there&#8217;s nothing quite like the slightly widened eyes of mildly startled.</p>
<p>Howard: Come on, admit it. We got you, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Please, fright depends on an element of surprise. The simple fact is, because I am much smarter than you, and able to anticipate your actions, it is highly unlikely that you two rubes could ever surprise me. <em>(Leonard creeps out behind him wearing a Star Trek Balok mask)</em></p>
<p>Raj: He&#8217;s probably right.</p>
<p>Howard: We can&#8217;t beat him. He&#8217;s just too smart.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Gentlemen. <em>(Turns and sees Leonard. Screams and faints).</em></p>
<p>Howard: Who had money on faints?</p>
<p>Raj: I had pee his pants.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hang on. Looks like everyone&#8217;s a winner.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence.</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: The comic book store.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Oh. Watch out, Sheldon. This little boy Casper is a g-g-g-ghost!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Droll.</p>
<p>Howard:Not as droll as a grown man passed out in a puddle of his own urine.</p>
<p>Leonard: That was pretty droll. With a hint of ammonia.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, enjoy your japes, gentlemen. You think you&#8217;ve poked fun at a milquetoast academic. Well, you&#8217;ve forgotten one thing. I am also a son of the Lone Star state. I&#8217;m Texas through and through. And we know how to settle scores down there. If you doubt me, ask Mexico.</p>
<p>Stuart: Hot girl, nine o&#8217;clock. Don&#8217;t everybody look at once!</p>
<p>Raj: What is she doing in a comic book store?</p>
<p>Stuart: I don&#8217;t know, she might be lost. Doesn&#8217;t matter. Watch and learn. Hi.</p>
<p>Hot girl: Hi.</p>
<p>Stuart: Um&#8230; it-it-it&#8230; <em>(returns to guys) </em>Shut up.</p>
<p>Hot girl <em>(to Leonard)</em>: Are you getting this Next Men?</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, yeah. It&#8217;s issue number 21. First appearance of Hellboy.</p>
<p>Hot girl: I know. I&#8217;ve been looking for it for years.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sorry.</p>
<p>Hot girl: Hey, if I pretended to hit on you, could I distract you enough to sneak it away?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yes, but you&#8217;d be using your superpowers for evil.</p>
<p>Hot girl: Damn, I&#8217;m forbidden by my Kryptonian father to do so. I am Alice.</p>
<p>Leonard: Leonard.</p>
<p>Alice: You are very cute, Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thanks. You, too. You know, go ahead and take it.</p>
<p>Alice: No, no. No, no, I, I, I did evil. Would you be open to a trade?</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, yeah, yeah, sure, I guess.</p>
<p>Alice: Okay. Here. This is my number, call me.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sorry, palm&#8217;s a little sweaty. What&#8217;s that word?</p>
<p>Alice: Alice.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, right, your name. That makes more sense than penis.</p>
<p>Alice: Later.</p>
<p>Howard: Did we just see you pick up a girl in a comic book store?</p>
<p>Stuart: &#8216;Cause if you did, you get your picture up there on the Wall of Heroes.</p>
<p>Leonard: No, I don&#8217;t think I picked her up. Besides, I have a girlfriend.</p>
<p>Stuart: Doesn&#8217;t matter. This is the closest anyone&#8217;s ever come. You&#8217;re going on the wall, my friend.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Entering the apartment building.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Be sure to check the mail.</p>
<p>Leonard: How many times are you gonna tell me? What&#8217;s with you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Nothing. It&#8217;s not suspicious that I&#8217;m fixating. It&#8217;s consistent with my personality.</p>
<p>Leonard: Right.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, guys.</p>
<p>Leonard: More Halloween candy? Didn&#8217;t you just buy a bunch of it yesterday?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh. Yeah. That&#8217;s gone. It&#8217;s a rough month when Halloween and PMS hit at the same time.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Leonard doesn&#8217;t have time to chat, he has to get the mail.</p>
<p>Leonard: Will you relax? I&#8217;ll get it in a minute. Hey, how was work?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Open the mail!</p>
<p>Leonard: Excuse me. <em>(Sheldon holds fingers in ears) </em>A couple of circulars, nothing important.</p>
<p>Penny: What&#8217;s with him?</p>
<p>Leonard: Hang on. <em>(Sheldon opens mail box. A loud horn blows and a balloon with Leonard&#8217;s face on pops out. Sheldon faints again.) </em>You might be from Texas, but I&#8217;m from New Jersey.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. Leonard is with Alice.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Check it out. Jim Lee drew this of me two years ago at Comic Con.</p>
<p>Alice: What are you wearing?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, you know, it&#8217;s Comic Con. I&#8217;m Lion-O from ThunderCats.</p>
<p>Alice: Wow, you must have gotten so laid.</p>
<p>Leonard: No. But Jessica Alba did rub my furry belly.</p>
<p>Alice: Want to see a comic I draw?</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re kidding. You have your own book?</p>
<p>Alice: Yeah. It&#8217;s kinda based on my life.</p>
<p>Leonard: Cool. Oh, look. That&#8217;s you having sex with a guy in the top half of a Chewbacca costume. Comic Con?</p>
<p>Alice: You&#8217;d think, but no.</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re very talented. This is really good. Did you do&#8230; <em>(she kisses him.)</em></p>
<p>Alice: So, can I trade you my comic for the Hellboy?</p>
<p>Leonard: You can have my car.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Sheldon&#8217;s office.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(creeps into office carrying a box)</em>: Oh, dear. <em>(Reaches into box, picks up a snake) </em>Oh, dear. <em>(Puts snake into Raj&#8217;s top drawer) </em>Oh, dear, oh, dear. Oh, dear, oh, dear. Oh, dear. Oh, dear. Purell, Purell, Purell, Purell.</p>
<p>Raj <em>(entering)</em>: Good morning, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It is, isn&#8217;t it? Oh, bother. Isn&#8217;t that just always the way? You go to staple something, and you&#8217;re out of staples. Gosh, I wish I&#8217;d known that earlier today when I was at Staples.</p>
<p>Raj: You have a thing of paper clips right there.</p>
<p>Sheldon: well, no, no, I need something more permanent to join these papers. Say, don&#8217;t you keep staples in your top desk drawer?</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t know. Maybe.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Be a lamb and check.</p>
<p>Raj: All right. <em>(Opening drawer) </em>Who do we have here?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s a snake. A terrifying snake.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, did some bad man put us in a drawer?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Stop talking like that. You&#8217;ve been rendered speechless by fear.</p>
<p>Raj: Let&#8217;s go to the biology lab and find you some nice yummy mice.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I tried to scare an Indian with a snake. Come on, Cooper. You&#8217;re better than this.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny&#8217;s apartment. There is a knock.</em></p>
<p>Penny: It&#8217;s open.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, you got a minute?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, sure, come on in.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thanks.</p>
<p>Penny: Want some mac and cheese?</p>
<p>Leonard: No. Lactose. Gas.</p>
<p>Penny: Glass of wine?</p>
<p>Leonard: No. Sulfites. Migraines.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, well, I&#8217;d offer you Halloween candy, but that&#8217;s gone. So, what&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, we used to go out, right?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, my God, that&#8217;s where I know you from.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m dealing with a situation and it&#8217;s kind of about my love life, so I know that might be weird for us to talk about, but in this area, as you know, all my other friends are just so stupid.</p>
<p>Penny: All right, what&#8217;s going on?</p>
<p>Leonard: So, you&#8217;re okay talking about this?</p>
<p>Penny: Yes.</p>
<p>Leonard: You&#8217;re sure it&#8217;s not weird.</p>
<p>Penny: It&#8217;s okay.</p>
<p>Leonard: You know what, if you ever want to talk to me about a problem in your life with a guy, then I would be fine with that.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, good, because there&#8217;s this one guy I used to date who&#8217;s about to be force-fed wine and cheese if he doesn&#8217;t get to the point.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay, uh, here it is. Is it cheating if a guy has a girlfriend&#8230;</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, probably.</p>
<p>Leonard: Come on.</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m sorry, go ahead.</p>
<p>Leonard: I met this girl, and she&#8217;s great. We have a lot in common.</p>
<p>Penny: Did you guys do it?</p>
<p>Leonard: No. We just made out a little.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, look at you, you bad boy. Did you tell her about Priya?</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, I was gonna, but there were too many tongues in my mouth.</p>
<p>Penny: That&#8217;s gross.</p>
<p>Leonard: Here&#8217;s the thing, I, I&#8217;m not one of those guys who sneaks around and sleeps with more than one woman.</p>
<p>Penny: Well, good for you.</p>
<p>Leonard: The problem is, I want to be one of those guys.</p>
<p>Penny: So sleep with the new girl and lie to Priya.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, that&#8217;s not who I am.</p>
<p>Penny: All right, then break it off with the new girl.</p>
<p>Leonard: Now, let&#8217;s not do anything rash. She&#8217;s really hot.</p>
<p>Penny: If you like this girl so much, why don&#8217;t you just end things with Priya?</p>
<p>Leonard: Priya and I are in love. I think we could get married someday.</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, you&#8217;re looking for a way to sleep with both women and have everybody be happy about it.</p>
<p>Leonard: Now we&#8217;re getting somewhere.</p>
<p>Penny: What does your gut tell you?</p>
<p>Leonard: Go ask Penny; she&#8217;ll know what to do.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. Sheldon is adjusting a device on his arm.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Hello, Howard. I&#8217;ve realized that you scaring me was all in jest. Allow me to say, job well done. <em>(Grabs his own hand. Electrocutes himself.) </em>Perfect.</p>
<p>Leonard: What are you doing?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Science. You wouldn&#8217;t understand.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, well, see you. I&#8217;m going out. <em>(Starts to go out. Doesn&#8217;t.)</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: I thought you were leaving the apartment.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, me, too. I can&#8217;t make up my mind.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Are you concerned because the world is filled with big dogs and bullies?</p>
<p>Leonard: No. I&#8217;m having a moral crisis.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, if it&#8217;s of any help, I&#8217;ve read all the great moral philosophers, including Dr. Seuss.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, what the hell. I&#8217;m supposed to go see that girl from the comic book store, Alice, but I don&#8217;t know if I should, because I&#8217;m going out with Priya, but she&#8217;s in India.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right. So the topic at hand is sexual fidelity. Probably won&#8217;t be relying on Seuss here. Although One Fish, Two Fish, Red Fish, Blue Fish might be surprisingly applicable. Go on.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, they say at the end of your life, you regret the stuff you didn&#8217;t do more than the stuff that you did, and I&#8217;m pretty sure Alice is the stuff I want to do.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You know, the German philosopher, Friedrich Nietzsche, believed that morality is just a fiction used by the herd of inferior human beings to hold back the few superior men.</p>
<p>Leonard: That actually does help.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s worth noting that he died of syphilis.</p>
<p>Leonard: Screw it, I&#8217;m going.</p>
<p>Sheldon: On your way home, will you pick up some orange juice?</p>
<p>Leonard: Do you mind? I&#8217;m questioning a lot of things in my life right now.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Is one of those things your fondness for orange juice?</p>
<p>Leonard: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Great. Tropicana, no pulp.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Howard&#8217;s house. Sheldon rings the doorbell.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Hey, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Bernadette <em>(off)</em>: Who is it?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s me, Sheldon, Mrs. Wolowitz.</p>
<p>Howard: That&#8217;s not my mom, it&#8217;s Bernadette.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Really? That&#8217;s very unsettling.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hi, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hi.</p>
<p>Howard: What&#8217;s up?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It just occurred to me that I never formally congratulated you on your pending nuptials. So I hopped on the first bus and hightailed it down here to shake your hand. Put &#8216;er there, you old so-and-so.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, I, I&#8217;m gonna see you at work in 12 hours, don&#8217;t you think it could have waited until then?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Holy smoke, why didn&#8217;t I think of that? You&#8217;re a better man than I, Howard Wolowitz. You put &#8216;er there, you son of a gun!</p>
<p>Howard: Whatever. <em>(Takes his hand. Starts to be electrocuted) </em>My&#8230; oh&#8230; it&#8217;s&#8230; <em>(Clutches heart and collapses)</em></p>
<p>Bernadette: Oh, my God, Howard! What did you do?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It was a harmless Halloween prank. Look.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howard has a heart condition! You know that!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, I thought he made that up. Isn&#8217;t hypochondria a common idiosyncrasy of Jewish people?</p>
<p>Bernadette: This is adrenaline, we&#8217;re gonna have to inject it into his heart.</p>
<p>Sheldon: We are?</p>
<p>Bernadette: You are. I&#8217;m not strong enough to get it through his chest plate, and we&#8217;ve only got one shot.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, no! I can&#8217;t!</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hurry! We&#8217;re running out of time!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Just do it!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, God! One, two, three!</p>
<p>Howard: Trick or treat, bubbeleh.</p>
