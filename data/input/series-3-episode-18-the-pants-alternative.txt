		<h2 class="title">Series 3 Episode 18 &#8211; The Pants&nbsp;Alternative</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The university cafeteria.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Okay in Avatar when they have sex in Pandora they hook up their ponytails, so we know their ponytails are like their junk.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, so?</p>
<p>Raj: So, when they ride horses and fly on the birds, they also use their ponytails.</p>
<p>Howard: What&#8217;s your point?</p>
<p>Raj: My point is, if I were a horse or a bird, I&#8217;d be very nervous around James Cameron.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It amazes me how you constantly obsess over fictional details when there are more important things in the real world to worry about. For example, why wasn&#8217;t William Shatner in the new Star Trek movie?</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, Sheldon, I was up in the administration office, and I happened to overhear the name of the winner of this year&#8217;s Chancellor&#8217;s Award for Science.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And you want to rub my nose in the fact that my contributions are being overlooked again? I am the William Shatner of theoretical physics. All right, I&#8217;ll play. What self-important, preening fraud are they honouring this year?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, I&#8217;m so glad you asked it like that. You.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I won?</p>
<p>Leonard: You won.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I won! This is astonishing. Not that I won the award, no one deserves it more. Actually, I guess I misspoke. It&#8217;s not astonishing, more like inevitable. I&#8217;m not sure what to do first. Maybe I should call my mother. Wait! I know, I&#8217;m going to conduct an interview with myself and post it online.</p>
<p>Raj: Well, good for him.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, the one thing the William Shatner of theoretical physics needed was an ego boost.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. The guys are watching Avatar in 3D. All are wearing 3D glasses except Raj.</em></p>
<p>Howard: Didn&#8217;t it look like that spear was going to go right through your skull?</p>
<p>Raj: No.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, you didn&#8217;t want a Slurpee at 7-Eleven, you don&#8217;t get glasses.</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(phone rings)</em>: Oh, that will be another congratulatory call for me. Uh, mute, please.</p>
<p>Howard: Uh, hang on, flaming arrow.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hello? Oh, Chancellor Morton, how are you, sir? Yes, I was expecting your call <em>(aside)</em> three years ago. I see. Wait. What happens if I choose not to give a speech? Uh-huh. And if I don&#8217;t want to forfeit the award? Well, you&#8217;ve got that tied up in a neat little bow. All right. Thank you. <em>(Hangs up) </em>Problem.</p>
<p>Leonard: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: They expect me to give a speech at the banquet. I can&#8217;t give a speech.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, no, you&#8217;re mistaken. You give speeches all the time. What you can&#8217;t do is shut up.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, before the movie, you did 20 minutes on why guacamole turns brown. It turned brown while you were talking.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I am perfectly comfortable speaking to small groups. I cannot speak to large crowds.</p>
<p>Leonard: What, to you, is a large crowd?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Any group big enough to trample me to death. General rule of thumb is 36 adults or 70 children.</p>
<p>Penny: Sheldon, congratulations. Brought you cheesecake from work. You know, &#8217;cause of your award, not because a busboy sneezed on it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m not accepting the award.</p>
<p>Penny: Why not?</p>
<p>Howard: Turns out the great Sheldon Cooper has stage fright.</p>
<p>Penny: That&#8217;s no reason to back out. You know, I once got a pretty big honour in high school, and I was terrified about appearing in front of a big crowd, but I went through with it, and you know what? The world looked pretty darn good sitting on a haystack in the back of a Ford F-150 as a member of the Corn Queen&#8217;s court.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thank you. Yeah, I&#8217;ll bear that in mind if I&#8217;m ever nominated for the Hillbilly Peace Prize.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon, you&#8217;re being ridiculous.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Am I? Let me tell you a story.</p>
<p>Howard: Where&#8217;s 70 children when you need ‘em?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I was 14 and graduating summa cum laude from college. Summa cum laude is Latin for with highest honours.</p>
<p>Penny: I just love how you always skip over the part where no one asks.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I was valedictorian and expected to give an address. Even now, I can remember that moment when I walked up to the podium and looked out at the crowd. There must have been thousands of people. My heart started pounding in my chest. I began to hyperventilate. My vision became blurry, and before I knew it&#8230; oh, dear. <em>(He faints.)</em></p>
<p>Penny: Oh, my God.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sheldon? Sheldon, are you okay?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Don&#8217;t trample me.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The stairwell. Sheldon is on the phone.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Come on, Mother, you know why I can&#8217;t accept the award. With all due respect, I don&#8217;t think praying will help. No, I have not heard the song, Jesus, Take the Wheel. No, no, no, no, no, no, no, you don&#8217;t need to start singing it. Yes, I&#8217;ll buy it on the iTunes, Mother. Good-bye, Mother. <em>(Enters apartment. The others are sitting as if waiting for him.) </em>Hello.</p>
<p>Leonard: Sit down, we want to talk to you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Am I in trouble? Did my mother call you?</p>
<p>Penny: Just sit.</p>
<p>Leonard: We think we can help you with your stage fright.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I doubt that. I haven&#8217;t figured out a way, and I&#8217;m much smarter than all of you.</p>
<p>Penny: Yes, but you&#8217;re not smarter than all of us put together.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I&#8217;m sorry, that is what I meant.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, your problem is, you&#8217;re trying to do this all by yourself.</p>
<p>Leonard: We can help you. We can be your team. Like, uh, Professor Xavier and his X-Men.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I do like the X-Men.</p>
<p>Penny: Did I see X-Men?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, we watched it last week. You said you liked it.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh. I say a lot of things, sweetie. So, how about it, Sheldon?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t know. If you&#8217;re my X-Men, what are your powers?</p>
<p>Penny: Okay. Well, I am going to take you shopping, get you a nice suit. Might give you more confidence.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s not exactly a mutation that would get you into Xavier&#8217;s School for Gifted Youngsters, but go on. Leonard?</p>
<p>Leonard: I thought I could try to analyze you and get to the root of your anxiety.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What qualifies you to attempt to understand my mind?</p>
<p>Leonard: My mother is a highly regarded psychiatrist, and I&#8217;ve been in therapy ever since she accused me of breast-feeding co-dependently.</p>
<p>Howard: Raj says he can teach you, what did you call it? I don&#8217;t know, some Indian meditation crap.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I see. Well, I assume, since the rest of you have set the bar so low, you&#8217;re saving the most impressive contribution for last. Go ahead, Howard. Dazzle me.</p>
<p>Howard: My power is the ability to pretend like I give a damn about your piddly-ass problems. And that&#8217;s 24-7, buddy.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And I appreciate the pretence.</p>
<p>Penny: So, what do you say, Sheldon? Are we your X-Men?</p>
<p>Sheldon: No. The X-Men were named for the X in Charles Xavier. Since I am Sheldon Cooper, you will be my C-Men.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, that&#8217;s not a good name.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment. Raj is lighting candles. Indian music is playing.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Okay, Sheldon. I&#8217;m going to be leading you through a series of meditation exercises. These methods come from the ancient gurus of India and have helped me overcome my own fears.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And yet, you can&#8217;t speak to women.</p>
<p>Raj: True, but thanks to meditation, I am able to stay in the same room with them without urinating. Now, close your eyes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay, but don&#8217;t punch me.</p>
<p>Raj: What?</p>
<p>Sheldon: When I was little, my sister would say to me, close your eyes, you&#8217;ll get a surprise, and then she&#8217;d punch me.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m not going to punch you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s what my sister used to say.</p>
<p>Raj: Do you want to do this or not?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry. Proceed.</p>
<p>Raj: All right. Imagine yourself in the one place you feel most at home. Where is that?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Sim City. More specifically, the Sim City I designed, Sheldonopolis.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, you&#8217;re in Sheldonopolis.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Where exactly? Sheldon Square? Sheldon Towers? Sheldon Stadium, home of the Fighting Sheldons?</p>
<p>Raj: Whatever you like.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I thought this was supposed to be a guided meditation.</p>
<p>Raj: Fine. You&#8217;re in Sheldon Square.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Really? This time of year? It&#8217;s a bit nippy.</p>
<p>Raj: Then, put on a sweater.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Suppose I could run downtown and pick up something at Shel-Mart.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, whatever. Just go buy a sweater.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You know, the nice thing about Shel-Mart is I own it, so I get a 15% discount.</p>
<p>Raj: You own the damn thing. Just take a freaking sweater!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Look, I didn&#8217;t turn a profit last quarter by taking product off the shelves willy-nilly.</p>
<p>Raj: All right. You&#8217;ve paid for a sweater, and you&#8217;re in Sheldon Square.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hang on. It&#8217;s a cardigan. I have to button it. Oh, no.</p>
<p>Raj: What now?</p>
<p>Sheldon: A Godzilla-like monster is approaching the city. I have to get my people to safety. People of Sheldonopolis, this is your mayor. Follow me. If the children can&#8217;t run, leave them behind. Oh, the simulated horror! <em>(Sound of door slamming) </em>Raj? Just as I suspected. Meditation is nothing but hokum.</p>
<p><em>Scene: A clothing store.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: I question your premise. How is a new suit going to prevent me from passing out in front of a ballroom full of people?</p>
<p>Penny: It&#8217;ll give you confidence. You know, sometimes when I&#8217;m feeling all stressed out about something, I go out and buy a cute top or a fun skirt and I have a whole new outlook on life.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Don&#8217;t you eventually realize you&#8217;re just the same stressed out person in a cute top or a fun skirt?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, that&#8217;s when I buy shoes. Now, let&#8217;s see what we&#8217;ve got. Ooh! This is nice.</p>
<p>Sheldon: It&#8217;s only one colour.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, so?</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s a lot of money for only one colour.</p>
<p>Penny: Fine. Why don&#8217;t you pick out what you like.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hmm. <em>(Cut to Sheldon exiting changing room in a loud check suit). </em>This is pretty sharp.</p>
<p>Penny: No, you&#8217;re wrong.</p>
<p>Sheldon<em> (now in a sparkly green suit with rhinestones): </em>This is great. I had a suit like this when I was six. <em>(Cut to Sheldon exiting in a white dinner suit with tails) </em>Okay, I think we have a winner.</p>
<p>Penny: Where the hell d’you find that?</p>
<p>Sheldon: In the prom department.</p>
<p>Penny: It&#8217;s ridiculous.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Says the former member of the Corn Queen&#8217;s Court.</p>
<p>Penny: Please just try this one on.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay. But anything I put on now is only going to suffer in comparison. <em>(Goes into changing room. Comes out in black suit looking terrific.) </em>This is absurd. I look like a clown.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: So, Sheldon, how you doing?</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s how you start a psychotherapy session? How am I doing? I was promised a riverboat journey into the jungles of my subconscious. Instead, I get the same question I hear from the lady who slices my bologna at Ralph&#8217;s.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;m sorry, I&#8217;ll start again.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Would it be helpful to you if I told you about my dreams?</p>
<p>Leonard: Um, I don&#8217;t know, maybe.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I recently had a dream that I was a giant. But everything around me was to scale, so it all looked normal.</p>
<p>Leonard: How did you know you were a giant if everything was to scale?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I was wearing size a million pants.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why don&#8217;t we just talk?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Ah, the talking cure. Classical Freudian, good choice. If it will help speed things along, uh, my answers to the standard Rorschach ink blot test are A, a bat, B, a bat, C, a bat, and D, my father killing my mother with a hypodermic needle.</p>
<p>Leonard: Why don&#8217;t I just start? Sometimes people have trouble accepting accolades if, on a subconscious level, they don&#8217;t feel they deserve them. Do you think maybe that&#8217;s what&#8217;s happening here?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Really, Leonard? You&#8217;re just going to try to recycle Adler&#8217;s doctrine of the inferiority complex? I could probably get that from the woman at Ralph&#8217;s. And she&#8217;d let me taste some pieces of cheese for free.</p>
<p>Leonard: But it could be part of your problem. Let me give you an example. When I was eight, I won a ribbon at the science fair for my project, &#8220;Do Lima Beans Grow Better to Classical Music.&#8221; But my mother pointed out that it was just a rehash of my brother&#8217;s earlier &#8220;Do Lima Beans Grow Worse to Rock &#8216;n&#8217; Roll.&#8221; I felt so guilty, I gave the ribbon back.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And how did that make you feel?</p>
<p>Leonard: Terrible. I worked really hard on that project. I stayed up all night singing the clown&#8217;s aria from Pagliacci to a lima bean sprout.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Go on.</p>
<p>Leonard: It wasn&#8217;t my fault. I had never seen my brother&#8217;s project. And my mother could&#8217;ve told me before instead of at the ceremony in front of everyone.</p>
<p>Sheldon: So, I hear you saying you&#8217;re angry with your mother.</p>
<p>Leonard: Damn right, I&#8217;m angry with my mother. For God&#8217;s sake, I was eight years old. She humiliated me. That&#8217;s when the bed-wetting started again.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Thank you, Leonard.</p>
<p>Leonard: For what?</p>
<p>Sheldon: If someone as damaged as you can find his way to crawl out of bed each morning, I think I can face a simple award ceremony.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wait, that&#8217;s it? I thought we had a whole hour!</p>
<p><em>Scene: The award ceremony.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: Good evening, ladies and gentlemen. My name is Dr. Leonard Hofstadter, and it is my very great honour to introduce the winner of this year&#8217;s Chancellor&#8217;s award for Science and my good friend, Dr. Sheldon Cooper. But before I do, I&#8217;d like to share with you a letter from Sheldon&#8217;s mother, who couldn&#8217;t be here tonight. Isn&#8217;t that nice? His mother sent him a letter. She&#8217;s proud of him. I wonder what that feels like. Dear Shelly. That&#8217;s what she calls him. Shelly, it&#8217;s a pet name. You know what my mother&#8217;s pet name for me is? Leonard. But I digress. Dear Shelly. I am so proud of&#8230; <em>(continues as background noise)</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, dear.</p>
<p>Penny: What&#8217;s the matter?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m getting dizzy.</p>
<p>Raj: Don&#8217;t worry. You&#8217;re surrounded by your C-Men.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I can&#8217;t do this. I&#8217;m going to faint.</p>
<p>Penny: Here, drink this. It&#8217;ll relax you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Alcohol? I don&#8217;t drink alcohol.</p>
<p>Penny: Fine, faint.</p>
