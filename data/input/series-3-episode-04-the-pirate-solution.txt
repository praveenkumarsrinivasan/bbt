		<h2 class="title">Series 3 Episode 04 &#8211; The Pirate&nbsp;Solution</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: This movie baffles me every time we watch it.</p>
<p>Leonard: What do you mean?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Well, the instructions are very clear, don&#8217;t feed the gremlins after midnight, don&#8217;t get the gremlins wet. How hard is that?</p>
<p>Penny <em>(arriving)</em>: Hi, guys. Hi, honey.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey.</p>
<p>Howard: Ooh, we&#8217;re honey now, are we?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes. Since their relationship became carnal, Penny has upgraded his designated term of endearment, thus distinguishing him from those she calls sweetie, usually in an attempt to soften a thinly-veiled insult.</p>
<p>Penny: You&#8217;re boring people sweetie.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Although, sometimes, she omits the veil entirely.</p>
<p>Penny: So, what are you guys doing?</p>
<p>Howard: Celebrating Columbus Day.</p>
<p>Leonard: We&#8217;re watching Goonies, Gremlins and Young Sherlock Holmes. They were all written by Chris Columbus.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay. What do you watch on Thanksgiving?</p>
<p>Sheldon: The parade.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, you know, that reminds me, I usually go back to Nebraska for thanksgiving, but this year they&#8217;re calling it off on account of my brother&#8217;s trial.</p>
<p>Leonard: What&#8217;s he on trial for?</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, just a big misunderstanding. You know, you&#8217;d actually like my brother, he&#8217;s kind of a chemist. Anyway, I was thinking I&#8217;d have thanksgiving here, and you are all invited.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh. I&#8217;ll be there.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Will you be serving cranberry jelly or cranberry sauce?</p>
<p>Penny: I guess I could serve both.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You guess? You don&#8217;t seem to have much of a handle on this.</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, I really wish I could, Penny, but every year my mother has all the relatives over and cooks up her famous tur-briska-fil.</p>
<p>Penny: Tur-briska-fil?</p>
<p>Howard: Turkey stuffed with a brisket stuffed with gefilte fish. It&#8217;s not as good as it sounds.</p>
<p>Penny: Raj, what about you?</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, he usually comes to my house. Right, pal? <em>(Raj bursts into tears and runs away) </em>All right, this year, you don&#8217;t have to eat the tur-briska-fil. I don&#8217;t even chew it. I swallow it like pills.</p>
<p><em>Credits sequence. </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>Scene: A few moments later.</em></p>
<p>Leonard: So, what&#8217;s going on with Raj?</p>
<p>Howard: Well, the good news is, he has no problem with my mother&#8217;s tur-brka-fil.</p>
<p>Penny: Hard to believe, but go on.</p>
<p>Howard: The bad news is, he says he&#8217;s getting deported.</p>
<p>Leonard: What do you mean, he&#8217;s getting deported?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I believe it means that the U.S. Government is going to expel him from the country. He could then either return to his native India, emigrate to another country that&#8217;s willing to accept him, or wander the high seas as a stateless pirate. Personally, I&#8217;d choose pirate.</p>
<p>Howard: Penny, would you mind stepping outside so we can speak to him?</p>
<p>Penny: Ugh, fine. But the man really needs to work on his girl issues.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Another reason to consider a life of piracy. Even today, I understand that&#8217;s an all-male profession.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, she&#8217;s gone.</p>
<p>Raj: Sorry. I lost my cool.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, what&#8217;s going on?</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, here&#8217;s the deal, six months ago, my research testing the predicted composition of trans-Neptunian objects ran into a dead end.</p>
<p>Howard: So?</p>
<p>Raj: So, my visa&#8217;s only good as long as I’m employed at the university, and when they find out I&#8217;ve got squat, they&#8217;re going to cut me off. By the way, when I say squat, I mean diddly-squat. I wish I had squat.</p>
<p>Leonard: So, wait, what have you been doing for the past six months?</p>
<p>Raj: You know, checking e-mail, updating my facebook status, messing up wikipedia entries. Hey, did you know Netflix lets you stream movies on your computer now?</p>
<p>Sheldon: And you&#8217;ve continued to take the university&#8217;s money under false pretences? Highly unethical for an astrophysicist. Although practically mandatory for a pirate.</p>
<p>Raj: I don&#8217;t want to go back to India. It&#8217;s hot and loud, and there&#8217;s so many people. You have no idea, they&#8217;re everywhere.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, guys, think, how do we keep Raj in the country?</p>
<p>Penny <em>(from outside)</em>: Why doesn&#8217;t he just get another job?</p>
<p>Howard <em>(after Raj whispers to him)</em>: What are you asking me for? I don&#8217;t know if you can talk now or not.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The university cafeteria.</em></p>
<p>Raj: Oh, beef, I&#8217;m going to miss you so much. Do you know, at the Mumbai McDonald&#8217;s, you can&#8217;t get a Big Mac? All you can get is a Chicken Maharaja Mac. And the special sauce, curry, which, in India,</p>
<p>believe you me, is really not that special.</p>
<p>Leonard: Don&#8217;t worry, you&#8217;ll find another job.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah let me start practicing for it. Do you want fries with that Maharaja Mac?&#8221;</p>
<p>Sheldon: Hi, Leonard. Hello, Raj.</p>
<p>Raj: Hello Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Forgive me, as you know, I&#8217;m no adept at reading facial cues, but I&#8217;m going to take a stab here, you&#8217;re either sad or nauseated.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m sad.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I was going to say sad. I don&#8217;t know why I hedged.</p>
<p>Raj: What are you eating?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Elbow macaroni with ground hamburger and tomato sauce.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, beefaroni. I think I&#8217;ll miss you most of all.</p>
<p>Leonard: I&#8217;ve always been a little confused about this. Why don&#8217;t Hindus eat beef?</p>
<p>Raj: We believe cows are gods.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Not technically. In Hinduism, cattle are thought to be like God.</p>
<p>Raj: Do not tell me about my own culture, Sheldon! In the mood I&#8217;m in, I&#8217;ll take you out, I swear to cow!</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry.</p>
<p>Raj: Me, too. I’m just, I&#8217;m a little on edge.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Understandable. Your entire life seems to be crumbling around you, and your future appears bleak at best.</p>
<p>Raj: Thank you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: And you&#8217;re wrong about Hinduism and cows.</p>
<p>Howard <em>(arriving)</em>: Hey, Raj, guess what. Professor Laughlin is looking for someone to join the stellar evolution research team.</p>
<p>Raj: You, you’re kidding. That’s fantastic!</p>
<p>Howard: Come on, what are you waiting for? Call him and set up an interview.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m on it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s happy, right?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Nailed it.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Professor Laughlin’s office.</em></p>
<p>Prof Laughlin: Dr. Koothrappali, come on in. I was surprised to hear you were interested In joining our little team. Giving up on those trans-Neptunian objects, are we?</p>
<p>Raj: No, no, it&#8217;s a very promising area. In a perfect world I&#8217;d spend several more years on it. But I just couldn&#8217;t pass up the opportunity to work with you on your tremendously exciting and not yet conclusively disproved hypothesis.</p>
<p>Prof Laughlin: Splendid. Uh, please sit down. Can I offer you a sherry?</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s a little early, isn&#8217;t it?</p>
<p>Prof Laughlin: Not on Proxima Centauri.</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s very good. Jolly amusing, but if you don&#8217;t mind, I&#8217;ll hold off until sunset on titan.</p>
<p>Prof Laughlin: Oh, well done. I have a feeling you&#8217;re going to fit in just fine, Dr. Koothrappali.</p>
<p>Raj: Thank you, sir. <em>(There is a knock on the door. A young woman enters)</em></p>
<p>Woman: I&#8217;m sorry. Am I late?</p>
<p>Prof Laughlin: No, no, no. Right on time. Dr. Koothrappali, may I present Dr. Millstone from MIT. She&#8217;ll be heading up our data analysis team.</p>
<p>Dr Millstone: It&#8217;s nice to meet you, Dr. Koothrappali. I read your paper on Kuiper belt object size distribution. I really enjoyed it. How did you correct for the selection bias? <em>(During this, Raj surreptitiously wanders over to where Prof Laughlin’s drinks are, pours himself a large sherry and knocks it back in one.)</em></p>
<p>Raj: Well, I ran a simulation that allowed me to correct for the observational efficiency.</p>
<p>Dr Millstone: That&#8217;s just fascinating.</p>
<p>Raj: Thank you. Would you like to hear more about it in my hot tub? So, when do I start?</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Howard: What do you mean you didn&#8217;t get the job? How could you not get it?</p>
<p>Raj: You know, he’s British, I’m Indian, ever since Gandhi they haven&#8217;t liked us very much.</p>
<p>Leonard: Wait, are you saying that he discriminated against you? Because we should file a complaint.</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s okay. A complaint has been filed. So, that&#8217;s it. That was my last hope. I&#8217;m going to be deported, sent home in disgrace, exposed to the sardonic barbs of my cousin Sanjay. Or, as you may know him, Dave from AT&amp;T customer service.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m really going to miss you.</p>
<p>Raj: Will you come visit me in India?</p>
<p>Howard: Gee, that&#8217;s, like, a 17-hour flight. How about I meet you halfway?</p>
<p>Raj: Halfway is 600 miles off the coast of Japan.</p>
<p>Howard: Tell you what, we&#8217;ll skype.</p>
<p>Sheldon <em>(arriving)</em>: Gentlemen.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Raj, did you get the job with Professor Laughlin?</p>
<p>Raj: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I assumed as much. But never fear. Like the subordinate male protagonist in countless action movies who disappears half way through the second reel, I have returned to save the day. Odd, usually, he&#8217;s met by cheers. Anyway, I was thinking about exploring the string theory implications of gamma rays from dark matter annihilations, and it occurred to me that I could benefit&#8230;</p>
<p>Leonard: Excuse me, Sheldon, how many reels before the subordinate male protagonist gets to his point?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry, if you didn&#8217;t cheer at my entrance, it&#8217;s too late to buy into the premise. Anyway, I got some extra money from the head of the department, and raj can come work for me.</p>
<p>Raj: You want me to work with you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: For me. You&#8217;re going to have to listen more carefully when you&#8217;re on the job.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, uh, please don&#8217;t take this the wrong way, but I&#8217;d rather swim buck-naked across the Ganges with a paper cut on my nipple and die a slow, agonizing death from a viral infection than work with you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: For me.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Sheldon’s office.</em></p>
<p>Raj <em>(knocking and entering)</em>: Sheldon, are you busy?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Of course I&#8217;m busy.</p>
<p>Raj: Shall I wait?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, please. <em>(After about 30 seconds) </em>How may I help you?</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;ve reconsidered your offer to let me work with you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: For me.</p>
<p>Raj: Yes, for you. I do, however, have a few conditions. First, at all times, I am to be treated as a colleague and an equal. Second, my contributions shall be noted in all published material. And third, you are never allowed to lecture me on Hinduism or my Indian culture.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m impressed, Raj. Those are very cogent and reasonable conditions.</p>
<p>Raj: Thank you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I reject them all.</p>
<p>Raj: Then you leave me no choice. I accept the job.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m sorry, I believe you&#8217;ve misunderstood. I&#8217;m not giving you the job. I&#8217;m simply affording you the opportunity to apply for it. Have a seat, we&#8217;ll get started with the interview.</p>
<p>Raj: Wha&#8230; You&#8217;re kidding!</p>
<p>Sheldon: Please.</p>
<p>Raj: All right.</p>
<p>Sheldon: So, that&#8217;s what you wear to an interview?</p>
<p>Raj: Come on, dude, we&#8217;ve been friends for years.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, pulling strings, are we?</p>
<p>Raj: Sheldon, for God&#8217;s sakes, don&#8217;t make me beg.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Bazinga! You&#8217;ve fallen victim to another one of my classic practical jokes. I’m your boss now. You may want to laugh at that.</p>
<p><em>Scene: The apartment.</em></p>
<p>Penny: Ah, it’s nice having the place to ourselves, isn&#8217;t it?</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh-huh. Now that Raj is working for Sheldon, I don&#8217;t have to chauffeur him around anymore. Plus, yeah, with them working late so much, we get some privacy.</p>
<p>Penny: Mm-hmm. Hey, want to get a little crazy?</p>
<p>Leonard: What are you thinking?</p>
<p>Penny: Let&#8217;s slide over to Sheldon&#8217;s spot a make out.</p>
<p>Leonard: You are a dirty girl.</p>
<p>Penny <em>(as the making out is interrupted by a knock on the door)</em>: Oh, God, how did he know?</p>
<p>Howard: Hello.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hi, Howard.</p>
<p>Howard: Am I interrupting?</p>
<p>Leonard: Little bit, yeah.</p>
<p>Howard: Guess I should have called.</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah, maybe.</p>
<p>Howard: Tonight&#8217;s the night I usually go line dancing with Raj at the palomino.</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh-huh.</p>
<p>Howard: But he&#8217;s working with Sheldon.</p>
<p>Penny: Yes, we know.</p>
<p>Howard: Want me to leave?</p>
<p>Leonard: You know, whatever.</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, I guess I can hang for a little while. So what are we watching? Sex and the city.</p>
<p>Yikes.</p>
<p>Penny: Hey, I happen to love this movie.</p>
<p>Howard: Fine, let&#8217;s watch it. Maybe all our periods will synchronize.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Sheldon’s office.</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: All right, we&#8217;re going to be designing an experiment to look for the annihilation spectrum resulting from dark matter collisions in space.</p>
<p>Raj: Ooh, dark matter. We better bring a flashlight. I was making a joke.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I&#8217;m the boss. I make the jokes.</p>
<p>Raj: Sorry, go ahead and make your joke.</p>
<p>Sheldon: This is not the time for joking. We&#8217;re doing serious research, which requires complete and utter focus.</p>
<p>Raj: All right, let&#8217;s buckle down and work.</p>
<p><em>(To the tune of Eye of the Tiger by Survivor, there is a montage of images, in all of which Sheldon and Raj are staring at the same equation on a whiteboard.) </em></p>
<p>Raj: Sheldon.</p>
<p>Sheldon: What?</p>
<p>Raj: I need an aspirin.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Top desk drawer.</p>
<p>Raj: Thank you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Alright?</p>
<p>Raj: Yes.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Good.</p>
<p><em>(Montage of scenes resumes) </em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>Scene: Penny and Leonard exiting Leonard’s bedroom. </em></p>
<p>Leonard: That was fun. Thank you.</p>
<p>Penny: Leonard, honey, you don&#8217;t have to say thank you every time we have sex.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh. Okay. Tomorrow you&#8217;re going to get a card in the mail. Just throw it away.</p>
<p>Howard: Top o&#8217; the mornin&#8217;o ya!</p>
<p>Leonard: What are you doing here?</p>
<p>Howard: Well, usually, on Sundays, I go with Raj to scam on hippie chicks at the farmers market, but he&#8217;s still working with Sheldon, so I thought I&#8217;d come over here and make you guys scrambled eggs and salami. It&#8217;s the perfect meal for apres l&#8217;amour.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, kill me.</p>
<p>Howard: By the way, I couldn&#8217;t help overhearing your big finish. Bravo, Leonard.</p>
<p>Penny: See, if you had killed me when I said kill me, I wouldn&#8217;t have had to hear that.</p>
<p>Howard: What do you guys think? Want to take in a matinee, maybe go rollerblading, catch a step class?</p>
<p>Penny: Do something.</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah, okay. Um, Howard, we need to talk.</p>
<p>Howard: Sure. &#8216;sup, homes?</p>
<p>Leonard: Uh, please understand that it&#8217;s not that we don&#8217;t want you around, but Penny and I occasionally need some alone time.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh. I get it, I&#8217;m the third wheel. Sorry, I should have seen that. I’ll get out of your way. Uh, you&#8217;re gonna want to eat those eggs while they&#8217;re still hot.</p>
<p>Leonard: Thank you. There&#8217;s lox and cream cheese in the fridge, the bagels are in the oven, I was warming them up.</p>
<p>Penny: That&#8217;s great.</p>
<p>Howard: Ill just hang out with my mom. That&#8217;s always fun.</p>
<p>Leonard: Good.</p>
<p>Penny: Are we terrible people?</p>
<p>Leonard: I don&#8217;t know. What do you want me to do?</p>
<p>Penny: Get him, bring him back.</p>
<p>Leonard: Are you sure?</p>
<p>Penny: Yeah.</p>
<p>Leonard: Okay. Howard come back.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, you guys had me scared for a minute.</p>
<p><em>Scene: Sheldon’s office. Sheldon is writing on the whiteboard.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Raj: No, no, no, no! That rate is much too low for what we&#8217;d expect from this collision. Do you understand that we&#8217;re talking about dark matter colliding in outer space?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Of course I understand. And who are you to tell me about outer space?</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m the astrophysicist. Astro means space.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Astro means star.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, well, let me just tell you, if we were having this argument in my native language, I&#8217;d be kicking your butt.</p>
<p>Sheldon: English is your native language.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay, you got me there, but you&#8217;re wrong about this!</p>
<p>Sheldon: There is a fine line between wrong and visionary. Unfortunately you have to be a visionary to see it.</p>
<p>Raj: My god, you think that every thought that comes out of your head is pure gold. Well, let me tell you something. Some of those thoughts are pure caca.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Caca?</p>
<p>Raj: It means doo-doo.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right! First of all, Dr. Koothrappali, when I first proposed that you work with me&#8230;</p>
<p>Raj: Aha! So I am working with you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: In this context, with me means for me.</p>
<p>Raj: Ah, well, in this context&#8230; <em>(blows raspberry)</em></p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh! If I&#8217;m wrong, prove it.</p>
<p>Raj: Okay. Here&#8217;s where we derive the mass of the dark matter particle.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, no, no, you&#8217;ve misstated the atomic weight of the target.</p>
<p>Raj: Let me finish.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You&#8217;re defacing my work.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m not defacing it, I&#8217;m fixing it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Give me the eraser.</p>
<p>Raj: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I said give it to me.</p>
<p>Raj: Come and get it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Fine. <em>(Uses hand as an eraser. Then realises his hand is dirty) </em>Oh, lord. Dr. Koothrappali, as your superior, I forbid you from writing on my board!</p>
<p>Raj: You are not my superior.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I am in every way.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, yeah? Can you do this?<em> (Performs  complex finger trick*) </em>Nice working with you. I&#8217;m sorry, for you. <em>(Exits. Sheldon stares a moment, then tries to perform the trick. Fails.)</em></p>
<p><em>Scene: Outside Raj’s flat.</em></p>
<p><em> </em></p>
<p>Sheldon: <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Raj. <em>(Knock, knock, knock) </em>Raj.<em> (Knock, knock, knock) </em>Raj.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;m busy.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Doing what? <em>(Performs finger trick again) </em>All right, you&#8217;ve made your point.</p>
<p>Raj: What do you want, Sheldon?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I looked over the board and it turns out you were right.</p>
<p>Raj: So you were wrong.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I didn&#8217;t say that.</p>
<p>Raj: That&#8217;s the only logical inference.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Nevertheless, I didn&#8217;t say it. Anyway, I would like you to come back and work for me.</p>
<p>Raj: For you or with you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: In this context for me could mean with me.</p>
<p>Raj: All right, but I have some conditions.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I reject them all.</p>
<p>Raj: I&#8217;ll take the job. See you Monday.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wait, you have to drive me home.</p>
<p>Raj: How did you get here?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I walked.</p>
<p>Raj: So walk home.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I can&#8217;t. There&#8217;s a big dog outside. On the way home, we can start thinking about methods of optimizing the detector for 500 GEV particles.</p>
<p>Raj: All right. <em>(As they walk down the corridor, Eye of the Tiger starts playing again.)</em></p>
<p><em>(* to perform trick, place both hands together as if praying, then fold two middle fingers over so that they pass to the right of one another, finally rotate right hand anti-clockwise and waggle both middle fingers)<br />

			function JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler() {
				var wrapperID;
				if ( ! jetpackLikesMasterReady ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='22px' width='68px' frameBorder='0' scrolling='no' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				} else {
					$wrapper.find( '.post-likes-widget-placeholder' ).after( "<iframe class='post-likes-widget jetpack-likes-widget' name='" + $wrapper.data( 'name' ) + "' height='55px' width='100%' frameBorder='0' src='" + $wrapper.data( 'src' ) + "'></iframe>" );
				}

				$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' );

				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).load( function( e ) {
					var $iframe = jQuery( e.target );
					$wrapper.removeClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loading' ).addClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-loaded' );

					JetpackLikespostMessage( { event: 'loadLikeWidget', name: $iframe.attr( 'name' ), width: $iframe.width() }, window.frames[ 'likes-master' ] );

					if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
						$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).Jetpack( 'resizeable' );
					}
				});
				setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 250 );
			}
