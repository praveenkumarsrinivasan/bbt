		<h2 class="title">Series 7 Episode 20 &#8211; The Relationship&nbsp;Diremption</h2>
			<div class="entrytext">
				<p><i>Scene: The cafeteria.</i></p>
<p>Leonard: I got to tell you, the more I read about the primordial gravity wave discovery, the more excited I get.</p>
<p>Raj: I know. Being an astrophysicist right now is like being a rock star.</p>
<p>Howard: Only without the sex.</p>
<p>Raj: Yeah, literally, none of it.</p>
<p>Leonard: What do you think about it, Sheldon?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Meh.</p>
<p>Raj: Are you kidding me? This may be the biggest scientific breakthrough of our lifetime. How can you, as a theoretical physicist, not care about this?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Maybe it&#8217;s because I&#8217;m not an elitist. What I&#8217;d like to know is, how does this gravity wave breakthrough help the man on the street? You know? Who&#8217;s looking out for Joe six-pack and all his quantum physics concerns?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, my God, you&#8217;re jealous.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why would I be jealous?</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, I don&#8217;t know. Maybe because the origin of the universe just got proven, the Higgs field just got proven, and you&#8217;ve been working on string theory for the last 20 years and you&#8217;re no closer to proving it than when you started.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yeah, well, I&#8217;ve had a lot on my plate. We happen to live in a golden age of television.</p>
<p>Barry: Excuse me, fewwas. Sowwy for eavesdwopping, but there actually was some big stwing theowy news today out of the Hadwon Cowwider.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Really? Did they find evidence to support extra dimensions or supersymmetry?</p>
<p>Barry: No, but they did find evidence that you&#8217;ll bewieve anything.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Why would you do that? You&#8217;re a string theorist as well.</p>
<p>Barry: Incowwect. I am a stwing pwagmatist. I say I&#8217;m gonna pwove something that cannot be pwoved, I appwy for gwant money, and then I spend it on wiquor and bwoads. Water.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Do you think he&#8217;s right? Am I wasting my life on a theory that can never be proven?</p>
<p>Howard: Maybe. But how great is Game of Thrones?</p>
<p><i>Credits sequence.</i></p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Penny: Hey, you&#8217;re up early.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I couldn&#8217;t sleep.</p>
<p>Penny: I told you those Walking Dead pillowcases were a bad idea.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No, that&#8217;s not it.</p>
<p>Penny: Is something bothering you?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes, but you wouldn&#8217;t understand.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, come on, try me.</p>
<p>Sheldon: All right. I&#8217;ve devoted the prime of my life to string theory and its quest for the compactification of extra dimensions. I&#8217;ve got nothing to show for it, and I feel like a fool.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay. I get it. I mean, not all the jibberjabber in the middle, but I know what it&#8217;s like to put your heart and soul into something and get nothing out of it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You mean your acting career?</p>
<p>Penny: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Your relationship with Leonard?</p>
<p>Penny: No.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Your failed attempt to go back to college?</p>
<p>Penny: No. I&#8217;m saying you and string theory sound like a relationship, and I know what it&#8217;s like to be in one and realize it&#8217;s never gonna turn out the way you want.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I said Leonard, you said no.</p>
<p>Penny: I&#8217;m talking about other guys.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Okay. Well, what do you do?</p>
<p>Penny: You have to have the courage to end the relationship. You know? Break it off, shake hands, walk away.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I don&#8217;t know if I can do that.</p>
<p>Penny: I know it&#8217;s hard, honey, but in the end, that&#8217;s how you grow.</p>
<p>Leonard: Penny, have you seen my good inhaler?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Break it off, shake hands, walk away.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The cafeteria.</i></p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, how&#8217;s dating two women going?</p>
<p>Raj: Um, kind of hit a bump. When I was honest and told Emily she wasn&#8217;t the only person I was seeing, it went great. So I tried the same thing with Lucy.</p>
<p>Howard: And?</p>
<p>Raj: She had mixed feelings. But when I said, Emily was cool with it. Emily&#8217;s the best. Why can&#8217;t you</p>
<p>be more like Emily? Those feelings became less mixed.</p>
<p>Leonard: Women. Who knows what&#8217;ll set &#8216;em off?</p>
<p>Raj: At least now I can focus all my energies on just one girl.</p>
<p>Howard: I hope I get to meet her as soon as possible.</p>
<p>Raj: Why the rush? She isn&#8217;t going anywhere.</p>
<p>Howard: She is. But I like that attitude.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Gentlemen, is anybody interested in my old string theory books?</p>
<p>Raj: You&#8217;re really going to do this?</p>
<p>Sheldon: I am. Yeah, Penny pointed out that what I&#8217;m going through is essentially a breakup. And according to Cosmopolitan magazine, one of the most effective techniques for moving on is to get rid of all reminders of the relationship.</p>
<p>Howard: You&#8217;re reading Cosmo?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Yes. As it turns out, there&#8217;s an article on how to get over a breakup in literally every issue. Anyway, it suggests that I set these on fire, but the smell of burning books reminds me of church picnics in East Texas.</p>
<p>Howard: What&#8217;s this?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, that&#8217;s just a doodle of a hyperelliptic Riemann surface.</p>
<p>Leonard: Oh, yeah. Wasn&#8217;t that the basis of your postdoc fellowship?</p>
<p>Sheldon: It was. This seemed so elegant at the time, but now I realize I was just a simple country boy seduced by a big city theory with variables in all the right places.</p>
<p>Raj: It&#8217;s going to be okay.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I know. As hard as this is, I have to move on. I can&#8217;t keep postulating multidimensional entities and get nothing in return. I have needs, too.</p>
<p>Howard: So, you and Emily still together?</p>
<p><i>Scene: Raj&#8217;s apartment.</i></p>
<p>Howard: I spoke to Bernadette. She&#8217;s free tomorrow night.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, okay, great. But if we&#8217;re really gonna do a double date, then we have to go over some ground rules about Emily.</p>
<p>Howard: Like when it turns out she&#8217;s made of rubber, I don&#8217;t say anything?</p>
<p>Raj: She&#8217;s very real.</p>
<p>Howard: Oh, that&#8217;s what it says on the box. Right next to dishwasher safe.</p>
<p>Raj: See? This is exactly the kind of thing I&#8217;m worried about you saying in front of her.</p>
<p>Howard: I promise I&#8217;ll be on my best behaviour.</p>
<p>Raj: You better be. No jokes about how close I am with my dog. Or the truth about how close I am with my dog.</p>
<p>Howard: You got it.</p>
<p>Raj: No jokes about the year I took ballet.</p>
<p>Howard: You took ballet?</p>
<p>Raj: God, you never listen.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Leonard: Are you sure you want to do this?</p>
<p>Sheldon: The magazine articles suggest that one of the ways to get over a breakup is a new look.</p>
<p>Leonard: What about your old look, well-groomed ventriloquist doll?</p>
<p>Penny <i>(treating Sheldon as a ventriloquist doll)</i><span style="font-style:normal;">: Oh</span> my God, I do look like that.</p>
<p>Sheldon: You stop it.</p>
<p>Penny: So, how do you want me to cut it?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, how about Bill Gates meets Nikola Tesla?</p>
<p>Leonard: So, business in the front, science in the back.</p>
<p>Penny: Or I don&#8217;t cut it and maybe just style it a little.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, I&#8217;m in your hands. Do a good job and I&#8217;ll tell you Cosmo&#8217;s ten dynamite tips to enjoy your PMS.</p>
<p>Penny: Can you keep him quiet? Maybe jangle some keys in front of him?</p>
<p>Leonard: That doesn&#8217;t work any more. He just thinks I&#8217;m taking him to the doctor. So, now that you&#8217;re no longer invested in string theory, what are you gonna pursue?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, there are so many exciting areas. Black holes, dark matter.</p>
<p>Penny: Oh, Leonard was telling me about dark matter, but I didn&#8217;t really understand it.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Don&#8217;t feel bad. Neither does he.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, what do you think?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Have you ever even seen a picture of Tesla?</p>
<p>Leonard: It actually looks good, Sheldon.</p>
<p>Penny: Doesn&#8217;t it?</p>
<p>Leonard: Yeah.</p>
<p>Sheldon: I suppose I could see myself in a scientific boy band. Of course, I&#8217;d be the dreamy one and the smart one.</p>
<p>Amy: Sorry I&#8217;m late. What did you do?</p>
<p>Penny: I gave him a new look. It&#8217;s cute, huh?</p>
<p>Amy: Yeah, it&#8217;s cute. That&#8217;s the problem. I don&#8217;t need other girls to see him walking around like sex on a stick.</p>
<p>Sheldon: She&#8217;s right. I&#8217;m too hot.</p>
<p><i>Scene: A restaurant.</i></p>
<p>Bernadette: I&#8217;m excited to meet Emily.</p>
<p>Howard: Me, too. I just hope he doesn&#8217;t blow it.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Why would you say that?</p>
<p>Howard: Because he&#8217;s Raj, that&#8217;s his thing. Beckham can bend it, Ralph can wreck it, Raj can blow it. Look, I don&#8217;t want it to happen, but odds are he&#8217;s gonna find a way to&#8230; Oh, crap, I know that girl.</p>
<p>Bernadette: How?</p>
<p>Howard: In a bad way, very bad.</p>
<p>Raj: Hey, guys.</p>
<p>Howard: Whatever you hear tonight, just remember I love you. Hey.</p>
<p>Raj: Emily, these are my good friends, Howard and Bernadette. Guys, this is Emily.</p>
<p>Emily: Nice to meet you.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Hi.</p>
<p>Emily: Have we met before?</p>
<p>Howard: Uh, no. I, I don&#8217;t, I don&#8217;t think so.</p>
<p>Emily: You sure? You look familiar.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, you sure don&#8217;t. You I know, you I know, you? Total stranger. Even if you had yummy candy, I would not get in your van.</p>
<p>Emily: Did you go to that spin class on Green Street?</p>
<p>Howard: That must be it.</p>
<p>Raj: Since when do you go to spin class?</p>
<p>Howard: Wow. Now who doesn&#8217;t listen?</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Amy: Sheldon, your food&#8217;s getting cold. What are you doing?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, looking through my textbooks for a new field of inquiry. Why do we have a geology book? Leonard, did you throw a children&#8217;s party while I was in Texas?</p>
<p>Penny: Wait. What&#8217;s wrong with geology?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Let me put this in a way you&#8217;ll understand, Penny. You remember how you explained to me that the Kardashians aren&#8217;t real celebrities? Well, geology is the Kardashians of science.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Have you considered studying standard model physics?</p>
<p>Sheldon: You want me to give up string theory for something that&#8217;s less advanced? You know, why don&#8217;t you break up with Penny and start dating a brown bear?</p>
<p>Penny: Like you could get a brown bear.</p>
<p>Leonard: Hey, I&#8217;ve got a good job. I could buy salmon. You don&#8217;t know.</p>
<p>Amy: Maybe you could make your new field of study the calculation of nuclear matrix elements.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, please. If I wanted to take up a fad, I&#8217;d get a tramp stamp.</p>
<p>Leonard: What about loop quantum gravity?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Oh, Duchess, look at me. My quantum gravity&#8217;s positively loopy.</p>
<p>Penny: Who&#8217;s the duchess?</p>
<p>Leonard: One of the people that lives in his head.</p>
<p>Amy: I hope he&#8217;s this distraught if he ever breaks up with me.</p>
<p>Leonard: Well, if he does, I&#8217;ll see if my bear has a friend.</p>
<p>Penny: Sheldon, have you ever considered not rushing into something new? I mean, why don&#8217;t you take your time, enjoy your freedom? Maybe something new to study will find you.</p>
<p>Sheldon: That&#8217;s actually not bad advice. You know, I didn&#8217;t seek out string theory. It just hit me over the head one day.</p>
<p>Amy: How does that happen?</p>
<p>Sheldon: A bully chased me through the school library and hit me over the head with the biggest book he could find.</p>
<p>Penny: Okay, how about we toast your newfound freedom?</p>
<p>Sheldon: Ah, normally I refrain from alcohol, but since my cerebral cortex is twiddling its proverbial thumbs, why not soak it in grape juice that&#8217;s been predigested by a fungus?</p>
<p>Penny: And you wonder why other children beat you with books. Cheers!</p>
<p><i>Scene: The restaurant.</i></p>
<p>Bernadette: Emily, what do you do?</p>
<p>Emily: I&#8217;m a dermatology resident at Huntington Hospital.</p>
<p>Raj: Mmm, and you know what that means. Someone&#8217;s skin&#8217;s about to be softer than ever.</p>
<p>Emily: This is making me crazy. I know I know you from somewhere.</p>
<p>Raj: Oh, uh, Howard was an astronaut. Maybe that&#8217;s where you saw him.</p>
<p>Emily: Wow. That&#8217;s amazing. What was that like?</p>
<p>Howard: It was the most incredible experience of my life. Up there in the space station, you&#8217;re&#8230;</p>
<p>Emily: Oh, my God, I remember.</p>
<p>Howard: Can I finish my astronaut story?</p>
<p>Emily: It was four years ago.</p>
<p>Howard: Please don&#8217;t say it.</p>
<p>Emily: We were set up on a blind date.</p>
<p>Howard: Please don&#8217;t say it.</p>
<p>Emily: You came to my apartment.</p>
<p>Howard: You&#8217;re saying it.</p>
<p>Raj: Wh, what happened?</p>
<p>Bernadette: Yeah, what happened?</p>
<p>Howard: Okay, I&#8217;ll say it. I was on the way to pick her up. My stomach felt a little funny. When I got there, I asked if I could use her bathroom.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Please don&#8217;t say it.</p>
<p>Howard: One roll of toilet paper and 20 minutes later, I was so humiliated, I snuck out the window and never saw her again.</p>
<p>Emily: You know what else I never saw again, my security deposit.</p>
<p>Howard: I&#8217;m sorry. I tried to unclog it, but you didn&#8217;t have a plunger, and water was spilling out everywhere.</p>
<p>Emily: Right, just water.</p>
<p>Howard: Look, I have felt terrible about this for years, and I&#8217;m glad I have the opportunity to tell you just how sorry I am.</p>
<p>Emily: It&#8217;s fine. All my friends love this story. They call you Clogzilla.</p>
<p>Raj: Get it? Because you clogged up her bathroom like a radioactive monster!</p>
<p>Howard: Yeah, yeah, I get it.</p>
<p>Bernadette: Howie.</p>
<p>Howard: Well, hey, it&#8217;s not like cotton candy comes out of you.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The apartment.</i></p>
<p>Amy: Come on, let&#8217;s get you to bed. You&#8217;ve had a lot to drink.</p>
<p>Sheldon: No more than Penny.</p>
<p>Amy: That&#8217;s what I&#8217;m saying.</p>
<p>Sheldon: Wait, wait, wait, wait, wait. <i>(Knock, knock, knock) </i>Empty room. <i>(Knock, knock, knock) </i>Empty room. <i>(Knock, knock, knock) </i>Empty room. If somebody says come in, I&#8217;m gonna freak out.</p>
<p><i>Scene: The restaurant.</i></p>
<p>Bernadette: So, Emily, why did you decide to specialize in dermatology?</p>
<p>Emily: I like cutting people with knives, and all the other jobs where you get to do that are illegal.</p>

				if ( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
					// We may have a widget that needs creating now
					var found = false;
					while( jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.length > 0 ) {
						// Grab the first member of the queue that isn't already loading.
						wrapperID = jetpackLikesWidgetQueue.splice( 0, 1 )[0];
						if ( jQuery( '#' + wrapperID ).hasClass( 'jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ) ) {
							found = true;
							break;
						}
					}
					if ( ! found ) {
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				} else if ( jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).length > 0 ) {
					// Grab any unloaded widgets for a batch request
					JetpackLikesBatchHandler();

					// Get the next unloaded widget
					wrapperID = jQuery( 'div.jetpack-likes-widget-unloaded' ).first()[0].id;
					if ( ! wrapperID ) {
						// Everything is currently loaded
						setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
						return;
					}
				}

				if ( 'undefined' === typeof wrapperID ) {
					setTimeout( JetpackLikesWidgetQueueHandler, 500 );
					return;
				}

				var $wrapper = jQuery( '#' + wrapperID );
				$wrapper.find( 'iframe' ).remove();

				if ( $wrapper.hasClass( 'slim-likes-widget' ) ) {
